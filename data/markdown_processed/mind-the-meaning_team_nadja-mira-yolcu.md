## Dissertation Project: Expressing Myself: Disavowals and the Mind (Working Title)

The dissertation project focuses on developing an expressivist analysis of negated avowals.

**Background:**
Expressivist theories (citing Wittgenstein 1953; Bar-On 2004, 2015; Finkelstein 2003; Freitag 2014, 2018) have traditionally concentrated on positive avowals. However, negated avowals (disavowals), such as "I don’t hope that it is raining," present a significant challenge to avowal expressivism. It remains unclear what the utterance of a negated avowal is supposed to express—specifically, how one can express the absence of a mental state.

**Goal and Thesis:**
The purpose of this dissertation is to examine negated avowals. The author tentatively claims that, despite the noted difficulties, disavowals can indeed receive an expressivist interpretation. The core proposal is that disavowals constitute cases of expressive denegation, thereby avoiding a descriptivist backlash. Furthermore, an expressivist interpretation of disavowals is expected to contribute to a deeper understanding of various philosophical problems, including Moore’s paradox and the suspension of belief.
