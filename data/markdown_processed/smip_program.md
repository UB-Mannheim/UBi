## PhD Program at the SMiP Group

PhD Candidates develop their dissertation projects jointly with their advisor during the initial months of doctoral studies. The research projects generally fall into three major thematic areas, each offering complementary and intertwined approaches:

### Thematic Research Areas

1. **Modeling Cognition and Social Cognition**

   - *Focus:* Cognition is defined as a summary term for all processes that contribute to our knowledge about the world.
   - [Learn more](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/program/possible-thesis-topics/modeling-cognition-and-social-cognition/)

1. **Modeling Motivation and Affect**

   - *Focus:* Affective states (e.g., feeling happy, relaxed, or fatigued) fluctuate significantly due to influences such as diurnal rhythms, daily events, and self-regulatory behavior.
   - [Learn more](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/program/possible-thesis-topics/modeling-motivation-and-affect/)

1. **Modeling Individual Differences**

   - *Focus:* Interindividual differences in personality attributes and cognitive performance are central to many domains of psychological research.
   - [Learn more](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/program/possible-thesis-topics/modeling-individual-differences/)

Within these thematic areas, projects can adopt two primary perspectives:

- **Model Development Perspective:** Projects focusing primarily on developing new models and statistical refinements.
- **Model Application Perspective:** Projects aiming at innovative applications of existing models to address new research questions.

PhD Candidates benefit from diverse opportunities to discuss their projects with fellow candidates, researchers from the SMiP group, and external experts.

### Qualification Program Support

To support and accelerate the PhD phase, the SMiP group offers a unique [qualification program](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/program/qualification-program/). This program spans 6 [semesters](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/program/semesters/) and includes various teaching activities, such as:

- Core courses in statistical modeling.
- Skill trainings (e.g., academic writing and publishing).
- Workshops on diverse topics led by SMiP group members or renowned international experts from partner institutions.

In addition to coursework, candidates receive support for active participation in teaching and conferences, and have the opportunity to visit external labs in research groups abroad.
