# Multilevel Measurement Models Workshop

**Instructors:** Lesa Hoffman (University of Iowa) & Jonathan Templin (University of Iowa)

This workshop focuses on the use of latent variable measurement models within multilevel sampling designs. The course structure integrates traditional lectures, guided practice in model building, and opportunities for individual data analysis.

### Workshop Content Overview

The workshop is structured across four days, covering increasingly complex modeling concepts:

- **Day 1:** Focuses on latent variable measurement models for normal, binary, and ordinal responses (all presented in slope–intercept form). Introduction to Stan for MCMC estimation.
- **Day 2:** Presents concepts of multilevel models using observed outcomes, transitioning into three-level models for item responses nested within persons nested within clusters.
- **Day 3:** Extends multilevel models to include latent variable measurement models with level-specific discrimination parameters.
- **Day 4:** Makes connections to models where item parameters are treated as random effects instead of fixed effects (e.g., for predicting sources of item difficulty and discrimination, as in explanatory item response models).

*Note: All instructional sessions are recorded for future participant use.*

### Prerequisites and Skills

Participants should have the following knowledge and skills:

1. Familiarity with R software for data analysis.
1. Some familiarity with Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) estimation (i.e., prior experience estimating models with MCMC).
1. Some prior knowledge of latent variable measurement models (e.g., confirmatory factor analysis for continuous responses; item response theory for binary and ordinal responses).
1. Some prior knowledge of multilevel models (e.g., hierarchical linear models, mixed-effects models).

**Tools:** The course uses Stan software run through R (using CMDStanR). No prior experience with Stan is assumed, but participants wishing to use their own devices should install Stan ahead of time. No readings are required beforehand.
