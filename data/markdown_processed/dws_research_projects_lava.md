# LAVA Project: Enhancing AI through Knowledge Graphs

The LAVA research project addresses the critical question of how Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be used to make comprehensible decisions. This initiative, funded by the [Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)](https://www.bmbf.de), is led by the DWS group.

## Understanding Knowledge Graphs (KGs)

Knowledge graphs are fundamental to modern AI and influence daily life, often operating in the background. They generally describe a model used to search for and link information. Examples include voice assistants providing weather forecasts or streaming platforms suggesting movies.

## Project Goal and Scope

The primary aim of the LAVA research project, led by [Prof. Dr. Heiko Paulheim](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/professors/prof-dr-heiko-paulheim/#c98925), is to automatically create and improve knowledge graphs that are integrated into AI systems.

### Focus on Medical Diagnostics

The project is conducted in collaboration with [medicalvalues GmbH](https://medicalvalues.de/), a Karlsruhe-based company specializing in AI solutions for medical diagnostics in laboratories and clinics.

The ultimate goal of LAVA is to develop a certified medical product that significantly aids doctors in making quick and precise diagnoses. In cases of rare diseases, the system will integrate and link diverse data types—such as X-ray images, blood values, and other relevant measurements—using a knowledge graph. This integrated view helps the treating physician determine the necessary next steps.

## Technical Approach: White-Box AI

Heiko Paulheim's team contributes software modules consisting of semi-automated knowledge graphs. These modules ensure that the knowledge graph remains continuously updated and error-free.

The project aims to provide reusable, well-documented components for **white-box AI**.

- **White-Box AI:** Refers to AI models that make the decision-making process transparent and understandable for humans. By using knowledge graphs, users can understand exactly what data a decision is based on.
- **Contrast to Black-Box Models:** This transparency is crucial, unlike "black-box" models (such as Chat GPT), where the reasoning behind an answer is often opaque.
