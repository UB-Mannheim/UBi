### Seminar Abstract

Professor Ottmar Edenhofer will examine why large-scale Carbon Dioxide Removal (CDR) is essential for meeting climate targets, establishing a critical third pillar of climate policy alongside emissions abatement and adaptation.

**Key Focus Areas:**

- **The Challenge:** Climate targets cannot be met without large-scale removal of CO2 from the atmosphere. The main barriers to CDR are no longer purely technical, but institutional, economic, and political, reflected in governance gaps.
- **The Solution:** CDR is presented as a necessary third pillar of climate policy. To scale CDR, new market structures and durable institutions are required.
- **Policy Mechanisms:** Prof. Edenhofer proposes **clean-up certificates**—rights to emit coupled with binding future removal obligations. These certificates offer a pathway to integrate removals into existing carbon market frameworks, such as the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS).
- **Governance:** He calls for a **European Carbon Central Bank** to issue and manage these certificates, oversee net emissions quantities, and correctly value different types of removals.
- **Conclusion:** Viewing CDR as a planetary carbon management system reframes climate policy around the 21st century’s central task: designing the governance needed to enable net-negative emissions.
