# Job Placement and Career Support for CDSE Students

At the end of the fourth year, students are expected to prepare for entering the academic or non-academic job market. The program provides extensive coaching and support for all students entering this phase.

**Available Support:**

- Presentation skills workshops.
- Mock interviews with faculty members.
- Opportunity to schedule a job talk in either a department's field seminar or the internal seminar.

All faculty members in Mannheim are committed to supporting students in their job market endeavors. The activities are guided by the department's placement officer, [Prof. Dr. Michelle Sovinsky](https://www.vwl.uni-mannheim.de/sovinsky/).

*For more information on job market candidates, please visit the [CDSE job market candidates page](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/job-market-candidates/).*

## Job Market Timeline and Requirements

Students must adhere to the following timeline for job market preparation:

- **By September (Start of Year 5):** Students intending to enter the job market must have at least one completed paper, which will serve as their primary "job market paper."
- **By October:** Students must set up a personal webpage. This page can then be linked to their profile on the [CDSE's placement website](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/alumni-placements/cdse-placements/), allowing interested parties to view their profile.

## International Academic Job Market

For students wishing to continue in academia after their doctorate, the program advises participation in the international academic job market, which includes opportunities at national and international organizations and in the private sector throughout the year.

**Key International Meetings:**

- **USA:** The international market takes place every January at the ASSA meeting.
- **Europe:** Alternatives of growing relevance occur in Spain (December) and the UK (London, January).

**Interview Process:**
Successful applicants at any of these meetings will be interviewed by interested universities. If the interviews are successful, students will be invited to present their work at the respective departments ("fly-outs").

**Preparation Deadline:**
Since the international job market meetings occur in December/January, students must have their complete job market package ready by **November**. This package must include:

1. The job market paper.
1. One additional completed paper.
1. One paper currently in progress.
