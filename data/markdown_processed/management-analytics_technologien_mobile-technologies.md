# Research Projects in Management Analytics

## 1. Competence Development in VET Enculturation Processes

**Authors:**

- Viola Deutscher ([https://www.uni-goettingen.de/de/prof.+dr.+viola+deutscher/685786.html](https://www.uni-goettingen.de/de/prof.+dr.+viola+deutscher/685786.html))
- Esther Winther, University of Duisburg-Essen

**Description:**
This DFG-funded research project analyzes competence development in vocational education training (VET) and identifies relevant success factors for commercial apprentices (n = 877). The project focuses on modeling VET enculturation processes at both group and individual levels, including the description of company learning conditions.

The study utilizes a validated competence assessment alongside an instrument designed to record the enculturation conditions of vocational learning. This assessment is conducted longitudinally—at the beginning, middle, and end of initial vocational training. The resulting practical findings aim to help companies improve their educational programs regarding the genesis of professional competences.

## 2. Maximizing Customer Lifetime Value through Strategic Channel Management

**Authors:**

- Gokhan Gecer
- Florian Kraus ([https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/))

**Cooperation Partner:**

- Chair of Sales & Services Marketing

**Description:**
This research addresses how customer engagement with mobile devices has changed online purchasing habits, specifically noting that using mobile apps for shopping increases the Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

The study proposes that online retailers should strategically steer customers toward the mobile channel by offering a permanent discount over the mobile app. While this strategy may decrease short-term income, it has the potential to increase CLV. Using a probability-based CLV model, the research determines:

- To which type of customer an online retailer should offer a discount (those who are either very likely or unlikely to increase their purchasing probability).
- The optimal value of that discount.

The findings emphasize that the discount should be used to increase customer retention, rather than for customer acquisition, and should not be offered to customers who already exhibit a high purchasing probability, as their CLV is already substantial.
