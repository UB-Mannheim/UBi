## Goal-Based Traffic Control System Project

The project addresses the critical issue of traffic congestion in urban centers, which leads to noise and air pollution, particularly during rush hours.

### Project Concept: Adaptive Traffic Control

The core idea is to develop an adaptive traffic control system that manages traffic lights based on a **Goal Recognition** approach.

- **Goal Recognition:** This technique involves predicting or recognizing the goal of observed agents by analyzing a domain model and a sequence of actions already executed by the agent.
- **Improvement:** Unlike static control patterns, which are currently used by many traffic lights, controlling lights based on the recognized destinations of observed traffic participants can significantly reduce congestion.

### Implementation and Methodology

In this Master Team Project, participants will develop a traffic control system within a simulated environment:

- **Simulator:** The system will utilize the **SUMO traffic simulator**.
- **Focus:** The development will focus on implementing the traffic control logic based on recognized goals of observed traffic participants.
- **Learning Objectives:** Participants will gain familiarity with the SUMO simulation tool and advanced goal recognition approaches derived from classical planning concepts.

### Participants

The project is a collaborative effort involving:

- Four students with a strong interest in Artificial Intelligence, particularly classical planning.
- Four software engineering students from Cluj-Napoca.
