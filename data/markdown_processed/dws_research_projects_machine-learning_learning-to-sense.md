# Learning to Sense (L2S)

Learning to Sense (L2S) is a major research initiative focused on the **joint optimization of sensor system designs and neural networks**. It explores the confluence of advanced machine learning techniques and physical sensor system development.

### Project Overview

The project was selected by the German Research Foundation (DFG) as Research Unit 5336. It represents dedicated fundamental research in artificial intelligence, specifically partnering with the interdisciplinary field of sensor system development.

### Academic Affiliations

- **Center:** The research is situated at the [Center for Sensors Systems (ZESS)](http://www.zess.uni-siegen.de/).
- **Primary Institution:** [University of Siegen](https://www.uni-siegen.de/start/index.html.en?lang=en).
- **External Collaboration:** The project involves external expertise from the Machine Learning Group at the [University of Mannheim](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/projects/machine-learning/learning-to-sense/), led by Margret Keuper.

### Research Focus

L2S builds upon over 30 years of experience in fundamental and application-oriented, interdisciplinary research at ZESS, aiming to advance the state-of-the-art in how AI models interact with and are optimized by physical sensor inputs.
