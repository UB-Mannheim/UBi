# CDSS Course Catalog: Fall 2024

This catalog provides an overview of the core doctoral courses offered by the Computational Social Science (CDSS) program at the University of Mannheim.

## 📚 Core Methodological & Quantitative Skills

### Computational Social Science (CSS)

**Description:** CSS is a young research field at the intersection of social science, data science, and computer science. The goal is to gain new insights into society through large amounts of data and the direct observation of human behavior. The course covers foundational studies, theories, and methods, discussing infrastructural, ethical, and legal challenges. It is application-oriented, familiarizing students with CSS methods and implementing them in R. Topics include data management, machine learning, visualization, statistical data analysis, and validation.
**Prerequisites:** None specified (but assumes basic programming skills).
**Assessment:** Regular small assignments (programming homework, developing research questions, and a final project); compulsory attendance; written term paper based on an analysis in R (max. 5000 words), due July 31, 2024.
**Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** Bi-weekly, 04.09.24 – 04.12.24 | Wednesday | 15:30 – 17:00 | B317 in A5, 6 entrance B |
- **Lecturer:** [Prof. Sebastian Stier](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/stier/)

### Advanced Statistical Modeling (Beyond Linear Regression)

**Description:** This course focuses on statistical models and estimators beyond classical linear regression, which is a prerequisite. Topics include violations of asymptotic properties (heteroscedasticity, endogeneity, proxy variables, IV-estimator), maximum likelihood estimation, and Generalized Linear Models (GLS) for binary choice decisions (Logit, Probit), ordinal dependent variables, and count data (Poisson, Negative Binomial). Classes include lab sessions for practice using the statistical package Stata.
**Prerequisites:** Good understanding of the classical linear regression model.
**Assessment:** Regular and active participation in lab sessions; presentation of a weekly exercise (including slides, Stata syntax, and output interpretation); written exam (graded, 90 min).
**Schedule:**

- **Lecture:** 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 | Tuesday | 13:45 – 15:15 | A103 in B6, 23–25 |
- **Tutorial:**

  - Sandra Morgenstern: 05.09.24 – 05.12.24 | Thursday | 10:15 – 11:45 | B318 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### Machine Learning (ML)

**Description:** This course introduces popular topics in machine learning, covering mathematical foundations, algorithmic mechanics, and applied use. It is structured into three blocks:

- **Block A (Foundations):** General overview of the ML landscape and mathematical foundations.
- **Block B (Statistical Learning):** Supervised and unsupervised methods (classification, regression, dimensionality reduction).
- **Block C (Preview):** Advanced techniques (textual/audio-visual data, deep learning, algorithmic fairness).
  **Prerequisites:** None specified.
  **Assessment:** Participants can obtain 6 ECTS points by submitting a short paper presenting an ML application to a social science research problem (max 3,000 words), due January 31, 2025.
  **Schedule:**
- **Seminar:** Bi-weekly, 09.09.24 – 02.12.24 | Monday | 12:00 – 15:15 | 211 in B6, 30–32 |
- **Lecturer:** [Dr. Ruben L. Bach](https://www.mzes.uni-mannheim.de/d7/de/profiles/ruben-l-bach)

### Data Analysis with Python

**Description:** This seminar guides students through comprehensive statistical analysis using Python. It covers statistical modeling, supervised/unsupervised machine learning techniques, and neural networks. The course is structured into three parts:

1. Introduction to Python and Essential ML Libraries.
1. Statistics and Statistical Modeling (from simple regressions to transformer architectures).
1. Principles of Machine Learning and Neural Networks (hands-on core concepts).
   **Prerequisites:** Laptop capable of running comprehensive statistical analyses (>2GB RAM).
   **Assessment:** Pass/fail (regular attendance is mandatory; absence can be compensated by submitting a Python script).
   **Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** 04.10.24 – 08.11.24 | Friday | 09:00 – 13:00 | 310 in B6, 30–32 |

### Causal Inference

**Description:** This course teaches fundamental concepts behind estimating causal effects, including potential obstacles to causal inference. Students learn to estimate causal effects in both randomized and observational settings, with a focus on careful study design.
**Assessment:** Lecture: Participation, written exam (graded, 90 minutes). Tutorial: Homework, oral participation, presentation.
**Schedule:**

### Quantitative Methods in Political Science

**Description:** This course provides an introduction to quantitative methods in political science. The first half focuses on linear regression models (mathematical bases, estimation, assumptions); the second half introduces likelihood theory (binary and count data). The lab sessions focus on practical issues like data preparation, statistical software use, and result interpretation.
**Assessment:** Homework, participation, take-home exam (graded).

## 🏛️ Political Science & International Studies

**Description:** Introduces first-year CDSS doctoral students to theoretically informed research approaches and substantive fields in Mannheim. Faculty members present their research fields and current work, allowing students to discuss potential topics.
**Assignment:** Develop a research project idea informed by faculty presentations (outside your field) and write a potential research design in a short 3-page paper, due October 31st.

### Game Theory (Introduction)

**Description:** Provides a graduate-level overview of game theory, covering concepts like normal form games, Nash equilibria, extensive form games, repeated games, and signaling games. The focus is on training in reading and writing formal models.
**Assessment:** Working in small groups on assignments, final exam (graded).
**Schedule:**

- **Lecture:** 02.09.24 – 02.12.24 | Monday | 10:15 – 11:45 | B244 in A5, 6 entrance B |
- **Tutorial:**

  - 06.09.24 – 06.12.24 | Friday | 12:00 – 13:30 | B317 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### Game Theory (Advanced/Continuation)

**Description:** A continuation of the introductory course, surveying key applications of game theory with an emphasis on theories, methods, and empirics in comparative and international politics. Topics include electoral competition, delegation, and war/crisis bargaining.
**Assessment:** Class discussion, paper presentation, participation, term paper (graded).
**Schedule:**

- **Lecture:** 02.09.24 – 02.12.24 | Monday | 15:30 – 17:00 | B244 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### International Political Economy (IPE)

**Description:** An introduction to current research topics in IPE, covering four major themes:

1. **International Trade:** Opportunities and challenges of global exchange.
1. **International Finance:** Global financial flows and their interaction with stability/crises.
1. **International Development:** Global economic inequality and its links to cooperation/migration.
1. **International Institutions:** The role of international organizations in the global economy.
   **Assessment:** Active participation, term paper (graded).
   **Schedule:**

- **Lecture:** 04.09.24 – 04.12.24 | Wednesday | 10:15 – 11:45 | B244 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### Contextual Effects in Political Behavior

**Description:** Addresses the conceptual, theoretical, and methodological issues in analyzing contextual effects on individual-level political behavior. Students review empirical studies and prepare research papers.
**Assessment:** Oral presentation of a literature review, active participation, term paper (ca. 8,000 words, graded).
**Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** 02.09.24 – 02.12.24 | Monday | 13:45 – 15:15 | B318 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### Human Rights and Repression

**Description:** Introduces foundational and recent research on human rights violations and repression globally. Topics include defining human rights, conditions facilitating repression (role of democratic institutions), and the international community's role.
**Assessment:** Participation, weekly reading, presentation of an empirical study, term paper (graded).
**Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 | Tuesday | 13:45 – 15:15 | B143 in A5, 6 entrance B |

### Welfare States and Crises

**Description:** Studies how welfare states mitigate economic crises (e.g., Great Recession, Great Pandemic). It compares different welfare state responses across European countries, examining job retention policies, unemployment benefits, and pension systems.
**Assessment:** Active participation, mandatory readings, oral presentation, term paper (graded).
**Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 | Tuesday | 10:15 – 00:00 | C216 in A5, 6 |

## 🧠 Psychology & Social Science Disciplines

### Research Design and Methodology

**Description:** A research design is a plan specifying how a project will be carried out. The course helps students develop ideas for their dissertation proposal, addressing questions of theoretical contribution, methodology specification, and empirical analysis.
**Assessment:** Mandatory readings, active participation, homework assignments, presentation of research proposal, and performing as a discussant of peers' proposals.
**Schedule:**

- **Workshop:** 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 | Tuesday | 10:15 – 11:45 | 211 in B6, 30–32 |

### Mental Health in Academia (Doctoral Studies)

**Description:** Familiarizes participants with typical risk factors and challenging constellations encountered during dissertations. The course uses literature-informed group discussions to foster knowledge about mental health and facilitate reflection on personal experiences.
**Assessment:** Willingness to read articles and discuss personal views in guided group discussions.
**Schedule:**

- **Seminar:** Bi-weekly, 18.09.24 – 27.11.24 | Wednesday | 10:15 – 11:45 | Room 409 in L9, 1–2 |

### Computational Psychology (Cognitive/Social)

*Note: Multiple workshops cover related topics (Clinical, Cognitive, Social).*
**Description:** Focuses on research activities in social psychology, or specific areas like cognitive psychology (memory, decision making) or clinical psychology. The goal is to improve research skills and communication of results.
**Assessment:** Active participation, final written exam (graded) or graded homework assignment.
**Schedule:**

- **Clinical Psychology:** Workshop, 05.09.24 – 05.12.24 | Thursday | 13:00 – 14:00 | 016–017 in L 13, 15–17
- **Cognitive Psychology:** Workshop, 02.09.24 – 02.12.24 | Monday | 15:30 – 17:00 | A 102 in B6, 23–25

### Work and Organizational Psychology

**Description:** Provides an overview of core topics, focusing on recent theoretical approaches and empirical findings (meta-analyses). Topics include work motivation, stress and health, leadership, teams, and personnel selection.
**Assessment:** Graded homework assignment.

## 📅 Program Overview

**CDSS Doctoral Thesis Supervision:**

- **Sociology:** Doctoral theses supervised by [professors in the department of Sociology](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/about/professors/sociology/) will be discussed. Check with individual chairs for dates and times.
- **Political Science:** Doctoral theses supervised by [professors in the department of Political Science](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/about/professors/political-science/) will be discussed. Check with individual chairs for dates and times.
- **Psychology:** Check with individual chairs in the [Psychology Department](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/about/professors/psychology/) for dates and times of research colloquia.

**CDSS Colloquia:**
CDSS doctoral students in political science and sociology can choose freely which weekly colloquium to attend. Colloquia must be attended regularly in year two and three of doctoral studies.

- **MZES Colloquium A:** “European Societies and their Integration”
- **MZES Colloquium B:** “European Political Systems and their Integration”
- *Alternative options:* Mannheim Research Colloquium on Survey Methods (MaRCS) or the MZES Social Science Data Lab.
- **Details:** Refer to the [MZES web page](http://www.mzes.uni-mannheim.de/d7/en/news/events/colloquium).
