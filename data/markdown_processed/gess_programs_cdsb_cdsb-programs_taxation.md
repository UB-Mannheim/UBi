# Taxation Program Overview

The Taxation program at the University of Mannheim places a strong emphasis on demanding quantitative methods, which serve as the essential toolkit for state-of-the-art research in this field.

**Program Structure:**

- **First Semester:** Students gain fundamental knowledge of relevant mathematical, econometric, and microeconomic principles.
- **Second Semester:** The curriculum introduces core insights and the current research frontier of taxation research.
- **Advanced Study:** Students then develop their specific strengths and interests through elective courses, allowing the coursework to gradually phase out and create room for the student's own research agenda.

## Research Focus and Expertise

Graduate students work closely with researchers affiliated to the **Leibniz ScienceCampus MannheimTaxation**. This campus is a joint initiative between the University of Mannheim and the ZEW – Leibniz Centre for European Economic Research, designed to consolidate expertise across multiple disciplines: business administration, economics, law, and political science.

MaTax researchers contribute to a wide range of critical topics, including:

- Company taxation
- Public finance
- Innovation taxation
- Taxation of the financial sector
- Tax harmonization in the European Union
- International and European tax law
- Tax competition
- Behavioral responses to taxation
- Tax incidence
