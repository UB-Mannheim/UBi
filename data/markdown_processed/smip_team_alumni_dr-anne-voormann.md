## Research Expertise

Dr. Voormann's research focuses on human cognition, with specific interests in memory, perception, visual attention, and the sequential effects observed during task performance. She is also highly interested in the applied field of human-machine interaction (HMI).

### Key Research Areas:

- **Recognition Memory:** Investigating the cognitive processes involved in recognition decisions. This includes examining differences between multiple recognition decisions and simple recognition decisions using cognitive modeling. She also investigates the role of guessing and the importance of mis-detection within the recognition process.
- **Sequential Effects:** Focusing on common aspects and modeling methods for sequential effects in various cognitive areas (e.g., visual attention, interference tasks, and task-switching).
- **Human-Machine Interaction (HMI):** Applying fundamental knowledge of human cognition to HMI. Current work includes investigating the impact of vibration on cognitive performance.
