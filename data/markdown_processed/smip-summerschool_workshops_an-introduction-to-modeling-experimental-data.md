# An Introduction to Modeling Experimental Data of Conflict Tasks with Dynamic Diffusion Models

**Authors:** Markus Janczyk (University of Bremen) & Valentin Koob (University of Bremen)

This workshop provides a comprehensive overview of describing and analyzing experimental data using cognitive models. The primary focus is on **interference effects in conflict tasks** (such as the congruency effect in Eriksen flanker, Simon, or Stroop tasks) and the specialized, time-dependent diffusion models developed for them.

Unlike standard diffusion models, the parameters covered here are time-dependent, meaning they change their values across the duration of the task.

## Workshop Objectives and Scope

The workshop aims to achieve two main goals:

1. Cover the theoretical framework behind these mathematical models and discuss their utility in formalizing verbal theories.
1. Guide participants from basic descriptive statistics to setting up advanced cognitive models for in-depth analysis.

Through interactive lectures and exercises, participants will learn the basics of describing, developing, programming, and estimating these mathematical models, maintaining a strong focus on conceptual foundations and the theoretical framework.

## Prerequisites and Required Skills

**Technical Skills:**

- Experience with the R programming language.
- Basic knowledge of programming in R (e.g., understanding basic data types and writing functions).
- Familiarity with the statistical concepts of random variables and density functions is helpful.

**Software Requirements:**
The workshop utilizes the R package **dRiftDM**, which provides flexible functionality for dealing with time-dependent parameters in the context of diffusion models without requiring simulation.

**Action Item:** Participants must bring their own laptops with the latest versions of R and RStudio, and the `dRiftDM` package installed (available at CRAN).
