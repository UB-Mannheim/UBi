# Graduate School of Economic and Social Sciences (GESS)

The Graduate School of Economic and Social Sciences (GESS) is a unique institution in Germany and one of the few graduate schools worldwide. It is dedicated to fostering the exchange of ideas, methods, and approaches to research across the boundaries of the economic and the social sciences. GESS equips students with a broad scientific vision during a critical phase of academic socialization, enabling them to achieve excellence in their specific field of research. Participation in a doctoral program at GESS prepares students for academic positions at leading universities and institutions globally.

## Benefits for GESS Students

Students benefit from a highly supportive and international academic environment, including:

- Innovative research topics supervised by internationally renowned professors.
- The opportunity to meet and work with graduate students from diverse social and cultural backgrounds worldwide.
- A vibrant and international research community.
- No tuition fees and guaranteed [financial support](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/about/student-financing/).
- Dedicated resources, including own office space, IT systems, and software.
- Access to one of the best-equipped [university libraries](https://www.bib.uni-mannheim.de/en/) in Germany, containing over 1.5 million books and approximately 5,000 journals.
- A supportive and collaborative administration.

## Doctoral Programs

GESS administers several specialized PhD programs in collaboration with the University of Mannheim's departments:

- **Center for Doctoral Studies in Business (CDSB):** Administers the PhD program at the [Business School](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/). This school is triple accredited by AACSB, EFDM (EQUIS), and AMBA.
- **Center for Doctoral Studies in Economics (CDSE):** Administers the PhD program at the [Department of Economics](https://www.vwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/).
- **Center for Doctoral Studies in Social and Behavioral Sciences (CDSS):** Administers PhD programs in Political Science, Psychology, and Sociology in close collaboration with the [School of Social Sciences](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/).

## Doctoral Degrees in Germany

In Germany, most universities confer doctoral degrees using specific Latin designations to indicate the field of study. This differs from the *Ph.D.* (doctor philosophiae), which is a broader title.

Both titles are awarded after an intensive study and research period, culminating in the successful submission and defense of a doctoral thesis.

At GESS, the following doctoral titles are conferred:

- **Faculty of Business and Economics:** Dr. rer. pol (doctor rerum politicarum)
- **Faculty of Social Sciences:** Dr. rer. soc. (doctor rerum socialium)
