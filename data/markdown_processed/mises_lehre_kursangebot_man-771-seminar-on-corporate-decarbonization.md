# MAN 771 Seminar on Corporate Decarbonization

This seminar provides an in-depth examination of the challenges and opportunities companies face on their journey toward achieving net zero carbon emissions. The core focus is on the economic analysis required for reducing corporate carbon emissions and formulating effective abatement strategies.

## Key Topics Covered

The seminar covers a wide range of critical areas in the transition to a sustainable economy, including:

- Climate-Tech Innovation
- Corporate Decarbonization Strategies
- Corporate Carbon Accounting and Reporting
- Competitiveness of Clean Energy Technologies
- Emergence of Sustainable Transportation Services
- Pathways for Industrial Decarbonization
- Climate Risk in Finance and Investment

The analysis of these topics requires reviewing the most current literature and utilizing advanced analytical and empirical research methods. Participants are expected to present and discuss key findings, culminating in a comprehensive seminar paper.

## Academic Details

**Learning Outcomes**
Participants will gain a deep understanding of the main challenges and opportunities associated with transitioning to a decarbonized energy economy. They will acquire both theoretical economic concepts central to this transition and knowledge of recent practical developments and tools. Furthermore, participants will improve their skills in writing a coherent, state-of-the-art seminar paper.

**Assessment Format**
The final grade is determined by:

- Written seminar thesis (50%)
- In-class presentation (50%)

**Credits:** 6 ECTS
**Lecturers:** Amadeus Bach, Gunther Glenk, Stefan Reichelstein
