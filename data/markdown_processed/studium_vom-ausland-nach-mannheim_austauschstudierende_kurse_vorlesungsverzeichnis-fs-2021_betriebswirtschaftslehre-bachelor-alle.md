# Business School Course Catalog Overview

This catalog outlines the available courses for exchange students at the Business School in Mannheim.

## 🎓 Eligibility Guidelines

**For Bachelor's Students:**
You should select courses only from the bachelor's level curriculum. Master's level courses are generally not open to undergraduate students, unless you are in your 4th year of an undergraduate program at the time of arrival.
*For questions, please contact the Business School Exchange Team via incoming.business@uni-mannheim.de.*

**For Master's/MBA Students:**
You are eligible to choose Business courses from all master's level courses. If your home university permits, you may also take selected bachelor's level courses.

## 📚 Core Curriculum Areas

### Area Accounting and Taxation

This area covers the principles and complexities of modern taxation and finance:

- Understanding the impact of tax codes on corporate decision-making.
- Analyzing tax regimes through international examples (Asia Pacific and Europe).
- Evaluating the consequences of taxation in cross-border transactions.
- Learning basic principles of international taxation.
- Identifying key tax issues affecting international investment tax burdens.
- Analyzing basic tax planning structures related to financing and holding companies.
- Understanding the BEPS (base erosion and profit shifting) debate and low-tax strategies.

### Area Finance

The core material for this area is based on the textbook **„Principles of Corporate Finance“ by Richard A. Brealey, Stewart C. Myers and Franklin Allen, 12th edition (McGraw-Hill 2016).**

- *Note:* Students can use previous editions (11th/2013 or 10th/2011), though minor changes may apply.
- All lecture slides and exercise materials are available on Ilias after student portal registration.

### Area Information Systems

This area focuses on modeling and managing complex business processes using integrated information systems:

- Modeling complex business processes using popular techniques.
- Discussing the requirements, characteristics, and effects of integrated information systems in industrial companies, including complex process interdependencies.
- Completing basic tasks across various functional areas within a wide-spread integrated information system.
- **Functional Areas Covered:**
  - Business Process Modelling
  - Application Systems in Research and Development
  - Marketing and Sales
  - Procurement and Warehousing
  - Production
  - Shipping and Customer Service
  - Finance, Accounting, HR
  - Planning and Control Systems

### Area Management

This area covers the human and organizational aspects of business:

- Human Resource Environment
- Job Analysis & Job Design
- HR Planning & Recruiting
- Selection
- Training
- Performance Management
- Employee Development
- Separation & Retention
- Compensation and Incentives (including International HRM)
- High-Performance Organizations

## 🔬 Shared Research & Methodology Skills

The following topics represent core research skills taught across multiple areas (e.g., Finance and Management):

**Research Methods:**

- **Literature Reviews:** How to conduct a rigorous and systematic review of the literature.
- **Research Methods:** A discussion of alternate research methods, covering:
  - **Qualitative Research:** Conducting qualitative studies and key readings.
  - **Surveys:** Using surveys in research and key readings.
  - **Experiments:** Using experiments in research and key readings.
- **Analysis & Presentation of Results:** Providing further resources and examples for applying each method.

**Environmental & Ethical Case Studies:**

- What is the state of Science regarding Environmental Change?
- What are the effects of Environmental Change on Business?
- Analysis of moral, legal, and business case studies.
