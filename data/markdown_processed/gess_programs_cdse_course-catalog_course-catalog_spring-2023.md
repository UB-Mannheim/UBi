## 🎓 Detailed Course Descriptions (Spring 2023)

### 1. General Equilibrium Theory (Advanced Microeconomics)

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703
**Assessment:** Final examination (120 min.), regular class participation, submission of problem sets, and good performance in exercise sessions.

**Course Content:**
This course provides an advanced introduction to the theory of general equilibrium. It builds on introductory microeconomics (E702) and uses mathematical tools from E700 and E701. Topics covered include:

- Aggregate Demand
- Production Theory
- Exchange Economies
- Production Economies
- Uncertainty and Arrow-Debreu Equilibrium
- Basic Asset Pricing

The course teaches the fundamentals of economic equilibrium theory, laying the foundation for fields like macroeconomics, international trade, and finance.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students learn core concepts of general economic equilibrium (e.g., Law of Demand, Walrasian equilibrium, arbitrage-free allocations). They gain the ability to analyze problems independently across various economic fields and assess the appropriateness of an equilibrium-theoretic approach.

**Lecturer:** Ernst-Ludwig von Thadden
**Teaching Assistant:** [Chang Liu](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/our-students/2019/chang-liu/)

**Schedule Highlights:**

- **Lecture:** Runs from February to March 2023 (Specific dates provided in the original catalog).
- **Tutorial:** Runs from February to March 2023.
- **Exam:** March 27, 2023.

### 2. Dynamic Economic Modeling under Rational Expectations

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703
**Assessment:** Final examination, solutions to problem sets, and participation in exercise sessions.

**Course Content:**
This module covers basic methods for dynamic economic modeling under rational expectations. Key topics include:

- Linear Rational Expectations (RE) Models: Determinacy, indeterminacy, and 'sunspot' equilibria.
- Linear RE models and Vector Auto-Regressions (VARs): State space representation, identification of economic shocks.
- Linear Quadratic (LQ) Dynamic Programming: Ricatti equation, stochastic problems, and certainty equivalence.
- Introduction to the New Keynesian Model and its Linearized Form.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students learn to apply basic tools used in business cycle analysis and follow recent literature on dynamic stochastic general equilibrium models.

**Teaching Assistant:** [Bjarne Horst](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/our-students/2021/bjarne-horst/)

**Schedule Highlights:**

- **Lecture:** Runs from February to March 2023.
- **Tutorial:** Runs from February to March 2023.
- **Exam:** March 29, 2023.

### 5. Heterogeneity in Macroeconomics

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803
**Assessment:** Written exam and assignments.

**Course Content:**
Focuses on heterogeneity in macroeconomics, introducing basic tools of modern macroeconomics. Topics include:

- **Consumption/Savings:** Permanent income hypothesis, self-insurance, incomplete market economies.
- **Investment:** Neoclassical theory, Tobin’s q theory, partial/lumpy capital adjustment.
- **Teaching Method:** Lecture (2 SWS) and Exercise (1 SWS).

**Competences Acquired:**
Students gain a deep understanding of mathematical methods in modern macroeconomics, preparing them for independent Ph.D. research.

**Teaching Assistant:** [Carl-Christian Groh](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/our-students/2017/carl-christian-groh/)

**Schedule Highlights:**

- **Lecture:** Runs from April to May 2023.

### 8. Macroeconomic Modeling with Heterogeneous Agents

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803
**Assessment:** Written exam and assignments.

**Course Content:**
The focus is on how household heterogeneity affects the economy and how policy/market frictions cause inequality. It introduces the standard incomplete-markets model, extending it to:

1. Models with overlapping generations (OLG).
1. Two-asset models.
1. Recasting the framework in continuous time.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students gain knowledge of numerical methods to solve dynamic programming problems, crucial for advanced macro research.

**Teaching Assistant:** [Carl-Christian Groh](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/our-students/2017/carl-christian-groh/)

### 10. Causal Machine Learning in Econometrics

**Prerequisites:** All first year CDSE or equivalent courses.
**Assessment:** Class participation (30%), Group project presentations.

**Course Content:**
Covers three parts:

1. Machine and statistical learning methods for prediction.
1. Popular causal research designs (IV, regression-discontinuity, difference-in-difference).
1. Causal machine learning: combining prediction methods with causal designs for reliable inference.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students obtain basic knowledge of popular machine/statistical learning methods and causal research designs, enabling them to conduct empirical studies using causal machine learning.

**Course Dates:** August 15 – 18, 2023

**Prerequisites:** All first year CDSE or equivalent courses.

### 13. Industrial Organization Theory

**Prerequisites:** All first year CDSE or equivalent courses.
**Assessment:** Paper presentation (50%), referee report (50%).

**Competences Acquired:**
Students learn to build simple micro models and use them as building blocks for empirical work.
