### Biography

Lesa Hoffman received her Ph.D. in Psychology at the University of Kansas in 2003. She completed a post-doctoral fellowship at The Pennsylvania State University before joining the Department of Psychology at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln as Assistant Professor in 2006 (and as Associate Professor in 2011). From August 2014 to January 2019, she served as Associate Scientist in the Research Design and Analysis (RDA) Unit of the Lifespan Institute and as Associate Professor of Quantitative Methods in the Child Language Doctoral Program. In January 2019, she became a Professor in the Educational Measurement and Statistics Program in the Department of Psychological and Quantitative Foundations at the College of Education at the University of Iowa.

### Research Areas

My research program focuses on empirically examining and disseminating how developments in quantitative methodology can advance empirical work in the social sciences. Particular attention is paid to the real-world behavior of:

- Longitudinal models
- Multilevel models
- Structural equation models

I teach graduate courses and intensive workshops on advanced quantitative methods, including latent trait measurement, structural equation models, longitudinal models, and multilevel models. All course materials (including videos) are freely available online. For more information about my research, teaching, and supplemental resources for my textbook on longitudinal analysis of within-person fluctuation and change, please visit my website.
