## Advertising Over Time: Moving in the speed-of-life?

**Authors:**

- Leonie Gehrmann
- [Florian Stahl](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/quantitativemarketing/researchgroup/florianstahl/)

**Cooperation Partners:**

- Hebrew University (Israel)
- Economist

**Description:**
This research analyzes how print advertising content has adapted to changes in "speed-of-life," defined as the "rapidity and density of experiences, meanings, perceptions, and activities" (Werner et al. 1985), over time.

**Key Findings & Applications:**

- **Image Mining:** The developed image mining algorithm is highly relevant and easily extendable beyond print advertisements to other images.
- **Expansion Potential:** The analysis can be continued for other magazines and social media postings, further refining the algorithm.
- **Marketing Implications:** The study addresses the recognized phenomenon of increasing advertising clutter, providing insights into the existing competition for consumer attention.

## Prediction of Consumer Behavior

**Authors:**

- Yasid Soufi
- [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/)

**Cooperation Partners:**

- Chair of Sales & Services Marketing

**Description:**
The project aims to gain insights into predictors of a customer’s purchase behavior and personal traits, enabling a more flexible and adaptive approach to the sales process.

**Methodology & Goals:**

- By utilizing data on behavioral patterns, consumer characteristics, and self-reported traits, the research seeks to understand and anticipate customer behavior in an app-based context.
- This is achieved through the application of machine learning algorithms to an enriched data set, leading to a better understanding of the mechanisms underlying consumer decisions.
- The ultimate goal is to leverage predictive abilities to optimize the sales process.

**Value Proposition:**

- Insights are valuable from both a customer and provider perspective. Optimized processes lead to higher customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.
- The data gathered, while valuable, often remains unsaved and unexplored, making these insights crucial for process optimization.
