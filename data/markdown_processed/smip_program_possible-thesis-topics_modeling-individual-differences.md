# Modeling Individual Differences in Personality and Cognition

Interindividual differences in personality attributes and cognitive performance are central to many domains of psychological research. However, assessing these differences can be complicated by various factors, including individual response styles, method effects of the assessment instruments, and situational fluctuations.

### Theoretical Foundations

**In Personality Research:**
The assessment of personality traits can be distorted by individual response styles (e.g., general preference for extreme, moderate, or affirmative options) and method effects of the assessment instrument (e.g., reversed item effects; self vs. other judgments). Furthermore, sources of error variance or systematic bias can arise from situation effects and the interaction between response styles and method effects. To properly investigate and control for the effects of the target trait, response styles, assessment method, and situation, researchers utilize advanced statistical analyses such as multidimensional IRT models, structural equation methods, or generalized multilevel models.

**In Cognitive Research:**
Theory-driven measurement models allow for the decomposition of global performance indices into specific measures of cognitive processes. This decomposition enables researchers to analyze interindividual differences in abilities (e.g., memory, attention), response tendencies (e.g., guessing), and cognitive strategies (e.g., information integration versus exemplar-based categorization) as person variables that jointly determine performance in a given task. Statistical approaches for achieving multidimensional cognitive assessment include multilevel extensions of multinomial processing-tree and mixture distribution models tailored to the specific task setting.

## Potential Dissertation Projects

Dissertation projects can focus on interindividual differences in personality or cognition, or on the interplay between individual differences in one domain (e.g., personality) and other fields of psychological research (e.g., processing strategy in judgment situations or memory tasks).

### Model Development Projects

These projects focus on developing and refining statistical models:

- **IRT models for unravelling trait assessment from response styles and method effects** (Advisors: Meiser, Lischetzke, Hilbig).
- **Exemplar-based and rule-based processes in judgment** (Advisors: Bröder, Hilbig).

### Model Application Projects

These projects apply existing or developed models to specific psychological phenomena:

- **The structure of cognitive speed** (Advisors: Voss, Meiser).
- **Memory-task response biases as cognitive traits and states** (Advisors: Kuhlmann, Hütter, Meiser).
- **A life-span perspective on controllable and uncontrollable learning in evaluative conditioning** (Advisors: Hütter, Klauer, Kuhlmann).
- **Modeling individual differences in judgment and decision making** (Advisors: Hilbig, Erdfelder, Meiser).
