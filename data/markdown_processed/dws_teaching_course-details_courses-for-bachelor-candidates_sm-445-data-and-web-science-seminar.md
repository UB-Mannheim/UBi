# SM 445: Data and Web Science Seminar (HWS 2023)

The Data and Web Science seminar explores recent topics in data and web science. This seminar is designed exclusively for **BSc students** and focuses on selected advanced topics in machine learning.

## 🎓 Organization and Prerequisites

- **Organizers:** Prof. Dr. Rainer Gemulla and [Adrian Kochsiek](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/alumni/dr-adrian-kochsiek/).
- **Format:** Available for BSc students (2 SWS, 5 ECTS).
- **Prerequisites:** Completion of BSc courses in Computer Science (CS) from terms 1–4, with a particular focus on Wifo4.

## 🎯 Learning Goals and Deliverables

Students are expected to develop deep research skills through the following deliverables:

- **Literature Review:** Read, understand, and explore technical and scientific literature.
- **Technical Report:** Write a concise technical report (10 single-column pages + references).
- **Presentations:** Give two oral presentations (a 3-minute flash presentation and a 15-minute final presentation).
- **Discussion:** Moderate a scientific discussion around a fellow student's presentation.
- **Review:** Review a draft report submitted by a fellow student.

## 🔬 Topic Areas and Focus

Students will be assigned a topic area in an active, relevant field of machine learning based on their preferences.

**Seminar Focus:**

1. **Overview (1/3):** Provide a short, concise overview of the assigned topic area (e.g., using a survey paper or book chapter). Discuss the main goals, challenges, and relevance of the field.
1. **Deep Dive (2/3):** Present a self-selected topic within that area in more detail (e.g., using a recent research paper). Discuss the concrete problem statement, solution, contribution, and personal thoughts.

**Guidelines for Topic Selection:**

- The area must be an active, relevant field in machine learning.
- It must *not* be part of the BSc Wifo (and specifically, not Wifo 4).
- It should not solely refer to a general architecture (e.g., RNNs, CNNs, Transformers) or a very narrow subfield.

**Examples of Suitable Topic Areas:**

- Pretraining
- Optimization for machine learning
- Machine learning frameworks
- Federated machine learning
- Differential privacy in machine learning
- Few-shot learning
- Reinforcement learning
- Explainable machine learning
- Transfer learning
- ML in a concrete application area
