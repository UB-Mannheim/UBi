# Mandatory Energy Efficiency Disclosure in Housing Markets

**Speaker:** Steven Puller
**Affiliation:** Texas A&M University
**Date:** June 14, 2021

## Seminar Abstract

Mandatory disclosure policies are implemented broadly despite sparse evidence that they improve market outcomes. This seminar studies the effects of requiring home sellers to provide buyers with certified audits of residential energy efficiency.

Using similar nearby homes as a comparison group, the research finds that this requirement:

- Increases price premiums for energy efficiency.
- Encourages energy-saving investments.

The presentation also highlights the market failure—incomplete information by both buyers and sellers—that prevents widespread voluntary disclosure of energy efficiency in housing transactions. The findings support that disclosure policies can improve market outcomes in settings with symmetrically incomplete information.

## Speaker Biography: Steven Puller

Steven L. Puller is a Professor at the Department of Economics at Texas A&M University. He is also a NBER Research Associate, a Research Affiliate at the International Growth Centre, a faculty affiliate at the E2e Project, and an adjunct professor at the Bush School of Government and Public Service.

He completed his undergraduate studies in Economics at the University of Chicago and obtained his PhD in Economics at UC Berkeley. Prof. Puller's research focuses on industrial economics, regulation, and public policy, with a specific emphasis on energy and transportation. He has published in top general interest and field journals, such as the AER, AEJ Economic Policy, RAND, JPubE, or the Review of Economics and Statistics. His most recent works concern energy efficiency policies and the design of electricity wholesale and retail markets.
