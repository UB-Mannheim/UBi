# Marius Cziriak Profile

Marius Cziriak is a researcher specializing in economic and financial topics. He joined the Taxation program of the Center of Doctoral Studies in Business in September 2019.

### Academic and Professional Background

Marius was born in Memmingen, Germany. His academic journey included:

- **Undergraduate Studies:** Economics at the University of Heidelberg. During this time, he worked as a student assistant at the chair of Public Finance under Prof. Christoph Vanberg and completed a semester abroad at the Warsaw School of Economics (SGH).
- **Internships:** He completed internships at the Institute of Economic and Social Research (WSI) in Düsseldorf and the Leibniz Centre for European Economic Research (ZEW) in Mannheim.
- **Master's Program:** He completed the Master's program in Economics at the Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität (LMU) in Munich.
- **Research Experience:** In Munich, he worked as a research assistant at the Munich Center for the Economics of Ageing at the Max Planck Society (MEA). His thesis focused on childhood circumstances, life-course investment decisions, and their effect on the accumulation of wealth in old age.

### Research Interests

His primary research interests lie in:

- Social Security and Pension Economics
- Household Saving and Personal Finance
- Fiscal Policies and Behavior of Economic Agents
