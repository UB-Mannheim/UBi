# Disentangling the effects of age and time in research on personality development using multilevel models and local structural equation modeling

**Speaker:** Eunike Wetzel
**Date:** 28 June
**Time:** 14:00

### Overview

A major challenge in research on personality development across the lifespan is distinguishing between developmental effects (changes within an individual over time) and age differences (e.g., older individuals having higher average trait levels than younger ones).

This talk presents two advanced statistical approaches designed to tackle this issue: **multilevel models** and **local structural equation modeling (LSEM)**.

### Methodological Approaches

- **Multilevel Models:** Age can be incorporated as a Level-2 predictor to effectively disentangle the effects of age and time.
- **Local Structural Equation Modeling (LSEM):** This method estimates parameters of interest (such as those derived from a latent change model) conditional on age, achieving the same goal of separation.

### Empirical Focus

Both methods will be illustrated using empirical data derived from large samples focusing on the development of the personality trait **narcissism**.
