# Department of Economics

The Department of Economics at the University of Mannheim is home to a robust academic community. The department includes twenty-two full-time senior and numerous junior faculty members who are engaged in diverse research projects.

**Research Focus Areas:**
The research spans the frontiers of various economic fields, including:

- Micro- and macroeconomics
- Econometrics and statistical methods
- Labor economics
- Industrial organization
- Financial economics
- Public economics
- Economic history
- International trade

**Funding and Support:**
Research activities are strongly supported by major institutional grants, including funding from the German Research Foundation (DFG) and European Union research and training grants. Additional funding is secured from various public and private institutions.

## Associated Research Centers and Institutions

The Department maintains extensive international and domestic institutional contacts, collaborating with numerous universities and research centers worldwide. Key associated research centers located in Mannheim include:

### SFB 884: The Political Economy of Reforms

This interdisciplinary collaborative research center investigates the success and failure of policy reforms. Its analysis focuses on competing interests, contextual factors, and the political process inherent in reform-making.
[Learn more about SFB 884](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/reforms/)

### ZEW: Centre for European Economic Research

ZEW is recognized as a leading institute for applied economic research in Europe. The center focuses on internationally comparative issues within the European context and maintains several important databases.
[Visit ZEW](https://www.zew.de/en/)
