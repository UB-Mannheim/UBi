### 📚 Accounting & Taxation Research Seminar

**Course Content**
The course focuses on current research topics in the field of accounting and taxation. Visiting researchers present their latest working papers and discuss their ideas with participating faculty and students. The presentations have a workshop format, similar to leading scientific conferences. For each presentation, a separate preparation session for Ph.D. students is offered by rotating faculty. Overall, the course deepens students’ insights into a variety of research methods popular in empirical and theoretical research.

**Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to follow up with and discuss current research topics in accounting and taxation. Interaction with leading researchers allows them to develop their own research ideas and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.

**Assessment**

- **Form:** Discussion/Participation (Implied)

**Schedule**

- **Monday:** 15:30 – 17:00 (Room O 048)
- **Tuesday:** 13:45 – 15:15 (Room SO 318)
- **Wednesday:** 13:45 – 17:00 (Room O 048)

### 📚 Empirical Accounting Literature Review

**Course Content**
This course provides a comprehensive overview of research topics and methods in influential seminal and contemporaneous papers in the empirical accounting literature. Topics covered include:

1. Introduction and "Accounting Classics."
1. Earnings Management.
1. Valuation (value relevance, ERC/event studies, accounting-based valuation).
1. Voluntary Disclosure.
1. Mandatory Disclosure.
1. International/Institutional Accounting and IFRS.
1. Corporate Narratives.

In each session, there is an overview lecture introducing core methods, followed by a joint discussion of selected empirical methods. Students are expected to prepare all readings and lead class discussions.

**Learning Outcomes**
Students should know the core issues of existing accounting research and established empirical methodologies. They should be able to place current research into the literature, critically evaluate its relevance and technical rigor, and develop meaningful research ideas.

**Assessment**

- Exam (90 minutes): 50%
- Paper Presentations: 50%

**Schedule**

- **Lecture:** 13.02.24 – 16.04.24 (Tuesday, 15:30 – 18:30)
- **Prerequisites:** Advanced Econometrics I or Applied Econometrics I

### 📚 Behavioral Finance Seminar

**Course Content**
This course reviews recent research on how human attitudes and behavior affect financial decisions, challenging the standard economic paradigm of rational investors. Topics include overconfidence, prospect theory, and heuristic-driven biases.

**Learning Outcomes**
Students understand how behavioral findings are integrated with neo-classical theory and can better understand how cognitive and emotional biases affect market prices and returns.

**Assessment**

- Exam (60 minutes)

### 📚 Econometrics/Causal Inference (Advanced)

**Course Content**
A refresher on econometric concepts like endogeneity, identification, and selection. The focus is on causal inference, covering the Rubin causal model, instrumental variable regressions, regression discontinuity, difference-in-difference, matching, and fixed effects estimation.

**Learning Outcomes**
Students gain knowledge of econometric concepts and their applications in finance, improving their ability to plan and carry out independent empirical research.

**Assessment**
