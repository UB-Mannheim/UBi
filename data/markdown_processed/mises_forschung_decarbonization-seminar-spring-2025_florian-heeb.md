## Seminar Abstract

This seminar reports on the results of a pre-registered field experiment concerning the impact of index provider engagement on corporate climate policy.

In the study, a randomly selected group of 300 out of 1227 international companies received a letter from an index provider. This letter encouraged the company to commit to setting a science-based climate target to maintain inclusion in its climate transition benchmark indices.

After one year, the researchers observed a significant effect:

- **Treated Companies:** 21.0% committed to setting a target.
- **Control Group:** 15.7% committed to setting a target.

The findings suggest that engagement by financial institutions can influence corporate policies, particularly when a feasible request is combined with a credible threat of exit.

## Speaker Biography

**Florian Heeb** is an Assistant Professor of Sustainable Finance at the Leibniz Institute for Financial Research SAFE. He also serves as a Research Affiliate at the MIT Sloan School of Management, where he previously worked as a Postdoctoral Associate.

His research focuses on exploring the real-world impact of sustainable investing and how investors integrate impact into their decision-making processes. His work has been published in prestigious journals, including the *Review of Financial Studies* (where it was selected as Editor’s Choice).

Florian holds an MSc in Environmental Science from ETH Zurich and received his PhD from the University of Zurich. He brings extensive experience in corporate sustainability and climate action from various management roles in the industry.
