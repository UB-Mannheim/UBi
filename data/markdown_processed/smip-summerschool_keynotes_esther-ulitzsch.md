## Exploring the Impact of Design Choices on Careless Responding with Confirmatory Mixture Models

**Speaker:** Esther Ulitzsch
**Date:** 4 July 2025

### Introduction: The Problem of Careless Responding

Careless and insufficient effort responding (C/IER) on self-report measures poses a major threat to the quality of survey data, as the responses may not accurately reflect the constructs being measured.

### Methodological Solution: Confirmatory Mixture Models

Confirmatory mixture models offer sophisticated tools for studying the occurrence of C/IER. These models translate theoretical considerations regarding respondent behavior into constrained component models, allowing researchers to study C/IER, its determinants, and contextual correlates.

### Case Studies and Applications

The presentation utilizes two case studies based on experimental survey data to demonstrate the practical application of these models:

**1. Cross-Sectional Data Analysis (Scale Type Comparison)**

- **Focus:** Examining whether increasingly used visual analogue scales elicit more or less C/IER compared to traditional Likert-type scales.
- **Method:** Utilizing models that account for the unique properties of each scale type.

**2. Longitudinal Data Analysis (Ecological Momentary Assessment)**

- **Focus:** Investigating how questionnaire length and sampling frequency impact C/IER occurrence in high-frequency longitudinal data collection (using screen time data).

### Conclusion and Guidelines

The presentation provides guidelines for study designs aimed at mitigating C/IER and yielding higher-quality data. A cautionary note is given regarding the risks of drawing erroneous conclusions when model assumptions are violated.
