## 🎤 Speaker Profile: Philipp Krüger

Philipp Krüger is a Professor of Responsible Finance at the University of Geneva (GSEM, GFRI) and holds a Senior Chair at the Swiss Finance Institute.

**Academic Background & Research:**

- He holds a PhD in Economics from the Toulouse School of Economics (2010).
- His research interests focus on corporate, sustainable, and behavioral finance.
- His work has been published in major academic Finance journals (e.g., *Journal of Finance*, *Journal of Financial Economics*, *Review of Financial Studies*).
- He is a regular speaker at leading global finance conferences and has received multiple awards, including the Moskowitz Prize (2015) and the ICPM Climate Change Research Award (2018).
- He currently serves on the board of experts for Inrate AG, Zurich.

## 📄 Seminar Abstract

This paper analyzes the green economy using a novel measure of a firm’s green revenues. The research sheds light on the factors driving global public companies' expansion of business activities to support the green transition toward a low-carbon and environmentally sustainable economy.

**Key Findings:**

- The green economy accelerated its growth following the Paris Agreement.
- This accelerated growth is attributed to both regulatory initiatives and innovative US firms converting green patents into actual revenues from green products and services.
- The analysis also documents that a stronger presence of institutional investors prior to the Paris Agreement is associated with higher green revenues afterward.
- Finally, the study examines the stock returns of firms with high green revenues, finding only modest evidence of a "green alpha," which appears concentrated in US stocks in the post-Paris period.
