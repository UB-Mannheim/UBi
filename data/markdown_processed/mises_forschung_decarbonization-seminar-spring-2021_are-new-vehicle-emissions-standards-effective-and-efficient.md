# Are New Vehicle Emissions Standards Effective and Efficient?

**Speaker:** Arthur van Benthem
**Affiliation:** University of Pennsylvania
**Date:** May 3, 2021

## Seminar Abstract

This paper assesses the effectiveness and efficiency of current air pollution regulations for vehicles. While optimal policy would involve a Pigouvian tax on emissions, most countries rely on exhaust standards for new vehicles (e.g., limiting carbon monoxide and nitrogen oxides).

The research demonstrates that:

- The emissions rate of new vehicles in the US has fallen by over 99% since exhaust standards began in 1967.
- Used vehicles have shown comparable declines, suggesting that exhaust standards are the primary cause of this reduction.
- However, exhaust standards are not cost-effective because they fail to incentivize the scrapping of old vehicles, which still account for a large share of total emissions.

To study counterfactual policies, the authors develop analytical and quantitative models of both new and used vehicle fleets.
