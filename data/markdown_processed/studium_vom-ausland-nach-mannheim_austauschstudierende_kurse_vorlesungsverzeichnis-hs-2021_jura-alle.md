# Academic Course Catalog: Law Studies

This catalog provides an overview of advanced legal courses covering German private law, constitutional law, international law, and specialized areas such as intellectual property and comparative law.

## 🏛️ Public and Constitutional Law

### German Constitutional Law (Grundgesetz)

This course provides a general introduction to German Constitutional Law, covering the structure, background, and contents of the *Grundgesetz* (Basic Law).

- **Core Focus:** The fundamental rights (Art. 1–19) and the development of the German democratic system since World War II.
- **Key Topics:**
  - The German legal system and the role of the *Grundgesetz* and the European Union.
  - Fundamental Rights and Constitutional Principles.
  - The Federal State structure, constitutional organs, and the legislative process.
  - The relationship between EU law and national law.
- **Learning Goal:** Students will be able to understand the basic principles of the *Grundgesetz* and solve simple cases regarding fundamental rights.

### International Law and Global Governance

This course examines international organizations (e.g., WTO, UN, OECD, ILO) as global governance actors.

- **Core Focus:** How international organizations regulate trade and business at regional and universal levels, moving beyond mere inter-state negotiation forums.
- **Key Topics:**
  - Economic relevance and institutional structure of international organizations.
  - International organizations as law-makers and standard-setters.
  - Monitoring and dispute settlement functions.
  - Interaction between international, EU, and national legal norms.

## ⚖️ Private and Comparative Law

### Comparative Law II – The Common/Civil Law Divide

This course compares the legal systems of Germany (Civil Law) with those of Australia/the United States (Common Law).

- **Core Focus:** Methodological differences between the two major legal traditions and their application to daily business issues.
- **Key Components:**
  - **Comparative Law Theory:** Origins, aims, tools, and methods of comparative law.
  - **Legal Traditions:** Analysis of Common Law and Civil Law development and current status.
  - **Legal Institutions:** Comparison of legal reasoning, statutes, judicial precedents, and the role of judges/juries.
  - **Practical Application:** Applying comparative legal analysis to international disputes.

### German Private Law

This course offers an introduction to German private law, specifically designed for foreign law students.

- **Core Focus:** Providing a systematic understanding of the German Civil Code (*BGB*) and its application.
- **Key Topics:**
  - Overview of legal sources of German law and the historical development of the *BGB*.
  - **Core Legal Areas:** Tort law, law of obligations, and property law.
  - **Supplementary Topics:** Short introductions to inheritance and family law, company law, and civil procedural law.
- **Learning Goal:** Students will be familiar with the structures of German civil law and able to handle smaller legal cases.

## 🌐 International Business and Trade Law

### International Business Law and Cross-Border Transactions

This comprehensive course addresses the legal and commercial backgrounds of international transactions.

- **Core Focus:** Analyzing the pitfalls and legal solutions for cross-border trade, integrating legal, economic, and political perspectives.
- **Key Modules:**
  - **General Trade:** Legal and non-legal means of contract enforcement, financing, and dispute resolution in international business.
  - **CISG:** Detailed study of the UN Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG), covering contract formation, obligations, and remedies.
  - **Law and Economics:** Applying economic theory (e.g., Coase Theorem, efficient breach) to understand how law affects human behavior and market efficiency.

### Regulatory Law

This course examines regulatory law, which supplements European Competition and EU Law, focusing on formerly monopolistic network infrastructure markets.

- **Core Focus:** The legal framework for creating competition in sectors like energy, telecommunications, postal, and railway law.
- **Key Topics:**
  - **Regulatory Basics:** Historical roots, EU competences, and legal limits of regulation.
  - **Regulatory Instruments:** Analyzing objectives and tools such as market, access, and price regulation.
  - **Specific Areas:** In-depth analysis of energy and telecommunications law, including the regulation of virtual digital networks, utilizing EU legislation and ECJ case law.

## 💡 Specialized Legal Fields

### Intellectual Property (IP) Law

This course provides an overview of intellectual property as a crucial tool for innovation and global competitiveness.

- **Core Focus:** Understanding the multi-level system of IP protection (worldwide, EU, and national levels).
- **Key Topics:**
  - **IP Rights:** Legal concepts and protection rules for patents, utility models, trademarks, designs, and copyrights.
  - **Legal Framework:** Sources of law (TRIPS, WIPO-Treaties, EU-Regulations).
  - **Market Interaction:** The impact of competition law on IP protection and the co-existence of national and Community IP-rights.
  - **Enforcement:** Contractual exploitation (licensing) and enforcement strategies.

### Law and Economics

This course applies economic concepts and methods to legal problems, providing a behavioral theory to predict how people respond to laws.

- **Core Focus:** Fine-tuning legal interpretations to achieve desired social or economic goals.
- **Key Areas of Application:**
  - **Property Law:** The Coase Theorem and the necessity of property rights.
  - **Tort Law:** Comparing negligence liability vs. strict liability.
  - **Contract Law:** Minimizing transaction costs and avoiding market failures (e.g., Market for Lemons).

## 🗣️ Professional Skills

### Advocacy and Communication in Law

This course is dedicated to the art and science of effective communication for legal professionals.

- **Core Focus:** Developing mastery of advocacy skills for written and oral presentations in highly professional contexts.
- **Key Skills Taught:**
  - **Advocacy:** Convincing a judge, presenting professionally at conferences.
  - **Writing:** Drafting clear and persuasive legal memos.
  - **Negotiation:** Principles of negotiation psychology, competitive, and cooperative negotiation.
  - **Communication:** Crisis communication, active listening, and interrogating witnesses.
