# Curriculum Overview: Law Studies for Exchange Students

This document provides an overview of advanced legal courses, covering comparative law, German private law, European Union law, and contemporary topics like platform regulation and e-commerce.

## 🏛️ Comparative Law (Comparative Law I)

This course provides an accessible introduction to the nature, functions, and aims of Comparative Law for students from diverse legal backgrounds. Given the difficulty of studying even a single legal system fully, the course adopts a selective approach, focusing particularly on European Legal Traditions and their role in European harmonization. The focus is on private law and business law aspects of comparative law.

**Key Components:**

1. **Origins and Utility:** Covers the aims, tools, and methods of comparative law research, including the principle of equality of all legal orders and the grouping of legal families.
1. **Business Law Concepts:** Examines common features of contract law and essential business law concepts (e.g., division of work, liability, tort law, and insurance).
1. **Legal Analysis:** Presents different possible solutions for these problems across various jurisdictions, followed by an analysis of how they are governed by different legal families, including lines of influence and hybrids.

**Learning Objectives:**

- Aims and methods of comparative law research.
- Basic concepts of contract law (party autonomy, formation of contract, performance).
- Basic concepts of business law (division of work, liability, tort law, insurance).
- Understanding hybrids and legal transplants.

## 🇩🇪 German Private Law (German Civil Law)

This course offers a comprehensive survey of German private law, including general rules of private law, commercial law, and civil procedure. The German Civil Code (BGB) is central, serving as the foundational "law book for citizens."

**Structure and Focus:**

- **Foundations:** Definition and historical development of private law; structure of the BGB.
- **Core Areas:** Detailed study of the law of obligations (contracts, torts, and unjust enrichment), property law, and law of succession and company law (including partnerships and corporations).
- **Context:** Understanding the influence of European Union (EU) law on German law, and mastering the accepted methods of interpretation.
- **Procedure:** Introduction to the system of law enforcement (civil procedure).

**Learning Outcomes:**
Students will be familiar with the structures of German civil law and equipped to handle smaller legal cases using the acquired knowledge.

## 🇪🇺 European Union Law & Market Freedoms

This module explores the profound impact of EU law on private individuals and companies operating in Europe. The internal market, built on the free movement of goods, persons, services, and capital, is a cornerstone of the EU.

**Key Topics:**

- **Market Freedoms:** Systematic survey of the market freedoms through the jurisprudence of the European Court of Justice.
- **Harmonization:** Examination of the harmonization of indirect and direct taxes by primary and secondary law.
- **Legal Principles:** Understanding the basic principles of European Law and the function of market freedom in fostering integration.

## 🌐 Specialized Legal Topics

### E-Commerce and Digital Law

This course provides a thorough introduction to legal issues arising from internet use and e-commerce, focusing on business-related activities.

**Key Areas:**

- **E-Commerce & Private Law:** Covering contract formation, standard terms and conditions, consumer protection, and internet auctions.
- **Advertising:** Rules of unfair competition, required imprint information, and liability under tele media law.
- **Intellectual Property:** Copyright restrictions for content presentation (including file sharing) and the protection of websites and domains.
- **Cross-Border Issues:** Addressing cross-border e-commerce challenges.

### Competition Law (Antitrust)

This course introduces the main areas of EU competition law, providing a solid basis for professional practice.

**Core Elements:**

- The fight against cartels and investigating/sanctioning cartels.
- Abuse of dominance and the importance of market definitions.
- Market power analysis for Articles 101 and 102 TFEU.
- European merger control and the relationship between EU and national competition law.

### International Dispute Resolution

This module compares cross-border litigation in state courts with international arbitration.

**Cross-Border Litigation:**

- International Jurisdiction of state courts (e.g., Brussels I Regulation).
- Provisional measures and procedural strategy.
- Recognition and enforcement of foreign judgments.

**Arbitration Law:**

- Advantages and disadvantages of arbitration.
- Drafting arbitration agreements and common rules (ICC, LCIA, Swiss Rules, DIS).
- Conducting proceedings and enforcing awards (New York Convention).

### Platform Regulation and AI Governance

This seminar series addresses the legal and societal challenges posed by digital platforms, AI, and algorithmic systems.

**Key Themes and Speakers:**

- **Regulatory Approaches:** Risk-based regulation as a principle of digital law; approaches to global understanding of platform regulation.
- **Governance:** Platform governance from a complementor perspective.
- **Bias and Risk:** Algorithmic bias and systemic risks of AI and platforms; discrimination risks.
- **Economic Impact:** Economic interests of platform operators and platforms as a digital public sphere.
- **EU Law:** Regulatory pitfalls with old and new intermediary powers in the EU.
- **Specific Regulations:** Company law and platform regulation; financial markets law as a blueprint for platform regulation; European liability principles for digital platforms.
- **EU Strategy:** Regulatory strategies, including regulated self-regulation, codes, and technical standardization.
