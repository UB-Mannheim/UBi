# Accounting for Climate Change

**Date:** February 21, 2022
**Time:** 05:00 – 06:15 PM (CET)
**Format:** Live virtual event (Registration required via Zoom)

## Speaker Profile

**Karthik Ramanna**
Professor of Business and Public Policy at the Blavatnik School of Government, University of Oxford.

Professor Ramanna is an expert on business-government relations, sustainable capitalism, and corporate reporting and auditing. His research focuses on how organizations build trust with stakeholders and the role of business in designing responsible "rules of the game." He has authored numerous articles and case studies on non-market strategies across various global regions (Africa, China, the EU, India, and the US) and has consulted with leading organizations such as Fidelity, KPMG, McKinsey, PwC, Sonae, and State Street.

## Seminar Abstract

The seminar addresses the growing pressure faced by corporations—from investors, advocacy groups, politicians, and business leaders—to reduce Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions across their operations and supply chains.

While nearly 90% of S&P 500 companies now issue ESG reports, the authors critique these documents as "catchall reports that are often made up of inaccurate, unverifiable, and contradictory data."

The proposed remedy is the **E-liability accounting system**. This system measures emissions using a combination of chemistry and engineering principles, applying cost accounting to assign emissions to individual outputs. The authors provide a detailed methodology for assigning E-liabilities across an entire value chain, using the complex example of a car-door manufacturer whose supply chain involves mining, shipping, steel production, and final delivery.
