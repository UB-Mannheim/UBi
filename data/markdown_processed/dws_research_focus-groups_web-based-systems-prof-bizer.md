# Web-based Systems Research Group

The Web-based Systems group conducts advanced research on methods for integrating data from large numbers of data sources within the context of the open Web and corporate data lakes.

Our core research areas include:

- **Data Integration:** Developing methods to combine disparate data sources.
- **Information Extraction:** Techniques such as entity matching, schema matching, and table annotation.
- **Data Discovery:** Identifying and structuring information from large Web corpora.
- **Modern Focus:** Current work heavily utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) and LLM-based agents for complex data integration tasks.

We apply these developed methods to practical challenges, such as integrating product data from numerous e-shops and constructing knowledge graphs like DBpedia. Our empirical research includes monitoring the adoption of `schema.org` annotations across the public Web by regularly extracting structured data from large Web corpora.
