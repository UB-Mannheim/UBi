# Constructing Carbon Abatement Cost Curves

This document outlines the methodology and principles behind constructing Carbon Abatement Cost Curves (ACC). These curves are essential tools used in sustainability planning and climate mitigation strategies to quantify the cost-effectiveness of various carbon reduction measures.

## Understanding Carbon Abatement Costs

Carbon abatement refers to the process of reducing or eliminating greenhouse gas emissions. The cost associated with this reduction is the abatement cost.

### Key Concepts

- **Abatement Cost:** The monetary cost required to reduce one unit of greenhouse gas emissions (e.g., €/ton CO₂e).
- **Cost Curve:** A graphical representation showing the relationship between the amount of emissions reduced (the abatement level) and the cost required to achieve that reduction.
- **The Concept of the "Knee":** The point on the cost curve where the marginal cost of abatement begins to rise sharply, indicating diminishing returns or the need for significantly more expensive technologies.

## Methodology for Curve Construction

The construction of an accurate ACC requires a systematic approach that considers technical feasibility, economic constraints, and policy frameworks.

### 1. Inventory and Baseline Assessment

Before calculating costs, a comprehensive baseline inventory of current emissions must be established. This involves:

- Identifying all emission sources (Scope 1, 2, and 3).
- Quantifying the current emission levels for each source.

### 2. Identifying Abatement Options

Potential reduction measures are categorized and evaluated. Examples include:

- **Energy Efficiency:** Upgrading insulation, optimizing HVAC systems.
- **Fuel Switching:** Replacing fossil fuels with renewable sources (e.g., switching from natural gas to heat pumps).
- **Process Optimization:** Implementing changes in industrial processes to reduce waste.
- **Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS):** Technologies designed to capture CO₂ before it enters the atmosphere.

### 3. Cost-Benefit Analysis

For each identified option, a detailed cost-benefit analysis is performed:

- **Capital Expenditure (CAPEX):** The initial investment required for the technology or measure.
- **Operational Expenditure (OPEX):** Ongoing costs, including maintenance and energy inputs.
- **Discount Rate:** Future costs and benefits are discounted back to their present value using an appropriate discount rate.

## Applications and Impact

The resulting ACC is not merely an academic exercise; it is a critical input for decision-making across various sectors.

### Strategic Planning

- **Investment Prioritization:** Helps organizations prioritize investments by identifying the lowest-cost abatement opportunities first.
- **Policy Design:** Governments use these curves to set realistic and economically viable emissions reduction targets.

### Sectoral Examples

| Sector | Primary Abatement Focus | Typical Cost Driver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Buildings** | Insulation, Heat Pump Installation | CAPEX of retrofitting |
| **Transportation** | Electrification, Public Transit | Infrastructure investment |
| **Industry** | Process redesign, Waste heat recovery | Technology adoption risk |
