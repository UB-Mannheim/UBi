## 🎤 Speaker Biography

**Ivo Welch** is a Distinguished Professor of Finance and holds the J. Fred Weston Chair in Finance at UCLA Anderson. His academic career includes time at Yale’s School of Management and Brown University’s economics department.

Professor Welch is widely recognized as an influential economist, having authored numerous publications with significant citation impacts. He is a National Bureau of Research (NBER) research associate and has received prestigious awards, including the Humboldt Research Award. His expertise spans several areas:

- **Core Expertise:** Informational cascades, initial public offerings, capital structure, dividends, and earnings management.
- **Other Areas:** Market-timing, performance evaluation, overconfidence, socially responsible investing, and bankruptcy.
- **Publications:** He has written a free textbook on corporate finance and serves as the editor of the Critical Finance Review.

## 💡 Seminar Abstract: Moving the Needle on Climate Change

Climate change poses a massive global challenge, potentially leading to significant warming by the end of the century. Professor Welch argues that current climate activism and international negotiations are insufficient to solve the problem.

**Key Arguments:**

- **Ineffectiveness of Current Efforts:** Individual carbon-footprint activism, setting optimal carbon tax levels, or relying on international treaties are unlikely to achieve major emission cuts.
- **The Real Challenge:** The ultimate outcome depends not on what the world *should* do, but what its *important decision-makers* will do.
- **Shifting Power Dynamics:** The OECD is no longer the sole determinant of global action. Non-OECD countries, which will house the majority of the world's population and emit a large share of greenhouse gases, must be considered.
- **The Solution:** The most critical task is the intelligent phasing out of fossil fuels. Subsidizing research in clean energy, particularly storage, is key. When cheaper clean energy arrives, it will displace fossil fuels globally, truly moving the needle.
