### Speaker Biography

Maximilian Auffhammer is the George Pardee Jr. Professor of International Sustainable Development and Chair of the Berkeley Division of the Academic Senate.

**Education & Career:**

- B.S. in environmental science (University of Massachusetts at Amherst, 1996).
- M.S. in environmental and resource economics (University of Massachusetts at Amherst, 1998).
- Ph.D. in economics (UC San Diego, 2003).
- He joined the faculty at UC Berkeley in 2003.
- He is a Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research (Energy and Environmental Economics group) and a Humboldt Fellow.
- He has served as a lead author for the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC).

**Research Focus:**
Professor Auffhammer's research focuses on environmental and resource economics, energy economics, and applied econometrics. His work has appeared in major journals including *The American Economic Review*, *The Review of Economic Studies*, *The Review of Economics and Statistics*, *The Economic Journal*, and *The Energy Journal*.

**Awards:**
Professor Auffhammer is the recipient of several distinguished awards, including:

- 2017 and 2021 Cheit Teaching Award (Haas School of Business).
- 2009 Campus Distinguished Teaching Award.
- 2007 Cozzarelli Prize (National Academies of Sciences).
- 2007 Sarlo Distinguished Mentoring Award.
