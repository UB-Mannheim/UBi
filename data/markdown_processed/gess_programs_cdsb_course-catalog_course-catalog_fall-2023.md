# CDSB Course Catalog (Fall 2023)

This catalog provides an overview of the courses offered during the Fall 2023 semester, covering advanced topics in economics, statistics, data science, and business research.

## 📚 Course Listings

### 1. Accounting and Taxation Research Seminar

- **Focus:** Current research topics in accounting and taxation. Visiting researchers present working papers, fostering discussion among faculty and students.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to discuss current research topics, develop original research ideas, and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper presentations and discussions (80%).
- **Prerequisites:** Mathematics for Economists, intermediate knowledge of microeconomics.
- **Schedule:**
  - Seminar: 04.09.23 – 04.12.23 (Monday, 15:30 – 17:00, O 048)
  - Seminar: 05.09.23 – 05.12.23 (Tuesday, 13:45 – 15:15, SO 318)
- **Details:** Seminar dates are announced [here](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/area-accounting-taxation/research/research-seminar/).

### 2. Applied Econometrics Methodology

- **Focus:** Applied introduction to empirical research methodology. Main topics include Ordinary Least Squares, instrumental variables estimation, and panel data econometrics.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students apply econometric methods common in economic research, with special attention given to interpreting empirical results and understanding potential caveats.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam (10 minutes) 50%, Class Participation 50%.
- **Prerequisites:** Basic mathematical knowledge.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 04.09.23 – 04.12.23 (Monday, 08:30 – 10:00, O226–28)
  - Lecture: 06.09.23 – 06.12.23 (Wednesday, 08:30 – 10:00, SO318)

### 3. Research Seminar in Econometrics (Advanced)

- **Focus:** Advanced treatment of econometric theory and applications. Topics include Matrix Algebra and Probability Theory, Ordinary Least Squares, Maximum Likelihood, Generalized Method of Moments, time series, and panel data econometrics.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain deep knowledge of econometric theory and practical application.
- **Assessment:** Written Exam (120 min) 90%, Problem sets 10%.
- **Prerequisites:** Basic mathematical knowledge.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 05.09.23 – 05.12.23 (Tuesday, 10:15 – 11:45, SO 318)
  - Lecture: 07.09.23 – 07.12.23 (Thursday, 10:15 – 11:45, O048)
  - Exercise: 06.09.23 – 06.12.23 (Wednesday, 15:30 – 17:00, 257 (L7, 3–5))
  - Exercise: 08.09.23 – 08.12.23 (Friday, 10:15 – 11:45, O 226–28)

### 4. Microeconomic Theory for Business Research

- **Focus:** Graduate-level introduction to microeconomic theory, highlighting aspects relevant to business research. Topics include consumer choice, theory of the firm, markets, strategic behavior, and asymmetric information.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Equips doctoral students with tools to read, discuss, and formulate research based on theoretically grounded hypotheses.
- **Assessment:** Midterm and final exams: 80–100%, Exercises: Up to 20%.
- **Prerequisites:** Mathematics for Economists, intermediate knowledge of microeconomics.
- **Schedule:** (Detailed schedule provided in the original document, covering multiple dates in Oct/Nov/Dec 2023).

### 5. Advanced Mathematics (Analysis and Linear Algebra)

- **Focus:** Covers fundamental mathematical concepts: metric/normed spaces, vector spaces, matrix algebra, linear transformations, and multivariate differentiability.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop rigorous mathematical proof skills and apply knowledge of limits, derivatives, and linear algebra to solve optimization problems.
- **Assessment:** Exam (120 min).
- **Prerequisites:** Basic mathematical knowledge.
- **Schedule:** (Detailed schedule provided in the original document, covering multiple dates in Sep/Oct/Nov/Dec 2023).

### 6. Sustainability Seminar (ESG)

- **Focus:** Latest advances and challenges in the transition towards sustainable business practices and net carbon emissions of zero. Topics include the economics and management of sustainability activities.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Introduces students to current research paradigms in sustainability and trains them in presenting and evaluating academic papers.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper presentations and discussions (80%).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 04.09.23 – 04.12.23 (Monday, 17:15 – 18:45, O 129)

### 7. Data Science in Action (Lecture Series)

- **Focus:** Online lecture series by the Mannheim Center for Data Science. A bridge course for GESS doctoral students.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Provides practical experience in data science projects, focusing on analyzing relational data and network science.
- **Assessment:** 5 ECTS points awarded for 80% attendance and a 15-page essay (pass/fail).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** Starts Thursday, 14 September 2023 (Online). [More information](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/datascience/details/vortragsreihe-data-science-in-action/)

### 8. Information Systems & Operations Management Research

- **Focus:** Current research topics in IS and Operations Management. Visiting researchers present working papers.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop research ideas and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects in IS/OM.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar format).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Seminar: 06.09.23 – 06.12.23 (Wednesday, 12:00 – 13:30, O 142)
  - Seminar: 26.10.23 – 26.10.23 (Thursday, 12:00 – 13:30, O 148)

### 9. Design Science Research in IS

- **Focus:** Comprehensive overview of design science in IS research, covering definitions, principles, theoretical foundations, and methodologies.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students understand the basic principles for successfully carrying out design science research.
- **Assessment:** Assignment, Presentation, Discussion.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Kick-off: 18.09.23 (Monday, 15:30 – 17:00, tba)

### 10. Philosophy of Science and Research Methods

- **Focus:** Basic understanding of the philosophy of science and its epistemological foundations. Covers knowledge derived from observation, induction, and refutation, including experimentalism.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students understand the underlying epistemological foundations of IS research and learn to critically assess research structures.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Interactive seminar).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.

### 11. Marketing Research Methods

- **Focus:** Fundamental research concepts and techniques in marketing.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to design and evaluate research in marketing, integrating diverse research techniques.
- **Assessment:** Essay: 30%, Presentation: 70%.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 07.09.23 – 07.12.23 (Thursday, 13:45 – 15:15, room 107 (L5, 2))

### 12. Discrete Choice Methods

- **Focus:** State-of-the-art discrete choice methods (logit, probit, mixed logit, etc.) used to examine consumer and agent choices.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students master the theoretical and empirical application of choice models.
- **Assessment:** Written Exam (60 minutes) 50%, Home Assignments 50%.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** (Multiple dates in Sep/Oct/Nov/Dec 2023, Friday mornings).

### 13. Intensive Longitudinal Methods (ILM)

- **Focus:** Research designs and questions for ILM. Covers data collection methods (diary, experience sampling, EMA) and advanced data analysis (multilevel analysis).
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students can develop and evaluate ILM research designs, data collection methods, and appropriate data analysis approaches.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar format).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 05.09.23 – 05.12.23 (Tuesday, 13:45 – 15:15, L4, 1, room 004)

### 14. Nonprofit Sector Management

- **Focus:** Basic understanding of the institutions and economic/managerial theories of the nonprofit sector.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Provides a basic understanding of the theory and management of NPOs.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar format).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** (Multiple dates in Sep/Oct/Nov 2023, Monday afternoons).

### 15. Quantitative Management Research

- **Focus:** Overview of statistical procedures and methods relevant in management research (e.g., Moderation, Mediation, Multilevel modelling, Missing data).
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students can identify issues, perform statistical analyses, and design quantitative research projects in management.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam (20 minutes) 75%, Presentation 25%.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Seminar: 03.11.23 – 17.11.23 (Friday, 09:00 – 17:00, EO 256)

### 16. Theoretical Foundations of Management

- **Focus:** Analyzing verbal theories and constructing coherent theoretical arguments without formal language. Draws on innovation management, organization theory, and sociology.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain skills in composing the front section of an academic manuscript.
- **Assessment:** Assignment 40%, Paper 50%, Class Participation 10%.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** (Multiple dates in Oct/Nov 2023, Wednesday/Thursday).

### 17. Finance Research Seminar

- **Focus:** Current research topics in finance. Visiting researchers present working papers.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop research ideas and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects in finance.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar format).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:**
  - Seminar: 04.09.23 – 04.12.23 (Monday, 15:30 – 17:00, L9, 1–2, room 004)

### 18. Taxation Law and Policy

- **Focus:** Impact of European Union Law on the taxation of individuals and companies. Covers EU law, indirect taxes, and fundamental freedoms.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Deep understanding of the legal framework governing taxation in Europe.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar format).
- **Prerequisites:** Basic understanding of EU Law and Tax Law.
- **Schedule:**
  - Lecture: 07.09.23 – 07.12.23 (Thursday, 12:00 – 13:30, EO 169)
