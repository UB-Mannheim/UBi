# Julia Haaf: Hierarchical Modeling and Individual Differences in Cognitive Tasks

**Date:** 30 June
**Time:** 14:00
**Event:** Summer School Archive 2023

## Overview

Julia Haaf discusses the utility of hierarchical models, which are crucial for repeated measures designs where multiple observations are nested within participants. These models optimally account for data structure, thereby reducing estimation bias and improving predictive accuracy compared to simpler alternatives. A key advantage highlighted is the ability to explicitly separate noise variability from true variability across people, thereby enhancing the understanding of individual differences in various cognitive tasks.

## Key Topics Covered

The talk outlines several recent collaborative projects utilizing hierarchical modeling:

1. **Introduction to Bayesian Hierarchical Modeling:** A brief introduction to the methodology (Collaborator: Myrthe Veenman).
1. **Speed-Accuracy Trade-off:** Discussion on how multivariate Bayesian hierarchical modeling can be used to account for the speed-accuracy trade-off (Collaborator: Dr. Suzanne Hoogeveen).
1. **Attentional Control:** Exploring whether cognitive Bayesian hierarchical modeling can improve the reliability of individual difference measures of attentional control (Collaborator: Michelle Hoogeveen).
1. **Development of Mathematics Skills:** Investigating whether combining Bayesian hierarchical modeling with computational modeling can help better understand the development of mathematics skills in elementary school (Collaborator: Şeyma Nur Ertekin).
