## Seminar Abstract

This seminar examines pricing dynamics within the Voluntary Carbon Market (VCM). The analysis utilizes a proprietary dataset detailing sales of emission reduction certificates (credits) from a leading VCM dealer.

Key findings include:

- **Price Dispersion:** Extraordinary price variation is observed, with carbon credits trading between a few cents and $100 per ton of CO2.
- **Pricing Factors:** Prices are systematically related to the credit project, the buyer, and specific trade characteristics, rather than to a single common value of carbon emissions.
- **Externalities:** Credits from technologies with positive non-carbon externalities, even if less reliable, are priced higher than trusted industrial solutions.
- **Buyer Premium:** Buyers in low-emission industries, wealthier countries, and large firms pay a premium for carbon credits, while heavy polluters and firms with explicit sustainability commitments do not.
- **Market Mechanics:** VCM pricing features volume and client relationship discounts typical of over-the-counter markets.
- **Futures Market Impact:** A causal link is documented between the introduction of the VCM futures market and the price premium for credits eligible for expiring futures settlement.

## Speaker Biography

**Florian Berg** is a research scientist at the MIT Sloan School of Management. He cofounded and co-runs the Aggregate Confusion Project.

**Research Focus:** His research interests cover sustainability-related measurement, with a specific focus on:

- ESG ratings and disclosure
- Investor impact
- Carbon accounting
- Voluntary carbon markets

**Experience:**

- **Education:** Received his PhD in financial economics from Paris-Dauphine University. During his PhD, he held a visiting research position at ETH Zurich.
- **Professional Roles:** Worked as a quantitative researcher at Amundi Asset Management and as a quantitative strategist/trader at Alphadyne Asset Management.
- **Teaching:** Taught at Paris-Dauphine University, Sciences-Po Paris, and MIT Sloan.
- **Recognition:** Has written op-eds for publications including the *Financial Times* and the *Wall Street Journal*. His research has won numerous awards, including the Pagano and Zechner price.
- **Affiliations:** He is a member of the Consultative Working Group of ESMA, the European securities regulator.
