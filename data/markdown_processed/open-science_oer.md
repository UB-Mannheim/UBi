# Open Educational Resources (OERs)

Open Educational Resources (OERs) are any type of educational material that is shared openly, allowing anyone to legally and freely reuse it, for example, within their own teaching. OERs can take many forms, including syllabi, presentation slides, lecture notes, assignments, tests, and textbooks, and may come in any format or medium. The reuse of this material is enabled via open licenses, such as the Creative Commons licenses.

## Finding and Using OERs

Reusing OER is generally allowed for anyone without needing to seek individual permission. However, with the exception of public domain / CC0 material, the source must always be mentioned, and other restrictions might be enforced by the specific license.

Different types of open educational resources can be found in various repositories and specialized search engines. Good starting points include:

- [Linkliste rund um Open Educational Resources (ZOERR)](http://hdl.handle.net/10900.3/OER_HCCBPVNQ)
- [OER-Verzeichnisse und -Services (OERinfo)](https://open-educational-resources.de/materialien/oer-verzeichnisse-und-services/)

These resources also contain good places to search for free images, icons, music, and videos that can be reused in teaching materials.

**Important Note on Copyright:**
It is crucial to respect copyright and indicate correctly any material sourced from someone else. For more detailed information, please consult:

- [„Urheberrecht“ site by the University Library](https://www.bib.uni-mannheim.de/lehren-und-forschen/urheberrecht/)
- [A simple rule (TULLU) to remember the most important parts](https://open-educational-resources.de/oer-tullu-regel/)
- In-depth article: [„Kombinieren, Bearbeiten, Remixen: OER richtig verwenden“](https://irights.info/artikel/kombinieren-bearbeiten-remixen-oer-richtig-verwenden/28560) (in German)

## Creating and Publishing OERs

You can share your own educational material as Open Educational Resources. When creating your own material, it is important that you are allowed to share any containing parts from others (e.g., if you are using existing OERs).

For publishing your Open Educational Resources, we recommend using [ZOERR](https://www.oerbw.de/), the Central Open Educational Resources Repository for all universities in the state of Baden-Württemberg and their partners. Alternative platforms or publishing options may be available depending on the type of media.

**Recommended Licenses:**
We recommend using the Creative Commons License [CC-BY](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/) or [CC-BY-SA](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/)-SA for your Open Educational Resources.
