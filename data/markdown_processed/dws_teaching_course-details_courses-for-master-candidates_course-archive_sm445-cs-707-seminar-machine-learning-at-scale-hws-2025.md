# Seminar: Machine Learning at Scale (SM445/CS 707)

This seminar focuses on **Machine Learning at Scale**, addressing the challenges and techniques required for modern ML applications. Efficiency and scale are critical, whether dealing with large training datasets, massive compute requirements, large models, high inference costs, large context windows, or deployment on edge devices. The seminar explores techniques to achieve this, including parallel processing and various cost-reduction approaches.

## 🎯 Learning Goals and Tasks

Participants are expected to develop deep research skills through the following tasks:

- **Literature Review:** Read, understand, and explore current scientific literature.
- **Report Writing:** Summarize a current research topic in a concise report (10 single-column pages + references).
- **Presentations:** Deliver two presentations on the topic:
  - A 3-minute flash presentation.
  - A 15-minute final presentation.
- **Discussion:** Moderate a scientific discussion about a fellow student's topic.
- **Peer Review:** Review (drafts of) reports from fellow students.

## 💡 Topic Areas and Focus

Students will be assigned a larger topic area in an active, relevant field of machine learning based on their preferences. The seminar structure requires two levels of depth:

1. **Topic Area Overview (1/4):** Provide a short, concise overview of the assigned topic area (e.g., using a survey paper or book chapter). This requires discussing the main goals, challenges, and relevance of the field.
1. **Deep Dive (3/4):** Present a self-selected topic within that area in more detail (e.g., using a recent research paper). This requires explaining the concrete problem statement, the solution/contribution, and providing personal analysis.

**Potential Topic Areas Include:**

- Parallel training
- Parallel inference
- Data management
- Lifecycle management
- Cost-savings (quantization, model distillation)
- Vector databases
- Systems for machine learning
- Sampling
- Federated learning
- Mixture of Experts (MoE)
