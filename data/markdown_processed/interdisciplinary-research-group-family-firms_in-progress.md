# Research in Progress: Family Firms and Interdisciplinary Studies

The study of family and business—two very different contexts—requires allowing for multiple perspectives and multiple truths. In the social system of a family, there are often as many views as family members, and these views change across interactions, situations, and time.

Family firm research is therefore **wholistic and inductive**. It recognizes that each member, view, and reality must be fully seen and understood, as relationships are the basis. This understanding may yield contradictory, contextual perspectives, especially when family and business logics diverge.

While allowing for a fluid and adaptive basis of multiplicity and divergence, family firm research must ultimately unearth coherences to move the firm forward. Meanings, which may only exist locally or temporarily, must be subjected to empirical testing to find their influence. Therefore, the research is **positivistic**.

In line with this, our research group builds bridges across several fields: Business Studies, Sociology, Psychology, Information Systems, and Equality & Intercultural Studies. We utilize supercomputing resources and carry out in-depth case studies, combining quantitative, qualitative, positivist, and inductive research methods.

## Ongoing Research Projects

We showcase selected ongoing projects across key thematic areas. Feel free to reach out if you work on similar topics.

### 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Firms & Entrepreneurship

- **Ambidexterity:** Stability and agility in family firms (Fritsch).
- **Strategic Alliances:** Examining strategic alliances among family firms (Kulandaisamy).
- **Leadership & Crisis:** Leader identity and crisis performance in family firms (Ahrens et al.; [Publication](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/08944865251314530)).
- **Crowdfunding:** Theorizing and understanding in crowdfunding (Ahrens).
- **Succession:** Research on succession and leader attributes (Riefolo & Ahrens et al.; [Publication I](https://familybusiness.org/content/should-former-leaders-stay-involved-in-the-family-firm)).

### 🤖 Artificial Intelligence & Human Organizations

- **Intelligent Organization:** Viewing the organization as an intelligent entity (Riefolo, Grosskopf, Ahrens; [Publication](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1877858525000415)).
- **Global Influence:** Supercomputing for the global identification of family influence (Ahrens, Istipliler, Kowalzick).
- **Management Science:** Utilizing supercomputer systems and management science (Baumgardt).

### ♻️ AI & Supercomputing for Sustainability

- **CEO Insiderness:** Investigating CEO insiderness and sustainability (Sami, Fritsch, Ahrens).
- **Midlife Crisis:** The Midlife Crisis and sustainability (Riefolo, Manganaro, Fritsch, Ahrens).
- **Twin Transformation:** Research on twin transformation (Fröhlich, de Groote, Felden, Ahrens).

### 🌍 Diversity, Equality, & Intercultural Management

- **Gender Equality:** Studies on gender equality and family firms (Ahrens, Bruna & Sami et al.; [Publication](https://familybusiness.org/content/better-let-your-brother-do-it-men-are-still-preferred-in-ceo-suc)).
- **Cultural Diversity:** Research on cultural diversity and sustainability (Grosskopf, Riefolo, Ahrens).
- **Culture & Sustainability:** Examining culture, segregation, and sustainability (Glowka, Fritsch, Stangej, Geissinger, Bromley, & Ahrens).

### 💖 Personality, Values, & Organizations

- **Self-Transformation:** Entrepreneurship as a transformation of the Self (Radtke).
- **Branding:** Personal brands and organizational image – familiness & sustainability in hospitality and luxury (Manganaro & Ahrens).
- **Marketing:** Branding & Marketing Strategies in Family Firms (Finkbeiner).
- **Relationships:** Marriage and modern relationships in entrepreneurial families (Stamm, Bernhard, Ahrens, Istipliler; [Publication](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11187-023-00832-9)).
