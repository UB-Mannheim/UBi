## 📝 Seminar Abstract

The European Union designates 26% of its landmass as a protected area. Protected area policies aim to limit economic development in favor of biodiversity.

This paper utilizes a high-resolution remote sensing dataset covering Europe. By analyzing the staggered introduction of protected area policies between 1980 and 2020, the study investigates two key questions: the selection of land for protection and the causal effect of protection on vegetation cover.

**Key Findings:**

- The results show that levels and trends in vegetation cover, measured by an NDVI index, are statistically indistinguishable for protected and unprotected land.
- The study concludes that land protected later does not exhibit different greening trends compared to land protected earlier.
- The overall conclusion is that European conservation efforts lack ambition because policymakers tend to select land for protection that is not currently threatened by development.
