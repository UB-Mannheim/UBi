# Responsible News Recommender Systems (ReNewRS)

## Project Overview

This interdisciplinary research project investigates whether online news recommender systems contribute to social polarization or radicalization. As news consumption is increasingly curated by algorithms, the project addresses the controversy surrounding "filter bubbles"—situations where users only perceive information that reinforces existing opinions while excluding conflicting viewpoints.

**Core Objectives:**
The ReNewRS project examines the occurrence and effects of filter bubbles caused by news recommender systems on user opinion formation.

**Methodology:**
The research is conducted through a series of experimental studies. To trace potential polarizing effects, the team selects divisive news topics, drawing article samples from various news sources with different political slants. These samples are used to run different versions of a prototypical news recommender system. By comparing the outcomes of various recommendation algorithms, the project aims to derive empirical guidelines for configuring responsible news recommender systems that mitigate polarization and enable more balanced opinion formation.

**Interdisciplinary Approach:**
The project integrates expertise from:

- Media and Communication Studies (foundation for analyzing user effects).
- Data Science and Computer Science (technical implementation and system development).
- Business Informatics.
