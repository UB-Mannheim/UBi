# Center for Doctoral Studies in Business (CDSB)

The Center for Doctoral Studies in Business offers seven fields of specialization:

- [Accounting](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/accounting/)
- [Finance](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/finance/)
- [Information Systems](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/information-systems/)
- [Management](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/management/)
- [Marketing](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/marketing/)
- [Operations Management](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/operations-management/)
- [Taxation](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/cdsb-programs/taxation/)

## Program Structure

The full-time doctoral program in business is divided into two main phases:

### 📚 Course Phase

The teaching program consists of core and elective courses. Students must:

- Take all scheduled core courses for their respective program (detailed in the [CDSB Program Regulations](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/student-information/regulations/)).
- Attend the regular area seminar, which is compulsory from the first semester.
- Submit a dissertation proposal at the end of the first year.
- Attend elective courses within their field of specialization or from other CDSB study programs.
- Complete the program’s English Academic Writing course and a bridge course in either the [Center for Doctoral Studies in Economics (CDSE)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/course-catalog/course-catalog/) or the [Center for Doctoral Studies in Behavioral and Social Sciences (CDSS)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdss/course-catalog/).

### 🔬 Research Phase

During the thesis writing phase, students work on their own research agenda under close supervision. Students are expected to:

- Write independent research papers.
- Present these papers in seminar form.
- Submit their work to international conferences and journals.

## Program Requirements

The successful completion of the CDSB programs requires meeting the following academic milestones:

### Year One

- Exams in all scheduled courses.
- Attendance at the Area Seminar.
- Submission of the Dissertation Proposal (by August 31st).

### Year Two

- Exams in scheduled courses, including the Bridge Course from the CDSE or CDSS course catalogue.
- Attendance at the Area Seminar.
- Passing the English Academic Writing Course (3 ECTS).

### Year Three (Onwards)

- Regular participation in Area Seminars.

## Degree Requirements and Process

To earn a doctoral degree, students must fulfill the following requirements:

1. Completion of the CDSB course program.
1. Completion, submission, and acceptance of the thesis.
1. Successful thesis defense.

**Degree Awarded:** Dr. rer. pol.

**Thesis Format:**
Doctoral theses typically consist of three original essays in one area, which are intended for publication in esteemed scholarly journals. However, the classical dissertation format (a longer text analyzing one topic in depth) is also possible, provided the content is intended for subsequent publication in scholarly journals. The final decision on the format is made by the student and their supervisor.

For detailed instructions on submitting the thesis, please refer to the [Degree Process](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/for-active-students/cdsb/degree-process/).

## Doctoral Degrees in Germany

In Germany, doctoral degrees often use specific Latin designations to indicate the field of study. This differs from the Ph.D. (doctor philosophiae), which is conferred across many academic fields.

At the GESS, the following doctoral titles are conferred:

- **Faculty of Business and Economics:** Dr. rer. pol. (doctor rerum politicarum)
- **Faculty of Social Sciences:** Dr. rer. soc. (doctor rerum socialium)
