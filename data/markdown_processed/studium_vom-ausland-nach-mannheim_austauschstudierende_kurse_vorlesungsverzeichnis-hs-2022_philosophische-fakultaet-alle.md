# Academic Course Catalog: Humanities, Languages, and Culture

This catalog provides an overview of seminar and lecture courses available in various humanities and language disciplines. The courses are designed for exchange students and are generally structured around a combination of theoretical study, close reading, and practical analysis.

## 🇩🇪 Germanistik (German Studies)

The seminars and lectures in this catalog are primarily aimed at students of the Philosophical Faculty. As exchange students, you can register for any of the courses listed below, regardless of your major. Please check for prerequisites such as language skills or academic level.

- Click on a course title for more information.

**Medievalism and Popular Culture**

- **Focus:** The seminar examines the concept of "Medievalism" (the reception, interpretation, or recreation of the European Middle Ages in post-medieval cultures). It analyzes how the Middle Ages are depicted in popular media—from books and film to video games (e.g., *Shrek*, *Monty Python's Knights of the Round Table*, *The Green Knight*, *Game of Thrones*).
- **Learning Objectives:** To understand how different medieval designs function in popular media and to analyze the interdisciplinary discourse surrounding the appropriation of the Middle Ages.
- **Assessment:**
  - **Studienleistung:** Students take a text sponsorship role, presenting an example and leading the seminar discussion.
  - **Modulprüfungen:** Either a 20-minute oral examination or a written term paper (20–25 pages).

**The Parzival Cycle (Wolfram von Eschenbach)**

- **Focus:** Complete reading of *Parzival* and discussion of selected themes for textual interpretation. The seminar addresses the complexity and density of the text, which narrates not only Parzival's journey but also the stories of his parents and the unsuccessful quest for the Grail.
- **Learning Objectives:** To develop skills in analyzing complex, allusive medieval literature and to contribute to the seminar's focus by formulating discussion questions on the assigned reading sections.
- **Assessment:**
  - **Studienleistung:** Students formulate discussion impulses and help determine the thematic and methodological focus of the seminar.
  - **Modulprüfungen:** Either a 20-minute oral examination or a written term paper (20–25 pages).

## 📚 Romanistik (Literary Studies)

### Maghreb Literature

- **Focus:** Examining the literature of Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia, which have achieved global significance. The seminar aims to provide an overview of key voices and analyze their global connections, including themes of migration, cultural/sexual identity, and gender justice.
- **Key Authors:** Albert Camus, Mouloud Feraoun, Assia Djebar, Abdellah Taia, Alice Zeniter, and Kamel Daoud.
- **Readings:**
  - Bertolio, Johnny L.: *Controcanone. La letteratura delle donne dalle origini a oggi.* Torino: Loescher, 2022.
  - Brogi, Daniela: *Lo spazio delle donne.* Torino: Einaudi, 2022.
  - Kahlert, Heike: „Differenz, Genealogie, Affidamento: Das italienische 'Pensiero della differenza sessuale' in der internationalen Rezeption“ (in: Becker/Kortendies, 2010).
  - Scharold, Irmgard (Hg.): *Scrittura femminile. Italienische Autorinnen im 20. Jahrhundert zwischen Historie, Fiktion und Autobiographie.* Tübingen 2002.
- **Assessment:** A full list of authors and presentation dates will be provided on ILIAS starting February 3rd.

### Gender Violence and Literature

- **Focus:** Analyzing various facets of violence against women, which is not limited to non-Western regions. The seminar examines the structural causes of patriarchal violence (from misogyny to serial femicide) using examples from Spanish and Latin American culture.
- **Themes:** Analyzing artistic representations of violence as strategies of resistance and reclaiming control over the "female territory" of the body and "speech power."
- **Readings:** Includes historical narratives on gender violence from Spanish literature (Emilia Pardo Bazán), Argentine *cuentos* (Jorge Luis Borges, Mariana Enriquez), and novels/films from Mexican and Chilean literature (Roberto Bolaño, Cristina Rivera Garza).
- **Assessment:** A literature and film list, along with presentation dates, will be provided on ILIAS starting February 3rd.

### Anglistik (English Linguistics)

#### Phonetics and Phonology

- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Describe, classify, and visualize individual speech sounds.
  - Use state-of-the-art software to analyze spectrograms of real speech and take basic acoustic measurements.
  - Understand how speech sounds work together in a system, deducing patterns and rules from real speech data.
  - Practice project and time management, critical thinking, and analysis.
- **Readings:**
  - Ashby, M., & Maidment, J. (2005). *Introducing Phonetic Science.* Cambridge University Press.
  - Edwards, J. G. H. (2023). *The sounds of English around the world: an introduction to phonetics and phonology.* Cambridge University Press.
  - Nathan, G. S. (2008). *Phonology: A cognitive grammar introduction.* John Benjamins.
  - Odden, D. (2005). *Introducing Phonology.* Cambridge University Press.
- **Assessment:** Klausur (written exam), Oral exam, Term paper.

#### Syntax

- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Deepen knowledge of English syntax, combining analyses of Present-Day English (PDE) with historical stages (Old, Middle, and Early Modern English).
  - Analyze major syntactic phenomena and explain the grammaticality of structures using theoretical models.
  - Apply the framework of generative syntax.
- **Prerequisite:** Basic grammatical knowledge is assumed.
- **Assessment:**
  - Short presentation of two assignments in class.
  - Passing of final exam.

#### Semantics

- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Develop a comprehensive understanding of the linguistic study of meaning.
  - Encounter different theoretical approaches to meaning and make connections between Semantics and other related disciplines.
  - Improve critical thinking skills by assessing different theoretical approaches to meaning.
- **Readings:**
  - Harley, T. A. (2014). *The psychology of language: From data to theory* (4th ed.). Hove, New York: Psychology Press.
  - Saeed, J. I. (2015). *Semantics* (4th ed.). Malden, Mass.: Wiley Blackwell.
- **Assessment:** Klausur (written exam), Hausarbeit (term paper), Prüfungsgespräch (oral exam).

#### Psycholinguistics and Language Contact

- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Understand the relationship between language and non-linguistic cognitive domains (e.g., emotion, color, decision making).
  - Become familiar with experimental paradigms used to study language contact.
  - Practice designing experiments and critically reflecting on questions like whether language affects non-linguistic cognition.
- **Readings:**
  - Malt, B. C. & Wolff, P. (2010). *Words and the mind – How words capture human experience.* Oxford University Press.
- **Assessment:** Full completion of coursework (Studienleistungen), Term paper, or Oral exam.

### International Cultural Studies & American Literature

**American Mythologies and Identity**

- **Focus:** Analyzing how American mythologies have evolved from the frontier narrative to apocalyptic visions. The seminar investigates the intersections of race, gender, and class in the construction of national myths.
- **Learning Objectives:** To develop a critical approach to how myths influence social, cultural, and political ideologies and to interpret literary works within historical and cultural contexts.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam or term paper.

**The American Gothic**

- **Focus:** Examining the defining characteristics of the American Gothic genre, particularly how it reflects and challenges American social values.
- **Learning Objectives:** To analyze how race and gender shape Gothic themes and settings, and to apply theoretical frameworks (feminist theory, critical race theory) to critique social hierarchies.
- **Assessment:** Hausarbeit (term paper) or Prüfungsgespräch (oral exam).

**American Literary History**

- **Focus:** Revising and deepening knowledge of American literary history, developing an understanding of the relationship between romanticism, modernism, and postmodernism.
- **Learning Objectives:** To analyze primary texts through specific historical and thematic frameworks and to understand the transformation of American founding ideals and myths.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam or term paper.

**Queer Literature and Theory**

- **Focus:** Deepening knowledge of the cultural and social history of the U.S. from the 1950s to the present, focusing on queer literature.
- **Learning Objectives:** To familiarize students with key concepts of Queer Theory and Gender Studies, and to analyze how political and cultural changes impacted queer writing.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam or term paper (10–12 pages).

**Global Varieties of English**

- **Focus:** Introduction to the major varieties of English used worldwide.
- **Learning Objectives:** To recognize, describe, and classify features of English varieties, and to apply linguistic analysis skills to World Englishes research models.
- **Assessment:** Presentation of one variety of English, passing an oral exam or writing a term paper.

## 🏛️ General Academic Skills & Assessment

### General Learning Outcomes

Across multiple disciplines, students are expected to:

- Analyze and interpret literary texts using text-based, context-based, and theoretical approaches.
- Identify, explain, and position themselves relative to the main claims and arguments of research articles.
- Formulate their own ideas on literary texts in the form of thesis statements.
- Develop, articulate, and defend individual academic theses (orally and in writing).

### Common Assessment Methods

- **Hausarbeit:** Term paper (typically 12–15 pages).
- **Klausur:** Written examination (e.g., 90-minute exam).
- **Prüfungsgespräch:** Oral examination (e.g., 20 minutes).
- **Presentation:** In-class presentation or seminar participation.
