# CDSB Course Catalog: Advanced Research Methods

This catalog provides an overview of advanced doctoral-level courses focusing on empirical research methods across various disciplines, including Accounting, Finance, Management, and Information Systems.

## 📚 Core Research Methodologies (General)

These courses provide foundational training in empirical research design, data analysis, and advanced econometric techniques, applicable across multiple social science disciplines.

### Advanced Empirical Research Methods

- **Focus:** Provides a comprehensive overview of research topics and methods using influential seminal and contemporaneous papers. The course covers core areas such as Earnings Management, Valuation, Voluntary/Mandatory Disclosure, International Accounting, and Corporate Narratives.
- **Content:** Each session includes an overview lecture on core methods and a joint discussion of selected empirical methods. Students are expected to prepare readings and lead class discussions.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to critically evaluate the relevance and technical rigor of current research, enabling them to develop meaningful research ideas.
- **Assessment:** Exam (50%) and Paper Presentations (50%).
- **Prerequisites:** Recommended: Empirical Accounting Research I, Advanced Econometrics I, Applied Econometrics.

### Causal Inference and Experimental Design

- **Focus:** Emphasizes experimental methods as a critical tool for examining advanced topics in entrepreneurship and management. Students learn about different types of experiments, how to design and implement their own studies, and how to conduct basic power analyses.
- **Content:** Covers the entire research lifecycle, from problem formulation to data acquisition, selection of methods, analysis, and presentation. Includes ethical considerations and preregistration.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain the ability to understand, design, and conduct experimental studies, culminating in a short paper suitable for a PhD thesis.
- **Assessment:** Presentation (20%) and Term Paper (80%).
- **Prerequisites:** Intermediate level of quantitative/econometric analysis.

### Data Science and Textual Analysis

- **Focus:** Guides students through scientific data-science projects, specifically focusing on analyzing textual data (e.g., news or social media datasets) using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.
- **Content:** Hands-on approach covering the entire project lifecycle.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain practical experience in conducting scientific data-science projects, improving presentation and communication skills.
- **Assessment:** Written Elaboration (90%) and Presentation (10%).
- **Prerequisites:** Knowledge of basic statistics and prior experience with R or Stata is helpful.

### Quantitative Research Design and Econometrics

- **Focus:** A hands-on, paper-based approach to understanding and analyzing data. It examines the interplay between sampling and experimental methods, statistical inference, and estimation of causal effects.
- **Content:** Uses the German Business Panel as a case study to implement cutting-edge methods. Students present and discuss research papers and write a short technical report.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Develops skills in designing questionnaires and survey experiments, data analysis, quantitative methods, and writing technical reports.
- **Assessment:** Technical Report and Participation.
- **Prerequisites:** Aimed at Ph.D. students in or beyond their second year.

## 🔬 Specialized Research Areas

### Systematic Literature Reviews

- **Focus:** Teaches the methodology for conducting systematic reviews, which are established as an independent research genre.
- **Content:** Introduces different types of literature reviews and deepens knowledge of systematic reviews according to standards like PRISMA or comparable statements.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Participants complete a systematic review related to their PhD topic by the end of the course.
- **Assessment:** Class Participation & Discussion (20%), Presentation (10%), Paper (70%).
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### Climate and Sustainable Finance

- **Focus:** Introduces graduate students to questions and methods in the rapidly evolving fields of climate/sustainable finance.
- **Content:** Connects global researchers and addresses the need for collaboration in climate change mitigation.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students are trained to present and evaluate papers in seminars and conferences, identifying promising avenues for future research.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper Presentations and Discussions (80%).
- **Prerequisites:** Assumes a background in core graduate-level finance.

### Accounting and Taxation Research

- **Focus:** Advanced empirical methods in accounting and taxation.
- **Content:** Covers core empirical methods for causal identification, including Regression Discontinuity (RDD), Instrumental Variables (IV), Discrete Choice Models, and Difference-in-Difference.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students become proficient in identifying appropriate empirical methods for their own research and critically discussing state-of-the-art literature.
- **Assessment:** Two Presentations (40%), Research Paper (40%), Participation (20%).
- **Prerequisites:** Advanced Econometrics I or Applied Econometrics I.

### Finance and Behavioral Economics

- **Focus:** Reviews recent research on possible mispricing in financial markets due to psychological biases (Behavioral Finance).
- **Content:** Covers topics like overconfidence, prospect theory, and heuristic-driven biases, integrating behavioral findings with neo-classical theory.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students understand how human and social cognitive and emotional biases affect market prices and returns.
- **Assessment:** Exam (60 minutes).
- **Prerequisites:** Formal: E 701, E 703, FIN 801.

### Marketing Research

- **Focus:** Teaches students how to develop and test theories in an applied and concrete way.
- **Content:** Discusses structural equation modeling (SEM) and provides an opportunity to develop and fine-tune specific theories for personal research projects.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to generate ideas, define concepts, and develop sound theoretical reasoning.
- **Assessment:** Project (40%) and Presentation (60%).
- **Prerequisites:** Formal: MKT 801 Fundamentals of Marketing Research.

### Management and Strategic Theory

- **Focus:** Familiarizes students with the most relevant theoretical perspectives applied in strategic management research.
- **Content:** Discusses prevalent theoretical lenses, their assumptions, and conceptual strengths and weaknesses.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Develops an advanced understanding of established theories and the ability to build substantial theoretical contributions.
- **Assessment:** Essay (50%) and Presentation (50%).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.

## 🌐 Discipline-Specific Seminars

### Accounting Seminar (General)

- **Focus:** Deepens insights into current research methods in accounting and taxation.
- **Content:** Students present their own research ideas at various stages (early ideas to working papers), involving interactive discussion on contribution, literature, and design.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to present and discuss their own research results in a scientific format and become skilled discussants.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Presentation (10%), Paper (70%).
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### Finance Research Seminar

- **Focus:** Discusses current research topics in finance.
- **Content:** Visiting researchers present working papers, and the course introduces students to various empirical and theoretical research methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop research ideas and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.
- **Assessment:** Oral Participation.
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### Information Systems (IS) Research Seminar

- **Focus:** Discusses current research topics in information systems and operations management.
- **Content:** Visiting researchers present working papers, introducing students to popular empirical and theoretical research methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop research ideas and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.
- **Assessment:** Oral Participation.
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### Operations Management Seminar

- **Focus:** Provides an overview of empirical research approaches to investigate research questions in operations management.
- **Content:** Focuses on moving from a basic research question to an appropriate research design and methodology, emphasizing the relationship between the question and the design.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain the competence to initiate, design, implement, and evaluate empirical research in the social sciences applied to operations management.
- **Assessment:** Oral Exam (60%) and Presentation (40%).
- **Prerequisites:** Recommended: Fundamentals of statistics.
