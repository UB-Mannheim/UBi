# CDSB Course Catalog: Spring 2022

This catalog provides an overview of advanced seminar courses for doctoral students, focusing on cutting-edge research methods and current topics across various disciplines.

## 📚 Core Methodological Courses

### Textual Analysis Methods (Python)

This course teaches how textual analysis methods work and how they can be implemented using Python.

- **Content:** The course covers prominent papers on textual analysis, including methods like the bag-of-words approach and basic machine learning (e.g., Naïve Bayes). It introduces frequently used text databases (e.g., EDGAR, LexisNexis). The largest part deals with implementation using Python, covering data retrieval, text editing, dictionary-based textual analyses, and advanced characteristics like language complexity.
- **Prerequisites:** None required for Python basics.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to implement state-of-the-art research methods for analyzing verbal information in accounting, finance, and economics, and obtain a solid programming knowledge in Python.
- **Assessment:** Assignment
- **Schedule:**
  - 22.03.22 | Tuesday | 10:00 – 17:00 | L 9, 1–2, room 001
  - 24.03.22 | Thursday | 10:00 – 17:00 | O 048
  - 28.03.22 | Monday | 10:00 – 17:00 | B6, 30–32, room 211

### Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (PRISMA)

This course introduces the method of systematic review according to the PRISMA statement.

- **Content:** Students apply the PRISMA statement to a research review question from their PhD thesis, culminating in a systematic review paper. Concepts and heuristics of qualitative and quantitative meta-analysis are covered, allowing students to critically reflect on methodologies used in existing research reviews.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain the ability to conduct a systematic review and critically evaluate research methodologies.
- **Assessment:** Class Participation (10%), Presentation (10%), Discussion (10%), Assignment (70%)
- **Schedule:**
  - 15.02.22 – 31.05.22 | Tuesday | 13:45 – 15:15 | L 4, 1, room 004

### Qualitative Research Methods (IS Focus)

This course provides an overview of qualitative research methods and their application in Information Systems (IS).

- **Content:** It begins with basic principles of qualitative research, providing deeper insights into different types of qualitative research based on their epistemological, ontological, and methodological stance. The course is highly interactive, requiring students to prepare and discuss readings.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain an overview of common qualitative methods in IS research, know quality criteria for improving designs, and are able to prepare, plan, and execute their own qualitative research endeavors.
- **Assessment:** Term paper (50%), Presentation (30%), Discussion (20%)
- **Schedule:**

  - 27.04.22 – 25.05.22 | Wednesday | 12:00 – 15:00 | L 15, 1–6, room 422_423
  - 02.05.22 – 23.05.22 | Monday | 12:00 – 15:00 | L 15, 1–6, room 422_423

## 💰 Finance and Accounting Research

### Accounting & Taxation Research Seminar

This course focuses on current research topics in accounting and taxation.

- **Content:** Visiting researchers present working papers, discussing ideas in a workshop format. The course deepens students’ insights into popular empirical and theoretical research methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to discuss current research topics, develop their own research ideas, and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.
- **Assessment:** Oral participation.
- **Schedule:**

### Finance Research Seminar

This course focuses on current research topics in the field of finance.

- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to discuss current research topics, develop their own research ideas, and gain insights into the design, execution, and presentation of research projects.

### Corporate Finance and Econometrics

A comprehensive course covering advanced econometric techniques and corporate finance theory.

- **Content:** Topics include Itô calculus, stochastic differential equations, Black-Scholes theory, hedging, and arbitrage pricing. The course provides basic concepts and techniques for modeling and analyzing financial price processes in continuous time.
- **Prerequisites:** E 701, E 703, FIN 801
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain basic concepts and techniques for modeling and analyzing financial price processes in continuous time.
- **Assessment:** Term paper (90%), participation (10%)
- **Schedule:**
  - 22.02.22 | Tuesday | 09:00 – 17:00 | online
  - 08.03.22 | Tuesday | 09:00 – 17:00 | L 9, 1–2, room 409
  - 26.04.22 | Tuesday | 09:00 – 17:00 | L 9, 1–2, room 409

### Behavioral Finance

This course examines how psychological biases affect financial decisions.

- **Content:** Reviews recent research on possible mispricing in financial markets due to psychological biases. Topics include overconfidence, prospect theory, heuristic-driven biases, and frame dependence.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students understand how behavioral findings are integrated with neo-classical theory and can better understand economic decisions and their effect on market prices.
- **Assessment:** Written exam (60 min.)
- **Schedule:**
  - 17.02.22 – 07.04.22 | Thursday | 08:30 – 11:45 | online
  - 02.03.22 – 27.04.22 | Wednesday | 12:00 – 13:30 | online

### Advanced Empirical Research in Tax

A reading course for advanced PhD students focusing on empirical tax research.

- **Content:** Discusses recent state-of-the-art papers in taxation research (mostly applied empirical). Students present papers, and the class discusses them, focusing on methodological rigor and critical evaluation.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop expertise in the literature, learn to present well, and improve critical evaluation skills.
- **Assessment:** Paper (referee report) (40%), Presentation (30%), Class Participation (30%)
- **Schedule:**
  - 14.02.22 – 30.05.22 | Monday | 10:15 – 11:45 | SO 133

## 📈 General Business and Social Science Research

### Economics and Politics: Big Questions

A seminar exploring major theoretical and ethical questions at the intersection of economics and politics.

- **Content:** Students read and discuss six key books (e.g., *Identity*, *The tyranny of merit*, *The moral economy*). The course aims to develop a personal point of view on central economic and political questions.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students develop intellectual dialogue skills, train their ability to distill a personal point of view, and improve writing and discussion skills.
- **Assessment:** Essay (50%), Class Participation (50%)
- **Schedule:**
  - Introductory Session | 18.02.22 | Friday | 10:00 – 13:30 | L 9, 1–2, room 409
  - 29.04.22 – 10.06.22 | Friday | 10:00 – 13:30 | B6, 30–32 room 212/213

### Marketing Research and Theory

This course focuses on developing and testing theories in an applied and concrete way within marketing.

- **Content:** Students learn to generate ideas, define concepts, and clarify relationships between concepts. Emphasis is placed on structural equation modeling (SEM) and developing theoretical frameworks for their own research projects.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to conduct sound academic research, develop a theoretical framework, and identify substantive theoretical contributions to the marketing strategy literature.
- **Assessment:** Project work (40%), Presentation of own research (60%)

### Management and Organizational Theory

This seminar provides an overview of organizational theory and entrepreneurship.

- **Content:** Covers classic theories (e.g., Max Weber’s Theory of Bureaucracy, Taylorism) and modern approaches (e.g., Neoinstitutional Theory, Network Theory).
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students understand basic concepts in organization theory and entrepreneurship, and can apply appropriate theoretical concepts to their individual research topics.
- **Assessment:** Essay (80%), presentation (20%)
- **Schedule:**
  - 23.03.22 – 01.06.22 | Wednesday | 15:30 – 17:30

### Operations Management Research

This module focuses on empirical research approaches in operations management.

- **Content:** Provides an overview of quantitative empirical research, emphasizing the process from a basic research question to an appropriate research design and methodology. Topics include quantitative vs. qualitative research, framing research questions, and measurement.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain the competence to initiate, design, implement, and evaluate empirical research in the social sciences applied to operations management.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam (30 minutes) (60%), presentation (40%)
