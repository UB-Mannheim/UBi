# Seminar: Are financial markets pricing the net zero carbon transition?

**Speaker:** [Matteo Gasparini](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/mises/forschung/decarbonization-seminar-fall-2023/matteo-gasparini/)
**Affiliation:** University of Oxford
**Date:** December 11, 2023

## Seminar Abstract

Prior research has highlighted a positive correlation between realized returns and carbon emissions. This paper argues that this "carbon premium" might be partially due to mispricing produced by climate policy uncertainty. Consequently, realized returns may not be representative of expected returns.

To demonstrate this, the speaker develops an asset pricing model that incorporates uncertain expectations about the future cash flows of fossil fuel firms. The model shows that the price-dividend ratio increases with uncertainty about a climate policy regime shift. This proposition is confirmed empirically using data on analysts’ forecasts.

The analysis finds that disagreement among analysts’ forecasts—used as a proxy for climate policy uncertainty—may explain part of the valuations of a large sample of fossil fuels stocks. Using the model, the speaker demonstrates, with forward-looking scenarios, that cash flow expectations implied in the valuations of fossil fuels firms may not be consistent with a net zero carbon transition.

## Speaker Biography

Matteo Gasparini is a DPhil (PhD) candidate at the University of Oxford's Smith School of Enterprise and Environment (SSEE) and the Institute for New Economic Thinking (INET). He also serves as an External Advisor to McKinsey & Co. and an External Lecturer in Sustainable Finance at Skema Business School in Paris. His research focuses on the Financial Economics of Climate Change and Sustainability, particularly the implications of climate change for firms’ valuations and financial regulation.
