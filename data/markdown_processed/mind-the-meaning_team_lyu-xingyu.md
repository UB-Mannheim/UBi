## Dissertation Project: Scientific Observation in Perspective

**Working Title:** Scientific Observation in Perspective

The dissertation project aims to develop a perspectivist account of scientific observation. The core question addressed is how the observational ability of human observers, which has been profoundly enhanced by sophisticated apparatus, should be interpreted.

The project challenges the assumption that observational results gained via apparatus can be treated as a simple continuum of human sensory perception. Lyu Xingyu responds to this question from a perspectivist viewpoint, arguing that no such simple continuum exists.

**Goal:**
The primary purpose is to develop a perspectivist theoretical framework designed to bridge the gap between apparatus-based observation and human sense-based observation.

**Research Focus:**
Within this project, Lyu Xingyu investigates self-ascriptions of visual experiences, particularly those related to color, using examples such as "I see a red door" or "This lamp seems black to me."
