## Seminar Abstract

This paper studies a long-term power purchase agreement (PPA) between a firm and a new renewable energy generator. The firm must dynamically satisfy uncertain electricity demand beyond its existing energy sources, while wholesale electricity prices evolve stochastically over time.

Upon signing a PPA, a new renewable facility becomes operational, and the firm owns its output for the contract duration. The new facility’s capacity is determined based on PPA terms. The firm dynamically chooses when to initiate the PPA and how much to pay to maximize its expected total discounted benefit.

**Key Findings:**

- The firm’s optimal timing follows a (time-dependent) threshold policy.
- Contrary to common wisdom, reducing investment costs for renewable technologies can lead to smaller renewable capacity, output, and emissions savings when projects are developed under PPAs. This finding suggests caution when applying investment tax credits in such contexts.
- Total renewable energy generation and emissions savings may decrease with higher site productivity, suggesting that restricting development to the most productive sites might be counterproductive under PPAs.

## Speaker Biography: Dr. Nur Sunar

Nur Sunar researches innovative business models, policies, and technologies, focusing on their impacts on inclusion. Her current research centers on applying management science to achieve positive outcomes, particularly in renewable energy technologies, sustainability, and smart city technologies (e.g., IoT, smart meters, and residential batteries).

**Expertise:**

- **Operations Management:** Teaches courses in operations management and focuses on innovative business solutions for online marketplaces and inclusive health.
- **Methodology:** Utilizes various advanced methods, including machine learning (clustering, deep learning, text mining), data-driven optimization, stochastic analysis, game theory, and econometrics.
- **Publications & Awards:** Has published in leading journals such as *Management Science*, *M&SOM*, and *Operations Research*. Her work has received multiple prestigious awards, including the INFORMS Data Mining Best Paper Award and the Amundi-ESSEC ESG Best Paper Award 2024.

**Education:**

- Received her PhD from Stanford Graduate School of Business at Stanford University.
- Holds a BS in industrial engineering from Bogazici University in Istanbul.
