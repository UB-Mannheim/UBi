### Seminar Abstract

The seminar addresses the concern that ambitious climate policy is often obstructed by corporate lobbying activities. The presentation will:

- Quantify corporate anti- and pro-climate lobbying expenses.
- Identify the largest corporate lobbyists and their motives.
- Establish the relationship between climate lobbying and corporate business models.
- Document whether and how climate lobbying is priced in financial markets.

*Key Findings:*

- Firms spend an average of $295,921 per year on anti-climate lobbying ($164,991 on pro-climate lobbying).
- Large anti-climate lobbyists tend to have more carbon-intensive business models and face higher future climate-related risks.
- Firms spending more on anti-climate lobbying may earn higher returns, potentially due to a risk premium.
- Stock prices reacted significantly to climate policy changes (e.g., rising when the Waxman-Markey Cap-and-Trade Bill failed, and falling when the Inflation Reduction Act was announced).

### Speaker Biography

Zacharias Sautner is a Professor of Sustainable Finance at the Department of Banking and Finance of the University of Zurich (UZH) and a senior chair at the Swiss Finance Institute (SFI).

**Expertise:**
Through his research on ESG topics, including climate change and biodiversity, he provides insights into how finance can contribute to a more sustainable future. His work has been published in leading international journals, and he has been cited over 5,000 times according to Google Scholar. His expertise is frequently referenced in major publications such as the *Wall Street Journal*, *New York Times*, and *Financial Times*, and in reports by international bodies like the IMF, ECB, and European Commission.

**Academic Roles:**

- Associate Editor at the *Review of Financial Studies*, *Journal of Banking & Finance*, *Financial Management*, and *Journal of Corporate Finance*.
- Regular Research Visitor at the ECB.
- Advises institutional investors on ESG issues.

**Career History:**
Prior to moving to Switzerland, he served as Professor of Finance at the Frankfurt School of Finance & Management, where he headed the finance department for many years.
