### Seminar Abstract

The environmental impact of Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) is highly dependent on whether drivers utilize fossil fuels or electricity. Using quasi-experimental variation in access to home charging stations within a sample of 836 PHEV company cars, the seminar estimates that this technology more than quadruples electric utilization, thereby reducing CO2 emissions by 38%. Financially, home charging stations—which are fully subsidized by the company—are projected to break even after six to eight years.

### Speaker Biography: Ulrich J. Wagner

Ulrich J. Wagner is a Professor of Quantitative Economics at the University of Mannheim.

**Research Focus:**
His research interests lie in environmental economics, industrial organization, and public economics.

**Academic Contributions:**

- He has published his work in leading peer-reviewed academic journals, including the *American Economic Review*, the *Review of Economic Studies*, the *Journal of Public Economics*, and the *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*.
- He serves as an editorial board member of the *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*.
- He has also been a co-editor of the *Journal of the Association of Environmental and Resource Economists* and *Economics: the E-Journal*.
- In 2015, he was awarded the 2015 Erik Kempe Award in Environmental and Resource Economics.
- His research projects are funded by major bodies, including the European Research Council and the German Science Foundation.

**Education and Career:**

- He received his doctoral degree in 2006 from Yale University.
- He subsequently worked as a postdoctoral research fellow at the Earth Institute at Columbia University.
- Before joining the University of Mannheim in 2015, he served as an Associate Professor of Economics at Universidad Carlos III in Madrid.
