### Speaker Biography

Ivo Welch is a Distinguished Professor of Finance and holds the J. Fred Weston Chair in Finance at UCLA Anderson. His academic career includes time at Yale’s School of Management and Brown University’s economics department.

Professor Welch is widely recognized as an influential economist, having authored numerous publications with significant citation impact. His work has earned multiple prestigious awards, including the Humboldt Research Award and the Michael Brennan Award (twice). He has served as the director of the UCLA Anderson Fink Center and the American Finance Association, and is a National Bureau of Research (NBER) research associate.

His research interests are broad, covering:

- Informational cascades
- Initial public offerings (IPOs)
- Capital structure and dividends
- Market-timing and performance evaluation
- Earnings management and overconfidence
- Socially responsible investing and bankruptcy

### Seminar Abstract: Moving the Needle on Climate Change

The seminar addresses the critical challenge of global warming, which is projected to cause significant miseries, particularly in poor countries.

**Key Arguments:**

- **Ineffectiveness of Current Activism:** The speaker argues that individual carbon-footprint activism and international negotiations (like those proposed by the UN) are unlikely to achieve major emission cuts.
- **The Role of Decision-Makers:** The ultimate outcome depends not on what the world *should* do, but what its *important decision-makers* will do.
- **Shifting Global Power:** The OECD is no longer the sole determinant of global policy. Non-OECD countries, which will house over four-fifths of the world’s population and emit more than three-quarters of greenhouse gases, cannot be blamed.
- **The Solution:** The most pressing task is the intelligent phasing out of fossil fuels. The focus must be on subsidizing research in clean energy, especially storage. Cheaper clean energy will eventually displace fossil fuels globally, truly "moving the needle."
