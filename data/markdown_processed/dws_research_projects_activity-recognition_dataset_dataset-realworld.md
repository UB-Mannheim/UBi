# RealWorld (HAR) Dataset (2016)

This dataset is designed for Human Activity Recognition (HAR) and covers various physical activities performed by multiple subjects.

**Citation Note:** If you use this data set, please refer to: “On-body Localization of Wearable Devices: An Investigation of Position-Aware Activity”.

## Dataset Overview

The dataset collects comprehensive data on several activities, including:

- Climbing stairs (up and down)
- Jumping
- Lying
- Standing
- Sitting
- Running/Jogging
- Walking

**Data Collected:**
The recordings capture data from multiple sensors simultaneously, including:

- Acceleration
- GPS
- Gyroscope
- Light
- Magnetic field
- Sound level

**Measurements:**
For each activity, the acceleration was recorded across seven specific body positions:

1. Chest
1. Forearm
1. Head
1. Shin
1. Thigh
1. Upper arm
1. Waist

**Subjects and Scope:**

- **Subjects:** 15 individuals (The group consisted of eight males and seven females, with similar data distribution between genders).
- **Recording Time:** Each subject performed each activity for approximately 10 minutes, with the exception of jumping (due to physical exertion, recorded for ~1.7 minutes).
- **Format:** All movements were recorded using video cameras to facilitate usage.

**Download:**
The full dataset can be downloaded as a zip file:
