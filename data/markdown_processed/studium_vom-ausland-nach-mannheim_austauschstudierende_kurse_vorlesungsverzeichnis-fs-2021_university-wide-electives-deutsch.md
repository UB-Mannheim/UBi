# University Wide Electives Course Catalog

This catalog lists elective courses available to exchange students from all departments. Registration is recommended via Portal2 before the lecture period starts.

## 🎓 Bachelor Level Electives

### 🗣️ Linguistics

- **Focus:** The course provides fundamental knowledge about various sub-fields, concepts, and methods of linguistics, with a focus on the German language.
- **Topics Covered:** Central categories and descriptive models across different analysis levels, including Pragmatics, Semantics, Syntax, Morphology, Phonology, Graphhematics, Text, and Discourse Linguistics.
- **Skills Developed:** Practicing linguistic analyses in the areas of sounds, words, and sentences; learning to accumulate and aggregate information from different sources.
- **Format:** The lecture is held online via ZOOM. Details regarding the curriculum, schedule, and examination format are provided in the first session.

### 💻 Computer Science & Algorithms

- **Topics:**
  - Algorithm design: step-by-step refinement, modularity, object-orientation (class hierarchies, inheritance), recursion.
  - The object-oriented programming language Java.
  - Simple data structures (linked list, binary tree, B-tree).
  - Modeling with UML: Class diagrams, activity diagrams, state diagrams.
  - Introduction to the theory of algorithms: Computability, complexity (O-notation), testing, and verification of algorithms and programs.
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### 🔢 Mathematics & Logic

- **Topics:**
  - Foundations of propositional logic (deduction, proof).
  - Sets, relations, and mappings.
  - Foundations of combinatorics (counting finite sets, countability).
  - Introduction to graph theory.
  - Algebraic structures (semigroups, groups, homomorphisms, factor structures).
  - Basic computational models/Finite automata.
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### 💼 Business & Information Systems

- **Process Modeling:** Ability to model complex business processes using popular modeling techniques.
- **Information Systems:** Discussion of the requirements, characteristics, and effects of integrated information systems in industrial companies, including complex process interdependencies.
- **Functional Areas Covered:**
  - Research and Development
  - Marketing and Sales
  - Procurement and Warehousing
  - Production
  - Shipping and Customer Service
  - Finance, Accounting, HR
  - Planning and Control Systems

### 👥 Human Resources Management

- **Topics:**
  - Job Analysis & Job Design
  - HR Planning & Recruiting
  - Selection
  - Training
  - Performance Management
  - Employee Development
  - Separation & Retention
  - Compensation and Incentives (including International HRM)
  - High-Performance Organizations

### ⚖️ Law (International Criminal Law)

- **Recommended Reading:**
  - Cassese, Antonio, *International Criminal Law* (Oxford, Oxford University Press, 2008).
  - Cryer, Robert, *An Introduction to International Criminal Law and Procedure* (Cambridge, Cambridge University Press, 2010).
  - Schabas, William A., *The International Criminal Court: A Commentary on the Rome Statute* (Oxford, Oxford University Press, 2010).
  - Werle, Gerhard, *Principles of International Criminal Law* (The Hague, Asser, 2005).
