# Operations Analytics Research Projects

## Employee Responses to Error in Monitored Production Environments

**Authors:**
[Hartmut Höhle](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/), Florian Pethig, [Fareed Zandkarimi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/team/wissenschaftliche-mitarbeiter-innen/alumni/fareed-zandkarimi/)
**Cooperation Partner:**
Aalberts Material Technology GmbH
**Description:**
This project investigates the effect of reclamations on the behavior of production staff. Using process mining, the study measures production-level behavior over a three-year period, analyzed via a Difference-in-Difference experiment. The research aims to deepen the understanding of employee responses to errors. The work utilizes core-production data from a large company, making a major contribution by applying statistical methods to production-level data. Furthermore, the study presents process mining as a method for investigating such phenomena. The expected outcomes provide practitioners with new insights for making effective decisions before or after an error is reported. Data collection involved 2.5 million production cases via the company’s ERP, requiring the development of over 500 lines of SQL code to transform the data into a process mining Eventlog. A dedicated Java artifact was also designed for the Dif-in-Dif analysis, with the aggregated dataset subsequently analyzed by Stata.

## Mergers and Acquisitions and the Reallocation of Labor

**Authors:**
Britta Gehrke, [Ernst Maug](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/maug/), Stefan Obernberger, Christoph Schneider, [Peter Severin](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/maug/team/alumni/peter-severin/)
**Cooperation Partner:**
Institut für Arbeit und Beruf (IAB)
**Description:**
This research examines the reallocation of labor following mergers and acquisitions in Germany. The study finds that targets experience significant restructuring, losing more than half of their labor force within two years post-acquisition compared to a matched sample, leading to a substantial increase in employee turnover. Acquirers generally possess a better-educated, better-paid, and more qualified workforce than targets, leading to the target workforce composition becoming more similar to that of the acquirers. While mergers create internal labor markets, these are quantitatively less significant than the changes in the external labor market. The authors argue that merged firms build "knowledge-based hierarchies," which are associated with improved productivity by better economizing on the scarce skills of managers and highly-skilled employees.

## Time-Dependent Queues with Retrials

**Authors:**
[Ömer Özümerzifon](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/stolletz/), Benjamin Legros, [Raik Stolletz](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/stolletz/)
**Cooperation Partner:**
EM Normandie
**Description:**
Queueing systems are vital in various service sectors, including repair facilities, healthcare, and call centers. A common issue is that customers leave the queue before service due to impatience, but they may re-enter later as retrials. This project focuses on the stationary and time-dependent performance evaluation of multi-server queueing systems incorporating retrials, specifically analyzing the impact of the distribution of the retrial time. The research develops a stationary backlog-carryover (SBC) approach and a modified stationary independent period-by-period (SIPP) approach to approximate key performance measures. Both analytical results and numerical insights demonstrate the significant impact of the retrial time distribution.

## Process Mining in Procurement Processes (P2P)

**Authors:**
[Prof. Dr. Christoph Bode](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/), [Prof. Dr. Hartmut Höhle](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/), [Jun.-Prof. Dr. Jana-Rebecca Rehse](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/rehse/), [Jonas Ronellenfitsch](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/team/alumni/dr-ronellenfitsch-jonas/), [Fareed Zandkarimi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/)
**Cooperation Partners:**
Schott AG, Hilti AG, HeidelbergCement AG, and Heidelberger Druckmaschinen AG
**Description:**
Procurement processes, such as purchase-to-pay (P2P), are critical to corporate operations. Inefficiencies in these processes can cause production delays and significant additional costs. However, limited research exists on these processes at the operational level—it is often unknown what inefficiencies occur during actual execution, their source, or how they can be prevented. To address this, an interdisciplinary team of process mining and procurement experts investigates how process mining methods (analyzing process execution data) can identify, measure, explain, and eliminate inefficiencies in P2P processes. The team analyzes P2P data from three large industrial companies to identify common patterns. This research leads to the development of new Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that precisely quantify P2P process inefficiency. These KPIs can be used, for example, to identify temporal patterns of inefficiency or to highlight the impact of external influences, such as the Corona pandemic.

## Analytical Approaches for Digital Production Optimization

**Authors:**
[Johannes Diefenbach](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/stolletz/team/promotionen-und-postdocs/dr-johannes-diefenbach/), [Fikri Karaesmen](https://mysite.ku.edu.tr/fkaraesmen/), [Justus Arne Schwarz](https://www.uni-regensburg.de/wirtschaftswissenschaften/fakultaet/org/produktionsmanagement/team/prof-dr-justus-arne-schwarz), [Raik Stolletz](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/stolletz/team/)
**Cooperation Partners:**
Koc University (Istanbul) and Robert Bosch GmbH
**Description:**
This project develops new analytical models and fast approximation methods for the performance analysis and optimization of dynamic semiconductor manufacturing processes. Methods from prescriptive analytics generate insights into how new products are introduced to the production system and the market, especially when yield is stochastic. This research is part of "Productive 4.0," Europe’s largest research project in the Digital Industry, recognized as an ECSEL Lighthouse Initiative and funded by the EU and the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF). It investigates how electronics and Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) can enhance production efficiency across the entire semiconductor supply chain and product life cycle. With 109 partners from industry and research institutions, Productive4.0 provides fundamental tools necessary for companies to transform the potential of the digital revolution into business success.
