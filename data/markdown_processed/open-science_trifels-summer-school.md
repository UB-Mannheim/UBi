# 8th Trifels Summer School on Open Science (2022)

The 8th Trifels Summer School was an annual event dedicated to the principles and practices of Open Science. Organized by the [University of Mannheim](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/) and held from September 26th to 30th, 2022, at the [Kurhaus Trifels](https://www-jugendstilhotel--trifels-de.translate.goog/?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=en&_x_tr_hl=en-US&_x_tr_pto=wapp) in the Palatinate Forest.

The series is hosted by the association [“Begegnungszentrum Kurhaus Trifels”](https://begegnungszentrum--kurhaus--trifels-de.translate.goog/beispiel-seite/?_x_tr_sch=http&_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=en&_x_tr_hl=en-US&_x_tr_pto=wapp), which includes several academic partners:

- Technical University of Darmstadt
- Heidelberg University
- Technical University of Kaiserslautern
- Karlsruhe Institute of Technology
- University of Koblenz-Landau
- University of Mannheim

The event was aimed at **PhD students** and **postdoctoral researchers** from all academic disciplines, focusing on topics such as open science, reproducible research, and science communication. Participation was free of charge and included accommodation and meals.

## Program Overview

The Summer School featured a mix of workshops, social activities, and a concluding panel discussion.

### Key Workshop Themes

The workshops provided deep dives into various aspects of modern research practice:

- **Reproducible Research:** Focused on methods to ensure research transparency and reliability.
- **Open Science Methods in Teaching:** Explored how to incorporate open science concepts and practices (e.g., open educational resources) into higher education teaching.
- **Research Data and Copyright:** Examined the legal and ethical questions surrounding research data ownership, suitable licenses for open publication, and the impact of national copyright norms on international cooperation.
- **Open Research Data:** Discussed the role of open data (quantitative and qualitative) in enabling transparent research, identifying challenges, and proposing solutions for data sharing.
- **Open Access Potential:** Covered strategies for making dissertations and publications openly accessible.

## Panel Discussion: Mit Open Science globalen Krisen begegnen

On Thursday, September 29, 2022, the event culminated in a public panel discussion titled: **"Mit Open Science globalen Krisen begegnen – Offenheit und Transparenz in der Wissenschaft"** (Facing Global Crises with Open Science – Openness and Transparency in Science).

- **Language:** The primary language of the discussion was German.
- **Moderator:** [Prof. Dr. Konrad Förstner](https://www.zbmed.de/kontakt/konrad-foerstner/) (ZB Med/TH Köln)
- **Panelists:**
  - [Dr. Rima-Maria Rahal](https://www.coll.mpg.de/rima-maria-rahal) (Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods, Bonn)
  - [Philipp Schmid-zum Berge](https://centire.org/index.php/about-centire/who-is-centire/industry-advisory-board/339-philipp-schmidt-zum-berge) (External R&D Cooperations Manager, Continental)
  - Prof. Dr. Konrad Wolf (Wissenschafts- und Kulturminister a.D., Rheinland-Pfalz)

**Key Discussion Points:**
The discussion addressed the concept of knowledge as a common good (Wissensallmende) and whether research results should be freely available. Key questions raised included:

- Does making research data and software openly available diminish the researcher's control or recognition?
- How does the push for Open Science align with the need for transparency following replication crises and the COVID-19 pandemic?
- What is the potential economic value of open data and software for businesses?
- Should science communication and knowledge transfer be considered integral parts of research, or is the discourse limited to experts?
- What new incentive systems are needed to encourage public engagement and participation in public research?
