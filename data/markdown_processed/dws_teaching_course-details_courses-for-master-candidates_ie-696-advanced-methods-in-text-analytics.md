# Advanced Methods in Text Analytics (IE 696)

This Master's level course is a deep dive into the latest state-of-the-art methods for Natural Language Processing (NLP). It serves as a follow-up to [IE 661 Text Analytics](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/information-systems/chairs/prof-dr-strohmaier/teaching/master-level-courses/ie-661-is-661-text-analytics/), which introduces basic concepts and methods for NLP.

The course heavily focuses on language models implemented using deep learning architectures. Key topics include:

- The transformer architecture and its applications.
- The design, training, and applications of Large Language Models (LLMs), including the technology behind products like ChatGPT.

**Note on Focus:** This course is not focused on *how to use* LLMs in applied settings, but rather on *how LLMs work internally*. The hands-on exercises are designed for the training and evaluation of LLMs, which is crucial for effective use.

*If you are interested in learning how to use LLMs in applied settings, consider the course: [Large Language Models and Agents](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/teaching/course-details/courses-for-master-candidates/ie-685-large-language-models-and-agents/).*

## Prerequisites

Students should have:

- BSc-level knowledge of linear algebra and probability theory, as well as basic calculus.
- It is strongly recommended to have passed one of the following courses:
  - [Machine Learning](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/teaching/course-details/courses-for-master-candidates/ie-675b-machine-learning/)
  - [Deep Learning](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/teaching/course-details/courses-for-master-candidates/course-archive/fss-2025/ie-678-deep-learning/)
