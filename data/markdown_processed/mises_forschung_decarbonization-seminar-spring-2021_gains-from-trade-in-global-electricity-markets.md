# Gains from Trade in Global Electricity Markets

**Speaker:** Matti Liski
**Affiliation:** Aalto University of Helsinki
**Date:** May 17, 2021

## Seminar Abstract

This paper examines consumer welfare gains resulting from new technologies that improve the efficiency of goods trade. Using micro-data derived from over 140 million bids across electricity markets in California, the Nordics, and Spain, the research reveals that the consumer surplus gain from efficiency improvements follows a markedly different pattern across these three markets.

Consistent with established theory, the results are explained by structural differences in excess demand, which arise from institutional and technological determinants. The study highlights the importance of the welfare mechanism for policies concerning new technologies and provides insights into the implications of technologies that introduce arbitrage between electricity markets.

## Speaker Biography

Matti Liski is a Professor of Economics at Aalto University Helsinki. He holds research affiliations with several prestigious institutions, including the MIT Center for Energy and Environmental Policy Research, the University of Cambridge Energy Policy Research Group, and CESifo Munich. He has also served as a visiting professor at the Toulouse School of Economics.

Matti is recognized as an award-winning teacher and a prolific researcher, with numerous articles published in renowned journals such as the *Journal of the European Economic Association*, *Economic Journal*, *Journal of Economic Theory*, *Journal of Public Economics*, and *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*. His research focuses on microeconomics—both theoretical and empirical—with applications in industrial organization, public policy, energy markets, and climate change.
