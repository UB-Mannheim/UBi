## Seminar Abstract

Even in a largely decarbonized economy, some emissions are difficult or extremely costly to abate. These residual emissions can be offset through negative emissions. Bioenergy with Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (BECCUS) is a key approach for achieving permanent carbon dioxide removal (CDR) from biomass power plants or energy-from-waste facilities.

The lecture will address critical questions, including:

- How much CDR is needed to cost-efficiently achieve climate targets?
- What are the potentials for CDR from BECCUS in Germany? How will costs evolve, and what are the current revenue opportunities?
- What market design could enable BECCUS to be financed purely through market mechanisms, and how could early adopters be supported during the ramp-up phase?
- Is a regulatory framework necessary for the development of CO₂ transport networks and storage infrastructures?
- What lessons can be learned from international examples (e.g., UK, Sweden, USA, France, Japan)?

The presentation will include specific project and investment plans by MVV in the BECCUS sector.

## Speaker Biography

**Oliver Kopp** heads the Department “Energy Economics and Energy Policy” at MVV Energie AG. He brings over 20 years of experience in energy market analysis and governmental affairs within the energy industry.

- **Education:** Economist by training, holding a doctorate (Dr. rer. pol.) from the University of Heidelberg.
- **Additional Role:** Oliver also serves as the Chair of the Board for the non-profit MVV Future Foundation (MVV Stiftung Zukunft).
