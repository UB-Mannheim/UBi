# SpatialBenchRAG: A Benchmark for Geospatial RAG Systems

The increasing reliance on spatial data in daily tasks and decision-making—such as requesting directions or finding local amenities—makes the integration of spatial information into AI systems critical.

## The Role of Knowledge Graphs (KGs)

Knowledge Graphs (KGs) are sophisticated data models used to search for and link information. They are particularly valuable for spatial data because their graph-based format supports:

- Advanced spatial queries.
- Spatio-temporal reasoning.

## Project Goal and Scope

The **SpatialBenchRAG** project aims to develop the first multimodal, multilingual benchmark specifically designed for evaluating Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems that are grounded in spatial KGs.

By addressing the current lack of standardized resources for spatial reasoning, the benchmark provides a robust platform for research and development.

### Methodology and Datasets

The project combines diverse geographic datasets, including:

- OpenStreetMap
- Wikidata

This combination allows for realistic, context-aware evaluation across complex tasks, such as:

- Spatial and visual question answering.
- Multi-hop reasoning.

## Impact and Applications

By enabling transparent and reproducible research, SpatialBenchRAG aims to accelerate the development of geographically grounded AI systems for critical applications, including:

- Disaster response planning.
- Geo-aware conversational assistants.
- Spatial fact-checking.
