## Gender Equality Action at SFB 884

The Collaborative Research Center SFB 884 “Political Economy of Reforms” is committed to supporting female researchers throughout their academic careers and facilitating the integration of family and work for parents of all genders through various measures.

### 👩‍🏫 Professional Development and Networking

The SFB offers multiple avenues to support the professional growth and networking of female researchers:

- **Role Models and Networking:** Senior researchers host events where they share their career paths, challenges, and solutions, serving as role models for junior colleagues.
- **Female Faculty Meetings:** These meetings foster interdisciplinary exchange among female researchers across all projects.
- **International Mentoring:** Interested female researchers can participate in international mentoring programs, often combined with the opportunity for a research stay abroad.
- **Workshops:** The SFB organizes specialized workshops (e.g., the All Female Workshop) where leading female researchers present their work, promoting new professional interactions.
- **Career Training:**
  - The English-spoken program, “Take off – Women in Academic Leadership,” is available for researchers at SFB and other DFG-funded institutions.
  - Coaching is offered, particularly to advanced researchers, to provide support on specific career development questions.
  - PhD students are encouraged to participate in university-wide programs such as the WOVEN-program or the Baden-Württemberg Mentoring and Training Program (MuT).

### 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Support for Parents and Work-Life Balance

The SFB provides comprehensive support structures to help researchers balance professional life with family responsibilities:

- **Child Care Facilities:**
  - A dedicated parent-child-office is available, connected to the Department of Gender Equality and Social Diversity.
  - Child care is available for all researchers (regardless of gender) for special circumstances, such as emergencies or outside standard opening hours.
  - The facility also provides a holiday program during school breaks and is available for guest researchers during SFB conferences.
- **Parental Support:**
  - All new SFB members receive a Welcome Brochure detailing regulations for parents, including maternity protection, parental leave, and Home Office options.
  - The Dual Career Network is actively supported if needed.
  - Meetings consider the usual opening hours of child care facilities.

### 📰 Information, Communication, and Quality Assurance

The SFB ensures that support measures are visible, accessible, and continuously improved:

- **Communication:**
  - All SFB members receive a Welcome Brochure detailing support options.
  - Female researchers receive an annual brochure detailing training opportunities for career development and social skills.
  - The SFB management provides individual counseling on personnel development, with participation costs covered by the SFB.
  - Communication regarding gender equality measures is continually improved, and these measures are now presented on the official website.
- **Quality Monitoring:**
  - The Gender Monitoring process is being developed further. Monitoring metrics include not only the share of female SFB members but also the number of parents, participation in trainings, and involvement in SFB-internal requests.
  - This continuous evaluation is central to quality assurance, allowing for targeted communication and measures.
- **Committee Support:**
  - The SFB checks whether female board members are eligible for special leave terms that waive teaching obligations or provide other forms of support.
