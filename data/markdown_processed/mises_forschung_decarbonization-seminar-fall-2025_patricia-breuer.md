## Seminar Abstract

Corporate activity is a major contributor to environmental pollution, and much is known about this issue. However, the extent to which individual firms affect the environment remains relatively unclear, possibly even to the firms’ management.

Growing efforts to collect information on firms’ environmental footprints—whether motivated by external reporting requirements or driven by independent monitoring initiatives (e.g., Climate TRACE)—may help firms’ management incorporate environmental considerations more efficiently in their decision-making. The seminar explores whether this hope is justified.

Using a survey experiment administered to millions of firms via email, the research collects information on firms’ beliefs about their environmental footprints and randomly informs a subset of the firms about independent estimates of their footprints. The talk explores:

1. Whether firms’ beliefs align with their actual footprints.
1. Whether firms update their beliefs when presented with diverging footprint information.
1. Whether (updated) beliefs influence their actions, including their decision to disclose their footprint, their stated intention to change their environmental footprint, and their observed operational actions and environmental footprint.

## Speaker Biography

**Patricia Breuer** is an Assistant Professor of Accounting at Erasmus School of Economics, Erasmus University Rotterdam. Her research focuses on the intersection of financial reporting, regulation, and sustainability, with a particular emphasis on transparency and corporate behavior. Her work has been published in leading journals, including *Science*, and has garnered attention for its policy relevance and interdisciplinary approach.

**Academic Background:**

- Dr. Breuer earned her doctorate (Dr. rer. pol.) from the [University of Mannheim’s Graduate School of Economic and Social Sciences](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/mises/forschung/decarbonization-seminar-fall-2025/patricia-breuer/).
- During her doctoral training, she was a visiting scholar at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and ESSEC Business School.
- Her academic journey also includes studies at Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich, Maastricht University, and Texas A&M University.

**Professional Activities:**

- She has presented her research at numerous international conferences and workshops, including the London Business School Accounting Symposium, the Harvard Business School, and the Annual Congress of the European Accounting Association.
- She has received multiple awards, including the ESE Top Talent Researcher Award and the Karin-Islinger Award.
- In addition to her research, she teaches courses on data analytics and programming and is an active member of the TRR 266 Accounting for Transparency consortium.
