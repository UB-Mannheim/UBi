# Büşra Eroğlu

Büşra Eroğlu is a doctoral student in the Center for Doctoral Studies in Business at the Graduate School of Economic and Social Sciences in Mannheim. Her research focuses on the intersection of psychology and economics, specifically within the field of behavioral finance.

## 🎓 Academic Profile

- **Current Status:** Doctoral Student (M.Sc.)
- **Supervisor:** Erik Theissen / Martin Weber
- **Education:**
  - Master's degree in Economics from Istanbul Bilgi University.
  - Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from Marmara University.
- **Research Focus:** Büşra is passionate about interdisciplinary work in behavioral finance, analyzing markets and financial decision-making processes. Her master's thesis involved developing a credit mechanism designed to encourage pessimistic entrepreneurs to participate in economic activities.
- **Research Interests:**
  - Behavioral Finance
  - Asset Pricing

## 💼 Professional Details

- **Previous Experience:** Research and teaching assistant at Bahçeşehir University in Turkey.
- **Funding:** Büşra receives a scholarship from the Foundation [“Geld und Währung”](https://www.stiftung-geld-und-waehrung.de/stiftung-de).
