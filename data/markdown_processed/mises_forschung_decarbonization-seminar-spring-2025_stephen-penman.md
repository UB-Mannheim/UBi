## 📝 Seminar Abstract

This paper introduces a novel accounting framework designed to report an entity’s progress in reducing carbon emissions. Similar to traditional financial accounting, it utilizes a balance sheet where assets that reduce carbon are offset by liabilities for carbon emissions. The resulting net position reflects the entity's overall carbon standing and its progress toward milestones, such as achieving net-zero carbon goals.

The quasi-income statement reports periodic emissions that contribute to the balance sheet's net position. By recognizing assets in the balance sheet, the framework addresses the timing problem between investing in carbon reduction and realizing its full effect.

This responsibility accounting framework offers several advantages:

- **Incentives and Monitoring:** It provides attractive incentive and monitoring features.
- **Budgeting:** It facilitates pro forma (budgeting) of carbon-reducing strategies, setting measurable benchmarks.
- **Consolidation:** It allows for consolidation across different entities to report carbon metrics for specific groups, such as entire industries.
- **Double Materiality:** Mirroring financial accounting, it supports "double materiality" assessments by comparing carbon metrics with financial metrics to evaluate trade-offs and sustainability generally.

## 👤 Speaker Biography

**Stephen Penman** is the George O. May Professor in the Graduate School of Business at Columbia University. He also serves as co-director of the Center for Excellence in Accounting and Security Analysis and director of the Masters Program in Accounting and Fundamental Analysis.

Professor Penman holds a first-class honors degree in Commerce from the University of Queensland, Australia, and M.B.A. and Ph.D. degrees from the University of Chicago. His research focuses on the valuation of equity and the role of accounting information in security analysis, with several papers addressing accounting policy issues.

He is a widely published scholar in finance and accounting journals and has conducted seminars for both academic and professional audiences. Notable achievements include:

- Founding editor of the *Review of Accounting Studies* (serving as managing editor from 2002–2006).
- Elected to the Accounting Hall of Fame in 2019.
- Elected to the Australian Accounting Hall of Fame in 2020.
