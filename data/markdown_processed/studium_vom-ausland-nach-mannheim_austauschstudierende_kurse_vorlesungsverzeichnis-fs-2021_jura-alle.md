# Academic Course Catalog: Law Studies (Spring 2021)

This catalog outlines advanced modules focusing on Comparative Law, European Union Law, and International Dispute Resolution, designed for exchange students.

## Comparative Law I

This course provides an accessible introduction to the nature, functions, and aims of Comparative Law, focusing specifically on European Legal Traditions. It adopts a selective approach, concentrating on private law and business law aspects, which are crucial drivers of European harmonization.

**Core Components Covered:**

- **Origins and Utility of Comparative Law:**
  - Aims and methods of comparative law research.
  - Principle of equality of all legal orders.
  - Grouping of legal families according to historic origin or structure.
- **Basic Concepts of Law:**
  - **Contract Law:** Party autonomy, formation of contract, performance.
  - **Business Law:** Division of work, liability, tort law, insurance.
  - **Advanced Topics:** Hybrids and legal transplants.
- **Development of European Private Law:**
  - The different layers of legal sources.
  - Uniform sales law, UNIDROIT, and PECL.
  - Excessive harmonization and the function of soft law.
  - Freedom of contracts as the cornerstone of European private law.
  - Restraints of party autonomy: the concept of the weaker party.

## European Union Law Modules

### Market Freedoms and EU Law

This module examines the foundational cornerstones of the European Union: the Market Freedoms. The course focuses on the free movement of goods, persons, services, and capital, analyzing how these freedoms grant direct, enforceable rights to private persons and legal persons. The study utilizes the jurisprudence of the European Court of Justice.

**Topics:**

- General concept and development of market freedoms.
- Leading case law of the European Court of Justice.
- Function of market freedom in fostering integration.
- Impact and compliance requirements of market freedoms.

### EU Competition Law (Antitrust)

This course provides a solid introduction to the main areas of EU competition law, illustrated by practical examples. It covers the development of EU competition rules, which are directly applicable to enterprises.

**Core Elements:**

- The fight against cartels and investigating/sanctioning cartels.
- Abuse of dominance.
- Horizontal and vertical restraints of competition.
- The importance of market definitions and techniques used.
- The role of market power for Articles 101 and 102 TFEU.
- Basic outlines of European merger control.
- Relationship between EU and national competition law.

### EU Tax Law

This module addresses the increasing impact of European Union law on the taxation of private individuals and companies. While the EU lacks an original tax authority, its law significantly influences national tax laws.

**Structure:**

- Introduction to European Union Tax Law, aims, and measures.
- Positive harmonization of indirect taxes (e.g., European directives).
- Secondary law harmonizing direct taxes (e.g., the Parent-Subsidiary Directive).
- The importance of fundamental freedoms for taxation in Europe.
- Analysis of key case law from the European Court of Justice.

## E-Commerce and Digital Law

This course offers a thorough introduction to the legal problems arising from the use of the internet and e-commerce, focusing on business-related activities.

**Key Areas of Analysis:**

- **E-Commerce & Private Law:** Formation of contract, standard terms and conditions, consumer protection, and specific issues related to internet auctions and mobile commerce.
- **Advertising for E-commerce:** Rules of unfair competition, required imprint information, the ban on spamming, and liability under tele media law (for hyperlinks, hotspot operators, and user-generated content).
- **Copyright Law and Online Shops:** Copyright restrictions for content presentation (including file sharing and streaming) and protection of content and websites.
- **Domains:** Explanation of the domain name system, its implications for trademark and unfair competition law, and methods for domain protection.

## International Dispute Resolution

This module compares cross-border litigation in state courts with international arbitration, analyzing the procedural and substantive differences between the two methods.

**Cross-Border Litigation (State Courts):**

- Impact of the forum on the dispute (lex fori and lex causae).
- International Jurisdiction of state courts (e.g., Brussels I Regulation).
- Provisional measures and procedural strategy.
- Access to evidence in cross-border litigation.
- Recognition and enforcement of foreign judgments (Brussels I Regulation).

**International Arbitration:**

- Advantages and disadvantages of arbitration.
- Drafting arbitration agreements.
- Introduction to common arbitration rules (ICC, LCIA, Swiss Rules, DIS).
- Conduct of arbitration proceedings.
- Enforcing arbitration awards and the New York Convention.
