# Blockchain Technology as Enabler for Supply Chain Sustainability

**Authors:**

- [Prof. Dr. Christoph Bode](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/)
- [Jonas Ronellenfitsch](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/team/alumni/dr-ronellenfitsch-jonas/)

### Overview of Research

This research project addresses the novel intersection between distributed ledger technologies (DLT) and supply chain sustainability. The momentum generated by cryptocurrencies and movements like Friday for Future has made Blockchain a prominent topic in both academic and industry supply chain discussions.

The study reviews the enabling roles of blockchain technology in enhancing supply chain sustainability and conceptualizes the corresponding operational domains.

### Key Contributions and Benefits

The research illuminates how the adoption of this novel technology drives sustainability by improving several critical areas:

- **Value Increase:** Enhancing the perceived and actual value throughout the supply chain.
- **Trust:** Establishing verifiable and immutable records, thereby increasing trust among stakeholders.
- **Inclusiveness:** Ensuring that all participants in the supply chain benefit from the technology.
- **Efficiency:** Driving both economic and environmental efficiency improvements.

These combined factors demonstrate how blockchain technology serves as a powerful mechanism to achieve comprehensive sustainability goals within complex supply networks.
