### General Equilibrium Theory

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703
**Grading:** Final examination, solutions to problem sets, and participation in exercise sessions.

**Course Content:**
The course provides an advanced introduction to the theory of general equilibrium. It builds on introductory microeconomics (E702) and uses mathematical tools from E700 and E701. Topics include:

1. Aggregate Demand
1. Production Theory
1. Exchange Economies
1. Production Economies
1. Uncertainty and Arrow-Debreu Equilibrium
1. Basic Asset Pricing

The course teaches the fundamentals of economic equilibrium theory, laying foundations for macroeconomics, international trade, and finance.
**Teaching Method:** Lecture (4 SWS) + Exercise (2 SWS)

**Competences Acquired:**
Students learn core concepts of general economic equilibrium (e.g., Law of Demand, Walrasian equilibrium, arbitrage-free allocations). They can analyze problems independently and apply concepts to various fields (international trade, macroeconomics, public finance).

### Dynamic Rational Expectations and VARs

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703
**Grading:** Final examination, solutions to problem sets, and participation in exercise sessions.

**Course Content:**
This course covers basic methods useful for dynamic economic modeling under rational expectations. Topics include:

- Linear Rational Expectations (RE) Models: determinacy, indeterminacy, and 'sunspot' equilibria.
- Linear RE models and Vector Auto-Regressions (VARs): state space representation, invertibility problems, identification of economic shocks.
- Linear Quadratic (LQ) Dynamic Programming: Ricatti equation, invariant subspace methods, stochastic problems.
- Introduction to the New Keynesian Model and its Linearized Form.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students can apply and understand basic tools used in business cycle analysis and follow recent literature on dynamic stochastic general equilibrium models.

### Panel and Time Series Econometrics

**Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803
**Grading:** Written exam and assignments

**Course Content:**
Part I focuses on panel data models (fixed- and random effects, GMM/IV estimation, dynamic panel models). Part II covers univariate time series analysis, starting with theoretical foundations, linear models (autoregressions), and non-stationary unit root time series.

**Competences Acquired:**
Students acquire demanding econometric, statistical, and mathematical techniques for panel and univariate analysis, enabling them to extend their methodological knowledge independently.

**Literature:**

- Cameron, A.C. and Trivedi, P.K. (2005), *Microeconometrics: Methods and Applications*.
- Hamilton, J.D. (1994), *Time Series Analysis*.
- Wooldridge, J.M. (2010), *Econometric Analysis of Cross Section and Panel Data*.

### Environmental Economics

**Prerequisites:** E700-E703
**Grading:** N/A

**Course Content:**
The objective is to introduce the main concepts and empirical tools used in the economic analysis of environmental policies. Topics include:

**Competences Acquired:**
