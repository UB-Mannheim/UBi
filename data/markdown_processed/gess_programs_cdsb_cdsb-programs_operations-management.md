# Operations Management Research Expertise

The Operations Management Area is supported by four renowned chairs: [Bode](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/bode/), [Fleischmann](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/fleischmann/), [Schön](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/schoen/), and [Stolletz](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/stolletz/). These chairs cover a broad spectrum of topics, ranging from procurement and production to supply chain and service operations management. While each chair maintains its individual specialization, the department operates with an integrated curriculum and a unified mission.

A core focus of the research is Operations Research (OR). Since most operational decisions involve complex trade-offs with multiple alternatives and impacts on costs or profits, OR utilizes powerful analytical models and tools to make these decisions systematically, thereby expanding participants' analytical skills.

The faculty is dedicated to producing high-quality, practically relevant research for decision-makers in business and society. Research findings are regularly published in top scientific journals (e.g., *Journal of Operations Management*, *Management Science*, *Manufacturing and Service Operations Management*) and presented at international conferences.

## Fields of Research

### Procurement

Research in Procurement focuses on understanding, describing, explaining, and predicting phenomena and mechanisms critical to the field. Current research thrusts include:

- Risk and disruptions
- Interfirm relationships
- Innovation and entrepreneurship
- Sustainability
- Strategies and performance

A particular interest lies in research questions situated at the intersection of procurement and related disciplines (such as logistics, marketing, management, or organization), requiring the integration of multiple theoretical perspectives to develop comprehensive models. The department maintains intensive collaboration with a network of organizations to bridge theory and practice.

### Logistics and Supply Chain Management

This domain encompasses managerial decisions regarding the design and operation of the supply chain in a broad sense. Key areas of research include:

- Optimal resource deployment
- Process optimization
- Coordination of individual processes to maximize overall performance
- Transportation and inventory management

The research approach combines scientific rigor with managerial relevance, relying heavily on quantitative modeling and operations research techniques to provide novel insights into complex supply chain issues.

### Service Operations

Research in Service Operations is concerned with developing quantitative models and operations research methods to provide decision support for practical problems in service operations management. The goal is to enable better, more systematic, and data-driven decision-making in complex situations. Current research areas include:

- Revenue management
- Service design
- Sustainable (Service) Operations Management and Green Product Design
- Strategic pricing and tariff design

### Production Management

The main field of research covers various managerial decisions in production and operations management. Current projects focus on quantitative decision support in the design and management of production systems. Examples include:

- Operations scheduling
- Management of dynamic systems
- Design of lean operations systems

Application areas are wide-ranging, including assemble-to-order (ATO) production systems, automotive flow lines, airport operations, and call center operations. The department applies and develops methods for deterministic optimization, performance analysis of queueing systems, and optimization of stochastic systems under both static and dynamic conditions.
