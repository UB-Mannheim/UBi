# Admission to the CDSS Program

The CDSS and the School of Social Sciences utilize an **empirical-analytical approach, informed by theory**, and rely heavily on **quantitative methods**.

## Application Process and Deadlines

- **Eligibility:** Highly qualified students of all nationalities holding a very good, research-oriented Master's degree are welcome to apply.
- **Consideration:** Applications from students with excellent (4-year) Bachelor degrees and applicants from neighboring fields are also considered, provided their background is deemed sufficient.
- **Evaluation:** All applications are evaluated solely on the basis of qualification and scientific potential.
- **Application Portal:** All applications must be submitted via the [Online application portal](https://gess-application.uni-mannheim.de/portal/). Applications sent via e-mail will not be considered.

**Key Dates:**

- **Applications Open:** Mid-November of the previous year (for the fall semester).
- **Early Application Deadline:** 15 January 2026. (Note: At least one Letter of Recommendation must reach the university by this date.)
- **Final Application Deadline:** 31 March 2026.

## Required Application Documents

A complete application must include the following components:

### 1. Letter of Motivation

The letter (in English, max 700 words) should explain your current goals, career plans, and reasons for interest in a structured doctoral program in Social Science. It is crucial to elaborate on your study plans and research interests, demonstrating how they align with the current research of the faculty member(s) you wish to work with.

### 2. Writing Sample

Submit a writing sample (in English or German) of up to 10 pages (max 3500 words, bibliography excluded). Acceptable examples include a paper written during your studies, a representative excerpt from your thesis, or an essay related to your research interests. *Do not submit a research or dissertation proposal.*

### 3. Certificates and Transcripts of Grades

You must provide a transcript and degree certificate for every academic degree program attended. These documents **must include** the university's grading scheme and show courses attended, grades received, and degrees obtained (mentioning rank or distinction, if applicable).

- **Translations:** A translation is required for any transcript not in English or German.
- **Preliminary Reports:** If you have not finished your current degree, attach a preliminary grade report (in English or German).
- *Note: Certified translations are not required at the application stage.*

### 4. English Proficiency Scores

Applicants whose native language is not English must provide proof of proficiency:

- **TOEFL:** Minimum score of 600 (paper test) or 100 (internet-based test). Scores are valid for five years. Ask ETS to report scores using code: 0933.
- **IELTS:** Minimum overall band score of 7.

### 5. Letters of Recommendation

Two letters of recommendation are required from faculty, scholars, or individuals who can assess your potential for academic research and teaching.

- **Action:** Contact your referees *before* applying to confirm their availability and willingness to write a reference.
- **Responsibility:** It is the **applicant's responsibility** to ensure that references reach the university by the given deadlines (early or final).
