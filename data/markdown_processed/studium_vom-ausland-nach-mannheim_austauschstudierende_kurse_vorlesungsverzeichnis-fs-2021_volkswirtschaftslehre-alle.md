# Economics Course Catalog for Exchange Students

This guide provides important information regarding the selection of economics courses for exchange students at the University of Mannheim.

## Course Selection Guidelines

### 🎓 For Bachelor's Students

- **Scope:** Bachelor's students must select courses exclusively from the bachelor's level curriculum. Master's level courses are generally not open to undergraduate students.
- **Prerequisites:** Exchange students from other departments may only attend economics lectures if they meet the required prerequisites.
- **Seminars:** Bachelor's level seminars are typically restricted to enrolled economics students.

### 🎓 For Master's Students

- **Scope:** Master's students have the freedom to choose elective modules from the department, provided they fulfill the prerequisites for each course.
- **Restrictions:** Core modules (E601–603 and E700–703) are generally not open to exchange students.
- **Exception:** Students may attend bachelor's level courses if their home university grants permission.

## Coordination and Eligibility

Exchange students from other schools and departments must check their eligibility and specific requirements with the relevant exchange coordinator:

- **General Inquiry (Bachelor's):** Please contact the exchange coordinator, Ms. Christiane Cischinsky, at [cischinsky@uni-mannheim.de](mailto:cischinsky@uni-mannheim.de).

## Available Course Topics (Examples)

The department offers modules covering various advanced topics in economics and management science, including:

- **Working with Text:** Regular expressions/regex, text parsing, parts-of-speech tagging, web-scraping, and dictionaries.
- **Text as Data:** Word-embeddings (from bag-of-words to word2vec/GloVe), topic models, sentiment analysis, and text-similarity.
- **ML with Text (in Python):** ML fundamentals, BERT, and text-classification.

*Note: All methods and concepts are illustrated using recent applications from economics and management science literature.*
