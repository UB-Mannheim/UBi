# Social Simulation Seminar (FSS 2020)

This seminar focuses on agent-based models (ABM) and their applications in social science. Students will summarize current research papers in presentations and form groups to develop their own agent-based simulation models for parking facility management using [NetLogo](https://ccl.northwestern.edu/netlogo/).

## Seminar Details

- **Organizers:** Prof. Dr. Heiner Stuckenschmidt and Kilian Theil.
- **Capacity:** Available for up to 12 Master or exceptional Bachelor students.
- **First Appointment:** 02/04/2020, 12:00–13:30 (Online via MS Teams).

## Learning Goals

By the end of the seminar, students will be able to:

- Understand the foundations of agent-based modeling (ABM) for social simulations and how to implement them in NetLogo.
- Review and summarize current research papers on social simulation.
- Develop their own agent-based simulation model for parking facility management in NetLogo.

## Requirements

- Basic programming skills in a language of choice.
- Good reading and presentation skills in English.
- Proficiency in LaTeX.

## Evaluation

The final grade is composed of two parts:

1. **Individual Research Paper Presentation (50%):** Students summarize a chosen recent research paper (15 min presentation + 5 min Q&A).
1. **Practical Group Project (50%):** Students form groups of 3 to 5 people to develop an agent-based model for parking facility management in NetLogo. This project is evaluated via:
   - A presentation (25 min + 10 min Q&A).
   - Submitted code.
   - A short report written in LaTeX.
