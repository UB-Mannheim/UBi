# DFG Research Training Group “Statistical Modeling in Psychology” (SMiP)

The DFG Research Training Group “Statistical Modeling in Psychology” (SMiP) is a transregional collaboration dedicated to advancing behavioral research through advanced quantitative methods.

## 🔬 Research Focus and Mission

The SMiP group addresses a critical challenge in behavioral research: the gap between substantive research in basic and applied fields of psychology and the latest developments in statistical modeling and psychometrics.

The core mission is to **counteract the segregation of psychology into substantive science and statistical methodology**. The group conceives statistical models as formal frameworks for:

- **Formalizing Psychological Theories:** Theoretical predictions can be phrased in terms of model parameters.
- **Testing Predictions:** State-of-the-art statistical techniques are used both to measure psychological constructs (via parameter estimation) and to test theoretical predictions (using model selection and model testing).

In essence, the SMiP group approaches substantive research questions by means of rigorous statistical modeling.

## 👥 Participating Researchers and Universities

SMiP is a collaboration of thirteen renowned behavioral researchers from five German universities:

- **University of Mannheim:** Arndt Bröder, Edgar Erdfelder, Beatrice G. Kuhlmann, Thorsten Meiser, Sabine Sonnentag
- **Albert-Ludwigs-University Freiburg:** Andrea Kiesel, Karl Christoph Klauer
- **Ruprecht-Karls-University Heidelberg:** Andreas Voss
- **RPTU Kaiserslautern-Landau at Landau:** Benjamin E. Hilbig, Tanja Lischetzke, Eunike Wetzel
- **Eberhard Karls University Tübingen:** Mandy Hütter, Rolf Ulrich

The participating researchers possess a strong background in advanced quantitative methods and are experts in diverse substantive fields of psychology, including cognition, social cognition, motivation, affect, and individual differences.
