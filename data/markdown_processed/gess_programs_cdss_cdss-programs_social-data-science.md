# Social Data Science (CDSS) Program

The CDSS Doctoral Program in Social Data Science offers advanced coursework in data analysis methods tailored for the social sciences, integrating principles from statistics and computer science. The curriculum is supplemented by topical coursework within the social sciences.

The program is designed to enable students to address complex research questions more effectively by utilizing new types of data and advanced analytical methods. Students have the opportunity to specialize in machine learning and its applications across various data modalities, including text, images, videos, and networks.

- **Course Catalog:** [CDSS Course Catalog](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdss/course-catalog/)

## Research at the Mannheim Center for Data Science

The [Mannheim Center for Data Science](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/datascience/) serves as a key platform for interdisciplinary research in data science, bringing together experts from diverse academic disciplines.

The Center's research activities are focused on several core areas:

- **Data Valuation and AI:** Advancing the understanding and valuation of data, particularly in conjunction with artificial intelligence methodologies.
- **Digital Transformation:** Investigating the digital transformation of individuals, the economy, and society by analyzing both structured and unstructured data.
- **Methodological Development:** Developing and refining appropriate methodological approaches to support these research objectives.
- **Ethical Data Use:** Identifying frameworks for the responsible use of data and analytical methods that serve the public interest.

### Supervision and Research Focus

The research activities of the doctoral supervisors for the Social Data Science track are characterized by:

- A strong foundation in research methods.
- A deep interest in answering substantive, real-world questions.
- A commitment to an interdisciplinary approach to scientific inquiry.
