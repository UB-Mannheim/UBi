## Seminar Abstract

This seminar examines the implications of supply chain Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) risk management on shareholder value.

**Key Findings:**

- Firms that demonstrate fewer supply chain ESG incidents exhibit higher future stock returns and improved accounting performance.
- Robust supply chain ESG risk management creates value through three mechanisms: (i) attracting pro-social stakeholders, (ii) enhancing supply chain stability, and (iii) hedging against regulatory risk.
- The lack of supply chain transparency currently prevents the stock market from fully capitalizing on the value generated by robust ESG risk management.

**Conclusion:** The presentation highlights the net benefits of strong supply chain ESG risk management and advises regulators and investors on the critical importance of relevant disclosure.

## Speaker Biography: Professor Aaron Yoon

Professor Aaron Yoon is a leading expert in integrating a firm's Environment, Social, and Governance (ESG) efforts into portfolio decision-making.

**Research Impact:**
His research on ESG was noted as a turning point in how investors view and integrate ESG information. The methodologies suggested in his work have been widely adopted by asset managers. His work is regularly cited in major financial outlets, including *Bloomberg*, *Forbes*, *The New York Times*, and *The Wall Street Journal*.

**Awards and Recognition:**
Professor Yoon has received multiple accolades for his research and teaching, including:

- Best 40 Under 40 Professors Recognition (Poets & Quants)
- Crowell Prize for Best Paper in Quantitative Investing (PanAgora Asset Management)
- Chair's Core Teaching Award (Northwestern Kellogg)
- Best Dissertation Award (American Accounting Association)
- Graham and Dodd Scroll Award (CFA Institute)

**Academic and Professional Background:**

- **Education:** Doctorate from Harvard University; Master’s and Bachelor’s from Northwestern University.
- **Experience:** Prior to academia, he worked as an equities salestrader and a research analyst at Credit Suisse.
