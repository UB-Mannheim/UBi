# CDSE Doctoral Program Overview

The doctoral program in economics is a comprehensive five-year commitment. It is structured into a two-year course phase, followed by a three-year research phase dedicated to the dissertation. The CDSE commits to funding students for this duration, contingent upon successful completion of the course phase and maintaining overall academic standing.

## Program Structure by Year

### Year 1: Core Curriculum

Students must complete compulsory courses in the following areas:

- Mathematics
- Microeconomics (I-III)
- Macroeconomics (I-III)
- Econometrics (I-III)

**Requirements:**

- **Semester 1:** Pass grade in all courses and a GPA of 2.5.
- **Semester 2:** Pass grade in all courses and an adjusted GPA of 2.5 (based on 5 out of 6 core courses).

### Year 2: Electives and Proposal

The focus shifts to specialized coursework and research planning.

- **Coursework:** Students attend elective field courses, earning 40–46 ECTS credit points. At least 4 of these electives must come from the CDSE course catalogue.
- **Proposal:** Students must submit their dissertation proposal at the end of the second year.

### Year 3 and Beyond: Research and Thesis Writing

The final three years are primarily devoted to the writing and completion of the doctoral thesis.

**Mandatory Coursework (Year 3):**
Students must complete the following minimum requirements:

1. **Bridge Course:** One bridge course (5 ECTS) from either the [Center for Doctoral Studies in Business (CDSB)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/course-catalog/) or the [Center for Doctoral Studies in Behavioral and Social Sciences (CDSS)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdss/course-catalog/).
1. **English Academic Writing:** (3 ECTS).
1. **Electives:** Two further electives supporting thesis writing (10 ECTS).

**Academic Engagement:**

- **Seminars:** Participation in the weekly [CDSE Seminar](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/course-catalog/cdse-seminar/) and the [faculty seminar](http://fakultaetsseminar.uni-mannheim.de/) is required. Students are expected to present and act as discussants at least once in the third and subsequent years.
- **Research:** Students usually attend the research seminar of their respective field, earning credits by presenting their research.

**Thesis Planning:**

- Students decide on their thesis supervisor and topic during the 4th semester. The remaining members of the doctoral committee may be selected later.

## Degree Requirements and Thesis

To successfully earn a doctoral degree, the following steps must be completed:

1. Completion of the entire CDSE course program.
1. Completion, submission, and acceptance of the thesis.
1. Successful thesis defense.

**Thesis Format:**
The doctoral thesis can take two forms:

- **Classical Form:** A longer text analyzing one topic in depth.
- **Article Collection:** Three original essays in one area, intended for publication in scholarly journals.

The decision regarding the thesis format is made by the student and their supervisor. For detailed regulations, please consult the [CDSE Regulations Section](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdse/student-information/regulations/).

**Grading Scheme:**
The CDSE uses the following grading scale: 1.0 (excellent) to 4.0 (pass), with 5.0 indicating failure. Differentiation is possible in 0.3 steps (e.g., 1.3, 1.7).

## German Doctoral Titles

In Germany, doctoral degrees often use specific Latin designations depending on the field of study.

- **Faculty of Business and Economics:** Dr. rer. pol (doctor rerum politicarum)
- **Faculty of Social Sciences:** Dr. rer. soc. (doctor rerum socialium)
