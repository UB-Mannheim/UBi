# The Distributional Impacts of Real-Time Pricing in the Spanish Residential Electricity Market

**Speaker:** Mar Reguant (Northwestern University)
**Date:** March 8, 2021

## Seminar Abstract

While the benefits of Real-Time Pricing (RTP) of electricity are well known, less is known about their distributional impacts. This paper examines the distributional impacts of RTP by leveraging a country-wide field experiment conducted in Spain.

**Methodology:**

- Since 2015, RTP has been the default option for most Spanish households.
- The study utilizes access to hourly consumption data for over four million households spanning more than a year.
- This data allows the computation of bill impacts resulting from the switch from flat rates to RTP.

**Findings:**

- By examining the households' sociodemographic characteristics, the research documents which groups benefit and which groups lose from the implementation of RTP.
- The authors propose a method to infer consumers' unobserved income by combining highly granular data with information regarding the distribution of income at the zip code level.
- The results suggest that the distributional impacts of RTP were quite small and, if anything, slightly progressive.
