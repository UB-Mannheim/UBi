## Seminar Abstract

Companies appoint Chief Sustainability Officers (CSOs) to advance their Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) agendas. While CSOs are often part of the broader executive team, it is increasingly common for them to be included amongst a company’s top management team, suggesting an influential role.

This paper investigates how the placement of a CSO (on or off the top management team) relates to company sustainability and financial performance, and how CSO incentives moderate these relationships.

**Key Findings:**

- Having a CSO on the top management team is associated with higher sustainability performance, but lower financial performance, compared to having a CSO who is not on the top management team.
- This association is strongest when the CSO has ESG metrics included in their compensation contract.
- The composition of CSO compensation matters; CSOs on the top management team who have a higher proportion of compensation paid as salary are associated with the greatest subsequent improvements in sustainability performance.

## Speaker Biography

Naomi Soderstrom joined the Department of Accounting at the University of Melbourne in 2012. She received her PhD in Accounting and Information Systems from Northwestern University in Illinois, United States.

Dr. Soderstrom is an Editor of the *Journal of Managerial Accounting Research* and serves on the editorial boards of several other journals. She is a member of professional organizations across multiple continents, as well as academic honor societies (Phi Beta Kappa and Beta Gamma Sigma).

Prior to joining the University of Melbourne, she taught at the University of Colorado at Boulder, the University of Washington, and the University of Colorado at Denver. She has also served as a Visiting Professor at several institutions, including the Indian School of Business, the University of Maastricht (Netherlands), IE Business School (Spain), the University of Mannheim (Germany), and the Stuttgart Institute of Management and Technology (Germany).
