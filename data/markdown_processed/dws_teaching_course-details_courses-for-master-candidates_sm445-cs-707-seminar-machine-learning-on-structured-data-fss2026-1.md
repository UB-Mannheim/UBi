# Seminar: Machine Learning on Structured Data (SM445/CS 707)

This seminar explores state-of-the-art approaches for machine learning on structured data, including data frames, tables, relational databases, graphs, and knowledge bases. Topics covered include symbolic/neural approaches, end-to-end models, few-shot learning, and foundation models.

## 📚 Seminar Details

- **Course Code:** SM445/CS 707
- **Focus:** Machine Learning on Structured Data
- **Available Slots:** Up to 7 Master students (4 ECTS) and up to 5 Bachelor students (5 ECTS).
- **Prerequisites:**
  - **MSc students:** Solid background in machine learning.
  - **BSc students:** Wirtschaftsinformatik IV.

## 🎯 Goals and Learning Outcomes

Participants will be expected to:

- Read, understand, and explore scientific literature.
- Summarize a current research topic in a concise report (10 single-column pages + references).
- Give two presentations on the topic: a 3-minute flash presentation and a 15-minute final presentation.
- Moderate a scientific discussion about a fellow student's topic.
- Review (drafts of) reports from fellow students.

## 💡 Topic Areas and Research Focus

Students will be assigned a larger topic area based on their preferences. The goal is to provide a short overview (1/4) and then present a deep dive on a self-selected topic within that area (3/4).

**Core Topic Areas:**

### 1. Models for Tabular Data

- **Symbolic Approaches (BSc):** Gradient-boosted decision trees ([https://openreview.net/forum?id=XEFWBxi075](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XEFWBxi075))
- **Neural Approaches:** TURL ([https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3542700.3542709](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3542700.3542709))
- **Foundation Models:** TabPFN ([https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08328-6](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08328-6)) / TabICL ([https://openreview.net/forum?id=0VvD1PmNzM](https://openreview.net/forum?id=0VvD1PmNzM))
- **LLM-based Approaches (BSc):** See survey ([https://openreview.net/forum?id=IZnrCGF9WI](https://openreview.net/forum?id=IZnrCGF9WI))

### 2. Models for Relational Data

- **Benchmarks and Feature Engineering (BSc):** RelBench ([https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/hash/25cd345233c65fac1fec0ce61d0f7836-Abstract-Datasets_and_Benchmarks_Track.html](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/hash/25cd345233c65fac1fec0ce61d0f7836-Abstract-Datasets_and_Benchmarks_Track.html))
- **Deep Learning Methods:** RelGNN ([https://openreview.net/forum?id=XXh3zmw2Uy](https://openreview.net/forum?id=XXh3zmw2Uy))

### 3. Knowledge Graphs

- **Symbolic Approaches (BSc):** AnyBURL (PDF) ([https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2019/0435.pdf](https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2019/0435.pdf))
- **Transductive Approaches:** ComplEx ([https://proceedings.mlr.press/v48/trouillon16.html?ref=https://githubhelp.com](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v48/trouillon16.html?ref=https://githubhelp.com))
- **Inductive Approaches:** NBFNet ([https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2021/hash/f6a673f09493afcd8b129a0bcf1cd5bc-Abstract.html](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2021/hash/f6a673f09493afcd8b129a0bcf1cd5bc-Abstract.html))
- **Foundation Models:** HYPER ([https://openreview.net/pdf?id=YLTQbMoAaX](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=YLTQbMoAaX)) / GraphRAG ([https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.16130](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.16130))
- **Survey:** See survey ([https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.00309](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.00309))

### 4. Implementations (GNN Frameworks)

- **Frameworks (BSc):** PyTorch Geometric ([https://pytorch-geometric.readthedocs.io/en/stable/](https://pytorch-geometric.readthedocs.io/en/stable/)) or Deep Graph Library ([https://www.dgl.ai/pages/start.html](https://www.dgl.ai/pages/start.html)). *Note: Must include experiments.*
- **Advanced Topics:** Accelerated / efficient GNN implementation (See survey: [https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/10882936](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/10882936))
