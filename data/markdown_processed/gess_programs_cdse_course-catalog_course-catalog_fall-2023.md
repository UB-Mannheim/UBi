# CDSE Course Catalog: PhD Level Courses

This catalog provides an overview of advanced courses offered to doctoral students in economics, covering topics from econometrics and microeconomics to public policy and data science.

## Course Listings by Semester

### Fall 2023 Courses

#### 1. Basic Mathematical Concepts (Economics)

- **Prerequisites:** Basic mathematical knowledge
- **Course Content:** The course covers four chapters: 1) basic mathematical concepts (sets, functions, relations); 2) metric and normed spaces (convergence, continuity, compact sets); 3) vector spaces (matrix algebra, linear transformation, eigenvalues); and 4) multivariate differentiability and optimization problems.
- **Competences Acquired:** Students gain basic mathematical concepts of analysis and linear algebra, understanding abstract concepts like metric and linear spaces, and applying knowledge to solve nonlinear optimization problems.
- **Lecturers:** So Jin Lee and Chang Liu
- **Assessment:** Exam (120 min)
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Monday (04.09.23 – 25.09.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Tuesday (05.09.23 – 26.09.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Wednesday (06.09.23 – 27.09.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Thursday (07.09.23 – 28.09.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001).
  - **Exam:** Friday (06.10.23), 08:00 – 10:00 (Palace, Room SN 163).
  - **Retake Exam:** Monday (04.12.23), 10:00 – 12:00 (B 6, 30–32, E-F, Room 212).
  - **Tutorials:** Various groups and days listed (e.g., Monday 13:45 – 15:15, Tuesday 13:45 – 15:15, etc.).

#### 2. Microeconomic Theory

- **Prerequisites:** E700
- **Course Content:** Provides a foundation for Ph.D.-level microeconomics. Part I covers decision theory (choice, preferences, utility, expected utility, behavioral models). Part II covers game theory (static games of complete/incomplete information, dynamic games, equilibrium concepts).
- **Competences Acquired:** Students acquire basic tools for graduate-level microeconomic analysis, learning to model decision-making and strategic interactions.
- **Lecturers:** Nicolas Schutz
- **Assessment:** Written exam (120 min, 90% weighting); Exercises (10% weighting).
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Monday (09.10.23 – 04.12.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Wednesday (11.10.23 – 06.12.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001).
  - **Exam:** Monday (18.12.23), 11:30 – 13:30 (Schloss SO 148 MVV Hörsaal).
  - **Retake Exam:** Friday (26.01.24), 10:00 – 12:00 (L9, 7, Room 308).
  - **Tutorials:** Wednesday (11.10.23 – 06.12.23), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5 Room P044); Thursday (12.10.23 – 07.12.23), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5 Room P044).

#### 3. Macroeconomic Theory

- **Prerequisites:** E700
- **Course Content:** Introduction to modern macroeconomic analysis, focusing on structural dynamic models. Topics include household preference, firm technology, market structure, and deriving macroeconomic aggregates from micro-founded decisions.
- **Competences Acquired:** Students become familiar with recursive methods and dynamic programming, and basic macroeconomic models.
- **Teaching Assistant:** Qinkun Yao
- **Assessment:** Problem sets (15%); Midterm (90 min, 35%); Final exam (120 min, 50%).
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Thursday (12.10.23 – 07.12.23), 15:30 – 17:00 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Friday (13.10.23 – 08.12.23), 08:30 – 10:00 (L7, 3–5 Room 001).
  - **Exam:** Thursday (14.12.23), 08:30 – 10:30 (Schloss SO 148 MVV Hörsaal).
  - **Retake Exam:** Wednesday (24.01.24), 10:00 – 12:00 (L 9, 1–2, Room 002).
  - **Tutorials:** Monday (16.10.23 – 05.12.22), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5 Room P044); Tuesday (17.10.23 – 05.12.23), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5 Room P044).

#### 4. Advanced Econometrics

- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806
- **Course Content:** Advanced theoretical econometrics, covering asymptotic theory, point estimation (MLE, GMM), hypothesis testing, linear regression, instrumental variables (IV), and the bootstrap.
- **Competences Acquired:** Students attain advanced theoretical knowledge in econometrics and are able to conduct research-oriented projects independently.
- **Teaching Assistant:** Giovanni Ballarin
- **Assessment:** Written exam (120 min).
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Tuesday (10.10.23 – 05.12.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001); Thursday (12.10.23 – 07.12.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5 Room 001).
  - **Exam:** Monday (11.12.23), 10:00 – 12:00 (Schloss SO 145 Heinrich Vetter Hörsaal).
  - **Retake Exam:** Monday (22.01.24), 10:00 – 12:00 (L 9, 1–2, Room 002).
  - **Tutorials:** Wednesday (11.10.23 – 06.12.23), 15:30 – 17:00 (L7, 3–5, Room P044); Friday (03.11.23 – 03.11.23), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5, Room P044).

#### 5. Environmental Economics Seminar

- **Prerequisites:** 2nd and higher year Ph.D. students from CDSE / 2nd year Master of Economic Research
- **Course Content:** Discusses latest advances and challenges in sustainability and net carbon emissions of zero, covering economics and management of sustainability activities and emission abatement strategies.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper presentations and discussions (80%).
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Monday (04.09.23 – 04.12.23), 17:15 – 18:45 (O 129).

#### 6. Economic History

- **Prerequisites:** First-year sequence in the Economics PhD program.
- **Course Content:** Focuses on quantitative economic history, covering topics like trade, growth of cities, institutions, human capital, and financial development.
- **Competences Acquired:** Students gain knowledge of empirical methods in applied economics and the main topics of economic history.
- **Schedule:**
  - **Seminar:** Monday (11.09.23 – 04.12.23), every two weeks, 13:45 – 15:15 (L9, 1–2, Room 002).

- **Prerequisites:** Internal/External speakers (No formal prerequisite listed).
- **Course Content:** A forum for discussing recent research in econometrics.
- **Contact Information:** Christoph Rothe (rothe@vwl.uni-mannheim.de).
- **Schedule:**
  - **Seminar:** Thursday (07.09.23 – 07.12.23), 15:30 – 17:00 (L9, 1–2, Room 003).

#### 8. Search Theory and Labor Markets

- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806
- **Course Content:** Covers search theoretic models and their applications to labor markets, including the McCall, Diamond-Mortensen-Pissarides models, and various equilibrium concepts.
- **Competences Acquired:** Students become familiar with cutting-edge search models used in labor, monetary, and finance economics.
- **Schedule:**
  - **Seminar:** Thursday (07.09.23 – 07.12.23), 15:30 – 17:00 (L7, 3–5, P043); Thursday (08.09.23 – 19.10.23), 17:15 – 18:45 (L7, 3–5, P043).

#### 9. Machine Learning for Economists

- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806
- **Course Content:** Introduction to modern machine learning (ML) methods for economists, focusing on supervised/unsupervised ML, causal inference, and policy analysis.
- **Contact Information:** Christoph Rothe (rothe@vwl.uni-mannheim.de).
- **Competences Acquired:** Students understand the theoretical foundations and practical application of ML methods in empirical economics.
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Monday (04.09.23 – 16.10.23), 10:15 – 11:45 (L7, 3–5, Room P044); Tuesday (05.09.23 – 17.10.23), 13:45 – 15:15 (L7, 3–5, Room P043).

#### 10. Public Economics I

- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806
- **Course Content:** Core ideas in market failures and public intervention, covering optimal taxation, public goods, tax incidence, and tax evasion.
- **Competences Acquired:** Introduces core topics in Public Economics, useful for analyzing policy issues.
- **Schedule:**
  - **Lecture:** Monday (04.09.23 – 04.12.23), 08:30 – 10:00 (L7, 3–5 Room P043); Wednesday (06.09.23 – 29.11.23), every two weeks, 08:30 – 10:00 (L7, 3–5 Room P043).

- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806

- **Competences Acquired:** Students are familiar with core models and methods used in modern international trade research.
- **Schedule:**
