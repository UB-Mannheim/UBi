# Machine Learning Methods for Modeling in Psychology

**Instructors:** David Goretzko (Utrecht University) & Philipp Sterner (Ruhr University Bochum, German Center for Mental Health DZPG)

This workshop introduces the basics of machine learning (ML) modeling, with a specific focus on applications in psychological research and assessment.

## Workshop Scope and Content

The course provides a comprehensive overview of advanced modeling techniques, covering:

- **Resampling Strategies:** Ensuring valid performance evaluation of complex modeling pipelines.
- **Supervised Learning:** Utilizing tree-based algorithms as state-of-the-art approaches for tabular data.
- **Model Optimization:** Techniques for regularization (to avoid overfitting) and hyperparameter-tuning (to boost generalizability).
- **Performance Evaluation:** Discussion of different performance metrics for both classification and regression tasks.
- **Benchmarking:** Setting up robust benchmarks for model selection.

## Advanced Topics for Psychologists

The workshop features two critical extensions to standard ML modeling:

1. **Interpretable Machine Learning (IML):** Addresses the "black-box" problem of complex ML algorithms. IML provides tools such as local explanations, feature importance metrics, and visualizations of marginal effects, making models understandable in high-stakes psychological contexts.
1. **Cost-Sensitive Learning (CSL):** Recognizes that classification errors carry unequal consequences (e.g., in suicide prevention). CSL provides methods to account for varying misclassification costs, which is critical for mitigating the impact of errors beyond simple accuracy optimization.

## Practical Application and Prerequisites

Participants will apply all discussed concepts and modeling strategies through hands-on exercises conducted in **R**. The exercises guide participants through the entire ML modeling cycle:

- Setting up a modeling pipeline.
- Evaluating various algorithms in a benchmarking experiment.
- Selecting the most suitable model and interpreting its predictions.
- Practical implementation of IML and CSL methods.

**Prerequisites:**

- Familiarity with R software for data analysis.
- A good understanding of (parametric) regression modeling.
- Participants are asked to bring their own laptops.
