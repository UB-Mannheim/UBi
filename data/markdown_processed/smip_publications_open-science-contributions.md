# Open Science Contributions

This section details the open-source software packages, analyses codes, and shared data developed by the research group, promoting transparency and reproducibility in psychological science.

## 💻 Software Packages

### Peer-reviewed Journal Publications

These packages and apps are associated with published research:

- **ABrox** [Python Module](https://github.com/mertensu/ABrox)
  - A user-friendly Python module for approximate Bayesian computation, with a focus on model comparison.
  - *Reference:* Mertens, U. K., Voss, A., & Radev, S. (2018). ABrox—A user-friendly Python module for approximate Bayesian computation with a focus on model comparison. *PLOS ONE*, 13(3), e0193981. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0193981)
- **CurtailedRRT** [R Shiny App](https://fabiolareiber.shinyapps.io/CurtailedRRT/)
  - Implements sequential hypothesis testing within a curtailed sampling plan using randomized response techniques.
  - *Reference:* Reiber, F., Schnuerch, M., & Ulrich, R. (2020). Improving the efficiency of surveys with randomized response models: A sequential approach based on curtailed sampling. *Psychological Methods*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000353)
- **gpt** [R Package](https://github.com/danheck/gpt)
  - Generalized processing tree models: Jointly modeling discrete and continuous variables.
  - *Reference:* Heck, D. W., Erdfelder, E., & Kieslich, P. J. (2018). Generalized processing tree models: Jointly modeling discrete and continuous variables. *Psychometrika*, 83, 893–918. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11336-018-9622-0)
- **MCMCprecision** [R Package](https://github.com/danheck/MCMCprecision)
  - Quantifying uncertainty in transdimensional Markov chain Monte Carlo using discrete Markov models.
  - *Reference:* Heck, D. W., Overstall, A., Gronau, Q. F., & Wagenmakers, E. (2019). Quantifying uncertainty in transdimensional Markov chain Monte Carlo using discrete Markov models. *Statistics & Computing*, 29, 631–643. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11222-018-9828-0)
- **multinomineq** [R Package](https://github.com/danheck/multinomineq/)
  - Multinomial models with linear inequality constraints: Overview and improvements of computational methods for Bayesian inference.
  - *Reference:* Heck, D. W., & Davis-Stober, C. P. (2019). Multinomial models with linear inequality constraints: Overview and improvements of computational methods for Bayesian inference. *Journal of Mathematical Psychology*, 91, 70–87. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jmp.2019.03.004)
- **rtmpt** [R Package](https://github.com/RaphaelHartmann/rtmpt)
  - An R package for fitting response-time extended multinomial processing tree models.
  - *Reference:* Hartmann, R., Johannsen, L., & Klauer, K.C. (2020). rtmpt: An R package for fitting response-time extended multinomial processing tree models. *Behavior Research Methods*, 52, 1313–1338. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3758/s13428-019-01318-x)
- **SPRT t-tests** [R Script](https://osf.io/wz8da/)
  - Implements Abraham Wald's Sequential Probability Ratio Test.
  - *Reference:* Schnuerch, M., & Erdfelder, E. (2020). Controlling decision errors with minimal costs: The sequential probability ratio t-test. *Psychological Methods*, 25(2), 206–226. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1037/met0000234)

### Packages without Journal Publications

These packages and apps are available for use but are not tied to a specific published paper:

- **IPV** [R Package](https://github.com/NilsPetras/IPV)
  - Generates plots based on the item pool visualization concept for latent constructs.
  - *Contributors:* Nils Petras & M. Dantlgraber
- **Waldian t tests** [R Shiny App](https://martinschnuerch.shinyapps.io/Waldian-t-Tests/)
  - Implements sequential Bayesian t tests with predefined thresholds to control error probabilities.
  - *Contributor:* Martin Schnuerch
- **WienR** [R Package](https://github.com/RaphaelHartmann/WienR)
  - Calculates the partial derivatives of the first-passage time PDF and CDF with respect to all parameters of the Wiener diffusion model.
  - *Contributors:* Raphael Hartmann & Karl Christoph Klauer

### Hack-a-thon: Building an Open Science Knowledge Base

At the Society for the Improvement of Psychological Science (SIPS) Conference 2019, Rotterdam, the Netherlands, Luisa Horsten co-led the hack-a-thon “Building an Open Science Knowledge Base” ([https://osf.io/xtqjy/](https://osf.io/xtqjy/)).

- **Presentation:** [https://how-to-open.science/](https://how-to-open.science/)
- **Outcome:** The core team established an open educational resources hub: [https://www.oercommons.org/hubs/oskb](https://www.oercommons.org/hubs/oskb)
