# Faculty and Research Infrastructure of the School of Social Sciences

The School of Social Sciences (Sowi) at the University of Mannheim is supported by a robust academic structure and extensive network of research partners.

### Academic Structure

- **Staffing:** The school comprises approximately 40 professors and 120 academic staff members who conduct research and teaching across the three departments of the School of Social Sciences ([Sowi Departments](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/about/professors/)).
- **Social Data Science Track (CDSS):** This newly established track unites faculty expertise from the departments of Political Science and Sociology, alongside the School of Business Informatics and Mathematics. Participating faculty are listed [here](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdss/cdss-programs/social-data-science/social-data-science-supervisors/).
- **Networking:** Sowi benefits from a well-established network of [partner universities and research institutions](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/international/) globally, and maintains close collaborations with research centers within Mannheim.

## Key Research Centers and Partners

The University of Mannheim collaborates with several leading national and international research institutes:

### University-Affiliated Research Centers

- **Mannheim Center for European Social Research (MZES)**
  - MZES is an interdisciplinary institute of the University of Mannheim and is recognized as the largest university-based research institute in the German social sciences.

### Major Research Partners

- **GESIS (Leibniz Institute for the Social Sciences)**
  - GESIS is part of the [Leibniz Association](http://www.leibniz-gemeinschaft.de/en/about-us/). As the largest European infrastructure institute for the social sciences, it provides essential and internationally relevant research-based services.
- **Central Institute for Mental Health (ZI)**
  - ZI is a major independent center dedicated to the treatment, education, and research concerning mental illnesses.
- **Leibniz Centre for European Economic Research (ZEW)**
  - ZEW is a non-profit and independent institute, also part of the [Leibniz Association](https://www.leibniz-gemeinschaft.de/en.html). It is one of Germany's leading economic research institutes with a strong European reputation.
