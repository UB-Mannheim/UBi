## 📄 Seminar Abstract

The transition from conventional cars to electric vehicles (EVs) necessitates a robust public charging infrastructure. This paper investigates the role of direct public investment, particularly by municipality-owned utilities, in developing this charging grid.

**Key Contributions:**

1. **Economic Model:** The speaker presents a two-sided model of EV adoption and charging station investment. While public investment can initially "crowd out" private investment in the short run, the model suggests it may spur accelerated and larger private investor entry in the long run.
1. **Empirical Testing:** The research utilizes a novel panel data set of municipality-level charging markets in Germany, including charging stations and EV registrations. To address identification challenges, the analysis leverages the variation in public ownership of charging stations across German municipalities and employs matching and synthetic control methods to correct for selection biases.

**Key Findings:**

- Municipalities with publicly-owned local investors tend to have a higher number of installed charging stations.
- The study further investigates whether public investment effectively incentivizes private investment or, conversely, discourages it, finding initial evidence of the latter.

## 👩‍🏫 Speaker Biography

**Marie-Louise Arlt** is an Assistant Professor at the [University of Bayreuth](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/mises/forschung/decarbonization-seminar-fall-2024/marie-louise-arlt/). She is also affiliated with the [Bavarian Center for Battery Technology (BayBatt)](https://www.baybatt.uni-bayreuth.de/en/index.html).

**Research Focus:**
Her research centers on new technologies within the energy and mobility sectors. She focuses on how economic tools and institutional frameworks can be leveraged to ensure the optimal integration and deployment of these technologies, thereby mitigating climate change. Her work operates at the intersection of economics, information systems, and engineering.

**Academic Background:**

- **Previous Roles:** She previously served as a Junior Research Group Leader at the [ifo Institute](https://www.ifo.de/en) and as a Postdoctoral fellow at the Chair for [Comparative Economics](https://www.en.compecon.econ.uni-muenchen.de/index.html) at Ludwig Maximilian University in Munich.
- **Education:** She graduated from Freiburg University in October 2020. During her PhD studies, she conducted research at and with [Stanford University](https://profiles.stanford.edu/ram-rajagopal), [SLAC](https://gismo.slac.stanford.edu/), and [Lawrence Berkeley Lab](https://gridintegration.lbl.gov/about-us).
