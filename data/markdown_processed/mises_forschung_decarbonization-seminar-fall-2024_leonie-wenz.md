### 📝 Seminar Abstract

Migration is one of the most consequential disruptions to human society anticipated under climate change. While empirical evidence of climate-induced migration is often focused on individual regions, making global assessments challenging, this seminar provides a quantification in a causal framework. By leveraging rich data on sub-national migration and detailed analysis of climate exposure, the talk demonstrates that both warming and exposure to extremes induce outward migration from poorer regions globally. Explicit cross-border flows from poor to rich regions are identifiable. With climate change amplifying these effects, the research projects 210 million climate migrants from low-income regions by mid-century and more than 4 billion migrants in 2100 under a high-emission scenario.

### 👩‍🔬 Speaker Biography: Dr. Leonie Wenz

Dr. Leonie Wenz is the deputy head of Complexity Science and a research group leader for Data-based analysis of climate decisions at the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK).

**Research Focus:**
Leonie conducts research on the societal and economic impacts of climate change using various quantitative methods. Her work specifically studies how individuals and societies respond to weather extremes and a changing climatic environment, aiming to derive robust estimates of the cost of warming and inform sustainable development pathways.

**Academic Background & Impact:**

- **Education:** Trained as a mathematician, she earned her Ph.D. from the University of Potsdam and PIK (awarded *summa cum laude* and the i.a. Leibniz dissertation award) with a dissertation at the intersection of Climate Physics and Economics.
- **Postdoc:** Completed a Postdoc at UC Berkeley as part of the Ciriacy-Wantrup programme in Natural Resource Economics and Public Policy.
- **Recognition:** She is an elected Member of the German Young Academy (Junge Akademie) at the Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Science and the National Academy of Sciences Leopoldina.
- **Publications:** Her research has been published in leading journals, including *Nature*, *Science*, *PNAS*, *Journal of Environmental Economics and Management*, and *Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control*.
- **Policy Influence:** Her findings have informed major governmental bodies, including the German parliament, the European Central Bank, and the US White House.
