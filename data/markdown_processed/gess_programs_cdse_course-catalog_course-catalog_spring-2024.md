## 🔬 Advanced Econometrics and Theory

### General Equilibrium Theory (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Advanced introduction to the theory of general equilibrium. Topics include Aggregate Demand, Production Theory, Exchange Economies, Uncertainty and Arrow-Debreu Equilibrium, and Basic Asset Pricing.
- **Skills Acquired:** Understanding core concepts like Walrasian equilibrium, market incompleteness, and the role of the price mechanism in various economic contexts.
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703.
- **Contact:** Prof. Dr. Ernst-Ludwig von Thadden.

### Dynamic Macroeconomic Modeling (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Covers basic methods for dynamic economic modeling under rational expectations. Topics include Linear Rational Expectations (RE) Models, state space representation (VARs), Linear Quadratic (LQ) Dynamic Programming, and the New Keynesian Model.
- **Skills Acquired:** Applying basic tools for business cycle analysis and following recent literature on dynamic stochastic general equilibrium models.
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703.
- **TA:** Bjarne Horst.

### Semi- and Nonparametric Estimation (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Introduction to semi- and nonparametric estimation methods in microeconometrics, including bootstrap theory and treatment effect evaluation.
- **Skills Acquired:** Advanced theoretical knowledge in econometrics, enabling independent research in specialized topics.
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803.
- **Contact:** Prof. Yoshiyasu Rai.

### Private Information and Mechanism Design (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Focuses on the strategic use of private information in various environments (markets, contracts, negotiations). Topics include Adverse Selection, Signaling, Screening, Moral Hazard, and Mechanism Design.
- **Skills Acquired:** Understanding the fundamental tension between private information and achieving social goals, and applying this knowledge to regulatory interventions.
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **TAs:** Julian Klix, Emre Oral.

### Macroeconomics and Heterogeneity (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Discusses dynamic decision making and heterogeneity in macroeconomics. Topics include Consumption/savings (complete/incomplete markets, OLG), Labor markets (frictional labor markets, wage dispersion), and policy implications.
- **Skills Acquired:** Deep understanding of mathematical methods in modern macroeconomics, connecting theory and data, and preparing for independent Ph.D. research.
- **Recommended Literature:** *Recursive Methods in Economic Dynamics* (Stokey, Lucas, Prescott).
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803.

### Econometrics and Time Series Analysis (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Covers basic methods for analyzing panel data and time series. Focuses on stationary time series set-ups.
- **Skills Acquired:** Proficiency in advanced econometric techniques for time series and panel data analysis.
- **Recommended Literature:** *Econometrics* (Hayashi, 2000); *Applied Time Series Econometrics* (Lütkepohl & Krätzig, 2004).
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703.

### Causal Inference and Applied Microeconometrics (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Focuses on estimating causal effects in applied microeconomic research. Discusses methods like random assignment, propensity score matching, instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, synthetic controls, and regression discontinuity designs.
- **Skills Acquired:** Ability to apply and evaluate causal inference methods in own research.
- **Prerequisites:** All first year CDSE or equivalent courses.

### Experimental Economics (Summer 2024)

- **Content:** Overview of experimental economics topics, including Measurement Theory, Preference Elicitations, Communication, Survey Methods, Repeated Games, and Choice Process Data.
- **Skills Acquired:** Designing "good" experiments with clearly spelled out goals and methods.
- **Prerequisites:** All first year CDSE or equivalent courses.

## 🏛️ Public Policy and Industrial Organization

### Public Economics II (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Second part of a two-semester sequence. Covers modern Public Economics, including behavioral responses to taxation and social insurance, and Public Economics & Development. Focuses on empirical work and frontier research.
- **Skills Acquired:** Critically evaluating current research in Public Economics and formulating independent research questions.
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803, E805, E806, or equivalent.

### Industrial Organization (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Studies strategic interactions of firms in markets. Topics include market power, market structure, and econometric analysis of competition policy. Covers Cournot and Bertrand competition, and empirical models of entry/exit.
- **Skills Acquired:** Developing and understanding econometric analysis of market power and competition.
- **Prerequisites:** Formal: 2nd year PhD students from CDSE.

### Economic Policy and Growth (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Discusses current research analyzing the effect of economic policy on growth and inequality using modern macroeconomics tools.
- **Skills Acquired:** Deeper understanding of the effect of economic policy on growth and inequality.
- **Prerequisites:** E700, E701, E702, E703, E801, E802, E803, E805, E806, or equivalent.

### Climate and Sustainable Finance (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Introduces graduate students to questions and methods in climate/sustainable finance, connecting global researchers.
- **Skills Acquired:** Understanding the role of private information in strategic environments related to climate change.
- **Course Requirement:** Students write a paper on a potential research topic.

## 💻 Research Skills and Methodology

### Empirical Development Economics (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Research seminar focusing on empirical development economics with microeconometric methods.
- **Skills Acquired:** Identifying and contextualizing research questions, and presenting current research stage to peers.
- **Prerequisites:** First and second year PhD courses.

### International Economics Research Seminar (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Research seminar for students writing theses in international economics.
- **Skills Acquired:** Developing and structuring research projects in international economics, and improving academic writing and presentation skills.
- **Prerequisites:** E700-E703, E801-E806.

### Environmental Economics (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Seminar where internal and external speakers discuss recent research in environmental economics.
- **Skills Acquired:** Presentation skills, participation in scientific discourse, and absorption of recent research in environmental economics.
- **Prerequisites:** None.

### Economic History and Methods (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Reading group for Ph.D. candidates and advanced master students. Discusses recent research in economic history, demography, and labor economics, including advanced methods (e.g., machine learning applications).
- **Skills Acquired:** Identifying and discussing new open questions for future research in economic history.
- **Contact:** Prof. Philipp Ager, Prof. Dr. Jochen Streb.

### Computational and Software Engineering (Summer 2024)

- **Content:** Structured learning covering shell, Git, Python, Data Management, and Software engineering principles for reproducible research.
- **Skills Acquired:** Making research projects reproducible, solving complex economic problems using a computer, and writing efficient code.
- **Lecturers:** Hans-Martin von Gaudecker, Tim Mensinger.

### Causal Machine Learning (Summer 2024)

- **Content:** Combines machine learning prediction methods with causal research designs (IV, DiD, RDD) to achieve reliable causal inference.
- **Skills Acquired:** Basic knowledge of popular machine/statistical learning methods and the ability to combine them for robust causal inference.

## 🧠 Doctoral Training and Professional Development

### Dissertation Research Seminars (Multiple Semesters)

A series of dedicated seminars guide Ph.D. students through their dissertation process in various fields (Empirical IO, Public Economics, etc.).

- **Goal:** To provide structured feedback, help students refine research questions, and improve presentation/writing skills.
- **Format:** Student presentations and discussion of relevant literature.
- **Prerequisites:** Varies (typically 2nd year PhD students).

### Mental Health in Academia (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Guided discussions on typical risk factors and challenging constellations doctoral students face during their dissertations.
- **Goal:** To foster knowledge about mental health during academic projects and provide a space for peer discussion.
- **Lecturer:** Dr. Matthias Volz.

### Philosophy and Politics in Economics (Spring 2024)

- **Content:** Discussion of big questions at the intersection of economics and politics (e.g., role of morals, implications of digitalization).
- **Goal:** To develop a personal point of view on central economic and political questions.
- **Assessment:** Final essay and class participation.
