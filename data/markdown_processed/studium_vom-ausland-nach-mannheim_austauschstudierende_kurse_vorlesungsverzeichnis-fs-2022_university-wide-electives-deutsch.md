# University Wide Electives Course Catalog (Spring 2022)

This catalog provides an overview of elective courses available to exchange students from various departments.

## 📚 Humanities and Social Sciences

### German Private Law (German)

This course offers an introduction to German private law, specifically tailored for foreign law students. The curriculum begins with an overview of the legal sources of German law, focusing on the historical development and special position of the Civil Code (BGB). Key areas covered include:

- Tort law
- Law of obligations
- Property law
- Short introductions to inheritance and family law, company law, and civil procedural law.

**Structure:**

- Definition of private law and historical development
- Structure of the BGB
- Tort law
- Law of obligations
- Property law
- Inheritance and Family Law, Company Law, and Civil Procedure Law at a Glance

**Goal:** To ensure students are familiar with the structures of German civil law and capable of handling smaller legal cases.

### Politics of Memory: Evolution of Ukrainian History Education

This interdisciplinary course provides a comprehensive understanding of how memory politics shape historical understanding. It delves into the evolution of history education in Ukraine since its independence in 1991.
**Key Learning Objectives:**

- Understanding the profound impact of memory politics on historical narratives.
- Gaining insights into Ukraine's historical transitions.
- Investigating the role of educational policies in shaping history education.
- Exploring the cultural dimensions influencing the portrayal of historical events.
  Students will engage in discussions on current debates and controversies, developing essential research skills through in-depth analysis.

## 💻 Computer Science and Information Systems

### Business Process Modelling and Integrated Information Systems

This course focuses on modeling complex business processes using popular techniques and understanding the requirements and effects of integrated information systems in industrial companies.
**Topics Covered:**

- Modeling complex business processes.
- Requirements, characteristics, and effects of integrated information systems.
- Completing basic tasks across various functional areas within a wide-spread integrated information system.

**Functional Areas:**

- Business Process Modelling
- Application Systems in Research and Development
- Marketing and Sales
- Procurement and Warehousing
- Production
- Shipping and Customer Service
- Finance, Accounting, HR
- Planning and Control Systems

### Human Resource Management (HR)

This module covers the entire human resource environment, providing skills for managing the employee lifecycle.
**Topics Covered:**

- Job Analysis & Job Design
- HR Planning & Recruiting
- Selection and Training
- Performance Management and Employee Development
- Separation & Retention
- Compensation and Incentives (including International HRM)
- High-Performance Organizations

### Algorithms and Programming (German)

This course provides a deep dive into theoretical and practical computer science concepts.
**Topics Covered:**

- **Algorithm Design:** Step-by-step refinement, modularity, object-oriented programming (class hierarchies, inheritance), and recursion.
- **Programming:** The object-oriented language Java.
- **Data Structures:** Linked lists, binary trees, and B-trees.
- **Modeling:** Using UML (Class Diagrams, Activity Diagrams, State Diagrams).
- **Theory:** Introduction to algorithm theory, including computability, complexity (O-calculus), and testing/verification of algorithms and programs.

### Discrete Mathematics and Logic (German)

This course covers foundational mathematical concepts essential for computer science.
**Topics Covered:**

- Foundations of propositional logic (deduction, proof).
- Sets, relations, and mappings.
- Fundamentals of combinatorics (counting finite sets, countability).
- Introduction to graph theory.
- Algebraic structures (semigroups, groups, homomorphisms, factor structures).
- Basic computational models/Finite Automata.

## 🗣️ Linguistics

### Synchronic Linguistics

This course provides a foundational understanding of synchronic linguistics and practical experience in linguistic analysis.
**Learning Outcomes:**

- Know the basics of synchronic linguistics.
- Practice linguistic analyses in the areas of sounds, words, and sentences.
- Identify areas of interest within linguistics.
- Learn to work with English textbooks.
- Develop skills in accumulating and aggregating information from diverse sources.

## 🎓 Academic Resources and Administration

### International Criminal Law Bibliography

A recommended reading list for advanced study in International Criminal Law:

- Cassese, Antonio, *International Criminal Law* (Oxford, Oxford University Press, 2008)
- Cryer, Robert, *An Introduction to International Criminal Law and Procedure* (Cambridge, Cambridge University Press, 2010)
- Schabas, William A., *The International Criminal Court: A Commentary on the Rome Statute* (Oxford, Oxford University Press, 2010)
- Werle, Gerhard, *Principles of International Criminal Law* (The Hague, Asser, 2005)

### Registration and Prerequisites

- **Registration:** Exchange students from all departments are welcome to register for these courses using **Portal2**. It is recommended to register before the lecture period starts to ensure access to reading materials.
- **Prerequisites:**
  - Some courses are designated for 1st-year students (No prerequisites).
  - Some courses are designated for 2nd-year students (Basic knowledge in Political Science is helpful).
  - Students should check the specific course requirements.
- **Optional:** Optional tutorials are also available and require separate registration via Portal2.
