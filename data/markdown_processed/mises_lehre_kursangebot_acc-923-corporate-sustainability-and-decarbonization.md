# ACC 923 Corporate Sustainability and Decarbonization

This seminar is designed for doctoral students at GESS, providing an in-depth look at the challenges and advances in corporate sustainability and the transition toward net-zero carbon emissions.

## Course Overview

The seminar hosts speakers from both academia and industry to discuss the latest developments in sustainable business practices. Topics covered include:

- The economics and management of sustainability activities.
- Emission abatement strategies across all sectors of the economy.

## Learning Objectives and Structure

**Primary Objective:**
The course aims to introduce students to current research paradigms in the field and help them identify promising avenues for future research.

**Skills Training:**
Students receive practical training on how to effectively present and evaluate academic papers in seminar and conference settings.

**Course Format:**
Participants are required to attend both seminar talks and internal sessions. During internal sessions, students are expected to:

- Present a paper.
- Take the role of a discussant.
- Readings may additionally include recent theoretical or empirical papers.
