# GESS Doctoral Program Structure

The teaching and training concept of the GESS is designed to guide doctoral students from active learners toward becoming independent researchers. The program is structured into three distinct phases: Course, Writing, and Placement.

## 🎓 Course Phase

In the initial course phase, graduate students complete up to two years of coursework. This phase is complemented by small group exercises, tutorials, and dedicated research training, culminating in examinations.

### Core Course Program

The curriculum includes mandatory core courses across all three doctoral centers. These courses cover essential topics such as research design, statistical modeling, and methods of causal inference.

### Field Course Program

Specialized field courses introduce students to the practical application of methodology within their chosen research area. The interdisciplinary nature of the Graduate School is emphasized throughout both stages of the course phase.

*Progression:* Advancement to the next phase is determined by the Admissions and Assessments Committee, based on performance in the examinations and the quality of the first- or second-year paper.

## ✍️ Writing Phase

During the thesis writing phase, doctoral students work intensively on their research projects under the close supervision of dedicated faculty members. This stage focuses on transitioning from guided research execution to the independent development of original research ideas.

Activities in this phase include:

- Writing research papers.
- Presenting work in seminars and colloquia.
- Submitting papers to international conferences and journals.

Students regularly present their research progress to supervisors and fellow students in area-specific and interdisciplinary doctoral seminars. The program encourages participation in international conferences and summer schools, helping students integrate into the global research network.

## 💼 Placement Phase

The final placement phase provides graduate students with comprehensive information and training for the academic and non-academic job market.

Each doctoral center appoints dedicated ‘placement officers’ who assist students in preparing their job market packages. Practical preparation includes:

- Mock interviews with faculty members.
- Practice job talks in the presence of faculty.

This preparation provides useful feedback for a successful job market experience. Recent placements of GESS graduates include positions as assistant professors and post-doctoral fellows in top departments, as well as roles in international organizations, public institutions, and the private sector.
