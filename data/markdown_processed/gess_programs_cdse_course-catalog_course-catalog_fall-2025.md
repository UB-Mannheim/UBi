### Specialized Electives

- **Elective courses from the CDSS:** Focuses on human rights research, aiming to:
  - Provide systematic knowledge of key debates on human rights violations and state repression.
  - Enable critical assessment of academic research (theoretical and empirical).
  - Familiarize students with key datasets in human rights research.
  - Foster constructive discussions and improve writing/presentation skills.
- **Elective courses from the CDSB:**
  - **European Union Tax Law:** Covers the increasing impact of EU law on taxation (both private individuals and companies). Topics include:
    - Introduction to EU Tax Law and European institutional measures.
    - Positive harmonization of indirect taxes (European directives).
    - Secondary law harmonizing direct taxes (e.g., Parent-Subsidiary Directive).
    - Fundamental freedoms and the case law of the European Court of Justice.
  - **Data Extraction and Automation:** Focuses on practical data skills:
    - Identifying requirements for effective data extraction.
    - Automating data collection from websites or APIs.
    - Parsing and cleaning data.
    - Navigating ethical and legal considerations.

## 🎓 Bridge Courses and Skills Training

Bridge courses are designed to provide foundational skills necessary for advanced research.

### Quantitative Methods Training (General)

This tutorial accompanies the course “Quantitative Methods” in the M.A. program in Political Science. The lab sessions focus on practical issues associated with quantitative methods, including:

- Obtaining and preparing data sets.
- Using statistical software and selecting appropriate tests.
- Graphing data effectively for presentation and analysis.
- Interpreting results.
- *Note: No prior knowledge of statistical programming is expected.*

### Statistical Programming (R)

This training focuses on implementing causal inference methods in R.

- **Prerequisites:** Students should bring their own laptop for all practice sessions and ensure R and R Studio are installed beforehand.
- **Skills:** Learning to implement causal inference methods in R.

### Specialized Bridge Courses

- **CDSS (Psychology):** Focuses on advanced statistical methods, including:
  - Generalized linear models (Dunn & Smyth, 2018).
  - Longitudinal analysis (Hoffman, 2015).
  - Statistical learning (James et al., 2013).
  - Structural equation modeling (Kline, 2023).
  - Multilevel analysis (Snijders & Bosker, 2012).
- **CDSS (Sociology):** Includes training in causal inference methods using R.
- **CDSB (Accounting), (Information Systems), (Management), (Operations Management):** Offer specialized bridge courses tailored to these respective fields.

## 📝 Dissertation and Research Skills

### Mandatory Research Courses

- **E800 CDSE Seminar:** A core seminar component.
- **SKL801 English Academic Writing:** Focuses on academic writing skills.

### Research Paper Requirements

The final research paper must adhere to the following structure and guidelines:

- **Length:** Approximately 5,000 words (±500), including references but excluding appendices.
- **Content Requirements:**
  1. **Research Question:** A clearly defined and original question.
  1. **Literature Review:** A critical review demonstrating the relevance of the research question and existing scholarship.
  1. **Argument:** A well-reasoned and evidence-based thesis.
  1. **Empirical Evidence:** Empirical examples that substantiate the argument.
- **Deadline:** January 15, 2026.

### Interdisciplinary Leadership Course (Gender Gap Analysis)

This course takes an interdisciplinary approach to examine the gender gap in leadership positions, with a special emphasis on the academic environment.

**Structure (Four Core Building Blocks):**

1. **Women in Leadership: The Economic Perspective:**
   - Examines economic reasons for gender gaps, analyzing statistics on women in leadership (especially academia).
   - Discusses time trends, gender quotas, and family-friendly policies.
   - Examines the impact on labor market choices and the gender pay gap.
1. **Women in Leadership: The Psychological Perspective:**
   - Addresses gender stereotypes (descriptive and prescriptive) as barriers to advancement.
   - Covers topics like the "Think Manager – Think Male" phenomenon, Glass metaphors (ceiling, escalator, cliff), Queen Bee effects, and backlash effects.
1. **“Raise Your Voice” – Rhetoric Training:**
   - A hands-on training on presenting research findings in a conference-like scenario.
   - Students receive individual feedback on presentation skills.
   - *Preparation:* Students must prepare a short presentation (5–7 minutes) on "What excites me about my research topic?" and bring a video-capable device.
1. **“Raise Your Pay” – Negotiation Training:**
   - Teaches negotiation skills, conflict resolution, and handling verbal attacks.
   - Students participate in a negotiation game and receive individual feedback.
   - *Prerequisite:* Students must submit one personal negotiation topic in advance.
