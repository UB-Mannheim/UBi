### Seminar Abstract

The seminar examines the current electricity pricing structure in California, noting that the price paid by most households is 2 to 3 times higher than the full societal cost of providing additional supply (including GHG emissions). This difference, described as an "electricity tax," averages $500-$800 annually per household.

These funds are used to cover various fixed costs and policy expenses, including:

- Climate change mitigation
- Wildfire adaptation and mitigation
- Legacy infrastructure
- Subsidies for new technology R&D, energy efficiency, low-income customers, and rooftop solar.

The presentation argues that high and rising prices undermine efforts to decarbonize transportation and buildings through electrification. Furthermore, the current rate structure is highly regressive. The discussion will focus on alternative, more efficient, and equitable ways to recover the costs of the electricity system, specifically exploring the creation of income-based monthly fixed charges on electricity bills. The talk also addresses the implications of the electricity tax for the private and social value of residential rooftop solar installations.

### Speaker Biography

Severin Borenstein is the E.T. Grether Professor of Business Administration and Public Policy at the Haas School of Business and serves as the faculty director of the Energy Institute at Haas. He is also the Director emeritus of the University of California Energy Institute (1994-2014).

**Education & Research:**

- Received his AB from UC Berkeley and PhD in Economics from MIT.
- His research focuses on business competition, strategy, and regulation, with extensive publications on the airline, oil, gasoline, and electricity markets.
- Current research projects include the economics of renewable energy, economic policies for reducing greenhouse gases, and alternative models of retail electricity pricing.
- He is a research associate of the National Bureau of Economic Research in Cambridge, MA.

**Advisory Experience:**
Borenstein has held numerous advisory roles, including:

- Board of Governors of the California Power Exchange (1997–2003).
- Member of the California Attorney General’s Gasoline Price Task Force (1999–2000).
- Member of U.S. Secretary of Transportation Ray LaHood’s Future of Aviation Advisory Committee (2010–11).
- Member of the Emissions Market Assessment Committee, advising the California Air Resources Board on Cap and Trade (2012–13).
- Chair of the California Energy Commission’s Petroleum Market Advisory Committee (2015–2017).
- Advisory Council of the Bay Area Air Quality Management District (since 2015).
- Governing Board of the California Independent System Operator (2019).
