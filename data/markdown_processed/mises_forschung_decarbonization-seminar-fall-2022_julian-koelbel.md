## Seminar Abstract

This seminar examines the novel phenomenon of sustainability-linked bonds (SLBs). These bonds link the issuer's coupon rate to the achievement of predetermined sustainability performance targets.

**Key Findings:**

- The analysis estimates the yield differential between SLBs and non-sustainable counterfactuals by matching bonds from the same issuer.
- Results indicate that investors generally pay for sustainability improvements, while issuers benefit from a sustainability premium.
- The sustainability premium is found to be larger for bonds with a higher coupon step-up and for callable bonds.
- The study highlights a potential 'free lunch' for some SLB issuers, where financial savings exceed potential penalties, especially when coupled with a call option.
- While SLBs suggest a lower cost of capital and incentivize sustainability, the findings caution that they can also be a form of greenwashing if issued purely for financial optimization without genuine commitment to sustainability improvements.

The corresponding academic paper can be found [here](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4007629).

## Speaker Biography

Dr. Julian Kölbel is an assistant professor of sustainable finance at the Center for Financial Services Innovation at the University of St. Gallen.

**Research Focus:**
His research covers the real-world impact of sustainable investing, the analysis of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) metrics, and investor preferences for sustainability.

**Experience:**

- **Academic Background:** Studied at ETH Zurich and the University of Oxford.
- **Post-Doctoral Work:** Worked as a Post-Doc at MIT Sloan, where he is a co-founder of the Aggregate Confusion Project.
- **Professional Roles:** Has worked at Bank Vontobel and RepRisk AG. He is currently a member of the investment committee at the Swiss pension fund Abendrot.
