# Accounting Analytics Research Projects

This section details current research projects focusing on accounting analytics, tax law, and financial transparency, conducted by the University of Mannheim.

## German Business Panel (GBP)

**Authors:**
[Jannis Bischof](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bischof/), [Dirk Simons](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/simons/), [Johannes Voget](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/voget/), Davud Rostam-Afschar, [Philipp Dörrenberg](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/doerrenberg/team/prof-dr-philipp-doerrenberg/)

**Cooperation Partners:**
DFG (German Research Foundation), Paderborn University, HU Berlin, LMU Munich, Frankfurt School of Finance and Management, WHU – Otto Beisheim School of Management, European School of Management and Technology Berlin, Goethe University Frankfurt, Bundesverband der Deutschen Industrie, Institut für Mittelstand, Zentralverband des Deutschen Handwerks.

**Description:**
The German Business Panel (GBP) is a representative panel of managers responsible for accounting and tax matters in German firms. These representatives are surveyed semi-annually on topics covering financial accounting, managerial accounting, and taxation. The panel encourages both firms and researchers to submit survey questions regarding firm behavior. Submissions are voted upon by firms, and a scientific committee selects the most suitable questions for inclusion. The panel regularly provides indicators for tax sentiments and other relevant issues.

**Website:** [https://gbpanel.org/](https://gbpanel.org/)

## Digitalization of the Tax Function

**Authors:**
[Christoph Spengel](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/spengel/), Alexander Maedche, Sven Scheu, Sarah Winter

**Cooperation Partners:**
Institute of Information Systems and Marketing (IISM) at KIT, EnBW AG

**Description:**
Due to new regulatory requirements, tax departments must provide increasing amounts of information to tax authorities. This project addresses the need to process structured and unstructured tax data in a timely manner and improve decision-making processes. In collaboration with a major German energy supplier, the research focuses on developing new concepts for the digital transformation of the tax function. The project aims to optimize tax department processes through digital means, enhance existing tax information systems, and improve intragroup information processing. Furthermore, it focuses on developing data-driven intelligent assistant functions using Process Mining, Business Intelligence, and general Data Science techniques.

## Towards Financial Transparency: CbCR Directive Examination

**Authors:**
[Hannah Gundert](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/our-students/2022/hannah-gundert/), [Christoph Spengel](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/spengel/), [Stefan Weck](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/spengel/)

**Cooperation Partners:**
Chair of Business Administration and Taxation II, Taxation and Finance

**Description:**
The EU Directive 2021/2101 introduced a public Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR) regime to promote a level playing field for businesses within the EU Single Market. This directive aims to bolster tax transparency requirements for multinational enterprises (MNEs) to reduce disparities in international tax planning potential compared to smaller, domestic firms. This study examines whether the public CbCR Directive introduces unintended disparities between (1) MNEs domiciled in different EU member states and (2) MNEs domiciled within and outside the EU. The findings suggest that the current form and implementation of the directive likely introduce unintended disparities, contrary to its goal of establishing a level playing field, and recommend stronger guidance and fewer transposition options.
