# ACC 715: Seminar on Corporate Carbon Accounting

This seminar provides an in-depth examination of the challenges and opportunities companies face on their journey toward achieving net-zero carbon emissions. The core focus is on the accounting of corporate carbon emissions and the performance and risk measurement of competing abatement strategies.

## Key Topics Covered

The seminar covers a comprehensive range of topics essential for understanding the transition to a low-carbon economy, including:

- Corporate Carbon Accounting and Reporting
- Corporate Decarbonization Pledges and Pathways
- Carbon Pricing and Regulation: Theory and Implementation
- Competitiveness of Clean Energy Technologies
- Climate Risk in Finance and Investment

## Course Details

**Learning Outcomes**
Participants will gain a deep understanding of the main challenges and opportunities associated with transitioning towards a decarbonized energy economy. The course covers both theoretical economic concepts central to this transition and recent practical developments and tools. Furthermore, participants will improve their skills in writing a coherent, state-of-the-art seminar paper.

**Assessment and Credits**

- **Credits:** 6 ECTS
- **Form of Assessment:** Written seminar thesis (70%) and in-class presentation (30%)
