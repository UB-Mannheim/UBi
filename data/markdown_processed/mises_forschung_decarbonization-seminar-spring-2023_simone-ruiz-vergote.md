### 📝 Seminar Abstract

Historically, corporate disclosures regarding carbon emissions and decarbonization targets were primarily driven by shareholders rather than regulators. While data availability has improved, it remains sparse, particularly concerning Scope 3 (value-chain) emissions.

**Key Challenges & Focus:**

- **Comparability:** The lack of comparable data poses a significant challenge for investors trying to identify companies best positioned to outperform in a decarbonizing economy.
- **Regulatory Shift:** This situation is changing as global regulators and standard setters mandate how corporations must disclose their exposure to and impact on climate change.
- **Forward-Looking Data:** A critical element of these emerging standards involves forward-looking data, such as climate targets and scenario analysis.
- **MSCI's Role:** As a leading ESG & Climate data provider, MSCI serves global investors with relevant data to measure their portfolio’s financial carbon footprint and future exposure to climate risks.
