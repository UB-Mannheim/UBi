# SMiP Summer School: Advanced Statistical Modeling in Psychology

The SMiP Summer School is an intensive academic program focused on advanced statistical models in psychology. It is organized by the DFG funded Research Training Group on [Statistical Modeling in Psychology (SMiP)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/) in collaboration with the [Interuniversity Graduate School of Psychometrics and Sociometrics (IOPS)](https://www.iops.nl/).

## Program Overview and Dates

The Summer School has been held at the University of Mannheim, Germany, in recent years:

- **2025:** Took place from June 30 to July 4, 2025.
- **2023:** Took place from June 26 to June 30, 2023. (The archived website for 2023 is available [here](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip-summerschool/archive-2023/)).

## Content and Structure

The program provides an in-depth introduction to quantitative methods and advanced statistical modeling. Key components of the Summer School include:

- **Workshops:** Parallel workshops covering various topics in quantitative methods and statistical modeling ([details](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip-summerschool/workshops/)).
- **Keynote Talks:** Plenary sessions featuring expert talks ([details](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip-summerschool/keynotes/)).
- **Poster Session:** An opportunity for participants to present their research alongside senior researchers.
- **Special Topics:** Dedicated events, such as one addressing the [compatibility of work and private life](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip-summerschool/program/compatibility-of-work-and-private-life/).
- **Social Events:** Networking opportunities for participants.

## Participation Requirements

The Summer School is designed for young researchers specializing in psychology with a strong interest in statistical modeling and quantitative methods.

**Target Audience:**
The program is suitable for:

- Doctoral researchers in the SMiP and IOPS programs.
- Doctoral and early-stage researchers from other programs and institutions.

**Prerequisites:**
Participants should possess:

- Advanced knowledge of quantitative methods and multivariate statistics.
- Solid skills in data analysis.
- General experience with the R programming language.

*Note: The official language of the Summer School is English, so German language mastery is not a prerequisite.*
