# University Wide Electives Course Catalog (Fall 2021)

This catalog provides an overview of elective courses available to exchange students, covering diverse fields from law and linguistics to social sciences and modeling.

## 🏛️ Law and Legal Studies

### German Private Law (Introduction)

This course provides an introduction to German private law, particularly for foreign law students.

- **Content:** Begins with an overview of the legal sources of German law, focusing on the special position and historical development of the Civil Code (BGB).
- **Focus Areas:** Systematic descriptions and case solutions in key areas:
  - Tort Law
  - Law of Obligations
  - Property Law
  - Short introductions to Inheritance and Family Law, Company Law, and Civil Procedural Law.
- **Goal:** To ensure students are familiar with the structures of German civil law and capable of handling smaller legal cases.
- **Structure:** Definition and historical development of private law; Structure of the BGB; Tort law; Law of obligations; Property law; Inheritance and Family Law, Company Law, and Civil Procedure Law at a Glance.

### German Constitutional Law (Grundgesetz)

This course provides a general introduction to German Constitutional Law, covering the constitution, its background, contents, and methods for working with constitutional legal texts.

- **Background:** The German Basic Law (Grundgesetz), established after WWII, is the formal constitutional document of the Federal Republic of Germany. It is a reliable foundation for the democratic system and federal state.
- **Focus:** The provision of fundamental rights (Art. 1–19) places the Grundgesetz in the tradition of European and North American human rights thinking.
- **Content:**
  - The German legal system
  - Constitutional Law
  - The Grundgesetz and the European Union
  - Fundamental Rights
  - Fundamental Constitutional Principles
  - The Federal State
  - Constitutional organs
  - Legislative procedure
  - Public administration
  - The judicial system
- **Learning Target:** Students will understand the basic principles and most important provisions of the Grundgesetz, solve simple cases regarding fundamental rights, and be familiar with the structure and function of the federal state and its constitutional, administrative, and judicial organs.
- **Examination:** Oral Exam
- **Lecturer:** Katharina Longin
- **Literature:** No specific textbook is required. Additional reading recommendations will be provided.

## 🗣️ Linguistics

### University Wide Electives – Linguistics

This course offers a comprehensive introduction to linguistic studies.

- **Skills Acquired:**
  - Learning the basics of synchronic linguistics.
  - Familiarity with descriptive and analytic tools used in linguistics.
  - Practicing linguistic analyses in the areas of sounds, words, and sentences.
  - Developing the ability to accumulate and aggregate information from different sources.
  - Learning to work with English textbooks.
- **Goal:** To identify areas of interest within the field of linguistics.

## 🌍 Social Sciences and Sociology

### Social Structure of Germany and Europe

This course provides a systematic overview of the social structure of the Federal Republic of Germany and Europe in comparison to other European countries.

- **Focus:** Changes and trends in education, employment, population, household, and family structure, with special reference to income distribution and the development of prosperity.
- **Key Topics:**
  - Development of population, household, and family structures.
  - Social inequality and social mobility.
  - Education, employment, and profession.
  - Income distribution and prosperity development.
- **Literature:**
  - Hradil, S. (2004). *Die Sozialstruktur Deutschlands im internationalen Vergleich*.
  - Mau, S. und Verwiebe, R. (2009). *Die Sozialstruktur Europas*.
  - Statistisches Bundesamt (Hg.). *Datenreport 2006*. ### Migration and Integration Studies

This area covers research on migration and integration processes in Europe.

- **Key Research Areas/Topics:**
  - Migration and immigrants in Europe: historical and demographic perspectives (Van Mol et al., 2016).
  - Transnational migration studies: past developments and future trends (Levitt & Jaworsky, 2007).
  - Assimilation theories and challenges (Zhou, 1997; Alba, 2008).
  - Discrimination in housing and labor markets (Ahmed et al., 2010; Blank et al., 2004).
  - Intergenerational change in religious salience among immigrant families (Jacob & Kalter, 2013).
  - Ethnic and religious dynamics in Western Europe (Helbling, 2014; Maddox, 2019).

### Comparative Politics and European Integration

This area focuses on theoretical and methodological knowledge for the study of European integration and the European Union.

- **Required Knowledge:**
  - Basic literature in international relations and comparative politics.
  - Methodological knowledge of research design and statistics.
  - Understanding the state of research on main approaches to European integration and the EU.
- **Learning Goals:**
  - Ability to understand and select political science studies on European integration and the EU.
  - Scientific understanding of the functioning of integration steps and the political system of the European Union.
  - Applying skills and knowledge to related or future problems of European integration.

## 💻 Computational and Modeling Sciences

### Modeling Principles and Tools

This course focuses on the formal and practical aspects of modeling complex systems.

- **Core Concepts:**
  - Modeling principles and formal foundations of modeling languages (Logic, Petri Nets).
  - Understanding the basic principles and formal foundations for modeling application domains and processes.
- **Skills Developed:**
  - Describing domains and processes of simple and medium complexity using common languages and tools.
  - Understanding complex interrelationships, teamwork, and communicating modeling decisions.
- **Practical Languages:** UML, BPMN.
- **Examination:** Written Exam (90 minutes)

## 📚 General Academic Skills

### Research Methodology

- **Focus:** Developing advanced research skills necessary for academic study.
- **Key Areas:**
  - Understanding complex interrelationships and working in a team.
  - Communication of modeling decisions.
  - Applying theoretical and methodological knowledge to real-world problems.
