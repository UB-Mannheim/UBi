## Seminar Abstract

Decarbonizing the transport sector, one of the largest carbon emitters globally, is critical for achieving the Paris Agreement's climate goals. While battery electric vehicles are expected to dominate the passenger vehicle market, the efficient decarbonization of the road freight sector remains unclear.

The technological options include battery electric, green hydrogen, and synthetic fuel-powered vehicles. Currently, there is limited analysis on the competition between these "green" technologies, particularly how public policy can shape this competition and accelerate the sector's decarbonization.

The challenge of effective policy design is complex because most potential interventions occur locally, but their innovation effects can spill over to other jurisdictions. To address this, the seminar presents a system dynamic model to project technology market shares by region (including North America, China, and Europe) and application (from small urban to large long-haul trucks) over the next two decades.

**Key Findings:**

- Electric vehicles are projected to grow in most application segments.
- Electric vehicle growth can be substantially accelerated through policy intervention.
- Spillovers occur if strong policies are introduced in large regions like China and the European Union.
- While public interventions are essential for accelerating niche technology deployment, private interventions show minimal positive spillover effects.

## Speaker Biography

**Bjarne Steffen** is an Assistant Professor of Climate Finance and Policy and the head of CFP. His research, published in high-impact journals, focuses on policies related to the low-carbon transition in the energy and financial sectors. He works at the intersection of environmental/energy economics, financial economics, and innovation studies, covering both developed and developing countries.

**Research Focus Areas:**

- **Developed Countries:** Impact of public policy on technology pathways for energy storage, and incorporating technology- and country-specific cost of capital into energy system models.
- **Developing Countries:** Clean energy technology diffusion, and the role of multilateral development banks.
