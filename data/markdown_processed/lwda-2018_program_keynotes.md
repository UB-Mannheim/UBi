# Keynote Program Highlights

## Daniela Nicklas (Otto-Friedrich-Universität Bamberg)

### Who watches the watchmen? Online data quality in future IOT applications

**Abstract:** In many "Internet of Things" applications, sensor data plays a vital role for monitoring real-world phenomena. However, sensor data can have multiple data quality issues; to make things worse, these issues are context-dependent, i.e., they change over time. This keynote highlights some data management challenges that need to be addressed for future IoT infrastructures, with a focus on data quality in sensor-based applications. It illustrates these challenges with applications and research topics from the joint research programme "FutureIOT", funded by the Bavarian Research Foundation and introduces the "Living Lab Bamberg", an open platform for sensor-based research and long-running experiments.

**Video:** [Keynote 1](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/lwda-2018/videos/keynotes/#c90594)

## Kerstin Bach (NTNU Trondheim)

### Personalized Self-Management: A Case-Based Reasoning Application for Low Back Pain Patients

**Abstract:** This talk presents the selfBACK decision support system (DSS) project, which uses Case-Based Reasoning for supporting patients with low back pain. The DSS has been developed over the last two years and will now be tested in a pilot study and a multisite randomized controlled trial. The selfBACK DSS is designed for the patient to facilitate, improve, and reinforce self-management of LBP. Specifically, it assists the patient in deciding and reinforcing appropriate actions after consulting a health care professional. The decision support is delivered via a smartphone app, tailored to the patient's symptom state, symptom progression, goal-setting, and physical activity data from a wristband.

**Video:** [Keynote 2](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/lwda-2018/videos/keynotes/#c90598)

## Frank Giesler (Vice President, Capgemini Consulting)

### Digital Product Design: Praktische Erfahrungen aus der Entwicklung datengetriebener digitaler Produkte

**Abstract:** Machine Learning enables the development of innovative and novel digital products. The foundation for this is the integration of different data sources and their intelligent linking, combined with self-learning mechanisms. However, user acceptance is absolutely critical. Acceptance research shows that new and "voluntary" software must offer a 10-fold higher benefit than existing solutions to be adopted into daily use. Frank Giesler presents a proven approach that ensures successful data-driven digital product development through close involvement of future users.

**Video:** [Keynote 3](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/lwda-2018/videos/keynotes/#c90599)

## Stephan Mandt (Disney Research)

### Finding Hidden Structure in Data with Deep Probabilistic Models

**Abstract:** This overview covers recent developments in deep probabilistic modeling, which combines deep neural networks with probabilistic models for unsupervised learning. These models can synthesize artificial data highly resembling real training data, capable of fooling both machine learning classifiers and humans. They have numerous applications in creative tasks, such as voice, image, or video synthesis and manipulation. Furthermore, combining neural networks with strong priors results in flexible yet highly interpretable models for finding hidden structure in large data sets. The talk summarizes the group’s activities, including measuring semantic shifts of words over centuries, summarizing audience reactions to movies, and generating artificial video sequences with partial control.
