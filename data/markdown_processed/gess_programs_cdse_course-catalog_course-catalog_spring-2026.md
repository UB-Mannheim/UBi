### Elective Courses from the CDSS

These courses cover advanced topics in social science methodology and theory.

**UN Peacekeeping and the Protection of Civilians**
This course examines the problems and possibilities of United Nations (UN) peace operations. Peacekeeping mandates have evolved from simply monitoring ceasefires to complex tasks like protecting civilians, disarming ex-combatants, protecting human rights, and supporting free and fair elections. The vast majority of modern missions prioritize the Protection of Civilians (POC). This subject is relevant for those focusing on conflict or security studies, international organizations, global governance, or international relations.

**Comparative Political Behavior**
This lecture provides an introduction to theoretical approaches, key concepts, and substantive issues in comparative political behavior. It offers an overview of key theories in analyzing micro-level political processes and how they feed into macro-level societal and political institutions.

**Generative AI in Social Science Research**
Upon completion of the module, students will be able to:

- Present basic knowledge in Generative AI applied to social science research fields.
- Name the latest Generative AI developments in social science research.
- Describe in-depth knowledge of empirical approaches to Generative AI in the social science research fields covered.
- Critically evaluate the empirical literature and applications of Generative AI in the social science research fields covered.

### Elective Courses from the CDSB

These courses focus on specialized research methods and advanced topics in business and economics.

**Seminar on Decarbonized Energy Economy**
This seminar is aimed at doctoral students and hosts speakers from academia and industry to discuss advances and challenges in the transition towards a decarbonized energy economy. Topics include the economics and management of sustainability activities and clean energy technologies across all sectors (energy, transportation, manufacturing).

- **Learning Outcomes:** Introduction to current research paradigms and identification of promising future research avenues. Training in presenting and evaluating papers in seminars and conferences.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper presentations and discussions (80%).
- *Note: This course is also part of TRR 266 Accounting for Transparency.*

**Advanced Accounting and Organizational Research**
Meetings discuss recent advances in analytical accounting, tax, or organizational research. The focus is on academic rigor, topic relevance, and writing style to prepare students for publishing in top peer-reviewed journals.

- **Assessment:** Participation (25%), Paper moderation (25%), and written assignment (50%).
- **Responsible Teacher:** [Dr. Sebastian Kronenberger](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/simons/team/sebastian-kronenberger/)
- *Note: This course is also part of TRR 266 Accounting for Transparency.*

**Scientific Data-Science Projects (NLP Focus)**
Students gain practical experience conducting scientific data-science projects, from problem formulation to documentation. The focus is on analyzing textual data (e.g., news or social media datasets) using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.

- **Learning Outcomes:** Students will present results and write a paper about their research.
- **Assessment:** Written elaboration (90%) and presentation (10%).

**Quantitative Textual Analysis (Python)**
This course equips students with skills to analyze text data in business administration and economics.

- **Learning Outcomes:**
  - Implement state-of-the-art textual methods for analyzing text data.
  - Incorporate textual analysis methods to expand current knowledge.
  - Acquire solid programming knowledge in Python.
- **Prerequisites:** No prior Python knowledge is required, but experience is helpful. The course focuses on quantitative approaches.

**Causal Inference and Experimental Design (German Business Panel)**
This reading course provides a hands-on approach to understanding and analyzing data, focusing on experimental designs and causal effects. It uses the German Business Panel as a case study to implement cutting-edge methods.

- **Activity:** Participants present research papers and write a short technical report summarizing methods and implications for peers.

## Bridge Courses (General)

Bridge courses provide specialized training across various disciplines, often focusing on advanced quantitative methods.

**Bridge Course in Econometrics/Statistics**
This course provides a foundational understanding of advanced econometric methods.

- **Required Reading/References:**
  - Cameron, A. Colin and Trivedi, Pravin K. (2005). *Microeconometrics: Methods and Applications*. Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, UK.
  - Green, William H. (2008). *Econometric Analysis* (sixth edition). Prentice Hall, New Jersey, USA.
  - Wooldridge, Jeffrey M. (2001). *Econometric Analysis of Cross Section and Panel Data*. The MIT Press, Cambridge (MA), USA.

**Bridge Course in Data Science/NLP**
(Covers the content of the CDSB (Information Systems) and CDSB (Finance) courses)
Students are equipped with practical experience conducting scientific data-science projects. The goal is to guide students through the typical steps of a scientific data-science project, focusing on analyzing textual data using NLP techniques.

- **Assessment:** Written elaboration (90%) and presentation (10%).

**Bridge Course in Quantitative Textual Analysis**
(Covers the content of the CDSB (Marketing) and CDSB (Taxation) courses)
Students learn to implement state-of-the-art textual methods for analyzing text data in business administration and economics, acquiring solid programming knowledge in Python.
