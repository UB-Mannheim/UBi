# Seminar CS 717: Understanding Explainable AI Methods

This seminar covers classical attribution methods in Explainable AI (XAI). The focus includes techniques such as saliency maps, feature importance, and gradient-based explanations. The course aims to help students interpret and analyze the decision-making process of machine learning models.

## Course Goals

Upon completion of this seminar, students will be able to:

- Read, understand, and explore scientific literature related to XAI.
- Summarize a current research topic in a concise report (10 single-column pages + references).
- Deliver two presentations on the chosen topic: a 3-minute flash presentation and a 15-minute detailed presentation.
- Moderate a scientific discussion about a fellow student's topic.
- Review a draft report submitted by a fellow student.

## Organization and Prerequisites

- **Organizer:** Prof. Dr.-Ing. Margret Keuper
- **Prerequisites:** Background in Machine Learning
- **Capacity:** Maximum of 6 Bachelor's students
