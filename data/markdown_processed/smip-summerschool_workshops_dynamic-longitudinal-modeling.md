## Dynamic Longitudinal Modeling Workshop

**Speakers:** Manuel Voelkle (Humboldt University Berlin) & Charles Driver (University of Zurich)

### Workshop Overview and Goals

This workshop introduces participants to advanced modeling techniques, focusing specifically on dynamic approaches to analyzing change and variability.

The curriculum is structured to:

- Differentiate between static and dynamic models, discussing their respective strengths and weaknesses.
- Explore the fundamentals of dynamic modeling, including practical tools and software.
- Cover key advanced topics such as addressing heterogeneity, applying hierarchical models, and analyzing individual-level data.
- Introduce cutting-edge methods for causal inference and the integration of machine learning into dynamic modeling, with an emphasis on practical applications and current limitations.

While examples will draw from applied research, the workshop is designed for participants with a strong interest in quantitative methods.

### Prerequisites and Preparation

**Target Audience:**

- Participants with an interest in quantitative methods.
- Prior experience with multivariate analysis is beneficial but not required.
- Familiarity with structural equation modeling (SEM) and longitudinal data analysis is helpful.

**Technical Requirements:**

- Participants must bring their own laptops.
- Laptops must have the latest versions of R and RStudio installed.
- *Note:* While advanced knowledge of R is not necessary, those new to R are encouraged to familiarize themselves with its basic functionality.
