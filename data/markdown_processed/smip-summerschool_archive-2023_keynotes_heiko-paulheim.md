# Keynote: AI Algorithms and Their Interaction with Society

**Speaker:** Heiko Paulheim
**Date:** 26 June 2023
**Context:** Summer School 2023

## Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a pervasive topic in public discourse, especially following the rise of tools like ChatGPT. The talk addresses the mixed public feelings and general lack of knowledge regarding how AI algorithms influence daily life.

## Key Topics Covered

The presentation provides a comprehensive look at AI, covering three main areas:

1. **AI vs. Traditional Algorithms:** A general introduction explaining the fundamental differences between modern AI algorithms and traditional, non-AI algorithms.
1. **Research Insights (AI and Society):** Analysis of recent research projects focusing on the societal impact of AI:
   - **ReNewRS Project:** Analyzing polarization effects caused by news recommenders and discussing methods for mitigation.
   - **KarekoKI Project:** Investigating whether algorithmic pricing engines can learn to cooperate and coordinate prices, and assessing potential violations of antitrust legislation.
1. **Future Development:** Drawing conclusions from the research, the talk emphasizes that developing AI algorithms requires a distinct focus and skill set compared to the development of traditional algorithms.
