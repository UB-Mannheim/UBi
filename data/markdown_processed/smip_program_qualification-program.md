# SMiP Qualification Program Overview

The SMiP qualification program is designed for PhD Candidates and spans six semesters. The official language of the program, including all teaching and communication, is English.

The training program is comprehensive, covering four mandatory types of activities:

1. Core courses
1. Training in key competencies
1. Workshops
1. Colloquia (local and joint)

Due to the diverse nature of the activities, the typical format involves one- or two-day meetings held at one of the five participating universities.

## Core Courses

Core courses are mandatory for all PhD Candidates and take place only in the first year. They consist of two main classes:

- **Foundations of statistical modeling 1:** Held in the first semester (6 meeting days).
- **Foundations of statistical modeling 2:** Held in the second semester (6 meeting days).

These classes combine lecture series with application exercises. Thematically, they cover:

- Mathematical foundations of advanced statistical methods.
- Various model frameworks crucial for SMiP research.
- Statistical techniques forming the basis of SMiP research activities.

## Training in Key Competencies

This training is also mandatory for all PhD Candidates. The focus is on developing professional skills necessary for a career at the intersection of substantive science and statistical modeling. Topics include:

- Academic writing and publishing.
- Conference presentations.
- Good scientific practice and research ethics.
- Methods of research documentation.

## Workshops

Workshops constitute the most extensive part of the qualification program. The goal is to link substantive psychological research with statistical modeling techniques.

**Topics Covered:**

- **Substantive Science:** Diverse topics such as recognition memory, judgment and decision making, affective processes, emotion expression, person x situation interaction, social cognition, cognitive aging, and occupational health.
- **Quantitative Methods:** Training in software (e.g., Mplus, advanced R packages, Jags) and advanced techniques (e.g., Bayesian parameter estimation and inference, sequential tests, statistical power analysis, optimal design, model selection, simulation methods, and advanced item response models).

**Prerequisites and Focus:**

- Participants are expected to have solid knowledge of statistics, including common univariate and multivariate analyses, as acquired during standard master’s programs in psychology.
- *Introductory software workshops* will be offered to refresh or extend necessary skills for advanced RTG workshops (e.g., standard applications of R and Mplus).
- **Note:** No workshop will be purely statistical. Every workshop includes the analysis of psychological data and illustrations of relevant research questions. At least one workshop must focus on advanced test theory and psychometric modeling.

## Colloquia

Two types of colloquia are held: local and joint.

### Local Colloquium

Each participating university organizes a local colloquium. This provides a weekly meeting point for students with their local supervisor, other SMiP members, and associated research groups. The focus is on presenting ongoing research and discussing associated challenges.

### Joint Colloquium (Retreat)

A retreat-like joint colloquium is held once per semester for all SMiP members and PhD Candidates. This typically covers two days and combines expert lectures with an overview of the status quo for each PhD project, along with a perspective on planned future research.
