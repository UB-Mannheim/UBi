# Entrepreneurship & Family Firms Research

Research on family firms highlights the unique blend of long-term management strategies, emotional ties, and the human element that drive these businesses. Understanding how family dynamics and personal relationships influence decision-making and resilience is key to unlocking their long-term success and adaptability across generations.

As international experts in this field, our research group focuses on the strategic dynamics that shape business leadership, innovation, and performance, particularly within the context of evolving social, gender-related, and regional factors.

### Core Research Areas

We investigate how family firms function as the backbone of the economy through various specialized topics, including:

- **Leadership and Governance:** Family leadership, governance, strategy, and CEO succession planning.
- **Gender Dynamics:** Female entrepreneurship and addressing gender bias in business.
- **Innovation & Funding:** Crowdfunding and new venture forms.
- **Social Impact:** Social, sustainable, and international entrepreneurship.
- **Theoretical Frameworks:** Social capital, socio-emotional wealth, and relationships in entrepreneurship.

By exploring topics such as family firm succession, female entrepreneurship, crowdfunding, and the future of work, our research aims to create more inclusive, resilient, and sustainable entrepreneurial ecosystems.
