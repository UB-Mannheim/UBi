### Quantitative Methods (M.A. Level)

This tutorial accompanies the course “Quantitative Methods” in the M.A. program in Political Science.
The lab sessions focus on practical issues associated with quantitative methods, including:

- Obtaining and preparing data sets.
- Using statistical software.
- Selecting appropriate statistical tests.
- Graphing data effectively for presentation and analysis.
- Interpreting results.
  The seminar also serves as a software tutorial. No prior knowledge of statistical programming is expected.

### Causal Inference Methods (R Programming)

Students will learn how to implement causal inference methods in R.

- **Requirement:** Students must bring their own laptop for all practice sessions.
- **Preparation:** Ensure R and R Studio are installed before the first practice session.
- *Note: Previous knowledge in R is not necessary although advantageous.*

## 🎓 Specialized Bridge Courses

### Bridge Course – from the GESS Course Offer (Pass/Fail)

This course requires full and active participation across four building blocks to pass:

1. **Paper Presentation:** Give a paper presentation in building block 1.
1. **IAT Participation:** Participate in an IAT in building block 2.
1. **Conference Presentation:** Give a conference presentation in building block 3.
1. **Negotiation:** Participate in a negotiation in building block 4.

**Interdisciplinary Focus: Gender Gap in Leadership**
This course examines the gender gap in leadership positions, analyzing both psychological and economic reasons.

- **Scope:** Leadership positions are defined broadly (politics, public, private institutions), with a special emphasis on the academic environment.
- **Topics Covered:** Women’s educational and labor market choices, fertility decisions, structural hurdles (stereotypes, discrimination, social norms), and the effectiveness of gender equality measures (e.g., quota systems).
- **Practical Skills:** Includes hands-on sessions to train rhetoric and negotiation skills.

**Core Building Blocks:**

1. **Women in Leadership: The Economic Perspective:** Discusses the status quo, time trends, and research evidence on gender equality measures (e.g., gender quotas, family-friendly policies) and their impact on economic variables (e.g., labor market choices, gender pay gap).
1. **Women in Leadership: The Psychological Perspective:** Addresses gender stereotypes as a barrier to advancement. Topics include "Think Manager – Think Male," Glass metaphors (ceiling, escalator, cliff), Queen Bee effects, backlash effects, and failure-as-an-asset effects.
1. **“Raise Your Voice” – Rhetoric Training:** Hands-on training to present research findings in a conference-like scenario. Students must prepare a short presentation (5–7 minutes) on "What excites me about my research topic?" and bring a video-capable device.
1. **“Raise Your Pay” – Negotiation Training:** Teaches negotiation skills, conflict resolution, and handling verbal attacks. Students participate in a negotiation game and receive individual feedback. *Prerequisite: Students must submit one personal negotiation topic in advance.*

## 📝 Research and Methodology Requirements

### Thesis/Dissertation Requirements (M.A. Level)

- **Length:** Approximately 5,000 words (±500), including references but excluding appendices.
- **Content Requirements:**
  - A clearly defined and original Research Question.
  - A critical Literature Review demonstrating relevance.
  - A well-reasoned and evidence-based Argument (thesis).
  - Empirical Evidence to substantiate the argument.
- **Deadline:** January 15, 2026.
