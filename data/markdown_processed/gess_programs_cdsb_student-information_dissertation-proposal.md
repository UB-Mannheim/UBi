# Dissertation Proposal Guidelines

The written dissertation proposal must be submitted to the admission and examination committee at the beginning of the third semester.

**Key Dates and Process:**

- **Deadline:** 31 August
- The mentor and prospective supervisor must comment on the proposal before the committee.
- Acceptance of the proposal by the admission and examination committee confirms the continuation of the doctoral program and recognizes the successful coursework of the first year of study.

## Required Proposal Structure

The proposal must be structured into the following sections:

### 1. Title Page

- (Working) Title
- Name
- Short Abstract

### 2. Introduction

This section must provide a comprehensive overview of the research:

- Overview of the research question.
- Explanation of the academic and/or practical importance/relevance.
- Outline of the main objectives, the anticipated contribution, the expected findings, how the findings will be interpreted, and the broader implications of the research.
- Provide detailed information on two or three key aspects of the research.

### 3. Relevant Literature and Theoretical Background

Demonstrate deep engagement with the existing academic discourse:

- Show awareness of the debates and issues raised in relevant bodies of literature.
- Identify your specific niche within the literature and explain how your research question will make a useful contribution to the area.
- This section may include the development of hypotheses supported by theoretical reasoning.
- State the main research techniques planned (e.g., econometric techniques, interviews, case studies, modeling).

### 4. Methodology

Demonstrate a strong understanding of research design and execution:

- Show awareness of the methodological tools available and select those suitable for your research.
- State the main research techniques you plan to use (e.g., econometric techniques, interviews, case studies, modeling).
- Indicate suggested data collection procedures, specifying sources and addressing potential difficulties.
- Name any analytical techniques intended for use.
- If applicable, describe any artifacts that will be created as part of the work, including the methods planned for their creation and evaluation.

### 5. Expected Research Results and Future Plan

This section looks forward, detailing the expected outcomes and the path to completion:

- What are the expected findings, and how do they relate to the hypotheses (if applicable)? Initial findings may also be presented.
- How will the results fit into the larger academic context?
- Include a proposed timetable of activities.
- Present a short outlook on future research topics.

### 6. Bibliography

- List all references used in the proposal.

## Formatting and Scope Guidelines

- **Length:** 20 pages +/- 10% (excluding appendix).
- **Font:** Arial
- **Font Size:** 11 pt
- **Line Spacing:** 1,5
