# Seminar on Explainable AI Methods (SM 459)

This seminar provides an in-depth exploration of classical attribution methods within Explainable AI (XAI). The course covers techniques such as saliency maps, feature importance, and gradient-based explanations, focusing on how these methods help interpret and analyze the decision-making process of machine learning models.

## 🎓 Seminar Details

- **Course:** SM 459: Seminar on Explainable AI Methods
- **Prerequisites:** Basic understanding of Machine Learning.
- **Target Audience:** Available for Bachelor Students.
- **Organization:** Prof. Dr.-Ing. Margret Keuper
- **Capacity:** Maximum of 6 participants.

## 🎯 Learning Goals

Participants will develop comprehensive skills in the following areas:

- **Research & Discussion:** Reading, understanding, and discussing a basic topic relevant within computer vision.
- **Reporting:** Summarizing the chosen topic in a concise report (10 single-column pages + references).
- **Presentation:** Delivering two presentations on the topic (a 3-minute flash presentation and a 15-minute detailed presentation).
- **Academic Engagement:** Moderating a scientific discussion about a fellow student's topic and reviewing a fellow student's report draft.

## 📚 Seminar Topics

Each student will work on a specific topic within the seminar area, guided by an accompanying reference paper. The presentation and report must explore the topic broadly, using the reference paper as a foundation, rather than simply summarizing the paper itself.

Example reference papers include:

1. **Grad-CAM:** [Grad-CAM: Visual Explanations from Deep Networks via Gradient-based Localization](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.02391)
1. **Visualizing CNNs:** [Visualizing and Understanding Convolutional Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1311.2901)
1. **Interpretable Recognition:** [This Looks Like That: Deep Learning for Interpretable Image Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.10574)
1. **Layer-Wise Relevance Propagation:** [On Pixel-Wise Explanations for Non-Linear Classifier Decisions by Layer-Wise Relevance Propagation](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0130140)
1. **Score-CAM:** [Score-CAM: Score-Weighted Visual Explanations for Convolutional Neural Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.01279)
1. **Cos-Networks:** [Cos-Networks: Alignment is All We Need for Interpretability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.10268)
