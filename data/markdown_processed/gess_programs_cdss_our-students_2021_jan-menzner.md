# Jan Menzner: PhD Candidate Profile

Jan Menzner is a PhD candidate at the University of Mannheim’s Center for Doctoral Studies in Social and Behavioral Sciences (CDSS). He also works as a research assistant in the FGZ-project „Political Conflict Regulation and Social Cohesion“ at Goethe-University Frankfurt am Main, affiliated with the Chair of Political Science, Empirical and Democracy Research in Mannheim.

## Academic Background

- **M.A. in Political Science:** Ludwig-Maximilians University Munich
- **B.A. in Journalism:** University of Applied Sciences Bremen

Jan's Master's Thesis focused on a survey experiment investigating general attitudes towards prenatal genetic testing and the moderating effects exercised by moralized framing and individuals' personality traits.

## Research Interests and Focus

Jan's research centers on the empirical analysis of how citizens’ attitudes and preferences are formed and influenced. His work explores the interplay between:

- Party politics and rhetoric
- Regulatory policies and media consumption
- Moral frameworks and framing

He is particularly interested in how citizens shape democratic processes. Topically, his focus areas include:

- The state and perception of freedom of speech.
- The politics of climate and mobility change.
- The regulation of moral policies (e.g., free exercise of religion, sexual identity, abortion, or drug policies).

Jan also maintains a strong interest in survey and survey experiment methodology.
