# CDSS Course Catalog: Advanced Social Science Methods

This catalog provides an overview of advanced methodological courses offered to CDSS doctoral candidates, covering quantitative techniques, theoretical modeling, and empirical research design across various social science disciplines.

## 📚 Core Methodological Skills (Consolidated Courses)

These courses are highly repetitive across departments (Sociology, Political Science, Psychology) and represent core methodological training.

### 📊 Multilevel Modeling

- **Description:** Teaches the logic, statistical background, and implementation of multilevel modeling, used when individual observations are nested within higher units (e.g., students in classes). Applications include international comparative research.
- **Key Topics:** International Social Survey Program (ISSP), PIONEUR project data.
- **Prerequisites:** Regression analysis.
- **Assessment:** Home assignments, presentation.
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Wednesday, bi-weekly).

### 📈 Longitudinal Data Analysis / Panel Data

- **Description:** Provides a broad overview of longitudinal data analysis, focusing on panel data. Discusses modeling approaches (fixed effects, random effects, first difference) and how to improve causal inference compared to cross-sectional data.
- **Format:** Includes a Lecture (theory) and a Tutorial (practical application using R).
- **Prerequisites:** None required, but prior knowledge of R is recommended.
- **Assessment:** Active participation, short assignments, written exam.
- **Schedule:** Lecture (Monday), Tutorial (Tuesday/Thursday).

### 🔬 Experimental Research Designs

- **Description:** Gives a broad overview of experimental methods (survey, field, lab-in-the-field, laboratory experiments) for causal identification. Students learn to design and analyze their own field experiments.
- **Key Focus:** Best practices like transparency (open science) and ethical considerations.
- **Assessment:** Active participation, presentation of Exposé, research design seminar paper.
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Thursday).

### 🌐 Big Data Revolution

- **Description:** Introduces students to the implications of the "big data revolution." Covers theoretical aspects and hands-on lab sessions on capturing, curating, and analyzing new data forms (social media, IoT, wearables, machine learning).
- **Assessment:** Participation, written term paper.
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Tuesday).

### 💻 Quantitative Text Analysis & NLP

- **Description:** An in-depth introduction to text analysis and Natural Language Processing (NLP). Focuses on applying state-of-the-art machine learning techniques to research projects.
- **Skills:** Participants should be proficient in R; Python will be taught.
- **Assessment:** Coursework (2 coding tasks and 1 final paper).
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Friday, bi-weekly).

### 📝 Dissertation Proposal Workshop

- **Description:** Provides support and crucial feedback on writing the dissertation proposal. Students learn to structure a research outline, define motivation, survey theory, and specify methodology.
- **Prerequisites:** Crafting Social Science Research, Literature Review.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Workshop/Feedback).
- **Schedule:** Workshop (Dates TBD).

### 📐 Formal Models in Social Sciences

- **Description:** Introduces basic prototypes for theory construction (e.g., exchange, strategic action, collective action). Focuses on the math behind models (game theory, difference equations, agent-based simulation) to enable participants to modify or extend existing models.
- **Prerequisites:** Regression analysis.
- **Assessment:** Participation, homework, term paper.
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Monday).

### 📊 Survey Design

- **Description:** Teaches the fundamentals of questionnaire design for social science surveys. Covers theoretical models of answering questions and evaluating question quality.
- **Key Sources:** German General Survey (ALLBUS), European Social Survey (ESS), European Values Study (EVS), German Socio-economic Panel (SOEP).
- **Assessment:** Active participation, term paper.
- **Schedule:** Seminar (Thursday).

## 🏛️ Departmental Specializations

### Political Science

- **International Political Economy (IPE):** Examines how international and domestic politics interact with global flows (goods, finance, people). Covers international trade, global finance, and development aid.
  - *Schedule:* Lecture (Wednesday).
- **Comparative Political Behavior:** Provides an overview of key concepts and theories in analyzing micro-level political processes embedded in macro-level processes.
  - *Schedule:* Lecture (Monday).
- **Elections and Voting Behavior:** Focuses on analyzing changes in voting behavior at individual and aggregate levels, addressing key concepts and methodological issues.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Thursday).
- **Economic and Political Theory (Big Questions):** A discussion-based course tackling major challenges in economics and politics (e.g., dignity, morals, values). Requires reading six key books.
  - *Assessment:* Essay (50%), Class Participation (50%).
  - *Schedule:* Lecture (Friday).

### Sociology

- **Gender and Sex:** Focuses on how sex and gender relate to and affect major dimensions of social inequality (education, work, income, welfare state).
  - *Assessment:* Term paper.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Friday).
- **Welfare States and Crises:** Studies the resilience of European welfare states during economic crises (e.g., Great Recession, Pandemic).
  - *Assessment:* Written term paper.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Tuesday).
- **Educational Inequalities in Europe:** Discusses classic and recent theories of educational inequalities, trends over time, and cross-national variation.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Wednesday, bi-weekly).

### Psychology & Organizational Psychology

- **Training Practices in Work/Organizational Psychology:** Practical seminar using behavior modeling training approach. Students develop and evaluate a training concept.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Monday/Saturday).
- **Diary Methods/Cognitive Psychology:** Discusses intensive longitudinal designs and diary interventions to model change in affect, attitude, and behavior over time.
  - *Schedule:* Seminar (Thursday).
