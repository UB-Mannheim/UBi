# CDSS Course Catalog: Research Methods and Theory

This catalog provides an overview of the core courses and workshops offered to CDSS doctoral students, covering methods in sociology, political science, and psychology.

## 📚 Core Methodological Training

### Current Research Perspectives (CRP)

This course introduces first-year CDSS doctoral students to the theoretically informed research approaches and substantive research fields that underpin social science research in Mannheim. Faculty members present an overview of current scholarly debates and ongoing research in political science, psychology, and sociology. Students discuss the talks with the respective lecturer.

- **Assignment:** Students must develop a research project idea informed theoretically or methodologically by insights from one or several CDSS faculty presentations (outside of their particular field). This idea must be written up, and a potential research design described in a short 3-page paper, due October 31st.
- **Talk Schedule:** [Talk schedule](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Einrichtungen/GESS/Dokumente/CDSS/CRP_2022.pdf)
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Lecture: 09.09.22 (Friday, 10:15 – 13:30, online)
  - Lecture: 23.09.22 – 07.10.22 (Friday, 10:15 – 13:30, online)

### Mathematics for Social Scientists

This course provides an introduction to mathematical logic and rigor, covering fundamental mathematical concepts essential for modern social science research. Topics include introductory set and function theory, analysis of functions, probability, and linear algebra, which form the basis of data analysis.

- **Basic Readings:** Knut Sydsaeter and Peter Hammond. 2008. *Essential Mathematics for Economic Analysis*. 3rd edition.
- **Additional Readings:** Includes texts by Chiang & Wainwright (2005), Gill (2006), Pemberton & Rau (2007), and Simon & Blume (1994).
- **Exam:** Scheduled for Monday, 12 December 2022, from 10–12 in room C 012 in A5, 6.
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Lecture: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 12:00 – 13:30, 211 in B6, 30–32)

### Quantitative Methods in Political Science

This course focuses on developing sound critical judgment about quantitative studies of political problems. It covers linear regression models, including mathematical bases, estimation, interpretation, and techniques for addressing assumption violations (e.g., heteroscedasticity, endogeneity). The second half introduces likelihood as a theory of inference, including models for binary and count data.

- **Goal:** To develop skills in basic quantitative analysis and provide a basis for advanced statistical methods.
- **Assessment:** Take-home exam (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Tutorial: 07.09.22 – 07.12.22 (Wednesday, 12:00 – 13:30, A 103 in B6, 23–25)
  - Tutorial: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 10:15 – 11:45, C 217 in A5, 6)
  - Tutorial: 09.09.22 – 02.12.22 (Friday, 10:15 – 11:45, A 102 in B6, 23–25)
  - Lecture: 07.09.22 – 07.12.22 (Wednesday, 08:30 – 10:00, B 244 in A5,6)

### Causal Inference

This course teaches fundamental concepts behind estimating causal effects, addressing potential obstacles to causal inference. Students learn to design and analyze experiments and apply techniques to estimate causal effects in both randomized and observational settings using real datasets in R.

- **Assessment:** Lecture (Participation, written exam); Tutorial (Homework, oral participation, presentation).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Tutorial: 07.09.22 – 07.12.22 (Wednesday, 13:45 – 15:15, A102 in B6, 23–25)
  - Tutorial: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 15:30 – 17:00, A 103 in B6, 23–25)
  - Lecture: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 13:45 – 15:15, A 103 in B6, 23–25)

### Advanced Multivariate Modeling

This advanced seminar provides an overview of standard multivariate methods and current developments, including general and generalized linear models, structural equation models, and multilevel modeling.

- **Goal:** To bring PhD candidates to a common level of statistical knowledge and data analytic skills.
- **Assessment:** Participation and written exam (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Seminar: 05.09.22 – 05.12.22 (Monday, 10:15 – 11:45, C216 in A5, 6 entrance C)

## 🔬 Subject-Specific Seminars

### Sociology

#### Comparative Method

This seminar introduces comparative strategies and methods, particularly for cross-national comparison of welfare states and market economies. Topics include traditional approaches (Durkheim, Weber), QCA, fuzzy set methods, process tracing, and within-case analysis.

- **Assessment:** Active participation and oral presentation; written term paper (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Seminar: 06.09.22 – 06.12.22 (Tuesday, 10:15 – 11:45, B 317 in A5, 6)

#### Poverty and Social Exclusion

This seminar provides an introduction to various concepts, dimensions, and measures of poverty and social exclusion. It discusses theories on causes, policies across Europe, and consequences in various domains.

- **Assessment:** Active participation, regular small assignments, term paper (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Seminar: Biweekly (07.09.22 – 21.09.22, 222 in B6, 4–5) and (26.10.22 – 07.12.22, 222 in B6, 4–5).

### Political Science

#### Game Theory

This course provides a graduate-level introduction to formal modeling in political science. It covers concepts like normal form games, Nash equilibria, extensive form games, and signaling games.

- **Assessment:** Working in small groups on assignments, final exam (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Tutorial: 09.09.22 – 09.12.22 (Friday, 08:30 – 10:00, C 217 in A5, 6)
  - Lecture: 05.09.22 – 05.12.22 (Monday, 10:15 – 11:45, B 244 in A5, 6)

#### Women in Leadership (Gender Gap)

This interdisciplinary course examines the gender gap in leadership positions, analyzing psychological and economic reasons. It covers structural hurdles, stereotypes, and the effectiveness of gender equality measures.

- **Assessment:** Active participation, data collection, peer-feedback (30 hours, 1 ECTS); Response Papers (60 hours, 2 ECTS); Learning Portfolio (30 hours, 1 ECTS); Contribution to survey design/publication (60 hours, 2 ECTS).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Lecture: 07.09.22 (Wednesday, 10:15 – 11:45, O 048)
  - Lecture: 21.09.22 – 19.10.22 (Wednesday, 10:15 – 11:45, O 048 then in O 145)
  - Lecture: 26.10.22 – 16.11.22 (Wednesday, 10:15 – 13:30, O 048)

### Psychology

#### Cognitive Psychology

This course provides an overview of core topics in cognitive psychology, including basic methodology, stochastic modeling of cognitive processes, and model selection.

- **Assessment:** Active participation, final written exam (graded).
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Lecture: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 15:30 – 17:00, B 244 in A5, 6)

#### Work and Organizational Psychology

This course provides an overview of core topics, focusing on recent theoretical approaches and empirical findings (meta-analyses) in areas like work motivation, stress and health, leadership, and teams.

- **Assessment:** Graded homework assignment.
- **Schedule (Fall 2022):**
  - Lecture: 08.09.22 – 08.12.22 (Thursday, 17:15 – 18:45, online)
