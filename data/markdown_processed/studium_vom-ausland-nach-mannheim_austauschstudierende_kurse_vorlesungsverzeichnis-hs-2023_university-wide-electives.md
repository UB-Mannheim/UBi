# University Wide Electives and Academic Programs

This section provides detailed descriptions of elective courses available to exchange students, covering topics in law, linguistics, and social sciences.

## 🌐 University Wide Electives – Bachelor

**Linguistics Elective:**
This course provides an introduction to synchronic linguistics, equipping students with descriptive and analytic tools. Topics covered include linguistic analyses of sounds, words, and sentences, and methods for accumulating and aggregating information from diverse sources.

- **Literature Examples:** *Introduction to Literary Studies* (Mario Klarer), *Heart of Darkness* (Joseph Conrad), *M. Butterfly* (David Henry Hwang).

**Digital Assets Regulation (Course Description):**
This course addresses the rapidly growing legal landscape of digital assets. It aims to provide understanding of digital assets, methods for protecting digital rights under current legislation and case law, and defining digital ownership.

- **Key Topics:**
  - Digital assets: notion, types, legal landscape
  - Digital ownership: European and American perspectives
  - Digital assets and data: the basics of data protection
  - Personal and machine-generated data ownership issues
  - Cryptocurrencies, NFT and other crypto-assets
  - Social media and online gaming accounts
  - Digital inheritance
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain a deeper understanding of the digital assets concept and approaches to protect digital rights.
- **Examination:** Essay.
- **Lecturer:** Prof. Dr. Kateryna Nekit (Research focus: digital property and the role of data).

**International Commercial Litigation (Course Description):**
This course covers the concept and practical relevance of international litigation.

- **Key Topics:**
  - Advantages and disadvantages of international litigation
  - Sources of international procedural law
  - International jurisdiction and coordination between different jurisdictions
  - Conduct of proceedings
  - Recognition and enforcement of foreign judgments
- **Literature Examples:** *International Commercial Litigation* (Fentiman, Hartley), *Internationales Zivilprozessrecht* (Junker).

**German Constitutional Law (Grundgesetz):**
This course offers a general introduction to German Constitutional Law, covering the constitution, its background, and methods for working with constitutional legal texts. The German Basic Law (*Grundgesetz*) is examined, with a particular focus on fundamental rights (Art. 1–19). Students learn about the federal structure, fundamental constitutional principles, and the integration of German law within the European Union legal system.

- **Contents:** German legal system, Constitutional Law, The Grundgesetz and the European Union, Fundamental Rights, Fundamental Constitutional Principles, The Federal State, Constitutional organs, Legislative procedure, Public administration, The judicial system.
- **Learning Target:** Students can understand the basic principles of the *Grundgesetz* and solve simple cases regarding fundamental rights.
- **Examination:** Oral Exam.
- **Lecturer:** Katharina Longin.

**International Law (Public International Law):**
This course provides an overview of international law, covering foundational principles and sources.

- **Literature Examples:** *Realizing Utopia: The Future of International Law* (Cassese), *Brownlie’s Principles of Public International Law* (Crawford and Brownlie), *Principles of International Law* (Hall).
