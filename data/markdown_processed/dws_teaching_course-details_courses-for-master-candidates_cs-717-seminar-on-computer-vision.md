# CS 717: Seminar on Explainable AI Methods

This seminar provides an in-depth exploration of Explainable AI (XAI) methods, with a specific focus on Concept Bottleneck Models (CBMs). The course covers the architecture and practical applications of CBMs, emphasizing how they enhance interpretability by linking high-level human concepts with machine learning predictions.

## 🎓 Course Details

- **Instructor:** Prof. Dr.-Ing. Margret Keuper
- **Prerequisites:** Background in Machine Learning.
- **Capacity:** Maximum of 6 Master's students.

## 🎯 Learning Goals and Tasks

Students are expected to engage deeply with the material through the following tasks:

- Reading, understanding, and exploring current scientific literature.
- Summarizing a current research topic in a concise report (10 single-column pages + references).
- Giving two presentations on the topic:
  - A 3-minute flash presentation.
  - A 15-minute final presentation.
- Moderating a scientific discussion about a fellow student's topic.
- Reviewing a draft report submitted by a fellow student.

## 📚 Seminar Topics (Concept Bottleneck Models)

Each student will work on a specific topic within the area of the seminar, guided by an accompanying reference paper. The presentation and report must explore the topic broadly, using the reference paper as a foundation, rather than simply summarizing the paper itself.

**Topic List:**

1. [Hybrid Concept Bottleneck Models](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2025/html/Liu_Hybrid_Concept_Bottleneck_Models_CVPR_2025_paper.html)
1. [Post-hoc Concept Bottleneck Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.15480)
1. [Editable Concept Bottleneck Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.15476)
1. [Semi-supervised Concept Bottleneck Models](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.18992)
1. [Multimodal Concept Bottleneck Models](https://openreview.net/forum?id=6r2ercqOo8)
1. [TabCBM: Concept-based Interpretable Neural Networks for Tabular Data](https://openreview.net/forum?id=TIsrnWpjQ0)
1. [Show and Tell: Visually Explainable Deep Neural Nets via Spatially-Aware Concept Bottleneck Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.20134)
1. [Partially Shared Concept Bottleneck Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.22170)
