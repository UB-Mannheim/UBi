# Storing Power: Market Structure Matters

**Speaker:** Natalia Fabra
**Affiliation:** Universidad Carlos III de Madrid
**Event:** Decarbonization Seminar (October 4, 2021)
**Source:** [https://www.uni-mannheim.de/mises/forschung/decarbonization-seminar-fall-2021/storing-power-market-structure-matters/](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/mises/forschung/decarbonization-seminar-fall-2021/storing-power-market-structure-matters/)

## Seminar Abstract

This seminar assesses how firms' incentives to operate and invest in energy storage depend critically on the underlying market structure. The research characterizes equilibrium market outcomes, considering scenarios with market power in storage and/or production, as well as vertical integration between storage and production.

**Key Findings:**

- Market power reduces overall efficiency through two primary channels: inducing inefficient use of storage facilities and distorting investment incentives.
- The worst outcome for consumers and total welfare occurs specifically under vertical integration.
- These results provide crucial insights for regulating energy storage, an issue vital for the successful deployment of renewable energy sources.

## Speaker Biography: Natalia Fabra

Natalia Fabra is a Professor of Economics at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid. She holds multiple research roles, including Research Fellow at the Centre for Economic Policy Research, Associate Member of the Toulouse School of Economics, and Research Fellow at the University of Cambridge Energy Policy Research Group. She is also a member of the Economic Advisory Group on Competition Policy (EAGCP) of the European Commission.

**Expertise:**
Natalia specializes in Industrial Organization, with a strong emphasis on Energy and Environmental Economics, and Regulation and Competition Policy.

**Academic Contributions:**
Her research is published in leading journals such as the *American Economic Review*, *Management Science*, *The Rand Journal of Economics*, *The Economic Journal*, *Energy Economics*, and *The Journal of Industrial Economics*. She serves as an Associate Editor for *The Economic Journal* and *The Journal of Industrial Economics*.

**Awards and Fellowships:**

- She has received two distinguished awards as Spanish Best Young Economist (awarded by the Banco Sabadell Foundation and the Madrid regional government).
- In 2017, she was awarded an ERC Consolidator grant for the period 2018–2023, focusing on "Current Tools and Policy Challenges in Electricity Markets."
- She has conducted research visiting fellowships at several prestigious institutions, including the University of California Energy Institute (Berkeley), Nuffield College (Oxford), the Toulouse School of Economics, Northwestern University (Chicago), and the Energy Policy Research Group (Cambridge).
