# Do Mergers and Acquisitions Improve Efficiency: Evidence from Power Plants

### 📄 Seminar Abstract

Using rich data on hourly physical productivity and 5000 ownership changes from US power plants, this seminar studies the effects of mergers and acquisitions (M&A) on efficiency and provides evidence on the underlying mechanisms.

**Key Findings:**

- Acquired plants experience a 4% efficiency increase 5–8 months after acquisition.
- Three-quarters of this gain is attributed to increased productive efficiency, while the remainder comes from dynamic efficiency at the plant level and allocative efficiency at the portfolio level.
- The findings suggest that acquisitions reallocate assets to more productive uses: high-productivity firms buy underperforming assets from low-productivity firms and subsequently make these acquired assets more productive.

### 👤 Speaker Biography

Omer Karaduman is an Assistant Professor of Operations, Information and Technology at the Graduate School of Business at Stanford University. His research focuses on the transition of the energy sector toward a decarbonized and sustainable future. He utilizes large datasets and game-theoretical modeling to provide practical policy suggestions.

- **Education:** Ph.D. in Economics from MIT (2020); Bachelor's degree in Economics from Bilkent University (2014).
