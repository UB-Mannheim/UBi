Patrick Bolton is the Barbara and David Zalaznick Professor of Business at Columbia Business School.

- **Education:** He holds a PhD from the London School of Economics (1986), a BA in economics from the University of Cambridge, and a BA in political science from the Institut d'Etudes Politiques de Paris.

### Seminar Abstract: Empirical Analysis of Firm Commitments

This seminar provides an empirical analysis of voluntary commitments made by companies to reduce their carbon emissions.

**Key Questions Explored:**

- What drives publicly traded companies to make voluntary commitments to reduce emissions by a certain date or reduce emission intensity?
- What are the actual effects of these commitments?

**Commitment Movements Analyzed:**
The seminar explores two major commitment movements:

1. The Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP).
1. The Science-Based Target Initiative (SBTi).

**Main Findings:**

- **Effectiveness:** While companies that make commitments subsequently reduce their emissions, the overall effect of these commitment initiatives on the total emissions of publicly traded companies (including those that do not commit) has been small.
- **Correlation:** Companies that agree to commit, and particularly those that make the most ambitious commitments, tend to be companies that already have lower carbon emissions.
- **Government Influence:** Firm commitments to reduce emissions are less prevalent in countries where national government commitments have already been made.
- **Conclusion:** The movements to encourage corporate commitment have been successful in engaging willing companies but have encountered greater resistance from the companies that need to reduce their emissions the most.
