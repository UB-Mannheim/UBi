# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## 🎓 Before You Apply

### Eligibility and Admission Criteria

- **Can you review my documents (resume, qualifications) to check my eligibility?**
  We do not assess documents prior to submission or provide a forecast on your chances. Admission criteria are specific to the program:
  - [Business](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/apply/admission-requirements/for-cdsb/)
  - [Economics](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/apply/admission-requirements/for-cdse/)
  - [Social Sciences](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/apply/admission-requirements/for-cdss/)

### Financial Information

- **Are there tuition fees?**
  No tuition fees are charged. There is a small semester fee of 194 Euro per term (2025/26).
- **Are scholarships or funding opportunities available?**
  Students accepted into the program are guaranteed funding for the duration. The length varies (3 years in Social Sciences to 5 years in Economics), which may involve a scholarship in the first year and research assistant positions in subsequent years. [Further information](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/about/student-financing/)

### Research and Supervision

- **Do I need a fully developed research proposal when I apply?**
  No, a research proposal is not required at the time of application. However, your motivation letter must clearly demonstrate your research interests and how they align with the current research of the faculty members you wish to work with.
- **Do I have to find a supervisor beforehand?**
  No, students do not need to find a supervisor prior to the semester start.

### Language Requirements

- **Do I need to speak German for admission?**
  No. The program is taught entirely in English.

## 📄 Application Materials and Submission

### Academic Records

- **Do I need to send transcripts?**
  Yes. Applicants must upload transcripts into the online application system. Official hard copy/paper transcripts are not required at the time of application.
- **What if I haven't completed my Master's/Bachelor's degree yet?**
  - **If applying with incomplete Master's thesis:** You are eligible, but you must provide proof that your Master’s degree will be completed by no later than November 30th of the year you are applying.
  - **If awaiting final transcript (e.g., completing degree by August 202X):** Submit your most recent transcript.
- **How do I upload multiple documents (e.g., transcripts and degree certificates) when only one space is provided?**
  Merge all PDFs into a single document. If the merged PDF exceeds the size limit, compress it.

### Standardized Tests

- **Which tests are required for admission?**
  Depending on the program, prospective students must provide:
  - **Center for Doctoral Studies in Business:** GMAT (or GRE) – no exemption
  - **Center for Doctoral Studies in Economics:** GRE – no exemption
  - **Center for Doctoral Studies in Social Studies:** TOEFL or IELTS
  - *Note: Refer to the [admission page of the respective program](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/apply/) for details.*
- **Can I be exempted from the TOEFL test (for CDSS) if my previous degree was taught in English?**
  Yes. Submit an official letter from your university confirming that the curriculum was taught entirely in English.
- **How should I submit my test scores (GRE/GMAT/TOEFL)?**
  Upload your test-taker's copy in the online application, but also ask the testing service to send the official scores to the university.

### Writing Sample and Experience

- **What are the guidelines for the Writing Sample?**
  Please attach a writing sample of up to 10 pages (not more than 3500 words, bibliography excluded). Examples include a paper written during your studies, a representative excerpt from your thesis, or an essay related to your research interests.
- **What should I upload in the Work Experience section?**
  You are required to upload a reference issued by your employer.
- **Can I provide details of my work experiences in the Stays Abroad section?**
  We are most interested in your academic stays abroad, but we will consider work experiences as well.

### Submission Logistics

- **Can I submit my application without a document and send it later?**
  No. Do not submit your application if documents are missing prior to the application deadline.
- **Will late submissions be accepted?**
  Submissions after the application deadline will not be accepted.
- **What if I need to upload a document later?**
  Do not submit your application until all required documents are available.

## ✉️ Letters of Recommendation and Troubleshooting

### Recommendations

- **How many letters of recommendation are required?**
  Two letters of recommendation are required from faculty or scholars who can assess your potential for academic research and teaching. We only accept reference letters submitted via our online system.
- **I need to change my referee’s data.**
  Write an email to `gess.application@uni-mannheim.de` with the new contact details.
- **My referees haven't been contacted after I filled in their data. What should I do?**
  Ask them to check their spam folders. If they still cannot find the request, contact us at `gess.application@uni-mannheim.de`, and we will resend the request.

### Technical Issues and Corrections

- **I made a mistake after submitting my application. How can I fix it?**
  Write an email to `gess.application@uni-mannheim.de` indicating clearly the correction and the section where the change needs to be made.
- **I am facing a technical error. What should I do?**
  Write an email to `gess.application@uni-mannheim.de` describing the error as detailed as possible. Attach a screenshot if possible. Our IT-support will contact you once the issue is fixed.

## 🗣️ Interview Process (CDSS Only)

### Preparing for the Interview

- **What should I consider besides scientific fit when interviewing with a potential supervisor?**
  Treat the first meeting like any job interview. Review the person’s web page beforehand. Since there is no fixed format, prepare questions to assess mutual fit. Consider asking:
  - What opportunities are available for active involvement in your projects?
  - How would you describe your supervision style?
  - How often do you meet with doctoral students?
  - How many doctoral students are you currently supervising?
  - How long does a dissertation project typically take?
  - Where does your team typically aim to publish its research?
  - How can I contact a couple of doctoral students at your chair?
  - *Tip: Ask questions based on your expectations (e.g., do you prefer 'hands-off' or weekly meetings?).*

### Offers and Follow-up

- **I received an offer, but no interview was held. How can I find out more about the position?**
  If you know the professor and are happy with the offer, you are in a strong position. Otherwise, consider contacting the potential supervisor to request a brief meeting to ensure you can make an informed decision.
