# Executive Compensation Tied to ESG Performance: International Evidence

**Speaker:** Gaizka Ormazabal
**Affiliation:** IESE Business School

## Speaker Biography

Professor Gaizka Ormazabal holds a PhD in Business from Stanford University and a PhD in Construction Engineering from Universitat Politecnica de Catalunya.

His research expertise focuses on **executive compensation** and **corporate governance**. His work examines the choice and valuation implications of corporate governance mechanisms. Current research projects analyze recent trends in managerial compensation, corporate governance intermediaries, financial regulation, bank accounting, and the role of corporate governance in sustainable development.

Professor Ormazabal's research is featured in leading academic journals, including the *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, *Journal of Financial Economics*, and *The Accounting Review*. He is a Research Affiliate in the Financial Economics program of the Center for Economic Policy Research (CEPR) and serves as the academic director at the IESE Center for Corporate Governance.

## Seminar Abstract

This seminar examines the increasing use of **ESG performance metrics** within executive compensation contracts.

The analysis documents that a growing fraction of publicly traded companies worldwide now incorporate ESG metrics into the compensation schemes of their top executives. The study links the reliance on these metrics to:

- Firm fundamentals.
- The geographic location of the firms.
- The prevalence of institutional shareholders.

The findings suggest that the adoption of ESG variables in managerial performance measures is followed by improvements in overall ESG performance and meaningful changes in the compensation structure of executives.
