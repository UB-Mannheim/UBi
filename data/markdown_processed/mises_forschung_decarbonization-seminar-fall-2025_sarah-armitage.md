# Sarah Armitage: Cross-State Spillovers of Regulation Under National Pricing Strategies: Evidence from Electric Vehicles

Sarah Armitage is an Assistant Professor at Boston University. This seminar examines how regional policies interact with national pricing strategies, using the US electric vehicle (EV) market as a case study.

## 📝 Seminar Abstract

When policies differ across regions but prices are not set independently, the assumed equivalency between demand- and supply-side policy instruments breaks down.

Drawing on price discrimination theory, the research demonstrates that regional demand-side tools (like consumer subsidies) generate effective price variation across regions, while producer subsidies do not. This creates different incentives for firms and ultimately leads to varied policy responses.

The study focuses on the interaction between firms' pricing strategies and the US state-level Zero-Emission Vehicle (ZEV) policy, which incentivizes manufacturers to sell electric vehicles in California and nine other states. Using data from 2012–2017, the research builds and estimates a structural model of the new passenger vehicle market, examining the impacts of the ZEV program in the regulated region and its spillovers to other states.

The findings compare the ZEV policy to a counterfactual demand-side subsidy and tax that holds incentives per vehicle in ZEV states fixed. Under the assumption that Tesla sets national prices, the estimates suggest that a demand-side policy would increase electric vehicle sales in the US by 13%.

## 👤 Speaker Biography

**Sarah Armitage** is an Assistant Professor at Boston University's Questrom School of Business.

- **Education:** She received her Ph.D. from Harvard University, specializing in environmental economics, industrial organization, and public finance. She holds an M.Phil. in Economic and Social History from the University of Cambridge (where she was a Gates Cambridge Scholar), and a B.A. in History from Yale University.
- **Experience:** During the 2022–2023 academic year, she served as an Economist Fellow at the Environmental Defense Fund. Prior to graduate school, she worked as a consultant at Industrial Economics, Inc., advising state and federal government clients on environmental projects, and as a research assistant at MIT's Center for Energy and Environmental Policy Research. She has also served as an Impact Fellow at Prime Coalition.
