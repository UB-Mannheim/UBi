# Psychology Research and Doctoral Programs

## CDSS Doctoral Program in Psychology

The **CDSS Doctoral Program in Psychology** offers advanced courses in topical research areas and advanced methods. These methods provide students with tools for rigorous theoretical and empirical analysis, including experimental design, multivariate and longitudinal analysis, evaluation and assessment methods, and modeling of psychic processes.

- [CDSS Course Catalog](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdss/course-catalog/)

## Research at the Department of Psychology

Research at the [Department of Psychology](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/research/psychology/) emphasizes a quantitative and experimental approach, which guides both basic and applied research.

### Research Focus Areas

**Basic Research:**

- Experimental Psychology
- Cognition and Individual Difference
- Research Methods and Psychological Assessment

**Applied Research:**

- Work and Organizational Psychology
- Consumer and Economic Psychology
- Health Psychology
- Psychology of Education / Educational Psychology
- Clinical Psychology, Biological Psychology, and Psychotherapy

### Research Methodology and Collaboration

Research within the department and the [School of Social Sciences](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/en/research/) is characterized by empirical-analytical research with a strong quantitative focus, methodological diversity, and interdisciplinarity.

The department maintains close collaborations with:

- The [Otto Selz Institute](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/osi/)
- The [Central Institute of Mental Health (ZI)](https://www.zi-mannheim.de/en.html)

Furthermore, the CDSS works closely with the Research Training Group ['Statistical Modeling in Psychology'](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/smip/) (SMiP). SMiP is a transregional collaboration involving twelve behavioral researchers from five German universities. Its goal is to bridge the gap between substantive basic and applied psychological research and the latest developments in statistical modeling and psychometrics.
