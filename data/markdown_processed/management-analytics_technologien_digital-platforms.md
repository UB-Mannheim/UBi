# Research Projects in Digitalization, Analytics, and Business Models

## Competence Development in VET Enculturation Processes

**Authors:** [Viola Deutscher](https://www.uni-goettingen.de/de/prof.+dr.+viola+deutscher/685786.html)
**Cooperation Partners:** Esther Winther, University of Duisburg-Essen; Vocational schools/companies with VET programmes

**Description:**
This DFG-funded research project analyzes competence development in vocational education training (VET) and identifies relevant success factors for commercial apprentices (n = 877). The project focuses on modeling VET enculturation processes at both group and individual levels, including the description of company learning conditions. Using a longitudinal design, the study simultaneously employs a validated competence assessment and an instrument for recording enculturation conditions at the beginning, middle, and end of initial vocational training. This approach generates practical findings about the genesis of professional competences, helping companies to improve their educational programs.

## LeAP – Learning Analytics Profile

**Authors:** [Dirk Ifenthaler](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/ifenthaler/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Learning, Design and Technology

**Description:**
Learning analytics is generating growing interest in data and computer science as well as educational science and hence becoming an important aspect of modern e-learning environments. However, implementing learning analytics into existing legacy systems in learning environments with high data privacy concerns is quite a challenge. This research shows how a learning analytics application has been implemented into such an existing university environment by adding a plugin to the local digital learning environment which injects user-centric information to specific objects within students’ and teachers’ learning environments. The LeAP platform can be used in various learning and teaching scenarios in order to provide adaptive support for learners and teachers.

## Ad-blockers and Content Differentiation

**Authors:** Gokhan Gecer, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/), Pinar Yildirim
**Cooperation Partners:** Wharton School (Philadelphia)

**Description:**
The increasing use of ad-blockers poses a threat to the main revenue stream of publishers. To address this, some publishers offer ad-free (premium) versions via subscriptions, while others ask users to disable their ad-blockers. We developed a game-theoretical model of a media ecosystem involving publishers and users. Publishers maximize profits from advertising income and/or subscription fees, while users are utility maximizers who decide on the amount of content they consume. The model analyzes two publisher strategies: (1) showing content only to those who disable ad-blockers, and (2) showing content to paying subscribers alongside those who disable ad-blockers. Analysis in monopoly and duopoly markets reveals that when content production is costly, publishers decrease content quality, leading to fewer users. Consequently, publishers offer a subscription option. Furthermore, the substitutability of content is crucial: publishers offer a subscription option when content is more substitutable (newspapers/tabloids) or when it is less substitutable but costly to produce (specific focus websites).

## Maximizing Customer Lifetime Value through Strategic Channel Management: How to Incentivize Customers to Use a Mobile App versus a Website

**Authors:** Gokhan Gecer, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Sales & Services Marketing

**Description:**
Customer engagement with mobile devices has changed online purchasing habits, particularly increasing the usage of mobile apps for shopping and thus boosting the Customer Lifetime Value (CLV). We suggest that online retailers should steer customers to the mobile channel by offering a permanent discount over the mobile app. While this strategy decreases short-term income, it may increase CLV. We develop a probability-based CLV model to determine to what type of customer an online retailer should offer a discount, as well as the optimal discount value. The analysis shows that discounts should be offered to customers who are either very likely or unlikely to increase their purchasing probability, or to encourage switching to the mobile app. Crucially, the strategy should be applied to increase customer retention, not for customer acquisition, as offering discounts to customers already with a high purchasing probability is unnecessary.

## The Scale of Sharing Transactions via Online Platforms and Issues for Taxation in the Digital Economy

**Authors:** Christopher Ludwig, [Christoph Spengel](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/spengel/), [Rainer Bräutigam](https://www.mosbach.dhbw.de/dhbw-mosbach/who-is-who/prof-dr-rainer-braeutigam/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Business Administration and Taxation II

**Description:**
This project utilizes publicly accessible Airbnb data, covering lodging type, price, facilities, location, and booking trends across 20 German cities. Based on this data, we project the annual turnover of hosts and the potential tax revenue arising from these transactions. The estimated annual turnover across the 20 considered cities is approximately 683 million euros. The analysis demonstrates that the estimated tax revenue from income and value-added tax liability generated by these sharing platforms is enormous. Given the scale of the potential tax revenue, we develop recommendations for adjusting existing tax systems to adapt them to the digital economy and ensure compliance among taxpayers.

## Analytical Approaches for Performance Evaluation and Optimization of Stochastic and Dynamic Processes in Digital Production

**Authors:** [Johannes Diefenbach](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/stolletz/team/promotionen-und-postdocs/dr-johannes-diefenbach/), [Fikri Karaesmen](https://mysite.ku.edu.tr/fkaraesmen/), [Justus Arne Schwarz](https://www.uni-regensburg.de/wirtschaftswissenschaften/fakultaet/org/produktionsmanagement/team/prof-dr-justus-arne-schwarz), [Raik Stolletz](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/stolletz/team/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Koc University (Istanbul) and the Robert Bosch GmbH

**Description:**
This project develops new analytical models and fast approximation methods for the performance analysis and optimization of dynamic semiconductor manufacturing processes. Methods from prescriptive analytics generate insights on how new products are introduced to the production system and the market, especially when the yield is stochastic. This research is part of "Productive 4.0," Europe’s largest research project in the field of Digital Industry, recognized as an ECSEL Lighthouse Initiative and funded by the EU and the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF). It investigates how electronics and Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) can enhance production efficiency in semiconductor supply chains across the entire value chain and product life cycle.
