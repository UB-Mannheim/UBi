# CDSE Seminar Fall 2019: Seminar Schedule

The CDSE Seminar Fall 2019 took place at the University of Mannheim. The seminar provided a series of academic presentations on various topics in economics and related fields.

## Seminar Overview

- **Event:** CDSE Seminar Fall 2019
- **Location:** L7,3–5, SO31 / L7,3–5, P 044
- **Time:** 3:30 pm – 5:00 pm

## Seminar Schedule (September – December 2019)

The following list details the topics, speakers, and dates of the seminar sessions:

### September 2019

- **September 3, 2019:**
  - **Topic:** Research strategy session I
  - **Speakers:** Michèle Tertilt, Christoph Rothe, Helena Perrone, Laura Grigolon
- **September 10, 2019:**
  - **Topic 1:** Weighted voting mechanisms
  - **Speaker:** Marco Reuter
  - **Topic 2:** Price discrimination, consumer search, and perfect information
  - **Speaker:** Carl-Christian Groh
- **September 17, 2019:**
  - **Topic 1:** Local (tax) policy responses to opening the border: The Swiss experience
  - **Speaker:** Justus Nover
  - **Topic 2:** Fake news: A supply side explanation
  - **Speaker:** Federico Innocenti
- **September 1, 2019:**
  - **Topic:** Digital technologies, inequality and well-being: A labor market adjustment approach
  - **Speaker:** Martin Reinhard

### October 2019

- **October 8, 2019:**
  - **Topic 1:** Endogenous bank runs and household heterogeneity
  - **Speaker:** Daniel Runge
  - **Topic 2:** Early life exposure to air pollution and noncognitive skills
  - **Speaker:** Beate Thies
- **October 22, 2019:**
  - **Topic:** Research strategy session II

### November 2019

- **November 5, 2019:**
  - **Topic:** Search frictions and labor force participation
  - **Speakers:** Johannes Gönsch, Mykola Ryzhenkov
- **November 12, 2019:**
  - **Topic 1:** Compulsory voting when information acquisition is costly
  - **Speaker:** Linnéa Rohde
  - **Topic 2:** Are payday loan users irrational? A credit scoring explanation (joint work with Tsung-Hsien Li)
  - **Speaker:** Jan Sun
- **November 26, 2019:**

### December 2019

- **December 3, 2019:**
  - **Topic 1:** Employment effects of monetary policy
  - **Speaker:** Mykola Ryzhenkov
  - **Topic 2:** Consumer credit, aggregate fluctuations, and financial frictions (joint work with Jan Sun)
  - **Speaker:** Tsung-Hsien Li
