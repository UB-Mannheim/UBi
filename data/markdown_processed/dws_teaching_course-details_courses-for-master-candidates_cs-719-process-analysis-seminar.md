# Seminar on Process Analysis (CS 719)

This seminar focuses on state-of-the-art research related to process analysis. Key subjects covered include business process management, process mining, process analysis, stream processing, and robotic process automation.

## Course Goals

Upon completion of this seminar, students will be able to:

- Read, understand, and explore scientific literature based on an assigned topic.
- Write a concise report summarizing the state of the art in the assigned topic (10–12 pages, excluding references).
- Deliver a presentation on the topic (15 minutes presentation + 10 minutes Q&A).
- Discuss research results with fellow students.

## Organization and Instructors

This master's seminar is organized by Han van der Aa and [Alexander Kraus](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/researchers/phd-students/alexander-kraus/).

The seminar is offered in both the FSS and HWS locations.

## Prerequisites

To be eligible for the seminar, students must have completed or be currently taking either **IE692** (Advanced Process Mining) or **IS515** (Process Management & Analytics).

## Topics and Literature Survey

Participants will be assigned a topic that serves as a starting point for a literature survey. Students should indicate three preferred topics in a dedicated sign-up form (provided later).

The list of potential topics can be found [here (PDF, 123 kB)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Einrichtungen/dws/Files_Teaching/Seminar_CS719/topics_process_analysis_seminar_hws23.pdf).

Alternatively, students are free to propose their own topic/paper by reviewing publications from key venues such as:

- International conferences on Business Process Management (BPM).
- Advanced Information Systems Engineering (CAiSE).
- Process Mining (ICPM).
- Journals such as *Information Systems* and *Decision Support Systems*.
