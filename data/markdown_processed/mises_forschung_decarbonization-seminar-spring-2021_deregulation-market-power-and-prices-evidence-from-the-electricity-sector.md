# Deregulation, Market Power, and Prices: Evidence from the Electricity Sector

**Speaker:** Ignacia Mercadal
**Affiliation:** Columbia University
**Date:** Monday, April 19, 2021
**Format:** Live virtual event (Seminar Talk)

## Seminar Abstract

When deciding whether to introduce market-based prices into a regulated market, a regulator faces a critical trade-off. On one hand, profit incentives may reduce costs through the more efficient allocation of resources. On the other hand, the presence of market power may lead to increased markups.

This seminar uses a detailed dataset on electricity transactions to investigate the impact of market-based deregulation within the U.S. electricity sector. The findings indicate that the increase in markups dominates despite modest efficiency gains, ultimately leading to higher prices for consumers. The conclusion suggests that deregulation does not necessarily lead to lower prices, and a consumer-oriented regulator may prefer to regulate rates to be consumer-friendly rather than allowing prices to be subject to market power.

## Speaker Biography

**Ignacia Mercadal** is an assistant professor in the School of International and Public Affairs at Columbia University. Her research focuses on industrial organization and energy economics. She has previously served as a postdoctoral fellow at the MIT Center for Energy and Environmental Policy Research and the MIT Sloan School of Management. Mercadal holds a PhD in Economics from the University of Chicago, an MSc from Tilburg University, and an MA and a BA from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile.
