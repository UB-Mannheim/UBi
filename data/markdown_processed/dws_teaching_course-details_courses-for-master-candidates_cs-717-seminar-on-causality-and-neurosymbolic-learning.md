# Seminar CS717: Causality and Neurosymbolic Learning in Computer Vision

The Computer Vision seminar explores advanced topics in computer vision, focusing specifically on **Causality** and **Neurosymbolic Learning** in the context of vision systems. This seminar aims to move beyond simple pattern recognition, teaching students how to build systems that can reason, explain, and understand cause-and-effect relationships.

- **Causality:** Helps models understand cause-and-effect, leading to better generalization and more robust decision-making.
- **Neurosymbolic Learning:** Combines the power of deep learning (neural networks) with the structure of symbolic rules, enabling vision models to work with concepts, logic, and human-like reasoning.

Together, these concepts aim to make computer vision systems more robust, interpretable, and intelligent.

- Review a fellow student's report.

## 📝 Report Requirements

The structure and depth of the report depend on the student's level:

### For Master Students

The goal is to demonstrate a deep understanding of the assigned paper *within the context of the broader literature*. The emphasis must be on **critical synthesis and contextualization**.

**Key Expectations:**

- The reader must understand the paper’s main contributions and significance without external sources.
- The report must situate the paper within the existing literature (e.g., identifying previous works, problems addressed, or relations to other approaches).
- Students are expected to read and provide references beyond the assigned paper.

### For Bachelor Students

Students will be assigned a specific chapter from the textbook. The presentation and report must focus on the fundamental concepts of the overall topic, with particular emphasis on the assigned chapter content.

**Textbook Reference:** J. Peters, D. Janzing, and B. Schölkopf. *Elements of causal inference: foundations and learning algorithms*. The MIT Press, 2017.

**Assigned Chapters:**

- §2, §3: Introduction into Causality & Binary SCMs
- §4, §5: Learning Binary SCMs & Connections to ML
- §6.1 – §6.5: Multivariate SCMs (Part 1)
- §6.6 – §6.11: Multivariate SCMs (Part 2)
- §7: Learning Multivariate SCMs
- §8: Connections to ML
