# Yufang "Sabrina" Sun

**Role:** PhD Student, Business School
**Affiliation:** University of Mannheim

### 🎓 Academic Background & Profile

Yufang "Sabrina" Sun grew up in Dalian, a harbor city on the Chinese East Coast. She studied the business bachelor and MMM programs at the University of Mannheim. Prior to this, she obtained bachelor’s degrees in psychology and mathematics from Indiana University Bloomington. She previously worked as a full-time research fellow at Harvard University, investigating the social psychological underpinnings of institutional corruption.

During her master's thesis at the Chair of Financial Markets and Financial Institutions at Mannheim, Yufang developed a strong interest in the role of social preferences in economic behaviors—a line of research she plans to pursue during her doctoral studies. She aims to apply theories and methods from other social sciences, particularly social psychology theories and experimental methods, to the study of economic and financial decision-making.

*Aside from her academic pursuits, Yufang is passionate about creating fusion cuisine and introducing children to Chinese language and culture.*

### 🔬 Research Interests

- Social preferences in economic behaviors
- Experimental methods
