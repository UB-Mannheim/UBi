## 📝 Seminar Abstract

Financial investors play a central role in financing the clean energy transition. However, their impact on decarbonization extends beyond financial markets to politics. This seminar explores how investors shape political support for, and opposition to, decarbonization policy.

## 👤 Speaker Biography

**Jonas Meckling** is an Associate Professor of Energy and Environmental Policy at the University of California, Berkeley, and a Climate Fellow at Harvard Business School.

At Berkeley, he leads the Energy and Environment Policy Lab and the Climate Program of the Berkeley Economy and Society Initiative. Dr. Meckling specializes in the politics of climate policy and the energy transition, with a specific focus on green industrial strategy.

He is a published author of two books and frequently publishes his research in leading journals, including *Nature Climate Change*, *Nature Energy*, and *Science*. He has received multiple awards, including the American Political Science Association's Emerging Young Scholar Award in the field of science, technology, and environmental politics.

His professional background includes roles as a visiting professor at Yale University, Senior Advisor to the German Minister for the Environment and Renewable Energy, a Research Fellow at Harvard University, and time working at the European Commission. He holds a Ph.D. from the London School of Economics.
