# E701 Advanced Microeconomics I

This course provides a graduate-level introduction to microeconomic theory, specifically tailored for doctoral students in all fields of business administration. It emphasizes aspects of microeconomics that are highly relevant for advanced business research.

## Course Objectives and Learning Outcomes

The primary objective is to equip doctoral students with the necessary tools to read, discuss, and formulate their own research based on theoretically grounded hypotheses.

**Learning Outcomes:**

- Understanding and critically evaluating the fundamental concepts of microeconomic theory, game theory, and mechanism design.
- Acquiring relevant tools and underlying assumptions necessary for economic analysis in ongoing research.

## Core Topics Covered

The curriculum covers advanced topics across several key areas of economic theory:

- Theory of consumer choice (under certainty and uncertainty)
- Theory of the firm, production cost, and supply
- Markets, equilibrium, and welfare analysis
- Strategic behavior (under complete and incomplete information)
- Incentives and asymmetric information

## Course Details

- **Target Audience:** Doctoral students in all fields of business administration.
- **Credits:** 8 ECTS
- **Assessment Form:**
  - Midterm and final exams: 80–100%
  - Exercises: Up to 20%
