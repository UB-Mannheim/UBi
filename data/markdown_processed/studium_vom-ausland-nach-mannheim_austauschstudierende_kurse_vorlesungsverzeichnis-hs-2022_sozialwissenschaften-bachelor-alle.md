# Exchange Student Course Catalog: Social Sciences (Fall 2022)

This document outlines the course offerings and requirements for incoming exchange students in Political Science, Sociology, and Psychology at the University of Mannheim.

## 🎓 General Exchange Student Guidelines

**General Enrollment Rules:**

- **Political Science (Master):** Courses are usually open only for incoming exchange students majoring in Political Science or for exchange students at the School of Social Sciences (Sociology, Psychology).
- **Sociology (Bachelor):** Courses are usually open only for incoming exchange students majoring in Sociology or for exchange students at the School of Social Sciences (Political Science, Psychology).
- **Psychology (Bachelor):** Courses are usually open only for incoming exchange students majoring in Psychology.
- **Eligibility for Other Students:** Exchange students from other schools and departments may only attend classes if:
  1. Places are left for other students.
  1. They have basic knowledge in the relevant subject area (e.g., Political Science and Statistics, or Sociology and Statistics).
  1. The departmental exchange coordinator explicitly approves their participation.

**Coordinator Contact:**

- **Political Science:** int-pol@uni-mannheim.de
- **Sociology:** International.sowi@uni-mannheim.de
- **Psychology:** International.sowi@uni-mannheim.de

**Course Selection Timeline:**

- **Spring/Summer Semester:** Nominated exchange students will be contacted by their departmental exchange coordinator via e-mail at the end of November/early December.
- **Fall/Winter Semester:** Nominated exchange students will be contacted by their departmental exchange coordinator via e-mail by the end of May/early June.

## 📚 Departmental Course Offerings

### Political Science (Master)

**Course Descriptions:**

- **Campaigns and Voting Behavior:** This seminar studies the role campaigns play in affecting voting behavior. Using different theoretical frameworks, it analyzes campaigns' effects on political opinion formation, electoral turnout, and vote choice.
- **Representation in Parliaments:** This seminar provides a close look at representation in democratic parliaments. It covers prominent theories of political representation, incentives for politicians, and the representation of underrepresented groups (e.g., women, migrants, LGBTQ+, youth). It also addresses factors that potentially hinder representation, such as lobbying and outside earnings.
- **Final Paper:** Students must prepare a research question related to the course topic and demonstrate the ability to write a literature review.
  - **Prerequisites:**
    1. Knowledge of basic literature in international relations and comparative politics (BA lectures).
    1. Methodological knowledge of research design and statistics (respective courses).
    1. High interest in cutting-edge political science research (published in international leading scholarly journals).
  - **Advanced Skills:** Ability to understand and select political science studies on European integration and the European Union, and excellent knowledge of the state of research on main approaches to European integration.

**Key Literature:**

- Dalton, R. J. (2018). *Citizen politics: Public opinion and political parties in advanced industrial democracies*. Cq Press.
- Dassonneville, R. (2022). *Voters Under Pressure: Group-Based Cross-Pressure and Electoral Volatility*. Oxford University Press.
- Fisher, J., Fieldhouse, E., Franklin, M. N., Gibson, R. K., Cantijoch, M., & Wlezien, C. (Eds.). (2018). *The Routledge handbook of elections, voting behavior and public opinion* (pp. 280–292). London: Routledge.
- Burnell, P. J., Rakner, L., & Randall, V. (Eds.). (2017). *Politics in the developing world*. Oxford University Press.
- Todaro, Michael P., and Stephen C. Smith. (2020). *Economic Development*. Thirteenth Edition. Hoboken: Pearson.

### Psychology (Bachelor)

**Course Descriptions:**

- **Memory and Cognition:** This course investigates memory for truth and falsity, focusing particularly on forgetting, inhibitory processes, and negation.
- **Experimental Design:** Participants are divided into small groups, each responsible for one study. Groups collaboratively develop the research question and select the appropriate experimental design. Findings are presented as a poster and summarized in an experimental report written by each participant.
- **Cognitive Processing:** This seminar introduces and discusses processes involved in encoding visual information, such as attention, visual grouping, object perception, and short-term memory storage.
- **Trauma and Dissociation:** This seminar offers an in-depth look at the phenomenology, etiology, diagnostics, and treatment of trauma-related disorders (focus: PTSD) and dissociative disorders. Students gain theoretical knowledge and practical experience in diagnosis and treatment.
- **Evaluation in Education:** This seminar aims to provide a fundamental understanding of evaluating psycho-pedagogical intervention measures, including methods of individual assessment (e.g., evaluation of the educational system, national/international achievement studies).
- **Digital Learning:** This seminar provides an in-depth understanding of the role and impact of digitalization on learning processes, covering the intersection of educational psychology and technology (e.g., AI, educational games, online self-reflection).
- **Learning Psychology:** This seminar focuses on the deepening of general topics in educational psychology, covering motivational, cognitive, and social factors of learning, including self-regulated learning, Expectancy-Value Theory, and pedagogical-psychological diagnostics.
- **Diversity and Equality:** This seminar discusses aspects of equality and diversity in the workplace, focusing on gender effects, diversity in work teams, and the role of organizational DEI (Diversity, Equity & Inclusion) programs.

**Key Literature:**

- Beller, S. & Bender, A. (2010). *Allgemeine Psychologie – Denken und Sprache*. Göttingen: Hogrefe.
- Bröder, A. & Hilbig, B. E. (2024). Urteilen und Entscheiden. In M. Rieger & J. Müsseler (Hrsg.), *Allgemeine Psychologie* (4. Aufl., S. 743–790). Berlin, Heidelberg: Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-662-68476-4_18.
