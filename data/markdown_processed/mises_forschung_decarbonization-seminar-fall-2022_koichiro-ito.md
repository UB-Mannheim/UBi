# Koichiro Ito: Market Integration and Renewable Energy Expansion in Chile

**Topic:** The Dynamic Impact of Market Integration: Evidence from Renewable Energy Expansion in Chile
**Date & Time:** November 14, 2022 – 05:15 – 06:30 PM (CET)
**Format:** Online only.

### Seminar Abstract

This seminar studies the static and dynamic impacts of market integration on renewable energy expansion. The theory highlights that, statically, market integration improves allocative efficiency through gains from trade. Dynamically, it incentivizes the new entry of renewable power plants.

Using two recent grid expansions in the Chilean electricity market, the presentation empirically tests these theoretical predictions. The research demonstrates that commonly-used event study estimation underestimates the dynamic benefits if renewable investments occur in anticipation of market integration. The study builds a structural model of power plant entry to correct for this bias.

**Key Findings:**

- Market integration resulted in price convergence across regions.
- It led to increases in renewable generation and decreases in generation cost and pollution emissions.
- A substantial amount of renewable entry would not have occurred in the absence of market integration.
- Ignoring this dynamic effect would substantially understate the benefits of transmission investments.

## Speaker Biography

**Koichiro Ito** is an Associate Professor at the University of Chicago Harris School of Public Policy.

**Education:**

- BA from Kyoto University
- MA from University of British Columbia
- PhD from the University of California, Berkeley

**Experience:**
Prior to joining the University of Chicago, he served as a SIEPR Postdoctoral Fellow at Stanford University and an Assistant Professor at Boston University.

**Research Interests:**
His research focuses on the intersection of environmental and energy economics, industrial organization, and public economics. Specific areas of interest include:

- How consumers respond to nonlinear pricing, dynamic pricing, and rebate programs in electricity markets.
- How intrinsic and extrinsic motivation affects economic decisions.
- How firms strategically react to attribute-based regulation (e.g., fuel economy standards).
- How firms respond to dynamic incentives in sequential forward markets in wholesale electricity markets.

His research methodology utilizes randomized field experiments and quasi-experimental designs to address policy-relevant questions in energy and environmental policy.
