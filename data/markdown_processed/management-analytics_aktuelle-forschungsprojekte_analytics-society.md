# Analytics & Society

This section presents current research projects exploring the intersection of data analytics and social structures.

## Friends with Drugs – The Role of Networks in the Spread of the Opioid Epidemic

**Authors:**

- [Kai Maeckle](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/gess/programs/cdsb/our-students/2019/kai-maeckle/)
- [Stefan Ruenzi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/ruenzi/team/prof-dr-stefan-ruenzi/)

**Cooperation Partners:**

- Chair of International Finance

**Description:**
The study examines the role of social connections in the spatial spread of the opioid epidemic, described as the most severe drug crisis in U.S. history. The research finds a positive correlation between having many friendship links to counties with high opioid exposure and increased overdose death rates. To establish causality, the study exploits the introduction of must-access Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs. The findings indicate that having more friends in counties affected by these shocks leads to more overdoses, but also show that strong local social networks can mitigate such negative spillovers. Overall, the results demonstrate how social connections contribute to the spread of non-infectious diseases.

## Helpful or Harmful? Negative Behavior Toward Newcomers and Welfare in Online Communities

**Authors:**

- Florian Pethig
- Kai-Lung Hui
- Andreas Lanz
- [Hartmut Höhle](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/)

**Cooperation Partners:**

- Hong Kong University of Science and Technology
- HEC Paris

**Description:**
While new members are crucial for the survival of online communities, hostility toward newcomers is often pronounced, manifesting through downvotes, rejections, and negative comments from established members. This paper investigates a new intervention designed to reduce this hostility: a "newcomer nudge." This nudge informs members when they are interacting with a newcomer post and encourages them to be more lenient. Using a natural experiment research design, the study analyzed 5,027 newcomer posts published in a 90-day window before and after the nudge's introduction. The results showed a strong increase in upvotes and responses, alongside a decrease in negative sentiment. Furthermore, the panel data analysis revealed that newcomers socialized with the nudge were significantly more likely to post again in the following 12 months compared to those socialized without it.
