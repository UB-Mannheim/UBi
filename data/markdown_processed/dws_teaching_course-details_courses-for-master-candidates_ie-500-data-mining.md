# Data Mining (FSS 2026)

This course provides an introduction to advanced data analysis techniques, serving as a foundation for analyzing business data and providing input for decision support systems.

## Course Content and Structure

The curriculum covers the following core topics:

- The Data Mining Process
- Data Representation and Preprocessing
- Classification
- Regression
- Clustering
- Association Analysis

The course structure combines a lecture component with accompanying practical exercises and culminates in student team projects.

- **Practical Exercises:** Participants gain initial expertise by applying state-of-the-art data mining libraries to realistic datasets.
- **Team Projects:** In the final third of the term, groups undertake sophisticated data mining projects of their choice. Students must report their findings through a written report and an oral presentation.

## Logistics and Requirements

### Instructors

- **Prof. Christian Bizer:** Lecture
- **Gastprofessor Dr. Ralph Peeters:** Exercise and Project
- **Aaron Steiner:** Exercise and Project

### Grading Breakdown

- **Written Exam:** 75% (Only a single exam is offered; no re-take is possible.)
- **Project Work:** 25% (20% report, 5% presentation)

### Registration

- **Lecture:** Registration is required via Portal 2. The course is limited to 90 participants. Priority is given to students in higher semesters and those who have previously failed the course.
- **Exercise:** No registration is required for the exercise component.
