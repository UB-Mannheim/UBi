# Finance Program and Research Areas

The field of Finance is highly interdisciplinary, combining strong theoretical foundations, broad empirical bases, and significant practical relevance. It addresses fundamental problems of individual decisions regarding risky financial instruments. From classical and behavioral perspectives, the field analyzes the existence, optimal management, and regulation of financial institutions (like banks and insurance companies), and studies the structure and valuation of optimal financial contracts under various market regimes.

Methodologically, Finance successfully integrates advanced mathematical modeling techniques, numerical solution procedures, econometric estimation methods, and statistical testing. Its strong empirical orientation is supported by excellent market databases, which are often supplemented by data on individual decision-making and non-traded financial contracts, all available at the University of Mannheim.

## Research Fields

The research conducted by the [Finance Group](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/finance/) covers all major areas of finance. The faculty includes experts such as [Albrecht](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/finance/), [Bucher-Koenen](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/bucher-koenen/), [Maug](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/maug/), [Niessen-Ruenzi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/niessen-ruenzi/), [Ruenzi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/ruenzi/), [Spalt](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/spalt/), [Theissen](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/theissen/), [von Thadden](https://www.vwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/von-thadden/), and [Weber](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/weber/).

Key areas of research include:

### Asset Pricing

This area focuses on theories of fair values for financial instruments and the impact of price, credit, and liquidity risk on asset prices. It aims to deepen the understanding of price movements in capital markets.

### Behavioral Finance

Behavioral Finance examines how individuals make decisions and build expectations. While initially focused on retail investors, the field now increasingly analyzes the behavior of corporate managers and professional investors.

### Corporate Finance and Governance

Corporate Finance addresses the optimal structuring of contracts involving agents, equity, and debt holders, particularly under conditions of symmetric and asymmetric information. Corporate Governance, relatedly, focuses on the rules and regulations that govern a firm and its optimal control.

### Investment Management

This field examines optimal investment strategies for both individual investors and institutions. It also analyzes the dynamics and challenges that arise when investors delegate investment decisions (e.g., to mutual funds or hedge funds).

### Risk Management

Closely linked to asset pricing, banking, and corporate finance, Risk Management studies the exposure of financial and non-financial firms to various risks, including market, credit, liquidity, operational, and economic risk.

## Program Structure

The curriculum is designed to reflect the demanding quantitative methods used in these fields, built upon strong microeconomic foundations.

**First Semester (Foundations):**
The core courses provide students with a strong foundation in relevant mathematical, econometric, and microeconomic principles of finance. The course on “Discrete-Time Finance” covers central topics such as consumption and investment allocation from a financial viewpoint, no-arbitrage equilibria, linear pricing rules, pricing kernels, and the change of numéraire.

**Second Semester (Specialization):**
The second semester courses represent the major subfields of Finance. Topics include:

- Individual decision-making (under neoclassical and behavioral views).
- Investment-consumption decisions and asset pricing in continuous time.
- Theoretical and empirical corporate finance problems.

The course “Econometrics of Financial Markets” extends general econometrics to specific financial issues, such as estimating dynamic market processes, testing asset pricing and market microstructure models, and designing event studies.

**Research Seminar Series:**
Throughout both semesters, students participate in the Finance research seminar series. This feature provides regular presentations by renowned researchers from top global universities, allowing students to engage with cutting-edge research from the outset.
