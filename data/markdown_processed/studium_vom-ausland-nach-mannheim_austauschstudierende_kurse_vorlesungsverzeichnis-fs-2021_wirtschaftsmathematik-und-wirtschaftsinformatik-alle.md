# Curriculum Overview: Business Informatics and Mathematics for Exchange Students

This document outlines the course catalog and required competencies for exchange students pursuing studies in Business Informatics and Mathematics at the University of Mannheim. The curriculum is structured across Bachelor and Master levels, covering core areas from algorithms and data science to advanced modeling and programming.

## 📚 Degree Programs and Core Topics

### 🎓 Bachelor Level Programs

#### 1. Wirtschaftsinformatik (Business Informatics)

This track focuses on the intersection of business processes and information technology.

**Core Topics:**

- **Algorithms & Data Structures:**
  - Grundtechniken des Algorithmenentwurfs sowie der Laufzeitanalyse (Divide and Conquer, Greedyheuristiken, Dynamic Programming,…)
  - Grundtechniken des Beweisens der Korrektheit von Algorithmen
  - Sortieralgorithmen, Hashing und hashingbasierte Algorithmen
  - Advanced Data Structures, Algorithmen für Suchbäume, Graphalgorithmen (Tiefensuche, Breitensuche, Minimum Spanning Trees, Kürzeste-Wege-Algorithmen)
  - Ausgewählte weitere Algorithmen (z. B. Pattern Matching, Automatenminimierung…)
- **Modeling & Systems:**
  - Business Process Modelling (Model complex business processes based on popular modelling techniques)
  - Application Systems in Research and Development, Marketing and Sales, Procurement and Warehousing, Production, Shipping and Customer Service, Finance, Accounting, HR
  - Planning and Control Systems
- **Foundational Theory:**
  - Grundlagen Aussagenlogik (Folgern, Beweisen)
  - Mengen, Relationen, Abbildungen, Grundlagen der Kombinatorik
  - Einführung Graphentheorie, Algebraische Strukturen (Halbgruppen, Gruppen, Homorphismen, Faktorstrukturen)
  - Grundlegende Berechnungsmodelle/Endliche Automaten
- **Advanced Topics:**
  - Problemeigenschaften und Problemtypen, Problemlösen als Suche (Anwendung im Bereich Computerspiele), Constraintprobleme und deren Lösung, Logische Constraints
- **Mathematics (Optional/Related):**
  - Basic notions of partial differential equations, method of characteristics, Laplace equations, heat equations, wave equation

  - Planning and Control Systems

### 🎓 Master Level Programs

#### 1. Wirtschaftsinformatik (Business Informatics)

Focuses on advanced data management, data mining, and knowledge representation.

**Core Topics:**

- **Data Mining & Analytics:**
  - Goals and Principles of Data Mining
  - Data Representation and Preprocessing
  - Clustering, Classification, Association Analysis, Text Mining
  - Systems and Applications (e. g. Retail, Finance, Web Analysis)
- **Knowledge Representation:**
  - Vision and Principles of the Semantic Web
  - Representation Languages (XML, RDF, RDF Schema, OWL)
  - Knowledge Modeling: Ontologies and Linked Data
  - Logical Reasoning in RDF and OWL
  - Commercial and Open Source Tools and Systems
- **Data Integration:**
  - Heterogeneity and Distributedness
  - The Data Integration Process
  - Web Data Formats, Schema Matching and Data Translation, Identity Resolution, Data Quality Assessment, Data Fusion
- **Advanced Theory:**
  - Decision Theory, Decision- and Business Rules, Planning Methods and Algorithms, Probabilistic Graphical Models, Game Theory and Mechanism Design

## 💻 Core Skills and Competencies (Consolidated)

The curriculum emphasizes the development of both technical and soft skills.

### Methodological Competence

- Ability to judge, select, and use traditional or non-traditional data management systems for a given data management task.
- Ability to solve computational problems involving large datasets.

### Technical Skills (Programming & Algorithms)

- **Programming:** Deep knowledge of Java basics, understanding of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) concepts (Inheritance, Classes, Interfaces, Exceptions), and proficiency in designing complex algorithms in Java without relying on imported methods.
- **Algorithms:** Ability to design and analyze algorithms (e.g., using Divide and Conquer, Dynamic Programming, Graph Algorithms).
- **Modeling:** Proficiency in modeling complex business processes and systems using techniques like UML (Class Diagrams, Activity Diagrams, State Diagrams).

### Personal Competence (Soft Skills)

- Independent study and self-management.
- Teamwork skills.
- Presentation and writing skills.
