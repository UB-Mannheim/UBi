# Knowledge Graphs (HWS 2025)

Knowledge graphs are a universal means of data representation that can be consumed by humans and machines alike. They are a key ingredient in many modern data-driven systems and AI applications, often requiring knowledge about the domain they operate in or general world knowledge.

Knowledge graphs are increasingly used in large organizations, with well-known applications such as the Google Knowledge Graph. Furthermore, large-scale open knowledge graphs like DBpedia or Wikidata are available for free use in powerful AI applications.

## Course Content

This course provides an introduction to the underlying standards of knowledge graphs, covering knowledge representation, query languages, and logical inference. Specific topics include:

- History of Knowledge Graphs and the Semantic Web
- Graph Representation Languages (XML, RDF)
- Graph Inference and Logical Reasoning (RDF Schema, OWL)
- Knowledge Modeling: Ontologies, Linked Data, and Property Graphs
- Knowledge Integration
- Data Quality in Knowledge Graphs
- Commercial and Open Source Tools and Systems

The course structure combines theoretical lectures with hands-on exercises and a practical project where students build a small knowledge graph-backed application.

## Requirements and Grading

**Prerequisites:**

- Java or Python programming skills are required.
- Experience with software development is preferred.

**Passing Requirements:**
To pass the course, students must fulfill two mandatory requirements:

1. Pass the final exam (a grade of 4.0 or better is required).
1. Successfully work in a group on a project idea (programming!), present the results, and write a report.

*Note: The final grade is determined by the final exam, but the project is mandatory for passing.*
