# Capturing the Economic Dynamics of Competing Power Generation Sources

**Event Details**

- **Speaker:** Gunther Glenk
- **Affiliation:** University of Mannheim (Assistant Professor for Accounting)
- **Date:** March 7, 2022
- **Format:** Live virtual event (Registration required via Zoom)

## Seminar Abstract

Competing power generation sources have experienced considerable shifts in both their revenue potential and their costs in recent years. This seminar introduces the concept of **Levelized Profit Margins (LPM)** to capture the changing unit economics of both intermittent and dispatchable generation technologies.

The framework is applied to the context of the California and Texas wholesale power markets. Key findings include:

- **Solar and Wind Power:** LPM estimates indicate that solar photovoltaic and wind power have substantially improved their competitive position between 2012 and 2019, primarily due to falling life-cycle costs of production.
- **California Market:** The gains in solar and wind power far outweigh an emerging "cannibalization" effect, which occurs when substantial additions of solar power make energy less valuable during the middle of the day.
- **Natural Gas:** The competitiveness of natural gas power plants has either improved or remained steady. For these plants, declining capacity utilization rates have been effectively counterbalanced by a "dispatchability price premium" that reflects the growing market share of intermittent renewables.

## Speaker Biography: Gunther Glenk

Gunther Glenk is an Assistant Professor for Accounting at the University of Mannheim. His research focuses on corporate decarbonization, examining how companies:

1. Calculate their carbon footprint.
1. Set appropriate targets for emission reductions.
1. Achieve these targets in a cost-efficient manner.

His recent work has concentrated on the competitiveness of clean energy technologies, including energy storage, zero-emission mobility services, and hydrogen production from intermittent renewable energy sources. His research has been published in leading academic journals in Sustainability Management and has received multiple awards. He holds a BSc, MSc, and Doctorate in Management and Technology from the Technical University of Munich.
