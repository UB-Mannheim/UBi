## Advanced Computing Architectures for IoT and Edge Environments

This document describes research into optimizing computing resources for resource-constrained devices, focusing on Computation Offloading and the management of complex Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS).

### 🌐 Computation Offloading for Edge Computing

**Authors:** Martin Breitbach, Christian Becker
**Cooperation Partner:** Prof. Janick Edinger, Universität Hamburg

**Description:**
The increasing demand for computational power in fields like machine learning and computer vision necessitates advanced solutions. Computation offloading addresses this by allowing resource-constrained devices (such as smartphones) to offload intensive workloads to remote resource providers, either in the cloud or at the edge.

This process not only accelerates computationally intensive tasks but also significantly reduces the energy consumption of the offloading device.

**Project Focus: Tasklet**
The Tasklet project is a DFG-funded initiative dedicated to developing a computation offloading system for fast and energy-efficient edge computing. The Tasklet system is designed to enable heterogeneous devices to offload arbitrary tasks to a heterogeneous pool of resources, making it highly attractive for various Internet of Things (IoT) applications.

### 🧠 Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) Management

**Author:** Melanie Feist

**Description:**
As computing devices become increasingly mobile, the research area of Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) has emerged from Pervasive/Ubiquitous Computing and the Internet-of-Things domain. CPS are now omnipresent, found in applications such as smart home environments, remote patient monitoring, and traffic management.

**Challenges in Mixed-Critical Networks:**
In these scenarios, multiple CPSs cooperate and interact. A major challenge is the management of Mixed-Critical Cyber-Physical Networks (MC-CPNs), where tasks with different criticality levels must be handled simultaneously within the network.

**Research Goal:**
To simplify the design, operation, and maintenance of MC-CPNs, a comprehensive approach is needed to handle their complexity autonomously at runtime. The goal is to develop learning self-adaptation mechanisms that optimize task execution based on their Quality-of-Service (QoS) requirements and criticality level. This capability is encapsulated within a middleware framework, ensuring a robust and flexible management layer for MC-CPNs.
