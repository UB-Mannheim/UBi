# Nader Hotait

**Role:** Doctoral Student (PhD Candidate)
**Center:** Social Sciences
**Supervisor:** [Frank Kalter](https://www.sowi.uni-mannheim.de/kalter/)
**Affiliation:** University of Mannheim, Mannheim

## Academic Profile and Experience

Nader Hotait is a PhD candidate in Sociology at the CDSS. His academic journey includes studies at the University of Augsburg and Humboldt University of Berlin, culminating in a Master of Arts in Sociology from the University of Potsdam, which was completed with distinction. His Master's thesis focused on unifying methodology and standard causal analysis using German sociological organizations as an empirical example.

His professional experience includes roles at:

- Berlin Institute for Integration and Migration Research (BIM) at Humboldt University of Berlin (currently working as a research associate).
- University of Potsdam.
- Berlin Social Science Center (WZB).
- German Center for Integration and Migration Research (DeZIM).

He has participated in various research projects, including D:ISLAM, DIGITSOC, PolDem, and #AFROZENSUS.

## Research Focus

Nader's research interests are highly interdisciplinary, combining social science theory with advanced computational methods.

**Topical Interests:**

- Religious communities
- Race and migration
- Health
- Relational social theories
- Computational social sciences
- Social inequality and stratification

**Methodological Interests:**

- Causality and inference
- Machine learning (ML)
- Natural language processing (NLP)
- Multilevel and panel models
- Clustering and multiway methods

**PhD Project:**
Nader's doctoral research investigates the strategies of radicalization—both by and against—the Muslim community from Islamists, examining these dynamics both online and offline.
