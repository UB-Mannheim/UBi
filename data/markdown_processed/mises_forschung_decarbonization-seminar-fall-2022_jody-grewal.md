## Seminar Abstract

This seminar investigates whether mandatory carbon reporting reduces the selective disclosure of favorable versus unfavorable environmental information.

**Key Findings:**

- **Mandatory Carbon Reporting (MCR):** The study examines the impact of regulations mandating firms to report carbon emissions.
- **Selective Disclosure:** Measuring selective disclosure as the difference between a firm's actual disclosure and the portion indicative of its true environmental impact, the research finds that MCR significantly reduces selective carbon disclosure. This suggests that mandated reporting forces firms to disclose relevant carbon information that they previously withheld when disclosure was voluntary.
- **Broader Impact:** MCR also curtails firms’ selective reporting of other, non-carbon environmental information disclosed voluntarily.
- **Behavioral Change:** Further analysis shows that worse carbon performers had higher levels of selective carbon disclosure before MCR. Their poor performance motivates them to decrease selective non-carbon disclosure even more after MCR.
- **Conclusion:** The reduction in selective disclosure opportunities under mandated reporting induces firms to make real changes, leading to a reduction in their carbon emissions. The study is highly relevant for policymakers considering mandatory reporting to mitigate greenwashing and address climate change.

## Speaker Biography

Jody Grewal is an Assistant Professor of Accounting in the Department of Management and the Institute for Management & Innovation at the University of Toronto Mississauga, with a cross appointment to the Accounting Area at the Rotman School of Management.

**Research Focus:** Jody examines the capital market effects and real effects (i.e., changes in behavior through investment and use of resources) of financial and sustainability information disclosed voluntarily and under mandatory reporting regimes.

**Recognition:** Her research is published in leading journals such as *Management Science*. She has been featured in media outlets including the *Wall Street Journal* and *Bloomberg*.

**Education:**

- Doctorate from Harvard Business School
- Master of Accounting and Bachelor of Accounting and Financial Management from the University of Waterloo
- Chartered Professional Accountant in Ontario
