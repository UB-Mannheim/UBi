# Seminar on Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models (FSS 2026)

Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, represent one of the most significant advances in Artificial Intelligence. While versatile, they can exhibit drawbacks like hallucinations, lack of transparency, and non-factuality. Knowledge Graphs (KGs), conversely, are key drivers of "white box AI," known for their transparency and factual basis.

This seminar explores the intersection of Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models, aiming to combine the strengths of both technologies.

### Areas of Interest

The seminar will cover, but is not limited to, the following advanced topics:

- KGs for LLM training and tuning
- KG Enhanced LLM inference and explanations
- Prompt engineering with KGs
- Completing/Refining KGs with LLMs
- Question Answering/Text Generation with KGs and LLMs
- Joint Reasoning with KGs and LLMs

## Seminar Details

### Goals

Participants will become familiar with various approaches to knowledge graph construction. The learning process involves reading research papers, specifications, and tool descriptions, conducting personal experiments where applicable, and discussing insights with peers.

**Participation Requirement:** Each participant is expected to introduce and present a specific technique for knowledge graph construction to the seminar group. All seminar papers undergo a peer review process. Presentations are scheduled for approximately 25 minutes.
