## Seminar Abstract

Net-zero portfolios (NZP), which aim to reduce carbon footprint exposure to zero by a target date, are becoming a popular vehicle to align investors' incentives with climate scenarios. This seminar characterizes the decision and timing to divest companies from NZP using a novel forward-looking measure: **distance-to-exit (DTE)**.

DTE calculates the distance, in years, until a company gets excluded from NZP. The research finds that companies with greater DTE values have higher valuation ratios and lower expected returns, consistent with the idea that DTE captures carbon-transition risk. This effect is stronger when climate pressure intensifies and remains robust to various specification choices.

## Speaker Biography: Professor Marcin Kacperczyk

Professor Marcin Kacperczyk is a Professor of Finance at the Imperial College London Business School and a Research Fellow at the Centre for Economic Policy and Research.

**Expertise:**
Professor Kacperczyk’s research primarily focuses on financial markets, financial intermediation, and asset management. His latest work analyzes the effects of unconventional monetary policy on the asset management industry and corporate sector. He has also written on a broad range of topics, including social norms, short-term debt markets, performance evaluation, labor unions, insider trading, income inequality, and portfolio choice.

**Publications and Recognition:**
His articles have appeared in top-tier journals such as *Econometrica*, *Journal of Finance*, *Journal of Financial Economics*, *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, and *Review of Finance*. He has also contributed to non-academic outlets including *Bloomberg*, *Financial Times*, *Forbes*, *New York Times*, *Wall Street Journal*, and *Business Week*.

**Academic History:**

- **Teaching:** Has been teaching at Imperial College since 2013.
- **Previous Appointments:** University of British Columbia Sauder School of Business and New York University Stern School of Business.
- **Education:** Obtained a Ph.D. in finance from the Ross School of Business at the University of Michigan.
- **Awards:** Received two student awards for Excellence in Teaching and two Faculty Excellence Awards from Imperial College.
- **Grants:** Currently holds two highly prestigious research grants: European Research Council Consolidator Grant and Marie Curie Integration Grant.
