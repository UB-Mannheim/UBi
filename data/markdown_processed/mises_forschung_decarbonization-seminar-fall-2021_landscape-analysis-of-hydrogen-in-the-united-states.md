# Landscape Analysis of Hydrogen in the United States

**Speaker:** Stephen Comello
**Affiliation:** Stanford University
**Date:** December 6, 2021
**Event Type:** Live virtual seminar (Registration no longer available)

### Speaker Biography

Stephen Comello is the director of the Energy Business Innovations focus area at Stanford Graduate School of Business and a senior research fellow at the Steyer-Taylor Center for Energy, Policy and Finance at Stanford University. He also serves as a techno-economic and policy lead for Stanford University’s Bits & Watts Initiative and StorageX Initiative, and is a co-instructor for the Building Energy Efficiency online course. His research focuses broadly on energy technology economics, policy, and innovation. Specifically, he investigates the innovation and competitiveness of low-carbon energy solutions in both developed and emerging economies by applying tools and methods from finance, economics, and systems analysis.

### Seminar Abstract

This seminar provides an industry analysis to understand the current and potential future states of the hydrogen supply chain in the United States. The core questions addressed are: (i) to what extent is hydrogen a decarbonization option for the United States, and (ii) how might this manifest across various industries.

**Methodology:**
The analysis employs qualitative social research, emphasizing contextual accuracy through direct observation and communication with participants. The primary tool used is the semi-structured interviewing approach. The research was based on 72 interviews conducted between April and July 2021, yielding over 55 hours of discussion. This analysis is further supported by reviewing cost, business model, and policy literature.

**Key Findings:**
The seminar yields three main types of findings:

1. **Cross-Cutting Themes:** Eight general themes are presented to synthesize the current state and future outlook of hydrogen in the United States.
1. **Industry View:** An assessment of the extent to which value chains and specific components within them have reached commercial viability, identifying areas for further development.
1. **Firm-Level View:** A description of generalized business models for currently viable and rapidly emerging hydrogen value chains.
