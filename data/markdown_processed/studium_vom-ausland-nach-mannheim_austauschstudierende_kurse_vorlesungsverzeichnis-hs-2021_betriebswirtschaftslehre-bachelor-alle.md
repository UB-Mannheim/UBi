# Course Catalog for Exchange Students (Business School)

## Course Selection Guidelines

The selection of suitable courses depends on your current academic level:

- **Bachelor's Students:** You must select courses only from the bachelor's level curriculum. Master's level courses are generally not open to undergraduate students. Exceptions are only considered if you are in your 4th year of an undergraduate program when you arrive in Mannheim.
- **Master's/MBA Students:** You are eligible to choose Business courses from all master's level courses. If your home university permits you to take selected bachelor's level courses, these are also available to you.

For specific questions regarding course selection, please contact the Business School Exchange Team at incoming.business@uni-mannheim.de.

### Area Finance

- Demonstrate a thorough understanding of the characteristics of international financial securities and the main theories of pricing currency derivatives (forwards, futures, options, swaps).
- Exhibit strong ability to apply pricing models of currency derivatives and financial risk management to decision making in international finance.
- Critically evaluate and analyze the concepts of arbitrage, speculation, hedging, and asset replication within different cases of international financial transactions.
- Spot exchange markets, arbitrage, and least cost dealing.
- Forward contracts in perfect and imperfect markets.
- Currency futures: hedging and speculation methods; risk management using futures.
- Currency options: basic market mechanism and put-call parity.
- Pricing currency options: the use of binomial, Black-Scholes, and log-normal models.
- Swap contracts.
- Credit derivatives, financial innovation, and securitization.
