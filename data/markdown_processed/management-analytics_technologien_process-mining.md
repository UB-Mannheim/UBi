# Research Projects: Applying Process Mining to Business Transformation

This document summarizes several academic research projects demonstrating the application of Process Mining techniques across various critical business functions, including tax management, procurement, and production process analysis. These projects aim to identify, measure, and eliminate inefficiencies using data-driven methods.

## 📊 Digitalization of the Tax Function

This project addresses the increasing regulatory requirements faced by tax departments, which necessitate the timely processing of both structured and unstructured tax data.

- **Goal:** To develop new concepts for the digital transformation of the tax function and optimize processes through digital means.
- **Methodology:** The project focuses on enhancing existing tax information systems and intragroup information processing. It aims to develop data-driven intelligent assistant functions using **Process Mining**, **Business Intelligence**, and general **Data Science** principles.
- **Authors:** [Christoph Spengel](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/spengel/), Alexander Maedche, Sven Scheu, Sarah Winter
- **Cooperation Partners:** Institute of Information Systems and Marketing (IISM) at KIT, EnBW AG

## 🏭 Process Mining in Production and Error Analysis

This research examines how employee behavior responds to errors (reclamations) when monitored by emergent technologies.

- **Goal:** To measure production-level behavior of workers and broaden the understanding of employee responses to errors.
- **Methodology:** Using **Process Mining**, the team analyzes core-production data from a large company over a three-year period. The analysis employs a **Difference-in-Difference (DID)** design and combines statistical methods with process mining applications.
- **Data Scope:** Over 1.4 million production cases were collected via the company’s ERP system, requiring the development of 500+ lines of SQL code and a Java program for data transformation and DID analysis.
- **Authors:** Rajiv Sabherwal, [Hartmut Höhle](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/), [Tobias Nisius](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/), [Florian Pethig](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/team/wissenschaftliche-mitarbeiter/alumni/florian-pethig/), [Fareed Zandkarimi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/)
- **Cooperation Partner:** Aalberts Material Technology GmbH

## 🛒 Process Mining in Procurement (P2P)

This project applies process mining methods to measure and improve inefficiencies within critical procurement processes, such as purchase-to-pay (P2P).

- **Goal:** To identify, measure, explain, and eliminate inefficiencies in procurement processes, which can cause significant delays and costs.
- **Methodology:** An interdisciplinary team analyzes P2P process execution data from three large industrial companies. The research develops new **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)** that precisely quantify process inefficiency, allowing for the identification of temporal patterns or the impact of external influences (e.g., the Corona pandemic).
- **Authors:** [Prof. Dr. Christoph Bode](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/), [Prof. Dr. Hartmut Höhle](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/), [Jun.-Prof. Dr. Jana-Rebecca Rehse](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/rehse/), [Jonas Ronellenfitsch](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/bode/team/alumni/dr-ronellenfitsch-jonas/), [Fareed Zandkarimi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/hoehle/)
- **Cooperation Partners:** Schott AG, Hilti AG, HeidelbergCement AG, and Heidelberger Druckmaschinen AG
