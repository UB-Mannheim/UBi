# CDSB Course Catalog: Fall 2024 Curriculum

This catalog provides an overview of the available seminar courses for the Fall 2024 semester, covering advanced topics in economics, business administration, and data science.

## 📊 Data Science & Quantitative Methods

### Data Science in Action (Ringvorlesung)

This lecture series, offered by the Mannheim Center for Data Science, serves as a bridge course for GESS doctoral students.

- **Course Content:** The series provides an introduction to data science techniques.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students must take part in at least 80% of the lectures and write a 15-page essay.
- **Assessment:** Pass/fail (15-page essay).
- **Schedule:** Online (TBC).
- **Registration:** For more information and registration, please visit the website: [https://www.uni-mannheim.de/datascience/details/ringvorlesung-data-science-in-action-hws-2024-donnerstags-12-1330-uhr-online-via-zoom/](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/datascience/details/ringvorlesung-data-science-in-action-hws-2024-donnerstags-12-1330-uhr-online-via-zoom/)

### Machine Learning and AI for Economics

This course provides a practical approach to analyzing data using machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques for economics and business.

- **Course Content:** Systematic review of supervised ML/AI methods benchmarked against conventional econometrics. Focus on causal inference, using the German Business Panel as a running example.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Develop skills in ML/AI application, analysis of big/unstructured data, classification, inference, and writing technical reports.
- **Assessment:** Paper (technical report) (50%), Presentation (25%), Class Participation (25%).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 (Tuesday, 15:30 – 17:00 | O 226–28).

## 📈 Econometrics and Quantitative Theory

### Advanced Econometrics Theory and Applications

This course offers an advanced treatment of econometric theory and applications.

- **Course Content:** Topics include Matrix Algebra and Probability Theory, Ordinary Least Squares, Maximum Likelihood, Generalized Method of Moments, time series, and panel data econometrics.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain proficiency in advanced econometric methods.
- **Assessment:** Written Exam (120 min) (90%), Problem Sets (10%).
- **Prerequisites:** None specified.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 03.09.24 – 03.12.24 (Tuesday, 10:15 – 11:45 | O 048).

### Microeconomic Theory for Business Research

A graduate-level introduction to microeconomic theory, crucial for business research.

- **Course Content:** Covers consumer choice theory (certainty/uncertainty), firm theory, markets, equilibrium, strategic behavior, and asymmetric information.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Equip doctoral students to read, discuss, and formulate research based on theoretically grounded hypotheses.
- **Assessment:** Midterm and final exams (80–100%).
- **Prerequisites:** Mathematics for Economists, intermediate knowledge of microeconomics.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 01.10.24 – 01.10.24 (Tuesday, 15:30 – 17:00 | Room O 131).

### Mathematical Methods for Social Sciences

This course provides a rigorous foundation in advanced mathematics necessary for quantitative research.

- **Course Content:** Covers basic mathematical concepts (sets, functions), metric/normed spaces, vector spaces, matrix algebra, and multivariate differentiability for optimization problems.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students master basic concepts of analysis and linear algebra, understand proof development, and can solve nonlinear optimization problems.
- **Assessment:** Exam (120 min).
- **Prerequisites:** Basic mathematical knowledge.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 02.09.24 – 23.09.24 (Monday, 10:15 – 11:45 | 001 (L7, 3–5)).

### Advanced Business Econometrics (Discrete Choice)

Focuses on state-of-the-art discrete choice methods in business and marketing research.

- **Course Content:** Covers major models like logit, probit, and mixed logit, along with advanced estimation procedures (e.g., maximum simulated likelihood).
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain expertise in applying and testing discrete choice models to real business phenomena.
- **Assessment:** Written Exam (50%), Home Assignments (50%).
- **Prerequisites:** Recommended: Fundamentals in mathematics (including linear programming).
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 20.09.24 – 20.09.24 (Friday, 10:30 – 12:00 | ZOOM-Lehre-013).

## 💰 Accounting, Finance, and Taxation

### Research Seminar: Accounting and Taxation

A seminar focusing on current research topics in accounting and taxation.

- **Course Content:** Visiting researchers present working papers, deepening students' insights into empirical and theoretical research methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to discuss current research topics and develop research ideas in the field.
- **Assessment:** Oral participation.
- **Schedule:** Seminar Dates are announced [here.](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/area-accounting-taxation/research/research-seminar/).

### Research Seminar: Corporate Sustainability (ESG)

This seminar addresses the latest advances and challenges in the transition towards sustainable business practices and net carbon emissions of zero.

- **Course Content:** Covers sustainability economics, emission abatement strategies, and research on ESG outcomes, asset pricing, and disclosure regulation.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students critically evaluate empirical research, master empirical strategies, and utilize key databases for sustainability research.
- **Assessment:** Referee report (individual), Coding project (group), Presentation (individual).
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 16.09.24 – 02.12.24 (Monday, 17:15 – 18:45 | O 129).

### Research Seminar: Finance

A seminar focusing on current research topics in finance.

- **Course Content:** Visiting researchers present working papers, introducing students to various empirical and theoretical research methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to discuss current research topics and develop research ideas in finance.
- **Assessment:** Oral participation.
- **Schedule:** Seminar Dates are announced [here.](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/finance/research/finance-research-seminar/).

### Seminar: Taxation Law and EU Law

Focuses on the increasing impact of European Union Law on the taxation of individuals and companies.

- **Course Content:** Covers EU Law, indirect taxes (European directives), and secondary law harmonizing direct taxes, with a focus on the European Court of Justice case law.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Understanding the legal framework of taxation within the EU.
- **Assessment:** Participation.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 05.09.24 – 05.12.24 (Thursday, 15:30 – 17:00 | EO 169).

## 💼 Management and Organizational Studies

### Quantitative Methods in Management Research

This module provides an overview of statistical procedures relevant to management research.

- **Course Content:** Covers moderation, mediation, control variables, common method variance, hypothesis testing, and multilevel modeling.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students can identify issues, perform statistical analyses, and design quantitative research projects.
- **Assessment:** Oral exam (75%), Presentation (25%).
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 15.11.24 – 22.11.24 (Friday, 09:00 – 17:00 | EO 256).

### Organizational and Innovation Research Seminar

A seminar providing an overview of fundamental topics in organization and innovation.

- **Course Content:** Students present and discuss papers, reviewing conceptual framing and methodology (strengths and weaknesses) across different quantitative and qualitative methods.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to critically assess literature, formulate research questions, and design research.
- **Assessment:** Presentation (50%), Discussion (50%).
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 17.09.24 – 17.09.24 (Tuesday, 12:00 – 15:00 | EO 256).

### Non-profit Management

A course providing a basic understanding of the institutions and theories governing the nonprofit sector.

- **Course Content:** Topics include "History and Scope of the Nonprofit Sector," "Nonprofits and the Marketplace," and "Mission and Governance."
- **Learning Outcomes:** Understanding the theory and management of nonprofit organizations.
- **Assessment:** Presentation/Discussion.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 16.09.24 – 16.09.24 (Monday, 10:00 – 12:30 | EO 256).

### General Business Research Seminar

A seminar aimed at guiding students through the process of scientific research, preparing them for a dissertation proposal.

- **Course Content:** Participants conduct a literature study on a given topic in business analytics, presenting results in a written report and an oral presentation.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to analyze academic literature, understand publication composition, and present findings.
- **Assessment:** Paper (70%), Presentation (30%).
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 09.09.24 – 09.09.24 (Monday, 08:30 – 10:00 | SO 322).

## 🧠 Professional Development & Skills

### Mental Health in PhD Studies

A course designed to familiarize participants with the typical risk factors and challenges encountered during doctoral dissertations.

- **Course Content:** Guided group discussions based on literature-informed topics addressing common difficulties in dissertation projects.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Foster knowledge about mental health during dissertations and facilitate reflection on one's own situation.
- **Assessment:** Participation/Discussion.
- **Prerequisites:** None.
- **Schedule:** Example dates: 18.09.24 – 27.11.24 (Wednesday, 10:15 – 11:45 | Room 409 in L9, 1–2).

### General Research Skills Seminar (Brown Bag Seminar)

A seminar focused on presenting and discussing original research ideas.

- **Course Content:** Students present their own research ideas at different stages (early ideas, preliminary results, working papers) in an interactive discussion format.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students gain skills in presenting, discussing, and assessing research contributions.
- **Assessment:** Participation/Discussion.
- **Schedule:** Course dates are announced [here](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/accounting-taxation/forschung/brown-bag-seminar/).
