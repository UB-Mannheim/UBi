# Course Catalog for Exchange Students in Social Sciences

This guide outlines the requirements and course structures for exchange students pursuing studies in Political Science, Sociology, and Psychology at the University of Mannheim.

## 🎓 General Information for Exchange Students

**Course Selection Process:**

- **Nominated Students:** Exchange students who have been nominated for a specific field will be contacted by their departmental exchange coordinator via email.
  - *Fall/Winter Semester:* End of May/early June.
  - *Spring/Summer Semester:* End of November/early December.
- **Students from Other Departments:** Exchange students from other schools and departments may only attend classes if:
  1. Places are available for other students.
  1. They possess basic knowledge in the relevant field and statistics.
  1. The departmental exchange coordinator explicitly approves their participation.

**Contact Points:**

- **Political Science:** `int-pol@uni-mannheim.de`
- **Sociology & Psychology:** `International.sowi@uni-mannheim.de`

## 🏛️ Political Science (Master)

Courses are primarily open to incoming exchange students majoring in Political Science, and to exchange students from the School of Social Sciences (Sociology, Psychology).

**Prerequisites for Non-Majors:**
Exchange students from other schools and departments must meet the general criteria listed above and receive explicit approval.

**Key Learning Objectives:**

- Understand the concepts of democracy, populism, and technocracism as governance types.
- Possess knowledge of the state of the advanced scholarly literature.
- Distinguish the research designs for theoretical and empirical analysis.
- Critically analyze quantitative studies.
- Replicate and evaluate findings.
- Engage in informed discussions about current debates and issues related to democracy, populism, and technocracism.

**Selected Literature:**

- Carmamani, Daniele. 2017. Will vs. Reason: The Populist and Technocratic Forms of Political Representation and Their Critique to Party Government. *American Political Science Review* 111: 54 – 67.
- Hahm, Hyeonho, David Hilpert and Thomas König. 2023. Divided We Unite: The Nature of Partyism and the Role of Coalition Partnership in Europe. *American Political Science Review*, First View.
- Mudde, Cas. and Rovira Kaltwasser. 2013. Exclusionary vs. Inclusionary Populism: Comparing Contemporary Europe and Latin America. *Government and Opposition* 48(2): 147–174.

## 🧠 Psychology (Bachelor)

Courses are primarily open to incoming exchange students majoring in Psychology.

**Prerequisites for Non-Majors:**
Psychology courses are generally restricted to students nominated for Psychology.

**Key Topics and Skills:**

- Standard and advanced linear models (including multiple regression with continuous and categorical predictors, product terms, regularization methods, and nonlinear regression).
- Generalized linear models (including logistic regression, Poisson models, and log-linear models).
- Supervised and unsupervised classification methods (including discriminant analysis, clustering methods, regression trees, and mixture models).

**Selected Literature:**

- Böckenholt, U., & Meiser, T. (2017). Response style analysis with threshold and multi-process IRT models: A review and tutorial. *British Journal of Mathematical and Statistical Psychology*, 70, 159–181.
- Debelak, R., Strobl, C., & Zeigenfuse, M. (2022). *An introduction to the Rasch model with Examples in R*. Boca Raton, FL: CRC Press.
- De Boeck, P., & Wilson, M. (2004). *Explanatory item response models*. New York: Springer.
- Chalmers, R. P. (2012). mirt: A multidimensional item response theory package for the R environment. *Journal of Statistical Software*, 48(6), 1–29.
- Embretson, S. E., & Reise, S. P. (2000). *Item response theory for psychologists*. Mahwah, NJ: Lawrence Erlbaum.
- Rizopoulos, D. (2006). ltm: An R package for latent variable modeling and item response analysis. *Journal of Statistical Software*, 17(5), 1–25.

## 🔬 Colloquium for Master's Students (Psychology)

**Overview:**
The Colloquium provides a forum to discuss current research projects at the Chair for Clinical and Biological Psychology and Psychotherapy. Master's students writing their thesis at the chair, as well as staff members or guests, present their current research ideas or the status of a project. The goal is to collectively reflect on the research endeavors of the chair.

**Preparation for the Colloquium:**

1. **Initial Contact:** The Colloquium is initially open to all Master's students writing their thesis at the chair.
1. **Recommendation:** Students interested in a thesis at the chair are advised to contact the staff (via email, see the chair's website) or Prof. Alpers directly (Office Hours: Tuesdays, 10:30–11:30 Uhr; Email: `alpers@uni-mannheim.de`) well in advance, ideally before attending the first Colloquium.
1. **Consultation:** Staff are available to advise on current thesis opportunities and work together to develop a research idea. Students can also propose their own topics or ideas for collaboration with external institutions (e.g., clinics).
1. **Supervision:** Thesis supervision must be arranged with Prof. Alpers or a responsible staff member outside of the Colloquium.

**Presentation Requirements:**

- **Study Performance:** The required academic performance in the Colloquium is the presentation of the current status of the Master's thesis.
- **Schedule:** The sequence of presentations is set at the beginning of the semester.
- **Coordination:** The contribution must be coordinated with the responsible supervisors at least one week before the presentation.
- **Flexibility:** It is acceptable to present initial, unfinished ideas; the scope and type of presentation depend on the current status and individual needs. However, active participation is expected from all attendees.
- **Methodology:** Regular exchange regarding research methodology and academic writing is maintained, allowing for the collection of questions.
- **Language:** When foreign participants or guests are present, the academic language used is English.

**Credit Points (ECTS):**

- 4 ECTS: Presentations with theoretical input and practical components.
- 6 ECTS: Presentations with theoretical input, practical components, and a written reflection.
