# Erin Mansur: Decarbonization and Electrification in the Long Run

Erin Mansur, an economist from Dartmouth College, presented a seminar on the long-term implications of decarbonization and electrification within the electricity sector.

## Seminar Abstract and Key Findings

The seminar analyzed a long-run model designed to capture crucial aspects of the electricity industry, including time-varying demand, renewable intermittency, optimal storage use, and the entry/exit of generation and storage capacity.

**Key Insights:**

- **Carbon Tax Effects:** A carbon tax can surprisingly increase overall electricity consumption.
- **Storage and Renewables:** Cheaper storage technologies may decrease renewable capacity by driving down prices when renewables are operating.
- **Electrification Impact:** Increasing electricity demand (e.g., through vehicle electrification) may actually lead to a decrease in emissions if it stimulates sufficient renewable capacity entry.
- **Model Calibration:** Using hourly U.S. market data, the model suggests that a carbon price of $150 or more essentially eliminates carbon emissions.
- **Policy Recommendations:**
  - For a modest decarbonization goal, a renewable subsidy performs better than a nuclear subsidy.
  - This ranking is reversed for an ambitious decarbonization goal.
  - Policies promoting transmission or storage are unlikely to yield significant benefits unless paired with subsidies for renewables.
- **Vehicle Electrification:** Electrifying 100% of car miles traveled (eliminating gasoline emissions) would increase electricity-sector carbon emissions by 23–27% if charging occurs at night, but could decrease emissions if charging occurs during the day.

The full NBER working paper is available for review [here](https://www.nber.org/papers/w30082).

## Speaker Biography

Erin Mansur is an economist specializing in energy economics, environmental economics, and industrial organization. His research interests include:

- Labor market implications of hydrofracturing.
- Environmental effects of electric vehicles.
- How natural gas prices affect electricity markets.
- Competition analysis in electricity markets following mergers.

He is a research associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) and teaches classes on energy economics.
