# Dynamic Longitudinal Modeling

**Instructors:** Manuel Voelkle (Humboldt University Berlin) & Charles Driver (University of Zurich)

### Workshop Overview

The goal of this workshop is to introduce participants to dynamic longitudinal modeling, with a specific emphasis on continuous time modeling.

The workshop structure includes:

- **Foundations:** Distinguishing between static and dynamic models for analyzing change, and discussing their respective advantages and disadvantages.
- **Continuous Time Basics:** Introduction to continuous time modeling, including the R package `ctsem`.
- **Special Topics:** Three specialized blocks covering:
  1. **Heterogeneity and Hierarchical Models:** Special emphasis on these advanced topics.
  1. **Time Series Analysis:** Focusing on time series (N = 1) data, oscillations, and accelerated longitudinal designs.
  1. **Cutting-Edge Research:** Exploring causal inference and machine learning approaches in continuous time modeling, including a discussion of current limitations.

**Target Audience:**
While the emphasis is on applied psychological research, the workshop is targeted to an audience with a primary interest in quantitative methods and prior experience in multivariate data analysis. Some experience with structural equation models and longitudinal data analysis is advantageous but not required.

**Practical Focus:**
Beyond general introductions, special emphasis will be placed on practical implementation using various datasets and packages in R ([www.r-project.org](http://www.r-project.org)).

### Prerequisites and Skills

- **Software:** Participants are requested to bring their own notebooks with a recent version of R and RStudio/Posit installed.
- **R Experience:** Participants who have not been exposed to R are requested to familiarize themselves with the basic functionality of the program. Advanced knowledge of R is not required.
