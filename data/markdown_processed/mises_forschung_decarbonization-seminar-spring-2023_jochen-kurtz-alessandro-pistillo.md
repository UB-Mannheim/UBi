# Product Carbon Footprint at Scale: Seminar on Decarbonization at BASF

This seminar, presented by Jochen Kurtz and Alessandro Pistillo of BASF, focused on the critical importance of accounting for product-level carbon emissions in driving corporate decarbonization efforts.

## 🗓️ Event Details

- **Topic:** Product CO2-sting at scale at BASF
- **Speakers:** Jochen Kurtz and Alessandro Pistillo
- **Date:** February 27, 2023
- **Location:** University of Mannheim (Room O 129, Ostflügel, Level 1)
- **Admission:** Open to the public.

## 💡 Seminar Abstract

The accounting of product-level carbon emission is of critical importance for companies, not only for disclosure requirements but also to steer and track decarbonization efforts. BASF has established a comprehensive program and a robust tool-set to determine the product carbon footprint of its entire portfolio of 45,000 products. Furthermore, the company is actively engaging with peers, customers, suppliers, and partners to drive carbon footprint transparency across entire value chains.

## 👤 Speakers

### Jochen Kurtz

Jochen Kurtz is the Vice President for Group Performance Management & Analytics at BASF. In his role as Corporate Controller, he manages both the financial performance and the sustainability reporting & controlling functions within BASF. He brings over 25 years of leadership experience in finance, controlling, and digitalization within BASF across Germany and Belgium. He holds a degree in Business Administration from the University of Mannheim and earned a Ph.D. in Economics from Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, based on a thesis on Circular Economy.

### Alessandro Pistillo

Alessandro Pistillo is the Director – Digital Strategic Projects in the Corporate Development Department at BASF. His focus lies particularly on customer experience and the intersection between digital technology and sustainability. Currently, he is leading the establishment of a business ecosystem designed to foster standardization and transparency in greenhouse gas emissions data. Throughout his 20-year tenure at BASF Group, he has held various leadership positions in product management, marketing, sales, strategy, and engineering across Germany, Switzerland, and China. He is a chemical engineer by education.
