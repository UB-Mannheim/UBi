## 🎓 Consolidated Course Descriptions

The following descriptions apply to multiple bridge courses and elective modules across the program.

### 1. Women in Leadership (Pass/Fail Course)

This interdisciplinary course examines the gender gap in leadership positions, focusing on the academic environment. It analyzes the psychological and economic reasons for the low fraction of women in leadership, covering structural hurdles like stereotypes, discrimination, and social norms.

**Core Building Blocks:**

1. **Women in Leadership: The Economic Perspective:** Discusses statistics on women in leadership, time trends, and the impact of gender equality measures (e.g., gender quotas) on economic outcomes like the gender pay gap.
1. **Women in Leadership: The Psychological Perspective:** Addresses gender stereotypes (descriptive and prescriptive) and their impact on leadership aspirations. Topics include the “Think Manager – Think Male” phenomenon, Glass metaphors (glass ceiling, glass escalator, glass cliff), Queen Bee effects, and backlash effects.
1. **“Raise Your Voice” – Rhetoric Training:** A hands-on training on presenting research findings in a conference-like scenario. Students must prepare a short presentation (5–7 minutes) on their research topic and bring a video-capable device.
1. **“Raise Your Pay” – Negotiation Training:** Teaches negotiation skills, conflict resolution, and career advancement tactics. Students must submit a personal negotiation topic in advance.

### 2. Quantitative Methods Tutorial (M.A. Political Science)

This tutorial accompanies the course “Quantitative Methods” in the M.A. program in Political Science.

**Focus:** Practical issues associated with quantitative methods, including obtaining and preparing data sets, using statistical software, selecting appropriate tests, and interpreting results.
**Format:** The seminar serves as a software tutorial.
**Prerequisites:** No prior knowledge of statistical programming is expected.

### 3. Human Rights and State Repression (CDSS/CDSE)

This module provides systematic knowledge and understanding about key debates on human rights violations and state repression.

**Learning Objectives:**

- Critically assess and evaluate academic research (theoretical and empirical contributions).
- Familiarize yourself with key datasets in human rights research.
- Engage in and contribute to analytical discussions about published research.
- Foster constructive discussions and effective reading.
- Improve writing and presentation skills.

### 4. Advanced Statistical Methods (CDSS/CDSE)

This module covers advanced statistical techniques and modeling.

**Topics Covered:**

- Generalized linear models (Dunn & Smyth, 2018).
- Longitudinal analysis: Modeling within-person fluctuation and change (Hoffman, 2015).
- Statistical learning (James et al., 2013).
- Structural equation modeling (Kline, 2023).
- Multilevel analysis (Snijders & Bosker, 2012).

### 5. Causal Inference Methods (Sociology/Psychology)

This module focuses on implementing causal inference methods using R.

**Practical Requirements:**

- Students should bring their own laptop for all practice sessions.
- Previous knowledge in R is not necessary, although advantageous.
- Students must install R and R studio before the first practice session.

### 6. Research Thesis/Paper (Doctoral Level)

This is a comprehensive research project requiring advanced academic skills.

**Requirements:**

- **Length:** Approximately 5,000 words (±500), including references but excluding appendices.
- **Content:**
  - A clearly defined and original Research Question.
  - A Literature Review demonstrating relevance and existing scholarship.
  - A well-reasoned and evidence-based Argument (thesis).
  - Empirical Evidence to substantiate the argument.
- **Deadline:** January 15, 2026.

### 7. Data Science and Extraction (Advanced Elective)

This course focuses on the practical skills needed for data collection and preparation.

**Skills Acquired:**

- Identify requirements for effective data extraction.
- Automate data collection from websites or APIs.
- Parse and clean data.
- Navigate ethical and legal considerations.
