# Admission to the CDSE Program (Doctoral Program in Economics)

The CDSE (Center for Doctoral Studies in Economics) at the University of Mannheim offers a doctoral program based purely on academic performance and potential. Admitted students are committed to funding for five years (contingent on successful completion and academic standing), which may include scholarships in the first two years and research assistant positions thereafter.

**Key Dates and Deadlines:**

- **Early Admission:** January 15, 2026
- **Application Deadline:** March 31, 2026 (for the Fall 2026 intake)
- Applications submitted after the early round will be reviewed with all other applications after March 31, 2026.

**Prerequisites:**

In principle, the prerequisite for admission is a Master’s degree in the respective field or an equivalent qualification. Candidates from neighboring or quantitative fields (such as mathematics or engineering) may be admitted if their background is deemed sufficient.

**Application Submission:**

- Applications must be submitted via the official [Online application portal](https://gess-application.uni-mannheim.de/portal/).
- Applications sent by mail will not be considered.

## Required Application Documents

Applicants must provide the following materials:

### 1. Personal Data

Standard personal information (Name, address, date of birth, etc.).

### 2. Letter of Motivation

A statement (in English, maximum 500 words) explaining:

- Current goals and career plans.
- Reasons for interest in the doctoral program in Economics.
- Detailed study plans and fields of interest.

### 3. Certificates and Transcripts of Grades

- A transcript is required from **each** college or university attended. The degree certificate alone is insufficient.
- The transcript must show courses, grades, and degrees received, including rank or distinction if applicable.
- **Translations:** Any transcript not in English or German requires a translation. (Note: While notarization is required upon acceptance, it is not necessary for the initial submission. An apostil is not required.)
- **Incomplete Studies:** If the current degree is not yet finished, please attach a preliminary grade report (in English or German).

### 4. GRE Scores

- All applicants must upload their General GRE test taker's copy via the online portal and send it through the testing service.
- **Important:** Only the General GRE is accepted; subject tests are not required. The GMAT cannot substitute for the GRE.
- Scores must not be older than two years. There is no minimum required score.
- Please ask the Educational Testing Service (ETS) to report your scores using the code: **0933**.
- *Note: The GRE General Test taken at home is not accepted.*

### 5. Letters of Recommendation

Two letters of recommendation are required from faculty members who can assess the applicant's academic ability and potential.

### 6. Writing Sample and Supplementary Information

- **Writing Sample:** Submit a writing sample (e.g., a paper written during studies) in English or German.
- **Supplementary Information:** Any other relevant documents pertaining to the application.
