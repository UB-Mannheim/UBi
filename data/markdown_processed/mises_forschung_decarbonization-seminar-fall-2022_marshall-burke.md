## Seminar: Attributing Local Climate Damages to Specific Emitters

**Speaker:** Marshall Burke
**Affiliation:** Stanford University
**Date:** December 5, 2022
**Time:** 05:15 – 06:30 PM (CET)
**Format:** Online only

### Seminar Abstract

This seminar presents a unified framework for calculating country-specific climate damages resulting from past or future greenhouse gas emissions from any known emitter.

The presentation will demonstrate four key applications of this method:

1. Calculation of “carbon debt” owed by individual emitters (e.g., individual people or firms) based on past emissions.
1. Calculation of country-level “loss and damage” payments owed by large historical emitters to developing countries.
1. Correction of official GDP time series to reflect climate externalities.
1. Construction of a damage function that maps different levels of future warming to global aggregate economic damages.

### Speaker Biography

Marshall Burke is an Associate Professor in the Department of Earth System Science and Deputy Director at the Center on Food Security and the Environment at Stanford University. He is also a Research Fellow at the National Bureau of Economic Research.

His research focuses on the social and economic impacts of environmental change, and on measuring and understanding economic livelihoods across the developing world. His work regularly appears in major journals, including *Nature*, *Science*, the *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, and *The Lancet*.

**Education & Ventures:**

- **PhD:** Agricultural and Resource Economics from UC Berkeley.
- **BA:** International Relations from Stanford.
- He is also co-founder of [AtlasAI](https://www.atlasai.co/), a start-up that uses satellites and machine learning to measure livelihoods.
