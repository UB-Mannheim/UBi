# Workshop on Learning to Sense (L2S) at NeurIPS 2025

The L2S Workshop explores the critical intersection of sensor hardware design and deep learning models, aiming to revolutionize how machines sense the world. We push beyond traditional image processing pipelines by focusing on the **joint optimization of sensors and machine learning models for visual tasks**.

## 💡 Workshop Focus and Core Themes

Recent advances in computer vision rely heavily on deep learning, but current models are often constrained by the limitations of traditional Image Signal Processing (ISP) pipelines, which were designed for human perception and discard valuable raw sensor information.

This workshop addresses this gap by focusing on end-to-end optimization of image acquisition and processing, enabling more robust, efficient, and scalable vision systems for real-world deployment (e.g., mobile, embedded, and autonomous platforms).

Key areas of discussion include:

- **Sensor Optimization:** Jointly optimizing sensor layouts (e.g., pixel arrangement, color filters) and network parameters, including low-bit data capture (4-bit, 6-bit, 8-bit).
- **Reimagining ISPs:** Shifting from fixed hardware pipelines to learnable, task-adaptive ISPs or direct RAW-to-task pipelines.
- **Task-Driven Sensor Design:** Designing image capture systems guided by the needs of downstream machine models, rather than human aesthetics.
- **Joint Optimization:** Exploring synergies by optimizing both sensor hardware and model architecture simultaneously.
- **Data Handling:** Investigating quantization of RAW data and developing new benchmarks and metrics for evaluating sensor-model systems.
- **Generalization:** Ensuring that optimized sensor-model systems remain robust and adaptable across diverse real-world environments and lighting conditions.
- **Negative Results:** We welcome discussions on failure cases, as understanding what *doesn't* work is crucial for advancing the field.

## 🎤 Keynote Speakers

We are honored to feature a distinguished lineup of confirmed invited speakers whose diverse expertise spans core areas at the intersection of sensing and intelligent perception:

- **Prof. Michael Felsberg** (Linköping University): Foundational research in artificial visual systems, 3D computer vision, and robot perception.
- **Dr. Daisuke Iso** (Sony AI): Industry-driven innovations in imaging systems and sensor-model co-design.
- **Prof. Michael S. Brown** (York University and Samsung AI Center–Toronto): Cutting-edge work on physics-based vision and in-camera processing.
- **Prof. Avideh Zakhor** (UC Berkeley): Pioneering contributions in 3D computer vision, image processing, and large-scale modeling.

## 🗓️ Important Dates & Schedule

The workshop will be held in-person in San Diego, CA.

**Workshop Dates:** December 6 or 7, 2025

**Submission Details:**

- **Submission Portal:** [https://openreview.net/group?id=NeurIPS.cc/2025/Workshop/L2S](https://openreview.net/group?id=NeurIPS.cc/2025/Workshop/L2S)
- **Submission Deadline:** August 22, 2025
- **Notification of Acceptance:** September 22, 2025
- **Camera-Ready Deadline:** October 15, 2025

**Submission Types:**
We accept two categories of submissions, all undergoing double-blind peer review via OpenReview:

1. **Extended Abstracts:** Up to 4 pages (non-archival). Ideal for early-stage ideas or ongoing work. Accepted papers will be presented as posters or short talks.
1. **Full Papers:** Up to 9 pages (excluding references) (archival). Selected papers will be published in the PMLR NeurIPS Workshop Proceedings. Presented as talks or spotlight posters.

**Workshop Schedule Highlights:**
The full-day workshop features 5 invited talks, 2 oral paper presentations, a panel discussion, and two poster sessions.

- **Morning:** Invited Talks (e.g., Prof. Michael Felsberg, Dr. Daisuke Iso) and Oral Presentation 1.
- **Afternoon:** Invited Talks (e.g., Prof. Michael S. Brown, Prof. Avideh Zakhor), Oral Presentation 2, and a Panel Discussion on Task-Driven Sensor Design.
