# Getting Help and Support for Doctoral Students

The GESS celebrates the diverse backgrounds and experiences of its doctoral students, recognizing them as key to maximizing human potential. In adherence to the [Code of Conduct of the University of Mannheim](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Universitaet/Dokumente/Forschung_Senatsrichtlinie_Partnerschaftliches_Verhalten_en.pdf), the GESS maintains a zero-tolerance policy for behavior that violates another person’s dignity and integrity.

The University also emphasizes work-life balance, offering support for compatibility between family and professional life. Doctoral students and staff are entitled to use parent-child-offices, and students with children benefit from discounts at the [Kinderhaus](https://www.stw-ma.de/en/studying-with-children/kinderhaus-en/) provided by the Studierendenwerk Mannheim.

## Diversity and Gender Equality Support

The **Gender and Diversity Committee** monitors and assesses the Graduate School's performance in this area, ensuring the GESS remains sensitive to matters of gender and diversity. It serves as a primary point of contact for doctoral students seeking advice and support regarding their studies and work. Committee members provide information on topics such as discrimination, parental leave, and child care services.

**Contact:**
For questions or problems, committee members can be contacted directly or via: gess.gdc-office@mail.uni-mannheim.de.

## Specialized Student Support

### Students with Children

Reconciling studies and family life can be challenging. The University of Mannheim offers dedicated support services, including:

- Child care services on campus.
- Special examination regulations for student parents.
- A Parent and Child Room.
  (See: [Information for Students with Children](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/gender-equality-and-equal-opportunity/information-for-students/))

### Studying with a Disability

The commissioner and counselor for students with disabilities or chronic illnesses provides advice and support for any difficulties encountered during studies due to a disability or chronic illness.
(See: [Advice for Students with Disabilities](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/academics/advice-and-services/services-for-students-in-special-circumstances/advice-for-students-with-disabilities-or-chronic-illnesses/))

### Physical and Mental Health Support

For students seeking help or information on physical and mental well-being, the University of Mannheim maintains a comprehensive list of support services and resources.
(See: [Mental Health Support](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/gender-equality-and-equal-opportunity/counseling/good-mental-health-at-university/looking-after-your-mental-health-while-studying-or-pursuing-your-doctorate/))

### Counseling Services

If you need advice—for example, in difficult situations among colleagues or in the event of conflicts with fellow students—the various counseling services at the university are available to offer support.
(See: [Counseling](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/gender-equality-and-equal-opportunity/counseling/))
