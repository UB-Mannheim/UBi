**Affiliation:** Stanford Graduate School of Business

## Seminar: Conditional Incentives for Improving Smallholder Farmer Welfare and Protecting Tropical Forests

*Date:* April 6, 2023
*Format:* Online only

### Seminar Abstract

Smallholder farmers are crucial to global agricultural commodity production but often face low incomes and limited productivity. To improve their livelihoods, they may expand their farmlands into tropical forests, leading to significant environmental damage. This seminar examines how conditional incentives—financial rewards combined with forest protection requirements—can help achieve the dual goals of enhancing farmer welfare and halting tropical deforestation.

The presentation develops game theoretic models to analyze the decisions of smallholder communities facing various incentives. Key findings include:

- Group-level conditional incentives are more effective than individual ones.
- The optimal conditions depend heavily on the priorities of the stakeholders and local field conditions.
- Using data from the Indonesian palm oil supply chain, the study suggests that while current price premiums may be insufficient, a modest carbon price could be adequate to mitigate considerable CO2 emissions.

### Speaker Biography

Dan Iancu is an Associate Professor of Operations, Information and Technology at the Stanford Graduate School of Business.

**Education & Experience:**

- B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from Yale University.
- S.M. in Engineering Sciences from Harvard University.
- Ph.D. in Operations Research from MIT.
- Previously served as a Goldstine Fellow in the Risk Analytics Group at the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center.

**Research Focus:**
Prof. Iancu's research spans operations, finance, and risk management. He develops tools for dynamic optimization under uncertainty and prescriptive analytics, applying them to complex value chains. His recent work focuses on designing better procurement, payment, and financing solutions in global supply chains, addressing the immediate and long-term impacts on both human lives and the environment.

**Publications & Recognition:**
His research has been published in top journals including *Management Science*, *Operations Research*, and *Manufacturing & Service Operations Management*. He has received several best paper awards (e.g., INFORMS Optimization Society 2009) and teaching prizes at Harvard and MIT Sloan.
