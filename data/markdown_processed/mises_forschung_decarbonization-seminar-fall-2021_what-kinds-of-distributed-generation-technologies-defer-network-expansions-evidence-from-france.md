# What Kinds of Distributed Generation Technologies Defer Network Expansions? Evidence from France

**Speaker:** Frank Wolak (Stanford University)
**Date:** September 20, 2021
**Event Type:** Live virtual seminar (Registration no longer available)

## Seminar Abstract

This seminar estimates the relationship between distributed generation investments and hourly net injections to the distribution grid across over 2,000 substations in France between 2005 and 2018.

**Key Findings:**

- A 1 MW increase in solar PV capacity has no statistically significant impact on the highest percentiles of the annual distribution of hourly net injections to the distribution grid.
- A 1 MW increase in wind capacity is predicted to reduce the 99th percentile of the annual distribution of hourly net injections to the distribution grid by 0.037 MWh.
- In contrast, a 1 MW investment in a distributed small hydro, non-renewable thermal, or renewable thermal generation unit predicts an almost five times larger MWh reduction in the 99th percentile of the annual distribution of hourly net injections to the distribution grid.
- A 1 MW investment in distributed solar PV or wind capacity predicts substantial absolute changes in both extremes of the annual distribution of hourly ramp rates of net injections to the distribution grid.
- For the remaining three distributed generation technologies, a 1 MW capacity increase does not predict a non-zero change in any percentile of the annual distribution of hourly ramp rates of net injections to the distribution grid.

**Conclusion:** These results argue that, at least for the case of France, increases in distributed solar and wind capacity are more likely to lead to increases, rather than decreases, in distribution network investments.

## Speaker Biography: Frank A. Wolak

Frank A. Wolak is the Holbrook Working Professor of Commodity Price Studies in the Department of Economics at Stanford University.

**Specializations:**

- Industrial Organization
- Econometric Theory

**Research Focus:** His recent work studies methods for introducing competition into infrastructure industries—including telecommunications, electricity, water delivery, and postal delivery services—and assessing the impacts of these competition policies on consumer and producer welfare.

**Professional Experience:**

- **California Independent System Operator (CAISO):** From January 1, 1998, to March 31, 2011, Wolak served as the Chair of the Market Surveillance Committee for the electricity supply industry in California.
- **National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER):** He is a Research Associate of the NBER.
- **Stanford University:** He currently directs the Program on Energy and Sustainable Development (PESD) in the Freeman-Spogli Institute (FSI) for International Studies and co-directs the Natural Gas Initiative.
- **Emissions Market Advisory Committee (EMAC):** From January 2012 to December 2013, Wolak was a member of EMAC, advising the California Air Resources Board on the design and monitoring of the state’s cap-and-trade market for Greenhouse Gas Emissions allowances.
