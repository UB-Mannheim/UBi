# The Business School, University of Mannheim

The Business School at the University of Mannheim is one of the largest and most renowned business schools in Europe. With over 36 fully ordained professors, one senior professor, numerous assistant professors, more than 200 additional faculty members, and approximately 3,500 students, the school emphasizes research and teaching at the highest international level.

The "Mannheim Philosophy" is built upon three core pillars:

1. Consistent quality management.
1. International focus.
1. Distinctive practical orientation.

Research at the school is strongly supported by large institutional grants from the German Research Foundation (DFG) and European Union research and training grants, supplemented by funds from other public and private institutions.

## Associated Research and Academic Centers

The Business School maintains extensive international contacts and is associated with numerous important research centers located in Mannheim, fostering a strong dialogue between academia and industry.

### Institute for Small and Medium Enterprise Research (ifm)

- **Website:** [http://www.institut-fuer-mittelstandsforschung.de/](http://www.institut-fuer-mittelstandsforschung.de/)
- **Focus:** The ifm conducts specialized research on current and structural issues related to Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs).

### Institute for Market-Oriented Management (IMU)

- **Website:** [https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/imu/](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/imu/)
- **Focus:** IMU serves as a forum for dialogue between scientific theory and practical application. Its research aims to generate scientific results highly relevant for market-oriented management.

### Institute for Enterprise Systems (InES)

- **Website:** [https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/ines/](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/ines/)
- **Focus:** InES actively promotes the valuable exchange between science and practice within the field of enterprise systems. The institute was jointly founded by the Ministry of Science, Research and Arts of Baden-Württemberg (MWK) and the University of Mannheim.

### Leibniz ScienceCampus MannheimTax

- **Website:** [https://www.mannheimtaxation.eu/](https://www.mannheimtaxation.eu/)
- **Focus:** This campus unites outstanding expertise in corporate taxation, public finance, and fiscal law. It intensifies the dialogue between researchers and industry representatives, supporting both theoretical and practical perspectives, and nurturing new generations of researchers.

### Management Analytics Center

- **Website:** [https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/management-analytics/](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/management-analytics/)
- **Focus:** The Center assists companies by providing research and practical transfer services, helping them identify potentials, introduce new methods, and make data-driven decisions.

### Mannheim Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation (MCEI)

- **Website:** [https://www.mcei.de/](https://www.mcei.de/)
- **Focus:** MCEI provides a dedicated platform for young entrepreneurs, potential founders, students, sponsors, and investors.

### Mannheim Institute for Sustainable Energy Studies (MISES)

- **Website:** [https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/mises/](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/mises/)
- **Focus:** MISES examines the challenges and innovation opportunities arising from the transition toward a decarbonized energy future.

### Centre for European Economic Research (ZEW)

- **Website:** [https://www.zew.de/en/](https://www.zew.de/en/)
- **Focus:** ZEW is a leading institute in applied economic research in Europe. It concentrates on internationally comparative issues within the European context and maintains several important databases.
