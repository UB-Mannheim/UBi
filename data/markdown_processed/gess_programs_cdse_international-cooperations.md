# European Network for Training in Economic Research (ENTER)

The CDSE offers students the opportunity to conduct part of their doctoral studies abroad at a partner university through the **European Network for Training in Economic Research (ENTER)** [https://www.enter-network.org/](https://www.enter-network.org/).

ENTER is a joint initiative of top departments and schools for economics at the following universities:

- [Katholieke Universiteit Brabant (Tilburg, Netherlands)](https://www.tilburguniversity.edu/research/institutes-and-research-groups/center/)
- [Stockholm School of Economics (Sweden)](https://www.hhs.se/)
- [Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (Spain)](http://economics.uc3m.es/)
- [Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (Spain)](http://idea.uab.es/)
- Universität Mannheim (Germany)
- [Université des Sciences Sociales de Toulouse (France)](http://www.tse-fr.eu/)
- [Université Libre de Bruxelles (Belgium)](http://www.ecares.org/)
- [University College London (Great Britain)](http://www.ucl.ac.uk/economics)
- [University of Stockholm](http://www.ne.su.se/english/)

## ENTER Programme Details

The network offers three main types of academic engagement for doctoral students:

### 1. ENTER Long-term Exchanges (Visiting Research Fellowships)

Visiting research fellowships are available at the ENTER nodes for doctoral students who wish to obtain advanced graduate education or specialized research training in economics and its applications.

- **Duration:** Typically one or two semesters, usually during the third or fourth year at the CDSE.
- **Financing:** Stays are organized within the ENTER network and can be financed by the network via the CDSE, based on merit.
- **Selection:** Selection is based exclusively on academic excellence. Successful applicants are treated by the hosting institutions as regular PhD students.

### 2. ENTER Exchange Seminars

This series allows selected students in an advanced stage of their dissertation to present their work in research seminars at any of the participating universities during a short-term stay. The CDSE seminar in Mannheim regularly hosts two to three external speakers from the ENTER network every semester.

### 3. ENTER Jamboree

These annual events take place at the beginning of each year (March/April) and serve multiple purposes:

- Familiarizing doctoral students with conference format presentations and discussions in a focused setting.
- Fostering interaction between students and faculty from the different networks, encouraging visits and exchanges.
- Coordinating the overall programs.

*Note: Slots for CDSE students are allocated by the ENTER coordinator based on the student's academic standing and participation in CDSE seminars and ENTER-related activities (e.g., acting as discussant for incoming ENTER speakers).*
