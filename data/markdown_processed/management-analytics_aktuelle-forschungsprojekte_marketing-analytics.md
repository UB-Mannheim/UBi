## Research Projects in Marketing Analytics

### Advertising Over Time: Moving in the speed-of-life?

**Authors:** Leonie Gehrmann, [Florian Stahl](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/quantitativemarketing/researchgroup/florianstahl/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Hebrew University (Israel), Economist
**Description:** This research analyzes how print advertising content has adapted to changes in "speed-of-life" (defined as the rapidity and density of experiences, meanings, perceptions, and activities). The study develops an image mining algorithm applicable not only to print ads but also to social media postings and other magazines. The findings offer direct insights into marketing practice, particularly regarding the increasing advertising clutter and the competition for consumer attention.

### The Impact of the Offered Product Mix on the Acquisition, Usage and Retention of Information Goods

**Authors:** [Daniela Schmitt](https://www.novasbe.unl.pt/en/faculty-research/faculty/faculty-detail/id/1170/daniela-schmitt), Raghuram Iyengar, [Florian Stahl](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/quantitativemarketing/researchgroup/florianstahl/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Wharton School (Philadelphia)
**Description:** This project addresses the challenge faced by information goods providers (e.g., news websites) in balancing free users and paid subscribers. Using a hidden Markov model, the research investigates how content consumption dynamics impact subscription probability (acquisition) and retention. The findings show that paid content increases the probability of subscribing, but the effect is heterogeneous, as some users abandon the site when paid content increases. The results provide actionable implications for firms using the freemium business model.

### A friendly turn: advertising bias in the news media

**Authors:** Florens Focke, [Alexandra Niessen-Ruenzi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/niessen-ruenzi/), [Stefan Ruenzi](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/ruenzi/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of International Finance & Chair of Corporate Governance
**Description:** This paper investigates whether newspapers report more favorably about advertising corporate clients compared to other firms. Using high-dimensional fixed effects and high-frequency advertising data, the study finds evidence of "advertising bias" in reporting among local and national newspapers. This bias is particularly noticeable in the less negative reporting following bad news events (e.g., negative earnings surprises). The findings suggest that press independence may be influenced by corporate pressure.

### Prediction of Consumer Behavior

**Authors:** Yasid Soufi, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Sales & Services Marketing
**Description:** The goal is to gain predictive insights into customer purchase behavior and personal traits to make the sales process more adaptive. By applying machine learning algorithms to enriched data sets (including behavioral patterns and self-reported traits), the project aims to anticipate customer decisions in an app-based context. The insights generated can optimize processes, leading to higher customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

### Marketing in Finance

**Authors:** Yasid Soufi, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Sales & Services Marketing
**Description:** This research explores how skillful communication in financial markets can develop new financing sources and improve corporate sentiment. High-quality Investor Relations (IR) is identified as a critical success factor. By crawling and analyzing large amounts of textual data, the project aims to determine how marketing communication should be framed within the context of Investor Relations, especially for companies undergoing an Initial Public Offering (IPO).

### Maximizing Customer Lifetime Value through Strategic Channel Management: How to Incentivize Customers to Use a Mobile App versus a Website

**Authors:** Gokhan Gecer, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/)
**Cooperation Partners:** Chair of Sales & Services Marketing
**Description:** Given that mobile apps increase customer lifetime value (CLV) in online purchasing, this project suggests that online retailers should strategically steer customers to the mobile channel by offering a permanent discount. Using a probability-based CLV model, the research determines the optimal discount value and the target customer segment. It concludes that discounts should be used primarily to increase customer retention, not for new customer acquisition.

### Variation in review writing behavior across time-of-the-day, week day, season, weather conditions, a city’s life-quality and other factors

**Authors:** [Andreas Bayerl](https://www.eur.nl/en/people/andreas-bayerl), [Florian Stahl](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/quantitativemarketing/researchgroup/florianstahl/), Jacob Goldenberg
**Cooperation Partners:** IDC Israel, Kununu.de
**Description:** This project analyzes over 10 million online reviews from two platforms to identify differences in review writing behavior based on external factors such as time of day, day of the week, season, weather, and city life quality. The study uses web scraping, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Machine Learning to demonstrate how online reviews, intended to be unbiased, can be influenced by these externalities.

### Ad-blockers and Content Differentiation

**Authors:** Gokhan Gecer, [Florian Kraus](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/kraus/), Pinar Yildirim
**Cooperation Partners:** Wharton School (Philadelphia)
**Description:** Addressing the threat posed by ad-blockers, this research develops a game-theoretical model of a media ecosystem involving publishers and users. Publishers maximize profit from advertising and/or subscriptions. The model analyzes two strategies: showing content only to users who disable ad-blockers, or showing content to both paying subscribers and those who disable ad-blockers. The results show that content substitutability is key: publishers offer a subscription option when content is more substitutable (newspapers) or when it is costly to produce but less substitutable.
