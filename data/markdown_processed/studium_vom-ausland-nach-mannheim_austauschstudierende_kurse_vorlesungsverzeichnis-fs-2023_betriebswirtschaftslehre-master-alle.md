# Business School Course Catalog for Exchange Students

This catalog outlines available courses for exchange students at the Master's level in Business Administration (BWL).

## 🎓 Course Selection Guidelines

**For Bachelor's Students:**
You are restricted to choosing courses only from the bachelor's level curriculum. Master's level courses are generally not open to undergraduate students. Exceptions are only considered if you are in your 4th year of an undergraduate program when you arrive in Mannheim.
*For questions, please contact the Business School Exchange Team via [incoming.business@uni-mannheim.de](mailto:incoming.business@uni-mannheim.de).*

**For Master's/MBA Students:**
You are eligible to choose Business courses from all master's level courses. If your home university permits, selected bachelor's level courses are also available to you.

### Area Management

- **Academic Readings:**
  - Battilana, J., & Lee, M. (2014). Advancing Research on Hybrid Organizing – Insights from the Study of Social Enterprises. *The Academy of Management Annals*, 8(1), 397–441. [DOI Link](https://doi.org/10.1080/19416520.2014.893615)
  - Battilana, J., Besharov, M., & Mitzinneck, B. (2017). On Hybrids and Hybrid Organizing: A Review and Roadmap for Future Research. In R. Greenwood, C. Oliver, T. B. Lawrence, & R. E. Meyer (Eds.), *The SAGE handbook of organizational institutionalism* (2nd ed., pp. 128–162). SAGE.
  - Thornton, P. H., Ocasio, W., & Lounsbury, M. (2012). Defining the Interinstitutional System. In P. H. Thornton, W. Ocasio, & M. Lounsbury (Eds.), *The Institutional Logics Perspective: A New Approach to Culture, Structure and Process* (pp. 50–75). [DOI Link](https://doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199601936.003.0003)
  - Besharov, M. L., & Smith, W. K. (2014). Multiple Institutional Logics in Organizations: Explaining Their Varied Nature and Implications. *Academy of Management Review*, 39(3), 364–381. [DOI Link](https://doi.org/10.5465/amr.2011.0431)
- **Course Structure:**
  - Setting: Group presentation (~3 students per group), with individual grading.
  - Presentation of a scientific paper (max. 12 minutes).
  - Subsequent questions/discussion (~20–30 minutes).
  - Application of knowledge from seminar to new settings.
  - Session 1: Kick-off, foundations of hybrid organizing (incl. sector model).
  - Session 2: Introduction to hybrid organizing in the context of institutional theory.
  - Session 4: Focus: Hybrid organizing in the nonprofit and public sector.
  - Session 5: Focus: Hybrid organizing in the for-profit sector.
  - Session 6: Focus: Hybrid organizing in social enterprises and beyond.

### Area Operations Management

- **Block 1:** Introduction to the topic and overview of airline functions, followed by self-organized group formation and the start of case work. Students can submit follow-up questions by email after this session, which will be discussed in the second block.
- **Block 2:** Recap of key concepts, discussion of submitted questions, and continued group work with the lecturer available for guidance.
- **Block 3:** Final group presentations and a plenary discussion of findings.
