## Research Focus

I am a Ph.D. candidate supervised by Prof. Simone Paolo Ponzetto and Prof. Goran Glavaš, focusing on neural methods for natural language understanding. My research centers on two main areas: Argument Mining and Language Representations.

**Argument Mining:**
The goal is to enable machines to understand human argumentation. This involves complex tasks such as identifying argumentative units (e.g., claims, premises) and relationships (e.g., attack, support), and predicting the overall argumentative quality of texts. I also apply these methods to scientific publication mining (in collaboration with Prof. Eckert, Stuttgart Media University), aiming to gain deeper insights into the argumentative nature of scientific writing—a field we call 'scitorics'.

**Language Representations and Ethics:**
Successful computational analysis of argumentation relies on high-quality language representations. I specialize in this area by injecting linguistic or common sense knowledge into text encoder models. Furthermore, I investigate the ethical dimensions of text representations, focusing on detecting and mitigating unfair biases such as sexism and racism through the development of debiasing algorithms.
