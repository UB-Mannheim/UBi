# Modeling Heterogeneity of Response Processes in Item Response Theory (IRT)

**Instructors:** Mirka Henninger (University of Zurich), Esther Ulitzsch (IPN Kiel), Thorsten Meiser (University of Mannheim)

This workshop provides a comprehensive overview of basic and advanced models of Item Response Theory (IRT) for cognitive and personality assessment.

## Workshop Content

### Introduction to IRT Models

The course begins with an introduction to fundamental IRT models for dichotomous and ordinal items. Topics covered include:

- Rasch model
- 2PL and 3PL models
- Generalized partial credit model
- Graded response model

The initial modules ensure that all participants acquire the necessary knowledge of essential models, concepts, and techniques required for advanced topics.

### Advanced Topics and Heterogeneity Modeling

Building upon the general IRT introduction, the workshop presents various extensions designed to analyze heterogeneity in latent response processes across individuals and/or items. Advanced models covered include:

- Multidimensional and mixture IRT models (for modeling individual response styles in personality assessment).
- Incorporating process data information.
- Accommodating disengaged responding in cognitive measures.
- Analyzing latent processes underlying fast or missing responses.

The workshop also covers advanced statistical methods, including Bayesian estimation procedures and extensions to IRT modeling derived from machine learning. All models are presented with their theoretical and statistical foundations and illustrated using real data.

### Practical Implementation

Model specification and estimation will be demonstrated using various R packages, and participants will engage in supervised hands-on exercises.

**Prerequisites:**
Participants are expected to have basic experience in using R and should bring their own notebook for the practical exercises. Required R packages will be installed during the workshop.
