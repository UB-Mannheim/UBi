### 📚 Core Methodological Courses (Applicable across Sociology, Political Science, Psychology)

#### 1. Current Research Perspectives

**Description:** This course introduces first-year CDSS doctoral students to the theoretically informed research approaches and substantive research fields that underpin social science research in Mannheim. A series of talks provides an overview of current scholarly debates and ongoing research in political science, psychology, and sociology. Faculty members present their research fields and provide an outlook on potential topics.
**Assignment:** Students must develop a research project idea informed theoretically or methodologically by insights from one or several CDSS faculty presentations (outside of their particular field). This idea must be written up, and a potential research design described in a short 3-page paper, due October 31st.

#### 2. Mathematics for Social Scientists

**Description:** This course provides an introduction to mathematical logic and rigor, covering fundamental mathematical concepts essential for modern social science. Topics include introductory set and function theory, analysis of functions, probability, and linear algebra (the basis of data analysis).
**Assessment:** The exam is scheduled for Tuesday, December 12, 2023, from 10 am to 12 pm in room 211 in B6, 30–32.
**Basic Readings:**

- Knut Sydsaeter and Peter Hammond. 2008. *Essential Mathematics for Economic Analysis*. 3rd edition. Harlow: Prentice Hall.
  **Additional Readings:**
- Alpha C. Chiang and Kevin Wainwright. 2005. *Fundamental Methods of Mathematical Economics*. 4th edition. Boston, Mass.: McGraw-Hill.
- Jeff Gill. 2006. *Essential Mathematics for Political and Social Research*. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.
- Malcolm Pemberton and Nicholas Rau. 2007. *Mathematics for Economists*. 2nd edition. Manchester: Manchester University Press.
- Carl P. Simon and Lawrence E. Blume. 1994. *Mathematics for Economists*. New York: W. W. Norton & Company. McGraw-Hill.

#### 3. Research Design

**Description:** This course helps students understand the core issues of research design—the plan for carrying out a project and using evidence to answer a question. Students learn to identify trade-offs in choosing a research design and develop potential research questions.
**Assessment:** Mandatory readings, active participation, homework assignments, presentation of a research proposal, and performing as a discussant of peers' proposals (term paper, circa 10 pages, graded).

#### 4. Bayesian Statistics in the Social Sciences

**Description:** This seminar introduces selected topics relating to Bayesian statistics in the social sciences. It provides insight into the current status of research in this field, helping to determine open questions and future research perspectives.
**Learning Outcomes:** Students will be able to present basic knowledge of Bayesian statistics, name methodological developments, describe empirical approaches, and critically evaluate the literature.
**Assessment:** Students write a term paper (5,000 words max.) either carrying out an empirical study using Bayesian methods OR conducting a critical literature review of Bayesian methods. The course is evaluated as 'pass/fail'.
**Taught by:** Prof. Joe Sakshaug ([Link](https://iab.de/en/employee/?id=76880)).

#### 5. Quantitative Methods in Political Science

**Description:** This course covers quantitative methods, starting with linear regression models (mathematical bases, estimation, interpretation, assumptions, and violations). The second half introduces likelihood theory, including models for binary and count data. The goal is to develop critical judgment in quantitative studies and provide skills for basic quantitative analysis.
**Assessment:** Homework, participation, and a take-home exam (graded).

#### 6. Causal Inference

**Description:** This course teaches fundamental concepts behind estimating causal effects, including potential obstacles to causal inference. Students learn to design and analyze experiments and apply techniques using real datasets in R, focusing on both randomized and observational settings.
**Assessment:** Lecture: Participation, term paper (graded). Tutorial: Homework, oral participation, presentation.
**Taught by:** Nan Zhang, PhD ([Link](https://nanzhangresearch.github.io/)).

#### 7. Multivariate Modeling

**Description:** This course provides an advanced overview of standard multivariate methods and current developments, including general and generalized linear models, structural equation models, and multilevel modeling. The goal is to bring PhD candidates to a common level of statistical knowledge and data analytic skills.
**Assessment:** Modules are held in three blocks (details below).

#### 8. Game Theory (Introduction/Advanced)

**Description:** This course provides a graduate-level introduction to formal modeling in political science. It covers concepts like normal form games, Nash equilibria, extensive form games, and signaling games. The focus is on training in reading and writing formal models.
**Assessment:** Working in small groups on assignments, online meetings on Zoom, and a final exam (graded).
**Literature:** Includes texts by McCarty et al. (2007), Tadelis (2013), and Osborne (2003).

### 🌐 Colloquia and Specialized Seminars

#### 1. CDSS Colloquia (Applicable to Political Science & Sociology)

CDSS doctoral students in political science and sociology can choose freely which weekly colloquium to attend. These colloquia must be attended regularly in year two and three of doctoral studies.
**Options:**

- MZES A Colloquium “European Societies and their Integration”
- MZES Colloquium B “European Political Systems and their Integration”
  **Details:** Refer to the [MZES webpage](http://www.mzes.uni-mannheim.de/d7/en/news/events/colloquium) for details.
  **Alternative:** Mannheim Research Colloquium on Survey Methods (MaRCS), announced via the Faculty of Social Sciences mailing list.
  **Requirement:** All 2nd and 3rd year students must submit a list of attended colloquia to receive 2 ECTS. Attendance of at least 80% is required.
  **Prerequisites:** Knowledge of statistics and empirical social research methods.

#### 2. Seminar: Educational Inequalities in Europe

**Description:** Discusses classic and recent theories of educational inequalities, trends over time, and cross-national variation in Europe. Focuses on selected dimensions of educational inequalities.
**Assessment:** Regular small assignments, compulsory attendance, active discussion, and a term paper (max. 5000 words, graded). Deadline: January 31, 2024.
