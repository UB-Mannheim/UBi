# Information Systems (IS)

Information Systems support individuals in organizations by providing information necessary for better or faster decision-making. They enable new business processes and business models, fundamentally altering the competitive landscape of a company. The discipline utilizes Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) for corporate activities from the perspective of distinct user roles. The nature of Information Systems is both theoretical and technological:

- **Theoretical-Empirical Contributions:** Focus on the management and use of Information Systems and Information Infrastructures within organizations.
- **Technological-Constructivist Contributions:** Aim at the design of Information Systems to facilitate new business processes and models.

## Core Research Fields

The research approach is complementary, combining both a theoretic-empirical and a technological-constructivist perspective. The fields can be grouped into the following major areas:

1. Development of Information Systems and Infrastructures
1. Innovative Information Systems
1. Management of Information Systems and Infrastructures
1. Electronic Business and Government

### 💻 Development and Design

In the context of Information Systems and Infrastructure Design, several interconnected themes are covered:

- **Software Product Management** and **Usability Engineering:** Investigate development processes with a specific focus on customer and end-user integration.
- **Component-based Software Development** and **Object Data Management:** Provide instruments for designing and developing specific business functions on the application layer, as well as managing underlying data.
- **Development Processes:** Research focuses on improving the productivity and impact of IS and Infrastructure development processes through better collaboration activities.

### 💡 Innovative Systems and Infrastructures

This area focuses on designing and evaluating new system concepts and solutions:

- **Distributed Systems** and **Context-Aware Computing:** Lay the foundation for inter-organizational Information Systems and Infrastructures that facilitate new forms of the value chain.
- **Middleware Layer:** Enables a modular and flexible design for contemporary application functions.
- **Enterprise Information Systems (EIS):** These systems, relying on enterprise software packages (e.g., Enterprise Resource Planning, Customer Relationship Management, Business Intelligence), form the backbone—and sometimes the differentiator—of high-performing corporations. Research takes an end-user perspective, covering social augmentation, information security, and flexibility.

### 📈 Management and Governance

This domain explores the full potential of IS research objects:

- **Information Management** and **Business Process Management (BPM):** Investigations are conducted to understand the "fit" between tasks, processes, technologies, and people.
- **IS Governance and IS Sourcing:** Complex arrangements are scrutinized on an infrastructure level.
- **Business Value of Information Systems:** Investigated to understand how IS can be deployed effectively to support corporate value creation.

### 🌐 Electronic Business and Government

IS and infrastructures facilitate new forms of **Electronic Business** and **Electronic Government**. Behavioristic empirical research investigates key drivers for efficient utilization, including:

- Adoption of system standards.
- Electronic business models.
- Cloud computing.
- Process virtualization.
- Online pricing and smart metering solutions.
