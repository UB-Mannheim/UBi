# CDSE Seminar Spring 2019

This document outlines the schedule and topics for the CDSE Seminar held in Spring 2019.

## Seminar Schedule Details

The seminars took place at L7, 3–5, PO44.

- **February 12, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Classroom composition, peer effects and teacher response
  - **Speakers:** Xi Lin, Mark Spils

- **February 19, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Classroom composition and the mental health of adolescents
  - **Speakers:** Michaela Paffenholz, Xi Lin

- **February 26, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Like mother, like daughter? Societal determinants of maternal labour supply – evidence from the German reunification
  - **Speakers:** Barbara Boelmann (University College London), Michaela Paffenholz

- **March 5, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Agriculture plagues and child health – evidence from a locust plague in Mali
  - **Speakers:** Bruno Conte (Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona), Mark Spils

- **March 26, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Demand for private disability insurance: Evidence from Germany
  - **Speakers:** Sebastian Seitz, Johannes Wacks

- **April 2, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Wealth inequality and earnings risk in an island model of the labor market
  - **Speakers:** Johannes Wacks, Timo Reinelt

- **April 9, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Wage inequality and occupation polarization: A dynamic perspective
  - **Speakers:** Tomás Martínez (University Carlos III de Madrid), Johannes Wacks

- **April 30, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** The misallocation channel of monetary policy
  - **Speakers:** Timo Reinelt, Alexander Matusche

- **May 7, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Does wealth inequality affect the transmission of monetary policy?
  - **Speakers:** Alexander Matusche, Sebastian Seitz

- **May 14, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Understanding housing wealth effects
  - **Speakers:** Frederick Zadow, Maximilian Jager

- **May 21, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** Exploiting your status-quo bias: Proposal for a laboratory experiment
  - **Speakers:** Mark Spils, Michaela Paffenholz

- **May 28, 2019** (3:30 pm – 5 pm)

  - **Topic:** The geographic complexity and structure of global banks: Implications for bank risk
  - **Speakers:** Maximilian Jager, Frederick Zadow
