## 💰 Accounting

### Mandatory Courses

- **ACC/TAX 910 Area Seminar**
- **ACC/TAX 920 Brown Bag Seminar**

### Core Research Methods

- **ACC 903 Empirical Accounting Research I: (Research Methods)**
  - **Description:** Provides a comprehensive overview of research topics and methods in influential seminal and contemporaneous papers in the empirical accounting literature. Topics covered include Earnings Management, Valuation, Voluntary/Mandatory Disclosure, International/Institutional Accounting, and Corporate Narratives. Students learn to critically evaluate research rigor and develop meaningful research ideas.
  - **Assessment:** Exam (90 minutes) 50%, Paper presentations 50%.
- **ACC 904 Empirical Accounting Research II: (Causal Inference)**

### Advanced/Specialized Courses

- **TAX 802 Applied Taxation Research I: Foundations and Core Methods**
  - **Focus:** Causal identification in empirical tax research.
  - **Content:** Covers conceptual foundations of business taxation and core empirical methods (potential outcome framework, surveys, difference-in-difference estimation).
  - **Assessment:** Presentation (40%), Essay (40%), Participation (20%).
- **TAX 803 Applied Taxation Research II: Advanced Methods and Own Research Topics**
  - **Focus:** Advanced causal inference and independent research project development.
  - **Content:** Builds on TAX 802, covering advanced methods like Regression Discontinuity (RDD), Instrumental Variables (IV), Discrete Choice Models, and the Bunching estimator. Students develop and write a full research paper on their own topic.
  - **Assessment:** Two Presentations (40%), Research Paper (40%), Participation (20%).

### Elective & Bridge Courses (Consolidated)

- **Bridge Course: Accounting/Taxation Focus (Causal Inference)**
  - **Description:** A hands-on, paper-based approach to data analysis. Focuses on the interplay between sampling and experimental methods, statistical inference, and estimation of causal effects. Uses the German Business Panel as a case study.
  - **Outcome:** Students present research papers and write a short technical report summarizing methods and implications.
- **Academic Writing Course**
- **FIN 803 Corporate Finance**
  - **Learning Outcomes:** Combines classic theory of modern corporate finance with empirical contributions. Students become familiar with the main empirical and theoretical tools used in corporate finance, suitable for first-year doctoral students.

## 🌐 Cross-Disciplinary & Social Science Bridge Courses

These courses are highly repetitive across the catalog and are consolidated here for clarity.

### 1. UN Peacekeeping and the Protection of Civilians

- **Focus:** An introduction to the UN’s role in maintaining peace and international security.
- **Content:** Examines the problems and possibilities of UN peace operations, from monitoring ceasefires to protecting civilians (POC).
- **Relevance:** Relevant for conflict or security studies, international organizations, and global governance.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar/Lecture format).

### 2. Comparative Political Behavior

- **Focus:** Theoretical approaches, key concepts, and substantive issues in comparative political behavior.
- **Content:** Provides an overview of key concepts and theories in analyzing micro-level processes of political behavior that feed into macro-level processes.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar/Lecture format).

### 3. Generative AI in Social Science Research

- **Focus:** Advanced empirical approaches to Generative AI in social science.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to present basic knowledge, name the latest developments, describe in-depth empirical approaches, and critically evaluate the literature and applications of Generative AI.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar/Lecture format).

### 4. Advanced Econometrics/Statistics (General)

- **Content:** Covers foundational texts and advanced topics in econometrics and statistics.
- **Required Reading:**
  - Cameron, A. Colin and Trivedi, Pravin K. (2005). *Microeconometrics: Methods and Applications*.
  - Green, William H. (2008). *Econometric Analysis* (sixth edition).
  - Wooldridge, Jeffrey M. (2001). *Econometric Analysis of Cross Section and Panel Data*.
- **Assessment:** N/A (Seminar/Lecture format).

### 5. Item Response Theory (IRT)

- **Content:** Advanced statistical modeling techniques.
- **Required Reading:**
  - Böckenholt, U., & Meiser, T. (2017). *Response style analysis with threshold and multi-process IRT models*.
  - Debelak, R., Strobl, C., & Zeigenfuse, M. (2022). *An introduction to the Rasch model with Examples in R*.
  - De Boeck, P., & Wilson, M. (2004). *Explanatory item response models*.
  - Chalmers, R. P. (2012). *mirt: A multidimensional item response theory package for the R environment*.
  - Embretson, S. E., & Reise, S. P. (2000). *Item response theory for psychologists*.
  - Rizopoulos, D. (2006). *ltm: An R package for latent variable modeling and item response analysis*.

## 🎓 Doctoral Research Skills (General)

These courses focus on developing core research skills applicable across all disciplines.

### 1. Advanced Data Science Lab II (Text Mining)

- **Focus:** Practical experience in scientific data-science projects.
- **Content:** Guides students through the typical steps of a data-science project, from problem formulation to analysis. Focuses on analyzing textual data (e.g., news, social media) using Natural Language Processing (NLP).
- **Assessment:** Written elaboration (90%) and presentation (10%).

### 2. Seminar: Sustainability and Clean Energy Technologies

- **Focus:** Latest advances and challenges associated with the transition towards a decarbonized energy economy.
- **Content:** Discusses the economics and management of sustainability activities and clean energy technologies.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Introduces current research paradigms and trains students in presenting and evaluating papers in seminars and conferences.
- **Assessment:** Participation (20%), Paper presentations and discussions (80%).

### 3. Seminar: Analytical Research Methods (Tax/Accounting Focus)

- **Focus:** Discussing recent advances in analytical accounting, tax, or organizations research.
- **Content:** Discussion centers on academic rigor, topic relevance, and writing style to prepare students for top peer-reviewed journals.
- **Assessment:** Participation (25%), Paper moderation (25%), and written assignment (50%).

### 4. Seminar: Research Skills and Academic Discourse (General)

- **Focus:** Developing critical thinking and communication skills for academic research.
- **Learning Outcomes:** Students learn to read and approach research papers, summarize main points, evaluate papers critically, and participate in scientific discourse.
- **Assessment:** Paper (referee report) 40%, Presentation 30%, Class Participation 30%.
