# Research Areas in Data and Web Science (DWS)

The central challenge of the modern information society is leveraging the massive volume of data available on the Web and within enterprise contexts. The **Data and Web Science Group** conducts research on methods for managing, integrating, and mining large amounts of heterogeneous information for companies, public institutions, and individuals.

Our research is focused on the following key areas:

- [Web-based Systems](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/web-based-systems-prof-bizer/) (Prof. Dr. Bizer)
- [Data Analytics](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/data-analytics-prof-gemulla/) (Prof. Dr. Gemulla)
- [Web Data Mining](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/web-data-mining-prof-paulheim/) (Prof. Dr. Paulheim)
- [Natural Language Processing (NLP)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/natural-language-processing-and-information-retrieval-prof-ponzetto/) (Prof. Dr. Ponzetto)
- [Artificial Intelligence (AI)](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/artificial-intelligence-prof-stuckenschmidt/) (Prof. Dr. Stuckenschmidt)
- [Process Analytics](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/process-analytics-prof-van-der-aa/) (Prof. Dr. Van der Aa)
- [Responsible AI & Digital Health](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/professors/prof-dr-philipp-kellmeyer/) (Jun.-Prof. Dr. Kellmeyer)
- [Computer Vision & Machine Learning](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/computer-vision-machine-learning-prof-dr-ing-margret-keuper/) (Prof. Dr.-Ing. Keuper)

## Detailed Research Focus Areas

### Web-based Systems (Prof. Bizer)

This focus area explores technical and empirical questions concerning the development of global, decentralized information environments. Our research tracks the evolution of the World Wide Web from a simple document publication medium into a global dataspace. We monitor the adoption of Semantic Markup and Linked Data technologies on the Web. Our technical work focuses on integrating data from large numbers of Web data sources, covering topics such as information extraction, identity resolution, schema matching, data fusion, and data search. We apply these methods to tasks like integrating product data from e-shops and creating large-scale knowledge bases (e.g., DBpedia). [More information...](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/web-based-systems-prof-bizer/)

### Data Analytics (Prof. Gemulla)

Our group researches systems and methods for analyzing and learning from large datasets, including practical applications. Key areas include:

- Machine learning with semi-structured/structured data.
- Combining unstructured and structured knowledge.
- Representation learning for multi-relational graphs.
- Efficient and scalable methods and systems for data-intensive processing.

### Web Data Mining (Prof. Paulheim)

We research methods for mining knowledge from large amounts of structured and unstructured data on the Web. To address the challenges of Web-scale data mining (size, heterogeneity, and dynamics), we focus on supervised or unsupervised methods that combine logical and statistical inference. Existing and automatically acquired knowledge is used to facilitate data integration, enrichment, and cleansing, and to bootstrap the overall data mining process. [More information...](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/web-data-mining-prof-paulheim/)

### Natural Language Processing (Prof. Ponzetto)

The NLP and IR group conducts research on knowledge acquisition and Natural Language Processing (NLP), applying these methods to support empirical research in Computational Social Sciences and Digital Humanities. We investigate a wide range of text understanding techniques—from representation learning and distributional semantics to symbolic, entity-based approaches leveraging wide-coverage knowledge graphs. Applications include computational semantics, multilinguality, information retrieval, and multimodal NLP. [More information...](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/natural-language-processing-and-information-retrieval-prof-ponzetto/)

### Artificial Intelligence (Prof. Stuckenschmidt)

We are an internationally visible research group in AI, comprising approximately 25 researchers and PhD students. We develop and improve AI methods in:

- Machine Learning from Structured Data.
- Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning and Learning.
- Fairness and Explainable Artificial Intelligence.

We also focus on real-world AI applications, cooperating with companies and researchers from other disciplines. These applications include:

- Business Process Analysis and Optimization.
- Data-Driven Demand Forecasting and Supply-Chain Management.
- Human Activity and Goal Recognition.
- Smart Cities, Smart Mobility, and Smart Logistics.

We maintain close cooperation with the AI Systems Engineering Group and regional organizations like SAP and the City of Mannheim. [More information...](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/research/focus-groups/artificial-intelligence-prof-stuckenschmidt/)

### Process Analytics (Prof. Van der Aa)

The Process Analytics group researches the interplay between business processes and information systems. We focus on developing automated methods for data-driven process analysis. Our research areas include process analysis, process mining, and robotic process automation. Primary interests include, but are not limited to, conformance checking, natural language processing, event abstraction, data privacy, and data uncertainty.

### Responsible AI & Digital Health (Jun.-Prof. Dr. Kellmeyer)

The responsible development of AI systems is a critical objective given the accelerating innovation dynamics of AI. In a highly inter- and transdisciplinary program, Jun.-Prof. Dr. Kellmeyer combines research on conceptual foundations with empirical mixed-methods research on human-technology interaction, with a specific focus on AI systems in digital health.

### Computer Vision & Machine Learning (Prof. Keuper)

Our research interests lie in Computer Vision and Machine Learning, with applications across various fields. Key research topics include:

- Improving Model Robustness and Generalization Ability.
- Neural Architecture Search.
- Grouping Problems (e.g., Image and Motion Segmentation, Multiple Object Tracking).
- Efficient Solvers for Large Grouping Problems.
- Motion Estimation and Motion Segmentation.
- Image Analysis and Image Generation/Deep Fake Detection.
