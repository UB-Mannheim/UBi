# Thesis Guidelines (Bachelor and Master)

These guidelines detail the process for students wishing to write their Master or Bachelor thesis under the supervision of a DWS professor.

## 1. Application Requirements

To apply for a thesis, you must submit the following documents:

- Curriculum Vitae (CV)
- Transcript of Records
- **Master Candidate Profile form** (Required only for Master thesis applications)

**Note on Company Cooperation:**
If your thesis involves cooperation with a company, you must include the company's name and contact details. Ensure the company can provide all necessary resources (e.g., datasets, servers). Before starting, the company must sign a **supervision agreement**.

**Topic Selection:**
You may suggest your own topic, or you can choose from topics suggested by the department (which are always available). If choosing from suggested topics, please detail your general interests on the Master Candidate Profile form.

## 3. Topic Proposal (Exposé)

Before starting the thesis, you must submit a convincing topic proposal (exposé).

**Guidelines:**

- The proposal must align with the [SMART criteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SMART_criteria).
- Use the official [proposal template](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Einrichtungen/dws/Files_Teaching/Theses/proposal_template.zip).
- The proposal must not exceed 2 pages (excluding references).
- Focus on *what* you want to do, rather than spending excessive space on preliminaries.
- It is recommended to discuss the focus and scope with your advisor.

**Structure:**
The proposal must be divided into three subsections:

1. **Introduction:** A short sketch (1 paragraph) of the topic.
1. **Background:** A compact description of relevant concepts and a scientific chain of arguments leading to the proposal's "what and why." (Introduction and Background combined should not exceed one page).
1. **Goals and Work Plan:** This is the most important section. It must express and motivate the tasks, goals, and research questions, and outline the plan to answer them.
   - Demonstrate a clear understanding of the scope, tasks, and challenges (e.g., possible approaches, outcomes, evaluation, planned time).
   - Structure this section by main research questions or tasks, including rough time estimates and milestones.
   - *Note:* Technical details (formulas, algorithms, long lists) should be avoided; prefer high-level, intuitive descriptions.

## 4. Registration

Once your proposal has been accepted, immediately register your thesis with the [secretary of the respective chair](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/administration/). This step fixes the topic, advisor, starting date, and end date.

## 5. Supervision

- **Meetings:** You are responsible for scheduling all meetings.
- **Progress:** Schedule regular meetings with your advisor to discuss progress.
- **Milestones:** Schedule milestone meetings with the professor/senior advisor approximately every other month. These meetings require a short presentation with well-prepared slides and meaningful results demonstrating progress.
- **Preparation:** Send presentation slides to the professor/senior advisor beforehand.
- *Note:* Advisors generally will not read drafts of your thesis, nor will they write or review your code.

## 6. Writing the Report

The report is the primary document for grading and must be written in English using the provided [LATEX template](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/media/Einrichtungen/dws/Files_Teaching/Theses/dws-templates.zip).

**Writing Tips:**

- Start writing early and keep detailed notes, including parameter settings for all experiments.
- Discuss the thesis outline with your advisor.
- Include an abstract.
- The target audience is assumed to have a similar background in computer science; avoid repeating basic textbook material.
- Stay concise and to the point. Use an appendix for low-level details (e.g., long code listings).
- It is recommended to read [Writing for Computer Science](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4471-6639-9) by Justin Zobel.
- Use examples and figures throughout.

## 7. Submission

Submit the following materials:

1. **Printed Copies:** Two printed copies of the thesis to the [secretary's office](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/people/administration/).
1. **Digital Copy:** A PDF version submitted via e-mail.
1. **Appointment:** Make an appointment upfront.
1. **Supplementary Materials:** Submit the source code, data, and instructions for running the code (on a CD-ROM, memory stick, or online repository like GitHub). If the data is too large, include a relevant sample. Discuss submission details with your supervisor.

*If a certificate of passing prior to final grading is required, inform the department at least two weeks before submission. The earliest possible date for this certificate is two weeks after submission.*

## 8. Hardware Usage

If your private computer is insufficient, you may utilize [powerful hardware](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/dws/teaching/large-computations/) remotely.
