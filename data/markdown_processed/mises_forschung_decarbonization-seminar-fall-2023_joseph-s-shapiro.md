### Seminar Abstract

This seminar examines how multinational production affects climate change. Microdata reveal that the emission rate of a foreign-owned plant increases with the emission rate of its home country, suggesting that firms bring green technology with them when operating abroad. The presentation develops and quantifies a multi-country general equilibrium model of multinational production, trade, and energy to assess how policies encouraging multinational production would affect global carbon emissions and welfare.

### Speaker Biography

**Joseph S. Shapiro** is an Associate Professor at the University of California, Berkeley, specializing in Agricultural & Resource Economics and the Department of Economics. He holds several roles, including Associate Editor of the *Journal of Political Economy* and Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research.

His research agenda focuses on three key areas:

1. The interaction between globalization and the environment.
1. The effectiveness, efficiency, and equity impacts of environmental and energy policies over the last half century (particularly concerning water, air, and climate pollution).
1. The importance of investments made by people to protect themselves against air pollution and climate change.

Shapiro holds a Ph.D. in economics from MIT, and Master's degrees from Oxford and LSE.
