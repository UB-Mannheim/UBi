# People Analytics Research Projects

The following projects explore the intersection of data science, machine learning, and human capital management, providing insights into modern organizational challenges.

## 📊 Private Equity Buyouts and Employee Health

**Authors:**

- Pilar Garcia-Gomez
- [Ernst Maug](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/en/maug/)
- Stefan Obernberger

**Cooperation Partner:**

- Chair of Corporate Finance

**Description:**
This research examines the role of employee health in Private Equity buyouts. Using employee-level data (including employment, wages, and medical prescriptions), the study conducts matched-sample difference-in-differences estimations involving over 55,000 buyout employees.

**Key Findings:**

- Employees with lower health status before the buyout face the most substantial losses of income and employment during and after the buyout.
- Buyouts do not measurably impact health outcomes or expenditures.
- While buyouts influence career paths, the health of unemployed employees deteriorates, whereas those who find new jobs show improvement.
- The study concludes that buyout-related restructuring has a stronger negative impact on the human capital of employees with pre-existing health problems.

## 🤖 Rethinking the Gold Standard With Multi-armed Bandits: Machine Learning Allocation Algorithms for Experiments

**Authors:**

- [Prof. Torsten Biemann](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/biemann/team/lehrstuhl/prof-dr-torsten-biemann/)
- Chris Kaibel

**Cooperation Partner:**

- Chair of Business Administration, Human Resource Management and Leadership

**Description:**
Traditional randomized controlled trials (RCTs) allocate subjects randomly and equally, failing to utilize new information gathered during the experiment. This project proposes a methodological improvement using machine learning.

**Methodology & Findings:**

- The researchers suggest a Bayesian multi-armed bandit algorithm for subject allocation in RCTs, drawing inspiration from computer science and medicine.
- Monte Carlo simulations compare this approach to fixed, balanced subject allocation.
- The findings indicate that a randomized allocation based on Bayesian multi-armed bandits is more efficient and ethical in most settings.
- Recommendations for researchers are provided, along with a discussion of limitations.

**Publications & Tools:**

- **Journal Publication:** Results were accepted for publication by the journal *Organisational Research Methods*: [https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1094428119854153](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1094428119854153)
- **Related Tool:** A deployment tool for Multi-armed bandits is available here: [https://randomization-with-bandits.shinyapps.io/bayesian-bandit/](https://randomization-with-bandits.shinyapps.io/bayesian-bandit/)

## 🧑‍💻 Who Should Judge Me When I’m Different? Hiring Algorithms and Applicant Perceptions

**Authors:**

- [Prof. Torsten Biemann](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/biemann/team/lehrstuhl/prof-dr-torsten-biemann/)
- [Dr. Chris Kaibel](https://lsz.at/speaker/chris-kaibel)
- [Dr. Irmela Koch-Bayran](https://www.bwl.uni-mannheim.de/biemann/team/wissenschaftliche-mitarbeiter/dr-irmela-koch-bayram/)
- [Max Mühlenbock](https://www.goinger-kreis.de/dr-max-muehlenbock.html)

**Description:**
While algorithm-based decisions offer a data-driven alternative to subjective human judgment in recruitment, the question remains whether these algorithms genuinely help organizations attract and recruit a diverse workforce with unique talents.

**Study Focus:**

- Building on procedural justice theory, two experimental studies were conducted to examine how applicants perceive selection processes managed by algorithms versus human decision-makers.

**Key Findings:**

- Applicants do not automatically perceive algorithms as purely positive or negative.
- Perceptions differ depending on the outcome variables and are influenced by the applicants’ personal experiences and beliefs.
- While algorithms may help attract applicants who have experienced discrimination, they appear to discourage applicants with unique characteristics.

**Publication:**

- Outcomes were published in the "Best Paper Proceedings" of the Academy of Management 2019.
